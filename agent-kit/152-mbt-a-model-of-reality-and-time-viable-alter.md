# A Model of Reality and Time: Viable Alternate Realities and Histories

## In one paragraph
Thomas Campbell continues building his computational model of reality by describing how PMR (Physical Matter Reality) was folded into the larger process of progressing OS (Our System) through successive M generations, beginning with what he calls the Big Digital Bang. He argues that TBC (The Big Computer) generates and stores every possible state vector — actualized and unactualized — of OS's history, present, and probable futures, all with equal ontological reality, differing only in whether our free will choices actualized them. He distinguishes rule-driven possibilities (physical/natural randomness governed by the space-time rule-set) from choice-driven possibilities (free will decisions of sentient beings, individually or in groups), and explains how their interplay, filtered through a psi uncertainty principle, produces the uncertain-yet-personally-focused texture of PMR experience. He closes by insisting that none of this requires anything mystical, perfect, or infinite — only a vastly more capable version of ordinary bookkeeping and computer science, comparable in scale to a petri dish or an aquarium of guppies.

## Core claims
- Thomas Campbell claims that the process of incrementing OS forward through M generations came to include PMR at the moment of the "Big Digital Bang" (also called the Big Set of Initial Conditions or the Big Start of the Simulation), when TBC began applying the space-time rule-set to given initial conditions one DELTA-t at a time.
- He argues that our physical universe is "big" only from our own (PMR) perspective; from the Big Picture it is exactly as small and insignificant as it is grand from within.
- He claims that when PMR was mostly rapidly changing matter and energy, the Fundamental Process generated all possible alternative space-time realities by sampling over randomness, but propagated forward only those universes whose PMRs proved uniquely useful, productive, or significant — one of which became our universe.
- He notes that simulation time can run much slower or faster than real-time, and that any result or intermediary state may be modified, saved, or deleted, giving an appreciation of the control AUM has over the eventual development of our local PMR and the evolution of the space-time rule-set (cross-referencing Chapter 5 and Topic 2 of Chapter 7 of this book).
- He asserts that AUM initiated, ran, and reran the simulation process (possibly many times), modifying, saving, or deleting results, until the initial conditions and rule-sets evolved to the point of supporting an effective "top-notch learning laboratory" for the consciousness cycle.
- He claims that when sentient beings from NPMRN began profitably inhabiting the evolving virtual PMR, their free-will-driven interactions generated the probable-reality state vectors and alternate reality state vectors that became the predecessors of our present OS.
- He adds that some sentient beings within NPMRN who existed before our PMR's digital Big Bang were themselves part of OS by virtue of their involvement in planning and executing OS's origins — "the seemingly mystical beginnings of our universe" — which he says is mystical only from a PMR perspective.
- He states that a "still living and still potent" history of OS — both actualized and unactualized — persists in TBC, available from NPMRN for inspection and for "what-if" tours of the probabilities, forming a huge database for data mining.
- He argues that unactualized history threads (different sequences of events that did not happen) can share a common state with our actualized history at some DELTA-t and then diverge, and that studying these what-if world-lines is "an excellent educational and evaluative tool" for revealing the long-term impact of choices.
- He claims each state vector carries an expectation value fixing its likelihood given its parent's initial conditions, and that a steady, low-noise, focused consciousness can intentionally thread its way through unactualized consecutive reality states.
- He asserts that a novice explorer of the RWW (reality-wide-web) network can easily get lost among these parallel/alternate realities, retrieving paranormal information that feels correct at the time but is later found false, inconsistent, or of little PMR value.
- He claims an unintentional experience of an unactualized computational reality is indistinguishable from ordinary present experience, disappearing only when focus shifts back to PMR — analogous to how PMR itself fades when awareness is absorbed by a mantra, a problem, entertainment, or drugs.
- He argues that actualized and unactualized state vectors are of "equal reality and validity" and identical data type within TBC; the only difference is us — which possibilities our free will intent actually actualized.
- He distinguishes rule-driven possibilities (physical/natural randomness such as radioactive decay, earthquakes, thunderstorms, governed strictly by the space-time rule-set) from choice-driven possibilities (free will choices of sentient beings, individually or as groups/organizations, described as a superposition of all relevant free wills).
- He claims sentient beings contain small random components in intent and larger random ones in perception/interpretation/reaction, and that while randomness can dominate short-term experience, the quality of one's choices is a much bigger driver of long-term outcomes; he calls random components "spice, not the main ingredients." On the up-side, he adds, randomness helps deliver an interesting, ever-changing array of experiential opportunities to which we interactively apply our free will intent at whatever level of quality we have thus far generated.
- He states that when free will meets an opportunity to interact with the internal or external environment, consciousness has the chance to grow, stagnate, or degenerate, and that this is why a virtual physical PMR-type learning lab — with straightforward interactive opportunities, clear results, and feedback available everywhere we focus our attention — is so useful; PMR is constrained to be a highly interactive experience at its most basic level of relationship.
- He argues interacting with other PMR players — an interaction of free wills — is "the main thing we do here in PMR" and the basis of the opportunity to evolve consciousness quality.
- He claims that group/organizational choice-driven possibilities are defined by the superposition of all sentient free will choices that influence that organization's actions, and that motivations/intents with large random components reflect a less developed (higher-entropy) consciousness, whether individual or group.
- He asserts that some clear, high-probability future events (e.g., "potential coming earth changes") derive their strong probability signals from an interactive mix of rule-driven and choice-driven calculations.
- He claims the psi uncertainty principle is precisely what allows NPMRN planning and manipulation to influence PMR happenings without becoming obvious or violating the space-time rule-set and PMR causality.
- He states that other PMRs (PMRK) within NPMRN run on TBC in a manner analogous to a dedicated server/calculation-space within a larger AUM mainframe process, all accessible through the same NPMR communications net and available on the RWW.
- He claims the "clever evaluative software" that filters non-productive, insignificant, redundant, meaningless, or useless reality states is not fundamentally more mystical than PMR artificial intelligence/expert systems, relying on evaluative criteria, pattern recognition, history files, statistics, and predictive algorithms, with accuracy depending on how detailed and complete its knowledge base is.
- He explicitly states he has not invoked, and will not invoke, any perfect or infinite process: OS contains finite beings, objects, choices, and random events, making the (large but finite) number of unactualized past and future state vectors computationally manageable, especially once non-interactive subsets of reality are handled separately.
- He argues that any sense that the storage/processing requirements are too large to be workable stems from a mindset "stuck in the PMR of the twentieth century"; TBC's speed and capacity, though finite, are so vastly greater than human imagination that the computational burden is "almost trivial," yet still not mystical or supernatural.
- He closes by likening the whole system, from the perspective of My Big TOE, to a straightforward bookkeeping/computer-science problem roughly the size of a petri dish, or as simple as raising guppies in an aquarium.

## Concepts
- **OS (Our System)** — the overall reality-system (physical plus nonphysical, actualized plus probable) that we belong to; progressed forward through successive M generations of calculation.
- **M generations** — the successive computational generations through which OS is progressed forward in order to represent everything significant that can possibly happen.
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — our physical universe/reality, described here as a virtual reality generated within the space-time simulation section of TBC, beginning with the Big Digital Bang.
- **Big Digital Bang / Big Set of Initial Conditions / Big Start of the Simulation** — Campbell's terms for the origin point at which TBC began applying the space-time rule-set to given initial conditions, incrementing PMR forward in time; he notes "Big Bang" is a catchy label even though the "bang" is only a digital event in calculation-space.
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — the all-encompassing computational substrate running the space-time simulation (and other PMRs); described as having far greater computational capacity and throughput than any human simulation, and as being organized so each PMR (PMRK) runs like a dedicated server/calculation-space within a larger AUM mainframe process.
- **DELTA-t** — the discrete time-step increment by which TBC advances the simulation, "one DELTA-t at a time," according to the space-time rule-set.
- **AUM** — the source/experimenter whose "continuing experiment in consciousness" is served by initiating the virtual material part of PMR; AUM is described as having initiated, run, and rerun the simulation process, with full control to modify, save, or delete results.
- **Fundamental Process** — named here as the driving mechanism ("always... in the driver's seat") that generated all possible alternative space-time realities by sampling over the randomness of material and energetic processes, propagating forward only those universes whose PMRs proved uniquely useful, productive, or significant.
- **Space-time rule-set** — the set of rules governing allowed interactions of matter/energy and their evolution within PMR; described (with reference to Chapters 5 and 7) as itself capable of evolving across simulation runs, and as the strict constraint under which objects/energy "choose" their paths.
- **NPMRN** — the nonphysical reality domain from which sentient beings inhabit/use PMR profitably; their free will choices interacting with PMR objects and each other generated the probable and alternate reality state vectors that became OS's predecessors; NPMRN also holds the "living history" of OS available for what-if tours.
- **State vector** — a data unit representing a particular reality state (of matter, energy, and sentient players) at a point along a history thread; each carries an associated expectation value fixing its likelihood given its parent's initial conditions.
- **Expectation value** — the calculated likelihood/probability weight of a given state vector's occurrence, derived from the initial conditions of its parent state.
- **Unactualized states / unactualized history threads** — chains of state vectors representing significant choices that could have been made differently, forming alternate sequences of events that did not happen but remain stored in TBC and NPMRN as data of equal validity to the actualized history.
- **History thread** — the continuous sequence of state vectors, actualized or not, that traces a particular path of events through time; multiple unactualized threads may share a common state with the actualized OS history at some DELTA-t before diverging.
- **What-if world-lines / sequential traces** — the studied paths through the unactualized state vector database, used as "an excellent educational and evaluative tool" for revealing the long-term impact of decisions, choices, and intents.
- **RWW (reality-wide-web) network** — the network through which one can explore (or get lost in) parallel/alternate/divergent realities; novice explorers may retrieve paranormal information from it that later proves false, inconsistent, or of little PMR value.
- **Parallel, divergent, or alternate realities** — from our view, the label for sequences of related unactualized states; computationally viable within TBC and populated by mathematical (statistical simulation) representations of sentient players, objects, and energy.
- **Rule-driven possibilities** — outcomes of physical or natural randomness (e.g., radioactive decay, earthquakes, thunderstorms) governed strictly by the space-time rule-set, not reflecting the moral quality of any chooser's consciousness.
- **Choice-driven possibilities** — outcomes derived from the free will choices of sentient beings, individually or (as a superposition of all relevant free wills) collectively through groups, clubs, organizations, or governments.
- **Superposition of free will choices** — Campbell's description of how group/organizational choice-driven possibilities are defined: as the combined effect of all the free will choices of all sentient beings in OS with influence over that organization's actions.
- **Probable reality surfaces** — (referenced back to Chapter 7) the surfaces whose peaks/lumps represent the expectation value of a particular event, calculated for each successive value of m; their uncertainty arises from the interplay of choice-driven and rule-driven actualization.
- **Psi uncertainty principle** — the principle of uncertainty that permits planning and manipulations originating in NPMRN to influence PMR happenings without being obvious, i.e., without stepping outside the space-time rule-set or violating PMR causality.
- **PMRK** — Campbell's notation for the other, different physical-matter reality systems (besides our own PMR) existing within NPMRN, each running on its own dedicated TBC calculation-space but connected via the same NPMR communications net and accessible on the RWW.
- **NPMR communications net** — the shared network connecting all PMRK systems within NPMRN, making them all available for inspection and participation via the RWW.
- **Evaluative software** — the "clever" filtering system that determines which reality states are "significant" (versus non-productive, redundant, meaningless, or useless) using evaluative criteria, pattern recognition, history files, statistics, and predictive algorithms; likened to PMR artificial intelligence/expert systems, with accuracy dependent on the completeness of its knowledge base.
- **Finite computation constraint** — Campbell's explicit claim that OS/TBC involves no perfect or infinite processes: finite beings, objects, choices, and random events, yielding a finite (if enormous) number of unactualized past/future state vectors, and made further tractable by handling non-interactive subsets of reality separately.
- **Data matrix** — the accumulated database (within TBC/NPMRN) of everything that could possibly have happened since PMR's creation, forming the resource used for "data mining" of alternate histories.
- **Petri dish / aquarium-guppies analogy** — Campbell's closing scale analogy: from the perspective of My Big TOE, the entire mechanism is "a relatively small (about the size of a petri dish) and relatively simple (like raising guppies in an aquarium) problem" in bookkeeping and computer science.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- No new fieldwork, experiments, or informant cases are presented in this unit; it is a theoretical/model-building chapter. Campbell instead uses illustrative analogies and thought experiments to make the model concrete:
  - **E = mc² / m = E/c²** — invoked as the physics relation he uses to frame the Big Digital Bang as beginning with "a huge virtual space-time energy... at a point (or within a small volume)."
  - **The "movie/actor/audience" analogy** — Campbell compares the experience of an unactualized computational reality to "seeing a movie where you are simultaneously an actor (exercising free will to drive the story line) as well as a member of the audience," to explain why such experiences feel indistinguishable from real PMR experience.
  - **The mantra/drug/entertainment comparison** — he notes that an unintentional excursion into unactualized reality "disappears when you shift your focus back to PMR," just as PMR itself can seem to disappear when awareness is absorbed by "a mantra, a problem, entertainment, or befuddled by drugs" — used to illustrate how focus of attention determines which reality is experienced as "real."
  - **Radioactive decay, earthquakes, thunderstorms** — cited as examples of rule-driven (not choice-driven) possibilities, i.e., outcomes of physical/natural randomness under the space-time rule-set rather than free will.
  - **"Potential coming earth changes"** (cross-referenced to Chapters 3 and 7) — given as an example of a relatively clear, high-signal future event whose strong probability arises from a mix of rule-driven and choice-driven calculation.
  - **Petri dish / guppies-in-an-aquarium analogy** — used at the close of the chapter to cut the whole model down to a manageable, non-mystical scale.
  - Campbell repeatedly cross-references his own earlier chapters as the model's supporting material rather than external evidence: Chapter 4 (other PMRs evolving in their own dimensions), Chapters 5 and 7/Topic 2 (space-time rule-set, probable reality surfaces), Chapter 7/Topic 8 (interactive subsets of NPMRN and the physical universe), and Chapters 18 and 20 of Book 1 (what counts as "mystical" is relative to perspective).

## Thread
This unit sits in Book 3's Section 5 ("Inner Space, the Final Frontier"), where Campbell moves from establishing the reality-system model (state vectors, probable reality surfaces, the space-time rule-set introduced earlier in Book 2/Section 4) to fleshing out its temporal and modal mechanics: how PMR was folded into OS's forward progression via the Big Digital Bang, how TBC stores and can regenerate any point on the history thread, and how unactualized alternate/parallel realities and histories are just as computationally real as the actualized one. It builds directly on the earlier chapters it repeatedly cross-references (Chapters 3, 4, 5, 7, and 18/20 of Book 1) for terms like the space-time rule-set, probable reality surfaces, and the relativity of "mystical" experience. It sets up the distinction between rule-driven and choice-driven possibilities and the psi uncertainty principle as the mechanism allowing NPMRN influence on PMR without violating causality — machinery that supports his broader claim (reiterated at the chapter's close) that the entire system, however vast, is finite, non-mystical, and modest in scale ("petri dish," "aquarium of guppies"), a claim that continues to underwrite his project of naturalizing paranormal and mystical phenomena as computational rather than supernatural.
