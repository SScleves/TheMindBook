# Dedications

## In one paragraph
This unit is the book's dedications page, split into two personal statements — one from each co-author — followed by the shared exclamation "Ad Astra!". Tom DeLonge dedicates the book to his children, Ava and Jonas, framing them as standing alongside their whole generation, which he says is "destined to learn more about who we really are." Peter Levenda offers a double dedication: first to everyone who has experienced "the Phenomenon" but has been ignored, ridiculed, or targeted "in the name of national security" without ever learning why, and second to the government and military figures now coming forward to bear witness at the risk of their reputations and livelihoods. In a few lines the page previews the book's core tensions: experiencers versus official secrecy, whistleblowers versus institutional risk, and a truth the authors say we both desperately need to hear and desperately fear to know.

## Core claims
- Tom DeLonge claims that his children's generation is "destined to learn more about who we really are" — an implied claim that disclosure of some deeper truth about humanity is coming for the next generation.
- Peter Levenda claims that people who have experienced the Phenomenon "in one way or another" have been ignored, ridiculed, or targeted in the name of national security — and that these experiencers "still don’t know why."
- Levenda claims that people in government and the military are "now coming forward to bear witness," and that doing so risks their reputations and their livelihood.
- Levenda claims the truth these witnesses carry is one "we desperately need to hear and just as desperately fear to know" — casting the coming disclosure as both necessary and frightening.

## Concepts
- **the-phenomenon** — Levenda's capitalized term (used without definition here) for whatever experiencers have encountered "in one way or another"; the dedication treats it as a real class of experience whose witnesses have been suppressed in the name of national security. The term's content is left for the body of the book.
- **national-security** — invoked here as the stated justification under which experiencers of the Phenomenon have been "ignored, ridiculed, or targeted"; the dedication frames it as the cover for suppression, setting up the book's secrecy-and-control theme.
- **ad-astra** — the closing exclamation "Ad Astra!" (Latin, "to the stars"), used as the authors' shared sign-off aspiration on the dedications page.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- No named cases or episodes appear, but the page identifies two concrete groups Levenda dedicates the book to: (1) experiencers of the Phenomenon who have been ignored, ridiculed, or targeted in the name of national security and still don’t know why; and (2) current government and military witnesses coming forward at risk to their reputations and livelihoods. These two groups foreshadow the book's cast of suppressed experiencers and modern whistleblowers.
- DeLonge's dedication names his daughter Ava and son Jonas as stand-ins for their entire generation, whom he says are destined to learn "who we really are."

## Key quotes
- "as they both stand in parallel to everyone else from their generation who are destined to learn more about who we really are." — DeLonge on his children Ava and Jonas, framing disclosure as generational.
- "To all those who have experienced the Phenomenon in one way or another but who have been ignored, ridiculed, or targeted in the name of national security … and who still don’t know why." — Levenda's first dedication, to experiencers.
- "risking their reputations and their livelihood for the sake of a truth we desperately need to hear and just as desperately fear to know." — Levenda's second dedication, to government and military witnesses.
- "Ad Astra!" — the closing exclamation of the dedications page.

## Thread
As the opening front-matter unit, the dedications compress the book's whole argument into miniature: there is a Phenomenon, its experiencers have been suppressed under a national-security rationale, insiders are now bearing witness, and the truth is both needed and feared. It sets the emotional and moral stakes for the Introduction ("Welcome to the War Room") and for the book's running theme of official secrecy and control, later crystallized in the Afterword's "Alien in the Panopticon" essay. DeLonge's generational framing also seeds the book's forward-looking disclosure arc.

## Connections
- Within this book: anticipates the Introduction (Welcome to the War Room) and the Afterword (The Alien in the Panopticon), where the national-security/secrecy theme flagged here is developed; the "witnesses coming forward" line anticipates the modern disclosure-movement material in the later chapters.
- Cross-book (speculative/thematic, not the authors' reference): the idea of a suppressed truth about "who we really are" thematically echoes the consciousness-and-reality reframings in the-holographic-universe and my-big-toe, and the hidden-layer-of-reality motif in the-simulation-hypothesis.
