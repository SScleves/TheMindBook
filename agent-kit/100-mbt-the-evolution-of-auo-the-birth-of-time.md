# Ch 29 — The Evolution of AUO: The Birth of Time

## In one paragraph
Campbell narrates the next step in the evolution of AUO (Absolute Unbounded Oneness): the invention of time. By getting some group of its reality cells to oscillate regularly, AUO evolves a clock, and time — which Campbell insists is a technology, a construct, a byproduct of change within an evolving consciousness — is born. Time proves enormously profitable evolutionarily: ordered events let sequences carry content, enable communication between AUO's parts, and catalyze entropy reduction. Campbell then argues that consciousness evolution, being bodiless, accelerates far faster than biological evolution, using the runaway acceleration of human technology as his illustrative (and cautionary) example. As complexity grows, second-order functions (intention, feedback, interaction, synthesis, integration) and then third-order functions (values, aesthetics, enjoyment, quality, moral choice) emerge, so AUO evolves values, play, compassion, and creativity on a scale we cannot imagine. The chapter closes by warning against anthropomorphizing AUO and by foreshadowing the claim that AUO's consciousness is fundamental while ours is derivative.

## Core claims
- Campbell claims AUO creates time simply by oscillating some individual or group of reality cells more or less regularly; these clock cells "keep time for everything else" like a metronome, and evolution ("works better" is what drives it) pushes toward a highly regulated constant frequency — the best clock oscillates as efficiently, regularly, and quickly as possible (its natural frequency).
- Campbell argues time is not fundamental but "a technology, a construct of a self-modifying evoking consciousness, an artifact of a system of energy improving its internal organization." Change creates the notion of time; time separates the "before" state from the "after" state; awareness of change necessitates the idea of a personal time.
- Campbell says the origin of the very first modification of AUO's oneness is unknowable — it may have been "a quirky unplanned fortuitous event" or related to AUO's unknowable external environment. As with the first biological cells in PMR, "no one can know," and (echoing his Chapter 25 line) it "is not an appropriate question."
- Campbell warns against filling gaps in understanding with assumptions and beliefs: pseudo-knowledge is "useless except as a pacifier for a needy ego." Accurately assessing one's fundamental limitations — distinguishing actual from apparent limitations — "differentiates the wise from the foolish."
- Time is evolutionarily profitable, Campbell claims, because ordered events allow sequences to carry and propagate content; complex interaction and causal logic chains evolve; entropy is reduced. Time is "a catalytic agent" that speeds up the interaction between the Fundamental Process and consciousness.
- Campbell equates lower entropy with higher quality: evolutionary pressure moves consciousness toward brighter, more aware, more highly organized configurations, with more energy available to do work (to more profitably organize).
- With time, AUO's parts can communicate: signals pass cell to cell as fast as one cell can change state in response to an "adjacent" cell changing state; if patterns encode meaning (as neurons, letters, gestures, or sounds do), AUO's parts start sharing data — roughly analogous to a biological nervous system.
- Campbell claims our CNS is "a highly constrained physical analog of consciousness functionality": it hosts our consciousness as a computer hosts an operating system and applications, serving as transducer, data port, and bi-directional translator between the virtual experience of the physical body and the individuated nonphysical consciousness that defines our existence.
- He argues it is reasonable to expect the information-processing mechanisms of the cell-based mind AUO to be similar to those of the CNS, since interface technologies on both the physical and nonphysical sides should be highly compatible; studying the brain may one day give us "a small, physical, highly tinted window" onto nonphysical consciousness processes.
- The maximum speed at which a reality cell can change state defines the smallest measurable unit of time — the fundamental quantum of time (one half cycle of the AUO clock; a full cycle is a state change and change back). For technoids: if state change is smooth (sin(wt)), the smallest practical unit reduces to the smallest discrete portion of a state change (Dwt) that AUO can consistently measure — but Campbell calls that a technical detail unimportant to the Big Picture.
- Words like "adjacent" and "speed" are only metaphors here — space is not yet defined. Campbell warns against thinking anthropomorphically in 3D concepts, as if AUO takes up space "out there"; he points to thoughts, which are not spatially large or small.
- Campbell claims consciousness evolution accelerates much faster than biological evolution because the birth-survival-propagation-death process is not part of it: "Without a body to drag around and care for, AUO travels light and moves fast within a much larger set of possibilities." Nonphysical mind has more degrees of freedom and fewer constraints.
- Learning and technology are his examples of bodiless accelerating evolution; each generation wrongly believes technological change must be about to slow, but "forward vision is always clouded by limited imagination."
- Campbell argues attempts to halt or suppress innovation (by Troglodytes, Luddites, religions, homicidal wackos, and governments — from smashing labor-saving machinery in the seventeenth century to outlawing stem-cell and cloning research in the twenty-first) have all failed and will always fail; attacking a specific result "will never defeat the Fundamental Process: Cut off one head and two more will pop up in its place."
- Humanity's only way out of technology-outrunning-wisdom, per Campbell, is to quickly develop clear long-term vision and high-quality judgment (wisdom) before the system "goes irretrievably unstable" or luck runs out — "Splat!" Depending on good luck to avoid multiple potentially catastrophic disasters "is not a clever plan."
- Great potential is, in Campbell's words, "a two edged sword": he pictures us as "all passengers in this local reality rocket," where the technological manipulation of our internal and external environments may soon produce more dramatically accelerating change, and says we must depend on the quality of our political, ethical, economic, technical, and philosophic institutions to grasp the opportunities and deliver the advantages while avoiding the pitfalls.
- Quality, understanding, and wisdom are individual attributes: institutions reflect the quality of the average individual who produces and populates them; "there are no innocent bystanders" — the onus is on everyone to substantially raise the quality of their personal consciousness. Compelling a solution by external force is usually counter-productive and never a good long-term solution.
- Campbell notes that consciousness evolution within NPMR-N does not logically preclude other ways a dimension like OS or PMR got populated: modifying existing OS entities through evolution, adding new beings to OS, or directly modifying selected OS entities (an "implant" of sentient beings or capability) — three non-mutually-exclusive processes that may all work together to optimize the whole.
- As awareness refines its mental capabilities, second-order functions arise (intention, feedback, interaction, synthesis, integration), dramatically expanding the number of profitable states; these in turn produce third-order functions — enjoyment, aesthetics, ideals, quality — from which values, preferences, good and bad, and moral choices become defined.
- Play is widespread among highly evolved creatures because it IS profitable to Big Picture consciousness evolution — "if having fun wasn't profitable... it wouldn't come so naturally or be so popular." The more sentient we become, the more important our internal environment becomes to our evolution.
- Campbell argues AUO — with "billions of trillions of gazillions times more native richness, selection, complexity, memory, and awareness than we humans" — would naturally evolve values, compassion, purposeful choice, play, communications, music, mathematics, computer science, creativity, and humor, since the Fundamental Process and the nature of consciousness are the same in all manifestations.
- Having these attributes is not enough: AUO needs a process and feedback mechanism to accurately deduce what is profitable and another to move the system toward lower entropy — mechanisms (of which we are a part) deferred to Sections 3 and 4.
- Campbell warns against the "outrageously limiting anthropomorphic temptation" to make AUO into a super-cool spooky humanoid: "It is not at all like us, although we may be a tiny little bit like it."
- The chapter ends foreshadowing that "AUO's consciousness and existence is fundamental while our consciousness and existence is derivative."

## Concepts
- **AUO (Absolute Unbounded Oneness)** — Campbell's primordial consciousness system, here evolving from a "state-flipping" cell-based entity into a "digital-thinking-being-thing" and "Creative-Supercomputer-Being-Consciousness-System-Thing (with emphasis on the 'Thing')" that invents time, communication, and eventually values. Its consciousness and existence are fundamental; ours derivative.
- **Reality cells** — the individual state-changing units making up AUO; groups of them can be oscillated to build clocks, and messages propagate as fast as one cell can change state in response to an "adjacent" cell.
- **Time** — per Campbell, "a technology, a construct of a self-modifying evoking consciousness, an artifact of a system of energy improving its internal organization"; the byproduct of AUO's first entropy-reducing internal change, separating "before" from "after." Change creates the notion of time; awareness of change necessitates personal time.
- **AUO's clock** — a regularly oscillating individual or group of reality cells that "keep time for everything else" like a metronome; evolution drives it toward constant frequency; AUO can make "one terrifically good clock" to produce, regulate, and integrate patterns, and patterns of patterns of patterns.
- **Natural frequency** — the frequency at which the best clock oscillates as efficiently, regularly, and quickly as possible.
- **Period** — one divided by the frequency of oscillation; the time to change state and change back; a handy unit of time (Campbell's examples: earth's rotation = one day; pendulum period counts seconds in grandfather clocks).
- **Fundamental quantum of time** — the smallest unit of time AUO's clock can directly measure, defined by the maximum speed with which a reality cell can normally change state (one half cycle); if state change is smooth and continuous (sin(wt)), it can reduce to the smallest discrete portion of a state change (Dwt) AUO can consistently measure.
- **Fundamental Process** — Campbell's universal evolutionary process ("works better" drives it); it interacts with consciousness, creates the optimal clock solution for AUO, and cannot be defeated by attacking its specific results.
- **Entropy** — in Campbell's usage, the inverse of consciousness quality: lower entropy = higher quality = brighter, more aware, more highly organized configurations with more energy available to do work (to more profitably organize).
- **Pseudo-knowledge** — beliefs and assumptions created to fill in for what one cannot understand; "useless except as a pacifier for a needy ego."
- **Big Truth** — the deep understanding worth sparing no effort to discover, tied here to clearly understanding the difference between actual and apparent limitations.
- **Belief traps** — self-created apparent limitations where none actually exist; "a great waste of potential."
- **Actual vs. apparent limitations** — Campbell's distinction: appreciating real limits is the first step toward wisdom, while inventing false ones wastes potential; the ability to accurately assess one's fundamental limitations "differentiates the wise from the foolish."
- **CNS (central nervous system)** — brain, nerves, neurons, synapses and electrical charges; "a highly constrained physical analog of consciousness functionality" that hosts our consciousness as a computer hosts an operating system and applications; a transducer, data port, and bi-directional translator between the virtual physical-body experience and the individuated nonphysical consciousness.
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — our physical reality, where the first biological cells arose and where the birth-survival-propagation-death process absorbs enormous time and energy.
- **NPMR / NPMR-N** — the nonphysical-matter reality (and Campbell's subscripted local instance NPMR-N) whose communities exhibit the facts of consciousness evolution.
- **OS (Our System)** — the reality subsystem containing us; its consciousness sub-system may be changed three non-exclusive ways: evolution of existing entities, adding new beings, or directly modifying selected entities.
- **Second-order functions** — intention, feedback, interaction, synthesis, and integration: mental capabilities produced by the complexity and interactive properties of evolving mental processes; once they drive evolution, the number of potentially profitable states dramatically expands. Humanity is "the current mental synthesis, integration, and interactive feedback champions" of earthbound biological evolution. Campbell adds that AUO (now also a "digital-thinking-being-thing") has the right stuff to one day be many orders of magnitude better than we are at these second-order analytic functions, and wonders whether there are "fourth, fifth and higher order functions we can't imagine."
- **Third-order functions** — enjoyment, aesthetics, ideals, and quality: concepts that arise when awareness and complexity of choice reach a sufficient level, modifying the expression of the Fundamental Process; they produce values, preferences, defined good and bad, and moral choices.
- **Values** — a product of second- and third-order processes within consciousness; AUO evolves values just as people did, since the Fundamental Process and the nature of consciousness are the same in all manifestations.
- **Play / fun** — evolutionarily profitable activity of the internal environment; widespread among highly evolved creatures precisely because it profits Big Picture consciousness evolution.
- **Evolutionary acceleration** — Campbell's claim that learning, growing, and evolving are accelerating processes ("the actualization of a given potential gives birth to a greater potential that gives birth to an even greater potential that ..."); much greater for consciousness systems than biological ones because consciousness is bodiless; cannot accelerate forever — eventually the Fundamental Process channels most energy toward only the better opportunities.
- **Degrees of freedom** — why nonphysical mind changes faster than the physical body: it contains more degrees of freedom and fewer constraints.
- **Anthropomorphism** — the "outrageously limiting" temptation Campbell repeatedly warns against: imposing 3D spatial concepts, human time/causality, or a humanoid image on AUO.
- **Fundamental vs. derivative consciousness** — the closing foreshadowed thesis: AUO's consciousness and existence are fundamental; ours are derivative.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The metronome / foot-tapping clock model** — Campbell's core model of time's birth: AUO oscillates a group of reality cells regularly (uniform, non-uniform, uniform, non-uniform...), creating a clock that keeps time for everything else, "as easy for AUO to invent (evolve) as it is for us to keep time by tapping our foot." Moving parts: clock cells, constant frequency, natural frequency, half-cycle = quantum of time.
- **Grandfather clock and earth's rotation** — everyday examples of using a period as a unit of time (pendulum period counts seconds; earth's rotation period is one day).
- **"Go ask your mother!" (Chapter 25 callback)** — Campbell's stock answer for the unknowable origin of the first modification of AUO's oneness: like the first biological cells in PMR, "it is not an appropriate question."
- **Intestinal bacteria analogy (used twice)** — (1) you and your gut bacteria must both accept that some things are forever beyond your limited reach; (2) imagining AUO's possibilities is like your intestinal bacteria speculating about the larger organism they live in.
- **Monkey snagging a banana** — analogy for coordinated activity among AUO's parts and patterns: arms, legs, brain, tail, and eyes interacting and coordinating.
- **Nervous-system analogy** — AUO's cell-to-cell signaling imagined as "roughly analogous to a biological nervous system communicating between body parts using sequenced patterns of neurons."
- **CNS-as-computer model** — the CNS hosts consciousness "as a computer hosts an operating system and applications"; also imaged as "a sophisticated top-end mainframe computer... connected through a simple interface to a bank of custom made sensors." Campbell uses it to argue interface compatibility implies functional similarity between physical and nonphysical information processing, and that brain science may become "a small, physical, highly tinted window" onto nonphysical consciousness.
- **Sin(wt) / Dwt technoid aside** — for readers "with their hands in the air": if state-change is smooth and continuous, the practical time quantum shrinks to the smallest discrete measurable portion of a state change — dismissed as a detail unimportant to the Big Picture (referencing the oscillating-function math of Chapter 26).
- **Thoughts as non-spatial objects** — thought experiment against 3D thinking: consider the "speed" of thoughts, the "space" between adjacent ideas, foreground and background thoughts; "great big thoughts and little tiny thoughts are not spatially large or small."
- **Education/learning as bodiless accelerating evolution** — the more someone knows, the more quickly they can learn related material (at constant intelligence).
- **Technology's runaway acceleration** — Campbell's extended worked example of accelerating bodiless evolution, complete with the runaway-vehicle vignette ("Uhh oh ...hey...how do you steer this thing!? ... no brakes! Criminy! Now what do I do?"); shows that consciousness evolution, like technology, is an accelerating natural process, and that humanity must gain wisdom fast.
- **The local reality rocket** — Campbell's image for humanity's exposed position: "we are all passengers in this local reality rocket," with the technological manipulation of our internal and external environments poised to produce still more dramatically accelerating change; he says we must depend on the quality of our political, ethical, economic, technical, and philosophic institutions to grasp the opportunities and deliver the advantages while avoiding the pitfalls.
- **Failed suppression of innovation** — historical range from "the smashing of labor saving machinery in the seventeenth century to the outlawing of stem-cell and cloning research in the twenty-first century"; Troglodytes, Luddites, religions, homicidal wackos, and governments all fail; hydra image ("Cut off one head and two more will pop up") and "a head in the sand always leaves an unprotected butt waving in the air."
- **Cloning and genetic engineering** — Campbell's predicted example of biological evolution making "discontinuous hops" through possibility space, accelerating a species' positive or negative potential.
- **The prehistoric horse slogan** — "'Homo sapiens do it better' (found etched on the rump of a prehistoric horse)": a joke making the point that humans are the second-order-function champions of earthbound biological evolution — even if Flipper and Shamu, with their significantly larger brains, disagree.
- **Sounds → language and music** — model of how a limited variable set yields vast application space: language serves the external demands of the Fundamental Process; music serves internal well-being ("evokes emotion, reduces stress, motivates and bonds people, communicates feeling, and increases milk production on progressive dairy farms"). "Just for fun" does not imply useless.
- **Three primary colors, twenty-six letters** — everything in human communications, art, literature, collective memory, science, and technology rests on combinations of this small set of fundamental variables; AUO has "a gazillion times more variables... within a huge manifold of multi-dimensionality."
- **Birds and dogs** — birds sing and soar; dogs fetch tennis balls and chase Frisbees: instances of play/third-order attributes in evolved creatures.
- **Buying stock in AUO** — comic image: "Don't you wish you could have bought stock in this binary state-flipping baby before it took over the reality market?"
- **The comforting-humanoid parody** — bracketed mock inner monologue ("Ahhh... just like us, how comforting...") dramatizing the anthropomorphic temptation Campbell tells the reader to resist.
- **Three-mechanisms-of-population model** — for how OS/PMR got its sentient beings: evolution of existing entities, implantation of new beings, or direct modification of selected entities — possibly all three together optimizing the whole.

## Thread
This chapter is a pivotal step in Section 2's evolution-of-consciousness arc: having established AUO as a cell-based, state-changing digital consciousness (with the oscillating-function groundwork of Ch 26 and the "inappropriate question" epistemics of Ch 25 explicitly recalled), Campbell now derives time itself as an evolved technology — AUO's clock — rather than a fundamental given. Time then unlocks communication, pattern propagation, and accelerating entropy reduction, which in turn yield second-order functions (intention, feedback, synthesis) and third-order functions (values, aesthetics, moral choice), so AUO matures from a state-flipping "thing" into a value-evolving "Creative-Supercomputer-Being-Consciousness-System-Thing." The chapter explicitly defers the profit-detecting process and feedback mechanism "of which we are a part" to Sections 3 and 4, and tees up the trilogy's core inversion — AUO fundamental, us derivative — that the following chapters will develop.
