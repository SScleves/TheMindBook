# Front Matter — About, Synopsis, Preface & Foreword

## In one paragraph
Campbell introduces *My Big TOE* as a trilogy — *Awakening*, *Discovery*, *Inner Workings* — written by a nuclear physicist who also spent almost thirty years researching altered states of consciousness, and which claims to deliver a Big Picture Theory Of Everything: a single scientific model unifying physics and metaphysics, mind and matter, the normal and the paranormal, objective and subjective experience. He tells the reader up front that the preface and foreword are integral parts of the story, that no leaps of faith or beliefs of any sort are required, that the reader should pace by depth of comprehension rather than percent of completion, and that the "My" in the title is a constant reminder that this reality model cannot serve as the reader's personal Big TOE until it is based upon the reader's own experience — "My Big TOE is the launch pad. Your Big TOE is the final destination."

## Core claims
- **A Big TOE, not just a TOE.** "TOE" is the physics community's term for a Theory Of Everything, the "Holy Grail" of physicists for more than fifty years; General Relativity, Quantum Mechanics, and String Theory have all tried and failed to produce one. Campbell's TOE is "Big" because it successfully integrates metaphysics (ontology, epistemology, cosmology) and physics into a single unified big-picture view of the larger reality. Contemporary physics is shown to be a special case of a more general set of basic principles.
- **Method: science applied to consciousness.** "I am a scientist. This trilogy is the result of a long and careful scientific exploration focused upon the nature of reality and the individual." The trilogy is based on careful scientific research and logical deduction; "Data, facts, and measurable results are the exclusive currency upon which this trilogy trades." Readers are "strongly dissuaded from becoming a believer" — the value of the model "must be measured in terms of the personal and objective results that it produces."
- **Minimum assumptions.** The whole model — time, space, consciousness, and the basic properties, purpose, and mechanics of reality — is logically inferred from just **two basic assumptions**, defined and developed in Section 2.
- **Dual credentials.** The author has one foot in traditional hard science (physics) and the other in the scientific exploration of consciousness (parapsychology, almost thirty years of research on altered states, in and out of formal laboratory settings), a unique position for the required synthesis.
- **Why a trade book, not a journal article.** Like Darwin's theory of evolution, the material requires broad explanations spanning multiple academic disciplines and must develop entirely new scientific and reality paradigms — "a goal that cannot be reached both quickly and effectively" in a condensed technical paper.
- **Why the digital/IT vocabulary.** American English is poor in nonphysical conceptual descriptors but unusually rich in communications and information-technology descriptors; it is precisely modern data-processing technology that provides the conceptual tools to model the larger reality for the Western mind.
- **Where prior physics fell short (Campbell's framing).** Einstein's Unified Field Theory asserted a nonphysical field as the basis of reality but missed the discrete digital properties of space and time and the role of consciousness as the primary energy field; Bohm, Bohr, Heisenberg, and Wigner made the consciousness connection but missed the digital connection; Fredkin's Digital Physics makes the digital connection (quantized space and time) but misses consciousness — "Digital physics has not yet discovered that consciousness is the computer." Belief-induced blindness in defining space, time, objectivity, and consciousness creates scientific paradoxes such as wave/particle duality and instantaneous communication between an entangled pair.
- **Big Truth is self-discovered.** Fundamental truth (Big Picture Truth or Big Truth), though absolute and uniformly significant to everyone, must be discovered by each individual within the context of that individual's experience; "little truth" is circumstantial and relative to the observer. A model is only an intellectual device — "do not confuse the model of reality with reality itself. Please repeat that twice before going on."
- **Trilogy organization** (each separate book shares the same dedication, synopsis, preface, foreword, and its own acronym list; sections are numbered continuously 1–6 across the trilogy):
  - *Book 1: Awakening* — Section 1: partial biography of the author pertinent to the trilogy's origins. Section 2: cultural belief traps, Big Picture epistemology and ontology, meditation, and the two basic assumptions.
  - *Book 2: Discovery* — Section 3: interface between "we the people" and our digital consciousness reality; ego, love, free will, larger purpose; the psi uncertainty principle interrelating psi phenomena, free will, love, consciousness evolution, physics, digital computation, and entropy. Section 4: an operational and functional model of consciousness; how artificial intelligence (AI Guy) leads to artificial consciousness, actual consciousness, and us; derives our physical universe and solves the mind-matter dichotomy by deriving physical reality directly from digital consciousness.
  - *Book 3: Inner Workings* — Section 5: the formal reality model — probable realities, predicting and modifying the future, teleportation, telepathy, multiple physical and nonphysical bodies, the fractal nature of an evolving digital consciousness reality. Section 6: wrap-up, personal perspective, and integration of My Big TOE with traditional Western science and philosophy.
- **Three challenges of delivery** (Foreword): (1) turn some portion of metaphysics into physics — metaphysics is where he must start, and contemporary physics flows naturally from it; (2) package a far-out subject as an interesting, non-threatening, one-on-one peer-to-peer informal discussion; (3) keep it credible — tightly logical while explaining the data of collective and individual experience.
- **How to read it.** Read the sections in order but whole-trilogy coverage matters more than order; asides are indented, set in special font, and marked with dingbats (double dingbats for nested asides); a "seeding technique" plants conceptual seeds ahead of full discussions; hold questions — most are answered within pages, and leftovers seed the reader's own quest for Big Truth in Section 6; his humor may read as sarcasm or arrogance — suspend judgment until about the end of Section 4.

Conceptual terms defined or introduced in the About/Synopsis/Preface/Foreword:
- **Big Picture / little picture** — the whole of the larger reality versus the limited, culturally habituated conceptualization of it; "Big Picture Significance and value have replaced little picture right and wrong as the primary measure of worth." Little-picture logic, science, and mathematics have natural, fundamental, unavoidable limitations.
- **Big Truth / little truth** — fundamental truth (Big Picture Truth), absolute and uniformly significant to everyone, yet discoverable only by each individual within the context of that individual's experience; like wisdom, it cannot be taught or learned from a book. Little truth's significance is circumstantial and relative to the observer.
- **Model (of reality)** — an intellectual device theoreticians use to achieve a more concrete understanding of an abstract concept; may resemble the reality it describes or merely describe inputs and outputs; not to be confused with reality itself; a good model should be predictive, explain what is known, produce useful new knowledge, and describe all the data all the time under all circumstances for everyone.
- **Western attitude / Western mind-set** — the belief that reality is built upon an inviolate objective causality; process-oriented, materialistic, objective; not geographically exclusive to the West and expanding worldwide; typically kept in a bipolar standoff with an inner belief-system that supplies personal security.
- **Objective causality** — the core Western assumption underpinning materialist science; the larger reality extends beyond it, beyond the reach of purely intellectual effort, into the personal subjective mind.
- **Belief-blindness / cultural belief** — culture lays out the worldview template "at the bone and sinew level of awareness"; basic assumptions are accepted without question as solid facts; belief-induced blindness in defining space, time, objectivity, and consciousness is what creates physics' paradoxes.
- **Psi uncertainty principle** — developed in Section 3; explains and interrelates psi phenomena, free will, love, consciousness evolution, physics, reality, human purpose, digital computation, and entropy. (One test reader calls it "one of the most significant ideas ever put forward.")
- **Digital consciousness reality** — the trilogy's central move: consciousness is digital and "consciousness is the computer"; our physical universe, science, and perception of physical reality are derived from the nature of digital consciousness, solving the mind-matter dichotomy.
- **AI Guy** — Section 4's embodiment of artificial intelligence, used to show how AI leads to artificial consciousness, which leads to actual consciousness and to us.
- **Consciousness evolution** — the trilogy explores its scientific and philosophical implications, "a subject that holds critical significance for everyone."
- **Digital Physics** — contemporary physicist Edward Fredkin's movement; makes the digital connection (quantized space and time) but is missing a solid connection to consciousness.
- **Unified Field Theory** — Einstein's attempt; asserted a nonphysical space-time field as the basis for matter and reality, moving science closer to the truth, but missed the discrete digital properties of space and time and consciousness (instead of space-time) as the primary energy field.
- **Asides / dingbats** — indented, specially-fonted side trips marked with dingbats at beginning and end (double dingbats for asides within asides; header dingbats when an aside fills a page), distinguishing them from the main text.
- **Seeding technique** — Campbell's practice of planting conceptual seeds — briefly introducing an idea — ahead of its full discussion, to prepare readers for wholly unfamiliar concepts.
- **All That Is** — what any Big TOE must describe: the basic oneness, continuity, and connectedness of everything.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The dual career.** In addition to an ongoing career in a traditional hard science, Campbell spent almost thirty years carefully researching altered states of consciousness both in and out of formal laboratory settings; My Big TOE is "the result of this unusual dual career in both physics and parapsychology." Section 1 of *Awakening* is a partial biography covering the "unusual associations, circumstances, training and initial research" behind the trilogy.
- **Test readers.** A half dozen independent test readers of various backgrounds evaluated the trilogy and recorded impressions: Pamela Knight (physicist), Ina Kuzman, Lyle Fuller (power engineer), Peg Rochine (founder and CEO, Clinical Research Associates), Kate von Seeburg (business owner), Trevor Goldstein (philosopher and futurist). One blurb calls Campbell "an unlikely combination of 'Meet Mr. Wizard,' Dr. Phil, and Bill Murray."
- **The Tibetan monk vs. American physicist.** Foreword thought example: their descriptions of reality are vastly different; asking which is right and which is wrong is "the wrong question" — a narrow and exclusive perspective; better questions ask which works and against what standard satisfaction, growth, and fulfillment of purpose are measured.
- **Book mechanics.** The trilogy is sold as three separate books and as one binding; each separate book repeats the shared front matter, greys out the other books' table-of-contents entries, restarts page/chapter numbering, but numbers the six sections continuously. Dedication: to Chris; to Bob & Nancy; to Dennis & Nancy Lea; to Todd, Lyle, Ina, and Trevor; to those in need of a new perspective; to all seekers of Big Truth; to Pamela, The One. ## Thread
The front matter is Campbell's contract with the reader: he promises a complete, logically derived Theory Of Everything built on only two assumptions, delivered in Western digital/IT metaphors, demanding skepticism rather than belief, and insisting that the intellectual model is only a launch pad — the reader must grow a personal Big TOE from direct experience. It also hands the reader the map (six sections across three books, each building on the last) and the toolkit (acronym list, asides convention, seeding technique, humor warning) for the whole journey.
