# Ch 46 — The Chairman of the Board, and Our Probable Relative Importance

## In one paragraph
Campbell uses this short chapter to shrink the reader's ego down to the scale his model requires: he argues that our physical universe, and humanity within it, is statistically almost infinitesimal relative to All That Is. He notes that even the most expansive traditional placeholder for ultimate reality — "The Mind of God" (MOG), as Stephen Hawking uses the term — is still a little picture view of AUM, because it grows out of the diminutive notion of a god who creates and animates only our physical universe. Reviewing his layered ontology (our universe as a sliver of the reality-space of one space-time dimension; our local reality as one branch among many divergent probable realities; many PMRs inside NPMR-N; NPMR-N as one of several NPMR-n reality systems within AUM), he builds an extended corporate metaphor in which AUM is chairman and CEO of "MOG Corp.," the "Big Cheese" is a division manager over many PMRs, low-level assistants administer Our System (OS), and we are the rank-and-file peons. Campbell then argues that even if we were more important than the statistics indicate, knowing it would be a disadvantage, because theories of special importance breed arrogance, disregard for others, and disrespect for the ecosystems that sustain us. He closes by acknowledging the ego-deflating harshness of Section 3's conceptual groundwork and framing the ideas as emancipating, empowering, and fun — encouraging the reader to self-direct the evolution of their consciousness.

## Core claims
- Campbell argues that a more accurate and humble perspective on our place in reality is a prerequisite for Big Picture understanding.
- Campbell claims that "The Mind of God" (MOG) — even as something expansive, open, and mystical, as Stephen Hawking invokes it at the end of *A Brief History of Time* — usually represents only a little picture view of AUM, because it is derived from the little picture perspective of god as creator and animator of our physical universe alone.
- Campbell asserts (recalling Chapter 33, Book 1) that our universe occupies only a minuscule sliver of the available physical reality-space within our own space-time dimension.
- Campbell asserts (anticipating Chapters 3, 9, and 10 of Book 3) that our local reality is just one branch of many divergent probable realities.
- Campbell claims there are many independent PMR-like realities within NPMR-N, which is itself only one of several more or less independent reality systems (the NPMR-n).
- Campbell concludes that our PMR universe is not at the core of the larger reality; its relative significance appears almost infinitesimal compared to the sheer bulk of All That Is — "itsy, bitsy, teeny, weenie is probably an outrageous overstatement" of our likely importance.
- Campbell argues that, statistically, we are not likely to be at the center of our PMR universe, much less of our larger reality, the Big Cheese's reality, or AUM's reality — "the odds are clearly against it."
- Campbell grants that Our System is important, but insists so are many other history threads running through other universes in many other reality systems, spread over many PMRs residing in the many NPMRs within each of AUM's many evolutionary manifestations.
- Campbell argues that even if we are more important than the statistics indicate, there is no advantage in knowing it and considerable potential disadvantage: such knowledge would encourage egos to invent grand theories of special importance (as a species or individuals), leading to seeing everything else as insignificant.
- Campbell warns that self-importance might breed arrogance, cause us to ignore opportunities to improve ourselves, produce haughty disregard for the significance of others, and foster a disrespectful attitude toward the ecosystems that sustain us — dysfunctional attitudes he says should not be encouraged "even if they do represent traditional values."
- Campbell frames the ego-deflating, bubble-bursting conceptual foundation he has been laying as preparation for the description of reality in the next two sections, and characterizes My Big TOE's premises and conclusions as emancipating, empowering, and fun.
- Campbell holds that lighthearted fun is an important component of learning, and invites both agreement checked against personal data and carefully formulated debunkers' rebuttals.

## Concepts
- **AUM** — Campbell's ultimate consciousness; here described as bigger than MOG, encompassing the entire evolutionary experiment ("substance, results, conclusions, and open ended possibilities — all combined, and all at the same time"), and containing many evolutionary manifestations, each holding many NPMRs. In the corporate metaphor, AUM is chairman of the board and CEO of MOG Corp. In a light metaphoric moment one could call AUM "the Mind of the God of god's god," but Campbell says AUM is much bigger even than that.
- **MOG (Mind of God)** — An unknowable something posited by those unsatisfied with traditional philosophy or religion; Campbell cites Stephen Hawking's use of the term at the end of *A Brief History of Time*. Campbell says MOG usually represents only a little picture view of AUM, being derived from the diminutive perspective of god as creator/animator of our physical universe; MOG is "only one hat" AUM appears to wear from our minute perspective.
- **MOG Corp.** — Campbell's "decidedly funky but business-like" metaphorical corporation for All That Is, of which AUM is chairman and CEO; used to place our reality in a chain of command.
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — Our physical-universe type of reality; Campbell says many independent PMR-like realities exist within NPMR-N, and our favorite PMR universe is not at the core of the larger reality.
- **NPMR / NPMR-N / NPMR-n** — Nonphysical reality systems. NPMR-N is the specific one containing many independent PMR-like realities (including ours); it is itself only one of several more or less independent reality systems, the NPMR-n. Many NPMRs exist within each of AUM's many evolutionary manifestations.
- **OS (Our System)** — The subset of reality containing our physical universe ("Our physical universe is a subset of OS"); in the metaphor, administered by a few low-level assistants and their helpers down the chain of command.
- **The Big Cheese** — In Campbell's metaphor, the division manager of "N" division, dedicated to a specific subset of evolutionary development models containing many PMRs — i.e., the metaphorical manager of NPMR-N under AUM.
- **All That Is** — The whole of reality against whose "sheer bulk" our universe's significance appears almost infinitesimal.
- **Probable realities** — Divergent branches of reality; Campbell states our local reality is just one branch of many divergent probable realities (to be developed in Book 3, Chapters 3, 9, and 10).
- **Reality-space** — The available physical space of a space-time dimension; per Chapter 33 (Book 1), our universe occupies only a minuscule sliver of it.
- **Big picture / little picture** — Campbell's recurring contrast between an accurate, humble, expansive view of reality and the diminutive anthropocentric view; MOG is little picture, AUM is big picture.
- **Ego** — The faculty that, given knowledge (or belief) of our special importance, would invent grand theories of significance breeding arrogance, disregard for others, and disrespect for sustaining ecosystems; the target of this chapter's deliberate deflation.
- **Consciousness evolution** — The self-directed pursuit Campbell encourages the reader toward ("encouraged to pursue and self-direct the evolution of your consciousness"); the point of enduring the ego-deflation.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **Stephen Hawking's "Mind of God"** — Campbell cites Hawking's use of the term at the end of *A Brief History of Time* as an example of positing an unknowable something when traditional philosophy or religion fails to satisfy. Campbell says even this expansive, mystical concept is still a little picture view of AUM, since it is anchored to god as creator/animator of just our physical universe.
- **The MOG Corp. corporate-hierarchy metaphor** — Campbell's central model of the chapter, with these moving parts: AUM = chairman of the board and CEO of MOG Corp. and of the entire evolutionary experiment; the Big Cheese = division manager of "N" division (a subset of evolutionary development models containing many PMRs, i.e., NPMR-N); further down the chain of command, a few assistants and helpers administer OS and its inhabitants; our physical universe is a subset of OS; we are "the rank and file peons who appear (from your point of view) to do all the work and receive none of the credit, just as in real life." Campbell adds, "Perhaps getting the credit is not as important as it first appears."
- **"God of PMR" job application** — A playful extension: if one of the low-level assistants administering OS applied for the job of "God of PMR," then AUM would be "the Mind of the God of god's god." Campbell flags he is "just having some fun, but you get the point" — the point being how many layers separate our god-concepts from AUM.
- **The Big Cheese's promotion** — Another comic extension: if "N" division is especially profitable, the Big Cheese might be promoted to junior vice president and awarded "a private space in which to park his mind, stock options, omniscience without looking, and a grand vacation where he can... go out of his mind for a few weeks." Illustrates the vast hierarchy above us in jest.
- **The "itsy bitsy teeny weenie yellowish polka-dot" image** — Campbell pictures us (our reality and history) as a tiny polka-dot on a much bigger picture, itself wrapped around "something more grand and sublime than we can imagine"; he then says even "itsy, bitsy, teeny, weenie is probably an outrageous overstatement" of our relative importance — statistically we are likely much smaller.
- **Nested-reality review (structural model)** — The chapter restates the layered ontology: our universe = minuscule sliver of reality-space within our space-time dimension (per Ch 33, Book 1); our local reality = one branch of many divergent probable realities (per Book 3, Chs 3, 9, 10); many independent PMR-like realities within NPMR-N; NPMR-N = one of several NPMR-n reality systems; many NPMRs within each of AUM's many evolutionary manifestations.
- **Thought experiment on the dangers of knowing we are special** — Campbell reasons: even if we were more important than statistics suggest, that knowledge would carry no advantage and considerable disadvantage — egos would invent grand theories of special importance, breeding arrogance, dismissal of everything besides ourselves, missed self-improvement, and disrespect toward sustaining ecosystems. He pointedly asks, "Are humans capable of that type of blind arrogance?"

## Thread
This chapter sits near the end of Section 3 ("Man in the Loop") and functions as the humility capstone of the conceptual foundation Campbell has been laying: before describing reality's structure and dynamics in detail, he wants the reader's anthropocentric ego deflated so that the model can be received without self-important distortion. It explicitly builds on Chapter 33 (Book 1) — our universe as a sliver of reality-space — and on the nested AUM/NPMR-n/NPMR-N/PMR/OS hierarchy developed earlier, and it explicitly sets up "the description of reality that will follow in the next two sections" (Section 4, "Solving the Mystery," and beyond) as well as the probable-realities treatment in Book 3 (Chapters 3, 9, 10 of that book). Its ethical corollary — that belief in special importance breeds arrogance and ecological disrespect — feeds the trilogy's larger claim that consciousness evolution requires shedding ego.
