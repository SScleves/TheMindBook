# Ramifications: Communications, Time Travel, and Teleportation

## In one paragraph
Thomas Campbell draws out practical "ramifications" of his larger model (physical reality as virtual, consciousness as fundamental) for four linked topics: instantaneous communication across any distance, time travel, teleportation, and the ability to exist as multiple bodies at once. He argues that because apparent distance and separation between realities are only virtual constructs generated by rule-sets, none of them impose real limits the way they seem to within PMR (Physical Matter Reality). He uses several analogies — a web page hosted in Australia loading as fast as one hosted next door, an AI character in a war-game simulation trying to jump into a different simulation, switching between virtual-reality games, and old-dog executives who cannot let go of hard copies — to explain why signal lag, spatial distance, and the need to physically transport a body are all PMR-bound illusions rather than fundamental limits. He also lays out a practical model of how nonphysical travel/remote viewing/out-of-body navigation actually works: via unique "addresses," focused intent, and the psi uncertainty principle as the real governing constraint, rather than physics-style distance or speed limits.

## Core claims
- Campbell argues that neither apparent PMR (physical matter reality) distance nor spatial separation affects signal quality on the "RWW net" (reality-wide-web) within NPMRN — a telepathic conversation with someone near Alpha Centauri (4.4 light-years away) would show no noticeable time lag.
- He claims that accessing any "grouping of data (dimension)" within TBC (The Big Computer) takes no longer than accessing any other, because distance is only virtual and meaningless outside a given PMRK.
- He argues that because physical reality is only a virtual rule-set defining individuated consciousness's experience within a space-time subset of AUM, teleporting or jumping between experience-sets in different times/places is not fundamentally impossible.
- He claims energy from NPMRN can be directed to any PMRK at any point on its actualized-history thread, including our own PMR, and that this is why "physical matter" transport between realities is conceivable, whether directly or via NPMRN acting as an intermediate hub/router — and that "the same goes for" transporting conscious embodied beings.
- He argues teleporting between totally different simulations (e.g., different "war games" running on the same larger computer) requires understanding and operating at a level above one's local reality, while teleporting within one's own simulation (being in two places at once) is comparatively easier but still requires skill and avoiding violation of the psi uncertainty principle.
- He claims there is little advantage, need, or incentive to bring one's PMR body along when exploring other realities, since physicality is relative to the observer ("we appear nonphysical to other PMRs as they appear nonphysical to us") and there is, in his view, "no physical body, except in your mind."
- He argues it is easier and more practical to manifest a new, appropriate body in whatever reality one visits than to "materialize/dematerialize" and drag a body across realities, and that having multiple simultaneous bodies (physical and/or nonphysical) across realities does not violate any rule.
- He claims traveling with only consciousness/awareness (leaving the physical body behind) is simpler than traveling with a body because it more easily satisfies local psi uncertainty requirements, and that people who don't understand this "never go anywhere" and remain unaware there is a larger reality — or even a purpose to their life — calling this a "belief trap."
- He argues every point, being, or thing in any PMR (or outside space-time) has a unique address, and conscious beings can use focused intent, alone or via NPMRN as an intermediate router, to navigate their awareness to any such address — a process he says is how remote viewers (RV), out-of-body experiencers (OOBE), and other "mind travelers" actually operate.
- He claims that address specificity must be complete (general-to-specific, like a mailing address) or the unspecified parts get filled in randomly or "smeared" across possibilities, and the explorer often never realizes inaccuracy or randomness has crept into the result.
- He argues that once awareness is projected to a chosen "reality-system state vector" (past, probable future, actualized or not), one can run DELTA-t forward or backward from that entry point to generate a realistic experiential "movie," while needing to avoid being sidetracked into an unintended reality at branch points.
- He claims that splitting, duplicating, or fragmenting consciousness into multiple bodies is natural and permitted, provided the rules of interaction and the psi uncertainty principle are satisfied, and that having one body physical and others nonphysical stresses the psi uncertainty principle less than having two identical physical bodies in the same reality.
- He argues that dematerializing/materializing and travel faster than light-speed are "merely local PMR issues" that make little sense from the Big Picture and are not important to personal growth, since the body and spaceship are themselves only rule-set-based experiential delusions of mass.
- He suggests that in the non-space-time portions of NPMRN, everything is effectively instantaneous, so "warp speed" is not the relevant question once one can simply materialize oneself directly into another space-time-reality.
- He speculates, self-deprecatingly, that humanity's attachment to hauling physical bodies around (rather than traveling as awareness alone) may resemble executives who insist on paper printouts of email or dislike teleconferencing — a habit of imagination rather than a real necessity.

## Concepts
- **Energy** — defined in this unit as "the ability to rearrange bits and affect organization, and thus content, within a system"; energy from NPMRN can be directed to any PMRK at any point on its thread of actualized history, which Campbell treats as the basis for physical-matter transport and teleportation.
- **RWW / reality-wide-web** — Campbell's term (by analogy to the World Wide Web) for the communication network operating within NPMRN across which signal quality is unaffected by apparent distance or separation between PMRs or NPMRs.
- **NPMRN** — the larger nonphysical reality/network within which the RWW net operates and which can serve as an "intermediate hub or router" for energy, matter, awareness, or beings moving between different realities (PMRs or other NPMRs).
- **PMR** — physical matter reality; in this unit, "our PMR" is treated as one particular instance among many possible PMRs, each running as if it were a self-contained virtual reality.
- **PMRK** — Campbell's generalized notation for "a" or "any" instance of PMR (with "our PMR as a particular instance of PMRK"), used to make claims about PMRs in general rather than only about ours.
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — the overarching computational reality system that hosts all PMRs and NPMRs as "groupings of data (dimensions)"; navigating between realities is described as "getting around within TBC" or hopping between "clumps of memory cells or locations in TBC."
- **AUM** — referenced here as the encompassing consciousness/system whose space-time subset defines the rule-set experienced as physical reality; also invoked alongside "the Big Cheese" as being satisfied ("happy campers") so long as interaction rules and the psi uncertainty principle are respected.
- **Big Cheese** — an informal name Campbell pairs with AUM for the governing authority/system that must be kept "happy" (i.e., not have its rule-set violated) when consciousness splits, duplicates, or teleports.
- **Psi uncertainty principle** — described (and cross-referenced to Chapters 13–14 of Book 2) as the real governing constraint on paranormal or reality-bending action: any rule-violating action (multiple bodies, teleportation, materializing/dematerializing) must be kept obscured/uncertain enough at both ends and across all PMRs so as not to break the local rule-set.
- **DELTA-t** — the mechanism/variable that, once awareness is projected into a chosen state vector, can be run forward or backward from that entry point to generate "a realistic movie from the sequential frames" — i.e., to experience a reality's unfolding sequence of events.
- **State vector** — the specific coordinate/entry point (a "reality-system state vector," which may be past or probable-future, actualized or non-actualized, in "OS" — Our System — or another reality) into which projected awareness is placed before running DELTA-t.
- **Energy packet exchange interface** — the interface between an individuated consciousness and a given reality's "experience simulator"; teleportation is described as switching consciousness to a different one of these interfaces (cross-referenced to Chapter 32, Book 2) after establishing the needed data links with other participants.
- **Matter-gram** — Campbell's coinage (by analogy to a telegram) for materializing yourself (and your spaceship) in another reality by "plugging your conscious awareness into some other reality's experience simulator" rather than physically traveling there.
- **Unique address / destination URL** — every 3D space-time point or being in any PMR (and points/beings outside space-time) has a unique address; navigation of awareness by focused intent to that address (general-to-specific, e.g., "reality, individual, stomach, ulcerated cells") is how travel/remote viewing/teleportation of awareness is accomplished.
- **Custom-designed wormhole** — Campbell's term for a route consciously created "from a point in your present space-time-reality to a point on our shared history thread, or to any other space-time-reality point in any other reality," used by beings sentient in NPMRN.
- **Remote viewing (RV)** — named as one practice by which people navigate awareness to a unique address by intent, one of the ways "mind travelers" get to and from other places, whether or not they describe it in these terms.
- **Out-of-body experience (OOBE)** — named alongside remote viewing as a practice of directing awareness (rather than the body) to a destination; successful practitioners are said to understand the need for a clear address, a suspended "noisy ego," and many hours of developmental experience.
- **Belief trap** — Campbell's term for the state of PMR residents who do not understand that awareness can travel independent of the body, and who therefore "never go anywhere," staying home to "baby-sit their bodies," unaware there is a larger reality to explore or that their life has a purpose/mission; he adds, "ignorance is not bliss, it is just ignorance."
- **Dimension hopping** — Campbell's phrase for moving between different realities/simulations, which he says "is not so difficult" once one accepts that "all is consciousness."
- **Teleportation** — reframed not as physically relocating a body but as switching consciousness to a different energy packet exchange interface (for one's own simulation) or, for jumping between wholly different simulations, as needing to "copy" oneself (algorithms, memory, data) into the new simulation and integrate with its "time loop drivers."
- **Time travel** — framed as directing DELTA-t forward or backward from a chosen state vector once awareness has been projected there, constrained mainly by the risk of getting sidetracked into an unintended branch reality.
- **Splitting, duplicating, or fragmenting (of consciousness)** — described as "a natural thing for your consciousness to accomplish," permitting more than one apparently physical (or nonphysical) body to exist simultaneously in one or more realities, so long as interaction rules and the psi uncertainty principle are met.
- **Garbage in, garbage out** — the "old computer-programming truism" Campbell invokes to describe what happens when intent used to navigate/address a destination is not unique, steady, clear, and complete: the outcome becomes randomized, "smeared," or inaccurate without the explorer necessarily realizing it.
- **AI Guy simulation analogy** — Campbell's thought experiment of an AI character in a WWII war-game simulation trying to get into a WWI or Vietnam war-game simulation running on the same computer, used to show the levels of understanding (of the "larger computer reality," and the ability to copy and reinsert oneself) needed to teleport between fundamentally different simulations versus merely within one's own.
- **Warp speed / faster-than-light travel** — treated as a moot or "local PMR issue" once one recognizes that materializing directly into another space-time-reality bypasses the need for speed altogether; in the non-space-time portions of NPMRN, Campbell says travel is effectively instantaneous.
- **Dematerializing and materializing** — described as an intuitive but, per Campbell, mistaken assumption that a body must first disappear from one place before reappearing in another; he argues it is simpler just to create/manifest an appropriate new body wherever one is, since the body was only ever virtual.
- **Big Picture** — Campbell's recurring term for the larger, non-PMR-bound perspective from which issues like dematerializing, warp speed, or dragging a physical body around are seen as unimportant to one's actual (spiritual) growth.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **Alpha Centauri telepathy example** — Campbell describes an earthbound individual telepathically conversing with someone near Alpha Centauri (stated as 4.4 light-years from Earth) and experiencing no noticeable time lag between question and answer, offered as evidence that PMR distance does not limit RWW signal quality.
- **Web page hosted in Australia analogy** — compares the instantaneous appearance of a web page from an Australian server versus one from the next room, illustrating that accessing any "dimension" within TBC takes no longer than any other because distance is only virtual.
- **AI Guy war-game simulations (WWII / WWI / Vietnam)** — a thought experiment asking how an AI character in a WWII simulation could get into a WWI or Vietnam simulation running on the same computer; Campbell concludes it would need to operate at levels above its local reality, copy its own algorithms/memory/data, and integrate itself into the new simulation's "time loop drivers" — used to illustrate what teleporting between fundamentally different realities would require versus teleporting within one's own.
- **Jungle Safari to Alpine Adventure analogy** — teleporting a virtual body between local realities is compared to switching between two virtual-reality games, said to be "entirely possible" but requiring skill at parallel processing and at "programming" the underlying computer.
- **Stomach ulcer remote-viewing address example** — Campbell illustrates the "unique address" concept with a scenario of checking on a friend's stomach ulcer in another PMR, giving the address structure as "reality, individual, stomach, ulcerated cells," provided the individual is unique within that reality.
- **City/street/state/house-number analogy** — used to show why an incomplete address (e.g., knowing city and street but not state and house number) produces a large, ambiguous solution set, since different states can have cities/streets with identical names.
- **Explorers: Christopher Columbus, Lewis and Clark, Captain Kirk** — invoked as a recurring historical/fictional lineage of explorers who had no detailed map of unknown territory but only specific addresses/waypoints, illustrating that getting an address right becomes harder the farther one strays from familiar territory.
- **"Beam me up, Scottie" / spaceship-and-warp-speed example** — Campbell riffs on Star Trek-style teleportation and warp speed, noting that once you realize your body and spaceship are "rule-set based experiential delusions of mass by a hallucinating nonphysical consciousness," warp speed and spaceships take on new meaning; he adds that teleportation would mainly be useful "for great gags" and convenience, not spiritual growth.
- **Old-dog executives / hard-copy email analogy** — compares humans' attachment to bringing their physical bodies wherever they go to executives who have secretaries print out email before they'll read it, or who dislike teleconferencing (can't shake hands) or telecommuting (can't monitor employees) — used to suggest current human limits on nonphysical travel are a failure of imagination rather than a hard constraint.

## Thread
This unit extends Campbell's virtual-reality/consciousness-as-fundamental model (developed earlier in the trilogy, including the psi uncertainty principle from Book 2, Chapters 13–14, and the "energy packet exchange interface" from Book 2, Chapter 32) into concrete "ramifications" for communication, time travel, and teleportation — topics that in a purely physicalist framework would be paradoxical or impossible. By treating PMR as one rule-set among many running on "TBC," and distance/time/body as artifacts of that local rule-set, Campbell builds toward practical claims about how remote viewing and out-of-body experience actually work (via unique addresses and focused intent) and toward his broader argument, continued elsewhere in Section 5 ("Inner Space, the Final Frontier"), that the "reality-wide-web" of nonphysical exploration is real, navigable, and constrained mainly by clarity of intent and the psi uncertainty principle rather than by physical limits.
