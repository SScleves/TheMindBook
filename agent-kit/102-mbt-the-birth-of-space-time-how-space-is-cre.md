# Ch 31 — The Birth of Space-Time: How Space is Created by Enforcing a Constant Speed Limit in PMR

## In one paragraph
Campbell here delivers his core account of where space and time come from: they are not physical things but a construct of consciousness — a pattern of constraints AUM imposes on energy transfers between individuated subsets of consciousness within a dedicated chunk of its own thought-space. He builds a bottom-up model in which time is fundamental (evolved by AUO as a regular oscillation) and space is derived from time by enforcing a constant maximum speed of information propagation, c. Space-time is modeled as a 3D isotropic matrix of conceptual "space-time cells," each of which can change state at most once per DELTA-t (PMR's time quantum), so that a distortion propagates at most one cell per DELTA-t — which is the speed of light. Campbell layers PMR time inside NPMR time inside AUM's fundamental time (each quantum vastly larger than the one below it), explaining why communication seems instantaneous in NPMR and why AUM can compute between our time increments. He closes by mapping the whole scheme onto a computer analogy (reality cells as transistors, the space-time rule-set as assembly-language algorithms, AUM as computer/programmer/operating system) and by framing PMR as a virtual-reality "learning lab" whose purpose is entropy reduction of consciousness.

## Core claims
- Campbell argues that space-time is not a physical substance or construct; it is created by imposing a set of constraints upon a subset of the larger reality — "a construct of consciousness."
- Campbell defines space-time as a particular pattern of constraints applied to the energy transfers between individuated consciousnesses, existing within the digital media that supports the rule-set defining our perception. It has a space-like (structural) and time-like (dynamic) component and acts as a "consciousness interface filter" that defines the experience of PMR for sentient entities.
- Campbell claims the experiences of PMR space, time, mass, energy, and gravity are all derived from the space-time constraints placed on interacting consciousness.
- When Campbell says "AUM invented space-time," he means it in the evolutionary sense — "the way fish invented lungs and legs" — and warns against anthropomorphizing AUM as an old man making people from clay.
- Campbell argues AUM, under evolutionary pressure to lower its own entropy, evolves profitable internal environments; space-time is one such environment, and the PMR rule-set makes us and our space-time "uniquely profitable to AUM."
- The purpose of space-time, per Campbell, is to produce a specialized consistent virtual experience for constrained subsets of consciousness (conscious beings); PMR is an "interactive multiplayer consciousness-quality development trainer."
- Campbell claims space-time cells are conceptual (a gedanken experiment in the mind of AUM) and must be uniform in form, function, and makeup to produce a simple isotropic experience-space.
- Campbell asserts an isotropic, Euclidean conceptualization of space in AUM's mind does not conflict with relativity: PMR space will still appear curved (non-Euclidean) to well-instrumented perceivers inside PMR, and general relativity remains "an elegant model of our perception within PMR." One "merely run[s] the traditional paradigm in reverse."
- Campbell defines DELTA-t, the smallest time increment in space-time (one quantum of PMR time), as some positive non-zero integer n times AUM's "fundamental time quantum"; DELTA-t is also the minimum time required to change the state of a space-time reality cell.
- Distortions can move through space-time at a maximum of one space-time cell per DELTA-t; that upper speed limit is c, evolved by AUM and measured in PMR as ~3x10^8 m/s.
- Campbell flatly claims "there is no such thing as physical space" — physical space and our experience of it is "an illusion, a trick of your individuated mind, a virtual reality within the actual reality of your consciousness" — and says Einstein and other top scientists will be heard making the same statement in Section 6 (Chapter 19, Book 3).
- Based on his and other NPMR explorers' experience that NPMR communication is seemingly instantaneous, Campbell infers n is an exceedingly large number; n must be large so NPMR "statisticians" have time to compute probable reality surfaces between successive DELTA-t (a Section 5 topic).
- Campbell claims whoever iterates with the smallest time quantum "is usually in the driver's seat"; AUM evolves the optimum n so it can study its results without getting bored.
- In NPMR there is no space or distance, yet information still requires NPMR time to propagate between the digital mind-spaces of sender and receiver (flipping the cell states representing content in the receiver) — analogous to display time or a computer shuffling core memory.
- Between PMR time increments, time stands still in PMR while NPMR races through ~10^18 more increments of activity; between NPMR increments, AUM has another ~10^18 fundamental units — modeled on how simulations increment local time variables within subroutines (nested time loops).
- c conceptually defines the virtual size of a space-time cell: cell width = (c)·(DELTA-t); the cells have the concept or attribute of size ("virtual size"), not actual size.
- Cells are conveniently conceived as spherical so propagation velocity is constant in all directions; sphere-packing voids are "not an issue in digital thought-space."
- Campbell claims time (equivalently, frequency) is a fundamental attribute of AUM, whereas space is derived from time by specifying the constraint c: "Time is fundamental; space is derived from time."
- Wormhole shortcuts or apparent faster-than-light data transfers do not overturn c as the limit on information propagation through space; they only show the rule-set has generality allowing special cases to appear to violate it.
- AUM arrived at the constant c by evolution — "occupying all the possibilities and building on the best results, by trial and error" — via the Fundamental Process; AUM's evolutionary experiments are like gedanken experiments or computer simulations and thus fast.
- Cells (reality cells, memory cells, binary cells) are discrete units of state-specific relational content — the basic building blocks of aware consciousness; all cellular/digital creations exhibit granularity, so both reality and our perception of it are "granular at the root."
- Campbell describes a consciousness cycle: the down-stroke is the creation of individuated high-entropy consciousness by bounding raw subsets; the up-stroke is each fragment evolving to low-entropy wholeness, lowering the entropy of the whole by the amount of its personal growth. When an evolved product begins to mimic its source, it becomes "a partner in the process of consciousness evolution."
- Our PMR is only one uniquely constrained space-time application within a space-time part (not the space-time part) of AUM; other PMRs exist in other dimensions. Dimensions are not "higher" or "lower" — they differ "the same way books are different."
- The space-time rule-set (a collection of patterns/algorithms within TBC) "subsumes our physics": it defines the limits and relational properties of physical experience, implemented at the lowest level by constraining reality-cell interactions and at the highest by constraining individuated subsets of awareness. The specific relationships of AUM's space-time instruction-set constitute the laws of PMR physics.
- Campbell says "space-time is a construct of consciousness," not "constructed of consciousness" — consciousness should be thought of as a digital media rather than a building material.
- Recapping AUO→AUM: reality cells arise from a this-way/that-way duality; their proliferation and interaction create complexity, then lower-entropy self-awareness, then intelligence, values, personality and purpose; lower entropy means more usable energy and eventually the ability to use directed conscious intent to reduce entropy further (bootstrapping).
- Time is "an incredibly effective engine for entropy reduction" and hence an evolutionary inevitability — the Fundamental Process "could not help but find time" — and our existence is enabled by the invention of time.
- Within PMR the energy-spring must slowly unwind (structural disintegration physicists call the second law of thermodynamics); Campbell says the second law is seen as bad long-term news only "because traditional scientists have only little TOEs."
- Given a finite digital consciousness system, constraints are inevitable; the ratio of system size to information content would tend toward a constant as AUM matures. At capacity, AUM can purge least-productive bits or start over; it may itself be a contributing player in a larger consciousness ecosystem, recycling its own consciousness. Either way, "an evolving digital dude is never done."
- Music's universal deep resonance seems reasonable to Campbell because our consciousness and entire reality "is constructed of rhythm and pattern."
- Consciousness evolution progresses not by epiphanies but by iteratively accumulating "nearly infinitesimal profitable choices"; trying to grow in great leaps impedes progress, and consciousness quality rises and falls on the intent animating everyday interactions.

## Concepts
- **space-time** — Campbell's construct of consciousness: a particular pattern of constraints applied to energy transfers between individuated consciousnesses, with a space-like structural component and time-like dynamic component; a consciousness interface filter that defines the perception of PMR. Not physical; it exists within the digital media supporting the rule-set defining our physical experience.
- **space-time rule-set (PMR rule-set)** — the collection of patterns/algorithms within TBC that defines the constraints, limits, and relational properties of our physical experience; it "subsumes our physics." Analogous to algorithms written in assembly language on top of a processor's instruction-set.
- **space-time cell / reality cell** — conceptual (not physical) cells in the space-time part of AUM's mind; a duality of this-way/that-way states (distorted vs. non-distorted). Adjacent cells communicate by changing state in sequence, letting distortions propagate through the matrix. Reality cells are "discrete units of state-specific relational content," the basic building blocks of aware consciousness — "atoms of consciousness" producing "molecules of space-time"; roughly analogous to transistors on a processor chip.
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — the virtual reality we experience as physical, defined by the space-time rule-set; a "virtual classroom or learning lab" designed to help us reduce individual entropy and grow the quality of our consciousness.
- **PMRk** — Campbell's notation for the family of PMR-type virtual-reality dimensions: if a chunk of AUM's thought-space is constrained by a rule-set defining space-time properties, "the virtual reality dimension created is one of the PMRk — perhaps our PMR."
- **NPMR / NPMRn** — Nonphysical Matter Reality, thought-space without space or distance; it has its own (much smaller) time quantum than PMR, so communication there seems instantaneous from a PMR view, though NPMR has its own speed limit too. OS (our system) contains interacting physical (PMR) and nonphysical (NPMRn) components.
- **AUO** — the primordial, relatively simple, uniform, high-entropy energy-form that is the basic foundation of consciousness ("unaware dim consciousness"); evolves time by maintaining a regular oscillation, and from it AUM evolves.
- **AUM** — the evolved large consciousness system ("consciousness-system-digitalbeing-thingamajig") within whose mind space-time is a subset; AUM is "the computer, the programmer, and the operating system," evolving under pressure to reduce its own entropy.
- **DELTA-t** — the smallest time increment (quantum) of space-time/PMR time; a positive non-zero integer n times AUM's fundamental time quantum; also the minimum time to change the state of one space-time reality cell. "All space-time will subsequently march to the beat of DELTA-t."
- **fundamental time quantum** — the smallest time increment fundamental to AUM, the smallest possible in the larger reality; all other clocks (NPMR's, PMR's) are defined as integer multiples of it.
- **n** — the number of AUM's fundamental time quanta ticking away during one DELTA-t of PMR time; inferred to be exceedingly large (10^36 in Campbell's illustrative example) so NPMR has processing time between PMR increments.
- **c (speed of light)** — the evolved constant upper limit on information propagation in PMR (~3x10^8 m/s): one space-time cell per DELTA-t. c conceptually defines the virtual width of a space-time cell and, by constraining propagation velocity, derives the concept of space from time.
- **isotropic space** — Campbell's deflated "$5 techno-speak" term: conceptual space-time is not directionally unique; it behaves the same however AUM interacts with it. Evolved because a simple 3D isotropic experience-space proved most profitable.
- **thought-space** — non-geometric, distanceless "space" within consciousness where space-time cells reside; the medium in which space-time is created and defined.
- **dimension** — a subset of specialized reality cells within AUM defining a unique (virtual) reality; dimensions are neither higher nor lower but different "the same way books are different," each specialized to contribute to entropy reduction of the whole.
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — the specialized binary computing part (a tiny subset of AUM's computing capability) within which the space-time rule-set resides and digital space-time is implemented; each reality is "contained in its own dimension or piece of thought-space within TBC, and all on the same 'network.'"
- **EBC (Even Bigger Computer)** — mentioned alongside TBC as discussed in Section 5 (Chapters 6 and 11 of Book 3); like TBC, "actually only tiny subsets of AUM's computing part."
- **Fundamental Process** — evolution's mechanism: "maintain the winners, discard the losers"; more or less random permutations and combinations applied statistically to systems with a significant array of potential outcomes; iterative and recursive, it developed complexity within primordial undifferentiated potential energy and derived c by trial and error.
- **entropy (of consciousness)** — Campbell's quality metric: lower entropy = more power, more energy available to do work, higher and more useful organization; lowering it is the point of both AUM's evolution and our existence.
- **consciousness cycle** — the down-stroke (creating individuated consciousness by bounding raw, high-entropy subsets) and up-stroke (those fragments evolving to low-entropy wholeness, lowering the whole system's entropy by their personal growth); the mechanism sustaining "the ecology of the larger consciousness system."
- **energy-spring** — Campbell's image for organized, structured, work-capable energy: consciousness evolution winds it up as entropy decreases, while within PMR it must slowly unwind (the second law of thermodynamics).
- **nested time loops / multiple levels of quantized time** — AUM's fundamental clock, NPMR's clock, and PMR's clock each with quanta vastly larger than the level below; modeled on how a simulation increments local time variables within subroutines — time "stands still" in a subroutine until it is next called.
- **Planck's length** — the measure (~16x10^-36 m in Campbell's citation) at which "some of the world's best physicists say that our 3D space becomes granular"; Campbell's illustrative cell width (3x10^-36 m) is deliberately forced near it.
- **space-time instruction-set** — the specific relationships defining AUM's constraints on space-time; these "constitute the laws of space-time physics (PMR physics)."
- **intent / free will choice** — the mechanism by which an individuated consciousness, operating in the causally consistent space-time trainer, evolves to lower entropy states; everyday intent drives consciousness quality up or down.
- **little TOE** — Campbell's jab at traditional science: a small-picture Theory of Everything; scientists lament the second law as bad news "because traditional scientists have only little TOEs."
- **Big Truth** — Campbell's term for deep truth one must come to voluntarily; illustrated by the jackass-at-the-water aphorisms closing the chapter.
- **open-minded skepticism** — the reader stance Campbell prescribes: "As always, open minded skepticism defines the correct approach."

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The space-time-cell model (the chapter's central construction)** — Moving parts: (1) a 3D isotropic matrix of uniform conceptual space-time cells in AUM's thought-space; (2) each cell a duality (distorted/non-distorted state); (3) sequential "pass-it-on" cellular interaction lets distortions propagate — the very concept that "led AUM to develop the idea of space"; (4) specialized clock cells with constant oscillations pace propagation (settable to any frequency below AUO's fundamental frequency); (5) DELTA-t = minimum cell state-change time = one quantum of PMR time; (6) max propagation = one cell per DELTA-t = c; (7) hence cell width = (c)·(DELTA-t) — cells acquire the *concept* of size (virtual size), not actual size; (8) cells conceived as spherical so any direction has equal virtual distance to neighbors (sphere-packing voids irrelevant in thought-space). Campbell's payoff: time is fundamental; space is derived by imposing the constraint c.
- **The illustrative time-quanta numbers (explicitly made up)** — AUM's fundamental quantum 10^-80 s ("ten nano-nano-…-nano-seconds"), NPMR's 10^-62 s, PMR's DELTA-t 10^-44 s — each a "billion, billion" (10^18) times the previous, giving n = 10^36 and 10^80 fundamental tics per PMR second. Cell width then = (3x10^8 m/s)·(10^-44 s) = 3x10^-36 m, near Planck's length (16x10^-36 m). Campbell says he purposely chose round numbers forcing the width near Planck's length — "for illustrative purposes only — do not take the actual values too seriously."
- **Campbell's own NPMR experience** — "From my experience, and that of many other NPMR explorers, communications are seemingly instantaneous in spaceless NPMR" — the experiential evidence he uses to infer n is exceedingly large.
- **Simulation-subroutine analogy for time standing still** — time appears frozen in a subroutine until it is next called and its local time variable increments; used to defuse the "time stands still between increments" puzzle, with nested time loops mapping AUM/NPMR/PMR clock layers (full treatment deferred to Section 5).
- **Computer core-memory / display-time analogy** — NPMR information transfer without space still takes NPMR time to flip receiver cell states, "analogous to display time, or similar to how your computer shuffles data within its core memory."
- **The computer-architecture stack analogy** — reality cells ≈ transistors (on/off, 1/0, this way/that way); AUM's basic cognitive functioning ≈ the processor's basic instruction-set; the space-time rule-set ≈ assembly-language algorithms; our experience ≈ "a simulation programmed in object oriented C++ where we are the objects"; AUM = computer + programmer + operating system.
- **DSP-chip analogy** — as understanding energy-transfer rules among transistors in a special-purpose Digital Signal Processor reveals its basic design relationships, understanding information-transfer rules between space-time cells "should produce some of the most fundamental relationships of physics" (promised for Section 4).
- **Fish-into-amphibians** — how "AUM invented space-time" should be read: evolutionarily, "the way fish invented lungs and legs with which they turned themselves into amphibians."
- **Old-man-with-white-beard warning** — the anthropomorphic creator "making people out of space-time clay on day seven" is "a perfectly nice metaphor" but not where the discussion logically leads.
- **Petri-dish / bacteria image** — "AUM's view of us may be like our view of some sluggish bacteria growing in a petri dish" — dramatizing the time-scale gap.
- **All-the-movies-ever-made image** — watching every movie ever made in a fraction of a second: what AUM's fast-forward capability looks like from our view.
- **The 8-million-year lifespan thought experiment** — imagine aging one year per 100,000 years with brilliant, nearly perfect memories: you could watch rivers and mountains come and go and study mutations in quickly breeding fruit flies — "a minute glimmer of AUM's perspective."
- **Einstein invocation** — the claim "there is no such thing as physical space" is not obviously dumb: "you will hear that same exact statement directly from Albert Einstein and other knowledgeable top-scientists in Section 6 (Chapter 19, Book 3)."
- **Relativity reconciliation** — the isotropic Euclidean conception in AUM's mind vs. curved non-Euclidean PMR perception: no conflict as long as there is no fundamental physical inertial frame in a chunky PMR; general relativity stays an elegant model of perception within PMR; physicists "having long ago buried Euclid" just need to run the paradigm in reverse.
- **Wormholes and apparent FTL** — shortcuts through wormholes don't change c as the limit on propagation through space; apparent superluminal data transfers only show the rule-set's generality allows special-case appearances of violation.
- **Alpha Centauri example** — over large distances (4.5 light-years to "the Alpha-Centauri triplet") even light-speed seems "agonizingly slow"; a caution against using direct experience to judge what is instantaneous.
- **Biological specialization parallel** — as a direct result of the space-time rule-set, clumps of biological cells ran "similar experiments in specialization": food-section (digestion), sensor-section (eyes, nose, skin, ears, taste buds), motion-section (tail, fins, flippers, legs, wings), control-section (CNS, brain), then inter-part and inter-entity communication.
- **AUM sprouting realities like Earth sprouts species / creative-writing analogy** — AUM creates dimensions and manipulates content the way we create ideas and separate them into paragraphs, books, documents; dimensions differ "the same way books are different."
- **AUM-at-capacity scenario** — a finite growing digital system's size-to-information ratio tends toward a constant; at capacity AUM can purge least-productive bits or start over, possibly recycling itself within a larger consciousness ecosystem just as we cycle individuated subsets through the PMR learning lab — "an evolving digital dude is never done."
- **Music observation** — music's universality and deep resonance is reasonable because our being and entire reality "is constructed of rhythm and pattern."
- **The jackass aphorisms** — "You can lead a jackass to water and, if you are clever enough, induce it to drink, but you cannot make it do the backstroke…" and "A little Big Truth rattling about within the small mind of a jackass can be a dangerous thing" — the trilogy is "strictly for open minded humans"; you are on your own at the watering hole of Big Truth.
- **Reader participation exercise** — Campbell invites the reader to try thinking a few big thoughts toward an original insight, then "heave a deep sigh of silent resignation in appreciation of your limitations" — both you and AUM must pull yourselves up by your own experiential bootstraps.
