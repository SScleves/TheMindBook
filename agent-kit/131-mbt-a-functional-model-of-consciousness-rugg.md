# Ch 60 — A Functional Model of Consciousness: Rugged Individualists Fail to Deliver the Goods

## In one paragraph
Campbell continues his functional model of consciousness by asking what happens when individuated consciousness units can freely filter their own experience: because each being lets in and sends out only what its free will wants, unpleasant energy packets can simply be ignored or deleted, and each individual becomes "an independent reality unto itself." He argues this "I'll have it my way" individualism produces only weak interactions — a weak link between intent, action, and consequence — and therefore slow evolution, which is why NPMR(N), though a rich and flourishing environment, was only a partial success as an evolutionary experiment: it lacks a forcing function with "sharp evolutionary teeth." Along the way he describes the nonphysical beings of NPMR as bounded, discrete thought forms whose bodies are energetically and functionally determined and who travel as information packets on the Reality Wide Web, and he explains why beings inside any virtual reality necessarily perceive themselves as physical and all other reality dimensions as nonphysical. He then recaps the whole developmental arc from AUO to AUM to individuated beings, and closes by stating that PMR and its rule-set were specifically designed to overcome NPMR's built-in learning disabilities by forging a strong accountability-feedback link — the optimal learning lab for budding consciousness.

## Core claims
- Campbell claims that the requirement to push out or direct discrete energy packets specifically to other beings means that, in a non-hostile environment, an individuated consciousness largely creates its own internal environment: its will, intent, and motivation admit and emit only what its free will wants, and unpleasant packets are ignored or deleted while the link to their source is simply turned off.
- Campbell argues that we in PMR cannot do this because an external reality exists with which we are strongly interdependent — it "waits for you, stalks you and will not let you ignore the results of your accumulated choices," forcing us to define and share a common reality with other players.
- Campbell claims that "I'll have it my way" individualism generates evolutionarily weak interactions: each individuated entity is essentially an independent reality unto itself, so the connection between intent-driven action and its consequences is weak.
- Campbell argues that evolutionary efficiency requires a strong accountability-feedback link connecting intent, action, and results, with direct consequences for both parties in an interaction.
- Campbell claims NPMR(N) — designed to provide a law-abiding, non-hostile external environment ("limited protection from rock throwers") — was only a partial success, because no forcing function exists there to optimize interactions for evolutionary growth among "individualistic puddles of consciousness" that can filter out everything they dislike; its intent → action → event → feedback → new intent learning cycle was vague and "lacking sharp evolutionary teeth," with no quick, clear, and dramatic mechanism to separate profitable intent-driven choices from unprofitable ones.
- Nevertheless, Campbell says NPMR(N) continues to flourish and evolve an immense selection of life-forms outside the various space-time PMR(k).
- Campbell describes NPMR beings as bounded, discrete thought forms (from a PMR resident's view): their individual existence is enabled by limitations and constraints applied to their consciousness; their individuality develops from how they react and change through interactions; the quality of their consciousness develops from the quality of their interactions, which depends on the quality of their intent and motivation; the boundary separating I from other identifies their bodies, while their awareness is their minds.
- Campbell claims that from AUM's perspective NPMR is a virtual reality containing other virtual realities — stacked simultaneous virtual realities, each in its own dimension, like multiple simulations with multiple levels of subroutines running in a big mainframe, within an apparently infinite digital consciousness reality where an Even Bigger Computer (EBC) contains TBC as a subset.
- Campbell claims the interactive players who inhabit the NPMR virtual reality game must follow their own rule-set and believe they possess bodies just as we believe we possess bodies — their bodies are as real and physical to them as ours are to us — and that when you visit their world physically, you interact with them, body and soul, according to their rule-set.
- Campbell argues that every individuated consciousness in a virtual reality believes it has a solid body — "that is, after all, the purpose of a virtual reality" — so when one virtual reality (NPMR) contains another (PMR), connected only "through the doorway of mind" (the common RWW connection), the entities within each reality must necessarily perceive themselves as physical and all other reality dimensions as nonphysical.
- Campbell claims the less aware beings in both realities think the other's existence is stupid and contradictory, while the more aware beings of both interact freely within a larger reality containing both local virtual worlds plus many others.
- Campbell urges the reader to climb out of the local reality-neighborhood ("step out of the sandbox") and interact "with the big kids in the big reality," pointing back to Chapter 23, Book 1 for how.
- Campbell claims NPMR beings have bodies with shape and form obeying their own local reality's rules: whereas our space-time bodies are genetically determined, cast in mass, and restrained to contiguous 3D motion, theirs are energetically and functionally determined, cast into habituated forms, and can travel as information packets on the Reality Wide Web — and sufficiently aware NPMR beings can change form and connect or disconnect to sources of interaction energy-exchange at will.
- Campbell claims beings in other space-time PMRs see themselves as physical beings in a physical universe, sharing many of our rules and limitations; a visitor to a foreign reality can remain invisible and nonphysical or create an appropriate body and interact as a local — each mode has its own rules, and breaking them gets "visitation privileges" revoked.
- Campbell claims nonphysical entities, like us, reflect the quality and direction of their evolution in their intent, and are driven and limited by awareness, fear, ego, needs, wants, and capacity for love; their growth choices are made by applying intent to interactions through free will — "functionally they are our ancestors, our brothers and sisters, in the great NPMR(N) consciousness experiment."
- In his round-up, Campbell claims: AUO (plain undifferentiated consciousness energy) plus the Fundamental Process of evolution developed dim awareness by differentiation; AUO morphed into AUM, a fully aware operational consciousness — "a purposeful low entropy mind with staggering storage and processing capability."
- Campbell claims consciousness is individual and interactive by nature; AUM's trick is to optimize and maximize the useful information per conscious entity in each intent → action → result → feedback interaction, achieved by limiting and precluding overlap of individual functions and processes, isolating each being's awareness, genuine intentions, and results.
- Campbell claims this isolated awareness, with its newfound sense of separateness, must exist "seemingly trapped" within a virtual external environment full of potential interactors for energy exchange — but that environment gives only weak incentive to evolve because its beings are only weakly interdependent, so the learning cycle "hang[s] from a weak thread of causal responsibility."
- Campbell concludes that PMR and its defining rule-set are specifically designed to overcome those built-in learning disabilities by providing "the optimal learning lab for budding consciousness."

## Concepts
- **individuated consciousness** — A discrete, bounded unit of consciousness whose existence is made possible by limitations and constraints applied to consciousness; in a non-hostile environment it largely creates its own internal environment, admitting and emitting only what its free will wants.
- **energy packets** — Discrete packets of energy that conscious beings push out or direct specifically to other beings; interaction currency between individuated consciousnesses. Unpleasant or unwanted packets can be ignored or deleted by the receiver.
- **accountability-feedback link** — The connection Campbell says must be forged between intent, action, and the results of that action, leading directly to consequences for both parties in an interaction; weak in NPMR, strong by design in PMR.
- **intent → action → result → feedback learning cycle** — Campbell's schematic of how conscious entities learn and evolve (also given as intent → action → event → feedback → new intent); in NPMR(N) it was "vague, difficult to define and lacking sharp evolutionary teeth."
- **weak interactions** — Interactions among individualistic consciousness units that can filter out everything they dislike; they produce slow development, slow rates of evolution, and slow experimental results because the causal thread between action and consequence is weak.
- **forcing function** — A mechanism that would optimize interactions for evolutionary growth; Campbell says none exists in NPMR(N), which is the core problem PMR's rule-set solves.
- **NPMR / NPMR(N)** — Nonphysical Matter Reality; NPMR(N) is our particular sector, designed to provide a law-abiding, non-hostile external environment ("limited protection from rock throwers"). Only a partial success evolutionarily, yet it continues to flourish and evolve an immense selection of life-forms outside the space-time PMRs; called "the great NPMR(N) consciousness experiment."
- **PMR / PMR(k)** — Physical Matter Reality; PMR(k) denotes the various space-time physical realities. PMR and its defining rule-set are specifically designed to overcome NPMR's learning disabilities — the optimal learning lab for budding consciousness, enforcing strong interdependence.
- **AUO** — Absolute Unbounded Oneness (used here as the label for plain undifferentiated consciousness energy) which, together with the Fundamental Process of evolution, develops dim awareness by differentiating parts of itself from other parts.
- **AUM** — What AUO morphs into "as dimness gives way to brightness": a fully aware operational consciousness, a purposeful low entropy mind with staggering storage and processing capability; the entity from whose perspective NPMR is a virtual reality containing other virtual realities.
- **Fundamental Process (of evolution)** — The evolutionary process that, applied to the organization of aware consciousness potential energy, stimulates inquisitive probing into the available states of being and drives interactivity.
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — Campbell's computer model of the reality system, here placed as a subset of an Even Bigger Computer within an apparently infinite digital consciousness reality.
- **EBC (Even Bigger Computer)** — The larger digital-consciousness container that contains TBC as a subset; supports the image of stacked simultaneous virtual realities as multiple simulations with multiple levels of subroutines running in a big mainframe.
- **virtual reality** — A reality dimension whose inhabitants must follow its rule-set and believe they possess solid, physical bodies — "that is, after all, the purpose of a virtual reality." Realities can be stacked, each in its own dimension, one containing another (NPMR contains PMR).
- **rule-set** — The set of rules governing interactive content within each reality-dimension; players must follow their own reality's rule-set, and visitors to another reality interact according to that reality's rule-set.
- **RWW (Reality Wide Web)** — The network connecting all consciousness within the larger consciousness system; the "doorway of mind" through which realities connect, and the network on which NPMR beings' bodies can travel as information packets (if they know how).
- **nonphysical body** — A body belonging to a being in another reality dimension; sounds like "a moronic oxymoron" from PMR's little perspective, but every reality's inhabitants experience their own bodies as real and physical and everyone else's dimension as non-physical. NPMR bodies are energetically and functionally determined and cast into habituated forms, versus our genetically determined, mass-cast, contiguous-3D-motion bodies.
- **quality of consciousness** — Develops from the quality of a being's interactions, which depends on the quality of its intent and motivation; the degree and direction of an entity's evolution is reflected by its intent.
- **free will** — The faculty by which an individuated consciousness chooses what to let in and send out, and by which choices (opportunities for growth) are made by applying intent to interactions with other beings.
- **intent / motivation** — The driver of choice quality; will, intent, and motivation determine what an individuated consciousness admits and emits, and intent reflects how and in what direction an entity has evolved.
- **low entropy mind** — Campbell's characterization of AUM: purposeful, fully aware, with staggering storage and processing capability (entropy as the inverse measure of consciousness organization).
- **love / fear / ego** — Alongside awareness, needs, and wants, the drivers and limiters of nonphysical (and physical) beings: they are "driven, animated, and limited by their individual awareness, fear, ego, needs, wants, and capacity for love."
- **learning lab** — Campbell's description of PMR: the optimal learning lab for budding consciousness, engineered via its rule-set to create strong interdependence and sharp accountability.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The "turn it all off" thought experiment** — Campbell asks the reader: "Wouldn't it be wonderful to be able to turn off or ignore everything that is annoying or unpleasant in your existence?" and answers why you can't: an external reality strongly interdependent with you "waits for you, stalks you and will not let you ignore the results of your accumulated choices." This contrast shows what NPMR beings *can* do (delete unpleasant packets, switch off links to their sources) and why PMR's enforced shared reality is evolutionarily superior.
- **"Rugged individualists" / "puddles of consciousness"** — The chapter's title example: "I'll have it my way" individualism seems ideal but yields weak interactions; individualistic "puddles of consciousness" that filter out everything unpleasant lack a forcing function for growth. Shows why NPMR(N) was only a partial success.
- **"Limited protection from rock throwers"** — Campbell's shorthand for NPMR(N)'s design intent: a law-abiding, non-hostile external environment.
- **The mainframe / stacked-simulations analogy** — An apparently infinite digital consciousness reality where an Even Bigger Computer (EBC) contains TBC as a subset; stacked simultaneous virtual realities, each in its own dimension, are "multiple simulations with multiple levels of subroutines running in a big mainframe." Shows that realities-within-realities is not a difficult concept in digital terms.
- **The reciprocal-physicality model** — When one virtual reality (NPMR) contains another (PMR), connected only through the doorway of mind (the common RWW connection), each reality's entities necessarily perceive themselves as physical and the other as nonphysical; less aware beings on both sides dismiss the other as "stupid and contradictory," more aware beings on both sides interact freely in the larger containing reality. This dissolves the "moronic oxymoron" objection to nonphysical bodies.
- **The sandbox/playground analogy** — "You need to step out of the sandbox before you can explore the larger playground, much less the city, state, country, and planet in which the sandbox resides": Campbell's exhortation to explore the bigger reality, with a pointer to reread Chapter 23, Book 1 for how.
- **Visiting NPMR physically** — The players inhabiting the NPMR virtual reality must follow their own rule-set and believe they possess bodies just as we believe we possess bodies; their bodies are as real and physical to them as ours are to us. When you visit their world physically, you interact with them, body and soul, according to their rule-set.
- **Visiting other realities (interaction protocol)** — When visiting a reality outside one's native dimension, one can remain invisible and nonphysical or create an appropriate body and interact as a local; each permitted mode has its own rules and conditions — "Obey the rules and don't make a mess of things or your visitation privileges will be revoked."
- **Body-determination contrast** — Our bodies: genetically determined, cast in mass, restrained to contiguous 3D motion. NPMR bodies: energetically and functionally determined, cast into habituated forms, able to travel as information packets on the RWW; aware NPMR beings change form and connect/disconnect to interaction energy-exchange at will.
- **The round-up recap (Campbell's model with its moving parts)** — (1) AUO = plain undifferentiated consciousness energy; (2) AUO + Fundamental Process → dim awareness via self-differentiation; (3) AUO morphs into AUM, a fully aware, purposeful, low entropy operational mind; (4) AUM sets up interactions among individual consciousness units, motivated by inquisitive probing into available states of being; (5) AUM's optimization trick: limit and preclude overlap of individual functions and processes to isolate each being's awareness, intentions, and results, maximizing useful information per intent → action → result → feedback interaction; (6) the isolated being exists "seemingly trapped" in a virtual external environment full of potential interactors; (7) weak interdependence there makes evolution inefficient; (8) PMR's rule-set is designed to fix exactly this.

## Thread
This chapter sits inside Section 4's "functional model of consciousness" sequence, following the account of how AUM individuated consciousness units and gave them interaction via energy packets. Its job is to expose the flaw in the first-draft design: NPMR(N)'s non-hostile, filterable environment lets rugged individualists opt out of consequences, so the intent-action-result-feedback learning cycle lacks evolutionary teeth. The chapter's own "round-up" explicitly recaps the arc from AUO through AUM to individuated, seemingly trapped beings in a virtual environment. By diagnosing weak interdependence as the problem, it sets up the trilogy's justification for PMR itself: a purpose-built rule-set enforcing strong accountability — the optimal learning lab — which the following chapters develop. It also advances the stacked-virtual-realities picture (EBC ⊃ TBC; NPMR ⊃ PMR, joined through the RWW "doorway of mind"), explaining why "nonphysical body" only sounds oxymoronic from inside one frame.
