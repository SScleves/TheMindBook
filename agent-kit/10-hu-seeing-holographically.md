# Ch 6 — Seeing Holographically

## In one paragraph
Talbot argues that ordinary vision is already a construction — the brain edits, filters, and fills in what we "see" — which raises the question of what real aspects of the world we routinely edit out. Following Pribram, he proposes that mystics and psychics who perceive the human aura are catching glimpses of the frequency domain, the interference-pattern reality behind appearances. He assembles testimony from psychic healers (Brennan, Dryer, Rich), medical professionals with "higher sense perception" (Karagulla's doctors, Krieger, Joy), and Valerie Hunt's electromyographic measurements to argue that a subtle, information-rich, nonlocal human energy field is real, holographically organized, and detectable. This field, he suggests, is more primary than the physical body — a blueprint or "implicate order" of the body from which illness and healing both flow — and it may even be where the mind itself resides, since both Libet/Feinstein's timing data and Hunt's EMG-before-EEG findings dethrone the brain as the seat of decision and awareness. The chapter closes by warning that at these subtle levels reality is participatory: the very structures observers find in the field (layers, Sanskrit letters in chakras) may be created by belief.

## Core claims
- Talbot argues the eye/brain is "not a faithful camera": visual input is edited by the temporal lobes before reaching the visual cortex, and some studies suggest less than 50 percent of what we "see" is based on information entering our eyes — the rest is pieced together from expectation (and perhaps "other sources such as reality fields").
- Talbot argues that if we see less than half of what is out there, there may be important aspects of reality — beyond even technological reach — that our brains have learned to edit out; per Pribram, mystical experiences are glimpses of the frequency domain.
- Talbot argues the aura/human energy field is a cross-cultural constant (97 cultures, 97 names) and is visible to individuals with a specially developed capacity — including Talbot himself, who reports seeing it.
- Talbot argues that talented aura-seers can make strikingly accurate medical and psychological diagnoses from the field, and that some can heal by manipulating it.
- Talbot argues that Valerie Hunt's electromyograph research experimentally confirms the field's existence: it radiates at frequencies averaging 100–1600 cps (sometimes far higher), is strongest at the chakra points, correlates measurably with the colors aura readers report, and yields a rich "chaos holograph pattern" from only 3–4 seconds of data.
- Talbot argues the field is holographic in several ways: it is a frequency phenomenon; its information (like EEG/EKG signals) is distributed globally through the body; every portion of the aura contains aspects of the whole; and it is nonlocal — readable at any distance — until "plucked out of the frequency domain by human perception."
- Talbot argues the field is not purely electromagnetic but composed of an as-yet-undiscovered, subtler energy — matching ancient Hindu claims of energies "beyond the atom" (paramanu) and Bohm's belief in subtle subquantum energies.
- Talbot argues that thoughts manifest as three-dimensional images and even moving "holographic movies" in the aura, written partly in the symbolic "forgotten language" of the unconscious; some occult traditions hold such images are a product of the third, or mental, layer of the aura, but until this can be confirmed or denied we must rely on the experiences of the psychics who see them.
- Talbot argues the energy field functions as a blueprint that molds the body (the body's "own version of an implicate order"): illness appears in the field weeks or months before the body, which would explain image-induced illness, visualization healing, and even stigmata.
- Talbot argues (via Tiller) that a "ratchet effect" carries patterns from mind level down through subtle levels to physical manifestation — and speculatively, that the universe itself coalesced the same way from a divine pattern "via a series of holograms."
- Talbot argues that reality at these subtle levels is participatory: psychics may be partly creating the structures (layers, chakra letters) they claim to discover, so we must be cautious about claims of discovered structure in the field.
- Talbot argues that examining the human energy field leads to precisely the same conclusion Pribram reached about the brain: we have two realities — one in which our bodies are concrete and precisely located in space and time, and one in which our very being exists primarily as a shimmering cloud of energy whose ultimate location in space is ambiguous. This raises the question: if brain and body are just holograms, the densest part of an increasingly subtle continuum of energy fields, what becomes of mind?
- Talbot argues the mind may not be in the brain at all but in the energy field: Libet/Feinstein showed the unconscious acts before conscious awareness, and Hunt showed the field responds to stimuli before the brain does.
- Throughout, Talbot presents psychic testimony (including his own experiences) as evidence to be taken seriously, not as established scientific fact — e.g., quoting Karagulla that "when many reliable individuals independently report the same kind of phenomena, it is time science takes cognizance of it."

## Concepts
- **Frequency domain** — Pribram's term, originally for the frequencies of the normal sensory world, now used for the interference patterns that compose the implicate order; reality at large is a frequency domain and the brain is a lens converting frequencies into the objective world of appearances. The ordinary world is the "image-object" domain.
- **Blind spot** — the spot in the middle of the retina where the optic nerve connects and there are no photoreceptors; the brain fills in the gap so seamlessly we never notice the hole (demonstrated with the book's figure 15 star/circle exercise, at about 10 to 15 inches).
- **Human energy field / aura** — a subtle field of energy around the human body, a halolike envelope of light just beyond normal perception; known as prana in India (sacred writings over 5,000 years old), ch'i in China (since the third millennium B.C., the energy of the acupuncture meridians), and nefish in the Kabbalah (sixth century B.C., an egg-shaped bubble of iridescence around every body).
- **Subtle bodies / layers** — many psychics say the field has seven main layers, actually three-dimensional energy bodies occupying the same space as the physical body at increasingly larger sizes and lesser densities; commonly the first four are named the etheric body, the astral/emotional body, the mental body, and the causal/intuitive body; the last three are variously named but relate to the soul and higher spiritual functioning.
- **Etheric body** — the energy body closest in size to the physical body; a kind of energy blueprint involved in guiding and shaping the growth of the physical body.
- **Chakras** — special energy centers (Sanskrit for "wheel") connected to endocrine glands and major nerve centers but extending up into the energy field, looking like spinning vortices of energy when viewed head-on; described in Indian yogic literature.
- **Crown chakra** — the chakra originating in the uppermost tip of the brain, associated with spiritual awakening; clairvoyants describe it as a little cyclone on top of the head, a few inches to a foot or more tall, growing taller and brighter in joy.
- **Etheric mucus** — Brennan's term for the sticky gray residue that drugs such as alcohol, marijuana, and cocaine create in the aura, dulling its healthy colors.
- **Higher sense perception (HSP)** — Karagulla's term for the faculty of seeing the human energy field.
- **Therapeutic touch** — Krieger's name (chosen to avoid the loaded terms aura and chakra) for the healing method of feeling and manipulating blockages in the human energy field with the hands, learned from Dora Kunz; now taught to thousands of nurses and used in hospitals worldwide.
- **Chaos / chaos patterns / strange attractors** — chaos is the study of phenomena so haphazard they appear lawless (turbulent smoke, waterfall base, epileptic brain fluctuations, colliding weather fronts); mathematical analysis converts such data into shapes on a computer screen — a straight line if no hidden pattern, a yarn-around-nails spiral shape ("chaos pattern" or "strange attractor") if hidden regularities exist.
- **Chaos holograph pattern** — Hunt's name for the never-repeating but highly dynamic and complex chaos pattern she obtained from the high frequencies of the human energy field.
- **Mystical personalities** — Hunt's category for people with energy-field frequencies above 900 cps; unlike psychics (400–800 cps) or trance channelers (narrow 800–900 cps band), mystics possess the wisdom to use information, are aware of cosmic interrelatedness, and are anchored in ordinary reality while extending far beyond — some measured up to 200,000 cps.
- **Anu / paramanu** — ancient Hindu terms: matter is composed of anu, "atoms," while the subtle vibratory energies of the human energy field exist paramanu, literally "beyond the atom."
- **Internal vision / X-ray vision** — the clairvoyant ability to adjust vision and look through flesh and bone "as if they were no more than layers of colored mist," seeing organs, glands, bone density, even microscopic structures; Brennan's term is "internal vision"; universal in shamanic traditions.
- **Holographic Body Assessment** — Jue's name (reframing Ernest Pecci's "body reading" in holographic-model terms) for psychometrizing a person's body by laying on hands to read the emotional scripts, core issues, and relationship patterns held in the body's energy patterns.
- **Wave-substance / portrayals** — Swedenborg's terms (nearly three hundred years ago) for the wave he saw around people and the visible thought-images within it.
- **The forgotten language** — Erich Fromm's name for the symbolic/metaphoric language of the unconscious found in dreams, myths, fairy tales, and religious visions — and, per Dryer, in some holographic movies in the aura.
- **Energy field as blueprint** — the idea that the physical body is just one more level of density in the field, a hologram coalesced out of the aura's interference patterns; since illness can appear in the field weeks or months before the body, the field may be more primary — "the body's own version of an implicate order."
- **Ratchet ("rachet") effect** — Tiller's mechanism by which thought-generated patterns at the mind level of nature manifest downward, first at the etheric level and ultimately at the physical level as disease (or, cosmologically, as the material universe itself).
- **Participatory reality** — the quantum-physics tenet that we are not discovering reality but participating in its creation; at subtle levels this becomes more pronounced, so structures "found" in the energy field (layers, Sanskrit letters) may have been created by observers' beliefs. Talbot notes the ratchet/template processes do not depend on the fields being stratified into rigidly defined layers — they would work even if the subtle fields are a smooth continuum.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **Talbot's misspelled No Parking sign (personal anecdote)** — Talbot stared transfixed at an ordinary No Parking sign until his friend exclaimed it was misspelled; as he watched, the wrong letter "changed" into an e. Both had unconsciously edited the sign into correctness. Talbot says this first showed him the eye/brain "is not a faithful camera, but tinkers with the world before it gives it to us."
- **Pribram's early vision studies (monkeys)** — Pribram found that visual information a monkey receives via its optic nerves does not travel directly to the visual cortex but is first filtered through other brain areas; numerous studies show the same in humans, where the temporal lobes edit and modify visual information. Some studies suggest less than 50 percent of what we "see" is based on information entering the eyes. Shows the brain, not the eye, sees.
- **Everyday editing examples** — not noticing a close friend has shaved his mustache; one's house looking strangely different after a vacation. We respond to what we think is there, not what is there.
- **Blind spot demonstration (figure 15)** — close the left eye, stare at the circle in the middle of a grid with the right eye, and move the page (about 10–15 inches) until the star vanishes on the blind spot; then close the right eye and stare at the star until the circle vanishes — the grid lines remain intact because the brain fills in what it thinks should be there. Shows the brain "reweaves the tapestry of our visual reality" without our awareness.
- **Perception beyond human range (technology examples)** — spiderwebs look drab white to us but are brightly colored (hence alluring) to the ultraviolet-sensitive eyes of insects; fluorescent lamps flicker on and off just too fast for humans to discern, but the strobelike effect is visible to honeybees, who must see every flower while flying at speed over a meadow. Shows real features of the world escape our senses entirely.
- **White & Krippner, Future Science** — writer John White and parapsychologist Stanley Krippner list 97 different cultures that refer to the aura under 97 different names. Talbot uses this to show the concept's cross-cultural universality (alongside prana, ch'i, nefish, and halo iconography in Christian, Chinese, Japanese, Tibetan, and Egyptian traditions).
- **Luminous saints** — Thurston devotes an entire chapter of his book on miracles to luminous phenomena associated with Catholic saints; both Neumann and Sai Baba are reported to have had occasionally visible auras; the Sufi mystic Hazrat Inayat Khan (d. 1927) is said to have sometimes given off so much light that people could actually read by it. Supports the claim that a very spiritual person's aura can become visible to normal perception.
- **Talbot's own aura sight (personal)** — his ability arose spontaneously; the first time he saw the mist of light around his arm he thought his sleeve had caught fire. He clearly sees only the crown chakra — a whirlwind a few inches to a foot-plus tall that grows taller/brighter in joy and "bobs and sways like a candle flame" when people dance; he wonders if this is what the apostle Luke described as the tongues of fire at Pentecost. He sees aura colors only occasionally.
- **Barbara Brennan's background** — began as an atmospherics physicist working for NASA at the Goddard Space Flight Center, later a counselor; as a child she could walk blindfolded through the woods avoiding trees by sensing their energy fields with her hands; several years into counseling she began seeing halos of colored light around people's heads and developed the ability into healing.
- **Brennan: uterus diagnosis case** — Brennan told a woman there was something abnormal about her uterus; the woman confirmed her doctor had found the same problem, which had already caused one miscarriage, and several physicians had recommended a hysterectomy. Brennan advised a month off with self-care; a month later the woman's physician confirmed the uterus had returned to normal, and a year later she gave birth to a healthy baby boy.
- **Brennan: broken coccyx case** — Brennan saw that a man's sexual performance problems traced to a coccyx (tailbone) broken when he was twelve; the still out-of-place coccyx was pressing on his spinal column, causing the dysfunction.
- **Brennan: disease and drug signatures in the aura** — early cancer looks gray-blue, turning black as it progresses; white spots appear in the black, and if they sparkle and look like they're erupting from a volcano, the cancer has metastasized. Alcohol, marijuana, and cocaine create "etheric mucus"; in one case she told a startled client which nostril he habitually used to snort cocaine because the field over that side of his face was always gray with it. Prescription drugs often cause dark areas over the liver; chemotherapy "clogs" the entire field; she has seen auric traces of supposedly harmless radiopaque dye (used to diagnose spinal injuries) a full ten years after injection.
- **Brennan: psychological signatures** — a psychopathic personality has a top-heavy aura; a masochistic personality's field is coarse, dense, and more gray than blue; a rigid personality's field is also coarse and grayish with most energy concentrated on the aura's outer edge.
- **Brennan: healing and endorsements** — she says illness can be caused by tears, blockages, and imbalances in the aura, and manipulating these with her hands and her own field enhances healing. Elisabeth Kubler-Ross calls her "probably one of the best spiritual healers in the Western Hemisphere"; Bernie Siegel says her work "is mind opening" and fits his experience.
- **Brennan: long-distance aura reading** — she can read a person's aura at a distance of many miles; her longest reading so far was during a telephone conversation between New York City and Italy. Talbot presents this as evidence the field "exists everywhere and is nonlocal until it is plucked out of the frequency domain by human perception." Her book is Hands of Light.
- **Carol Dryer's practice** — Los Angeles "human energy field consultant"; has seen auras as long as she can remember (got in trouble as a child for revealing intimate details about her parents' friends); over five thousand clients in a decade and a half, including celebrities (Tina Turner, Madonna, Rosanna Arquette, Judy Collins, Valerie Harper, Linda Gray) plus physicists, journalists, archaeologists, lawyers, and politicians; has assisted police and consults for psychologists, psychiatrists, and medical doctors.
- **Dryer's mode of seeing** — gives long-distance readings but prefers the same room; sees the field as well with eyes closed as open and usually keeps them closed to concentrate — yet it is "always in front of me as if I'm looking at a movie or a play... more real and more brightly colored" than the room. She does not see stratified layers or often even the physical outline; when she sees the etheric it is because it contains leaks or rips ("like a ripped blanket or a torn curtain") from trauma, injury, illness, or devastating experience. She experiences layers as changing textures and intensities — like ocean water of different temperatures washing by — her vision "telescoping through various levels and dimensions." Like Brennan she can diagnose illnesses from the aura, and when she chooses she can adjust her vision and see the chakras; her special skill, though, is the eerily accurate status report on a person's emotional, psychological, and spiritual being.
- **Dryer: fields as fingerprints** — she perceives kaleidoscopic clouds of color, complex images, glistening shapes, gossamer mists; shallow people have shallow, humdrum auras, complex people complex ones; "A person's energy field is as individual as their fingerprint. I've never really seen any two that look alike."
- **Talbot's first reading with Dryer (personal)** — as virtual strangers, she described specific, detailed assessments of his talents, vulnerabilities, and personality dynamics that not even close friends knew; some clients liken a session with her to six months of psychotherapy. Talbot also spoke with or heard session recordings of over two dozen of her clients, who almost without exception found her equally accurate.
- **Shafica Karagulla's investigation of HSP doctors** — neurologist/psychiatrist (M.D. from the American University of Beirut; psychiatry under Sir David K. Henderson at the Royal Edinburgh Hospital; three and a half years as research associate to Wilder Penfield, whose memory studies launched Lashley and Pribram). Began a skeptic, became a believer after confirming aura-seers' diagnostic accuracy; coined "higher sense perception" and in the 1960s sought doctors with the ability.

Don't tell me anything."). He scanned her body and described her health, including an internal condition that would eventually require surgery — a condition she had secretly already diagnosed. He was "correct in every detail."

Unknown to each other and thinking themselves alone and peculiar, they invariably described an "energy field" or "moving web of frequency" around and interpenetrating the body; some saw chakras without knowing the term, describing "vortices of energy at certain points along the spine, connected with or influencing the endocrine system." Almost all kept it secret to protect their reputations. Karagulla concludes science should take cognizance when many reliable individuals independently report the same phenomena.

Anxiety levels dropped 17 percent in authentically treated patients after only five minutes; no change in the "fake" treatment group. The study was the lead story in the Science Times section of the March 26, 1985, New York Times.

Brugh Joy's discovery (1972)** — USC heart and lung specialist (graduate of Johns Hopkins and the Mayo Clinic) was examining a healthy male in his early twenties when his hand passed over the solar plexus and he sensed "something that felt like a warm cloud" radiating three to four feet from the body, cylinder-shaped, about four inches in diameter. He found all his patients had palpable cylinderlike radiations at various body points; only on reading an ancient Hindu book did he realize he had rediscovered the chakras. He thinks the holographic model best explains the field, believes the ability to see auras is latent in all of us, and now mostly teaches others to sense the field.
- **Michael Crichton learns to see the field** — the bestselling author (The Andromeda Strain, Sphere; director of Coma and The First Great Train Robbery), who holds a medical degree from Harvard University Medical School, describes in his autobiography Travels how he learned to feel and eventually see the human energy field studying under Joy and other teachers: "There isn't any delusion. It is absolutely clear that this body energy is a genuine phenomenon of some kind."

She got interested after meeting a dancer who said she used her own energy field to help her dance; made EMGs of the dancer's muscles while dancing and studied healers' effects on patients' muscle electricity. Baseline biology: brain electrical activity runs 0–100 cps (mostly 0–30), muscles up to about 225 cps, heart to about 250 cps. Hunt's electrodes picked up an additional field radiating from the body — subtler and smaller in amplitude, averaging 100–1600 cps and sometimes higher, strongest not at brain/heart/muscle but in the areas associated with the chakras. "The results were so exciting that I simply was not able to sleep that night."

Four seconds of EKG data → straight line; same amount of EEG data → straight line; EMG → slightly swollen line, still no chaos pattern; even the field's lower frequencies → straight line. But the field's very high frequencies produced "the most dynamic chaos pattern you ever saw" — never repeating, so dynamic and complex she named it a "chaos holograph pattern." She believes it was the first true chaos pattern found in a major electrobiological system: researchers later found chaos patterns in EEGs but needed many minutes of data from numerous electrodes, whereas Hunt got hers from three to four seconds of data from one electrode — suggesting the field is far richer in information and more complexly organized than the brain's electrical activity.

Every portion of the "holographic field reality" of the aura contains aspects of the whole field, yet portions are not identical — the differing amplitudes keep the field dynamic and flowing rather than a static hologram.

Psychics almost universally describe it as higher-frequency than normal matter-energy; ancient Hindu literature says matter is made of anu ("atoms") and the field's energies exist paramanu ("beyond the atom"). Bohm likewise believes in many subtle subquantum energies unknown to science; he doesn't know whether the field exists but says attention reaching the implicate order's subtler levels should "see more than we ordinarily see." We don't know") — as with electrons, we can only describe behavior, so the energy field will ultimately be defined by how it behaves.

Dryer, his house guest, walked in and immediately said "Oh my, you must be thinking about your werewolf novel because you've become a werewolf"; comparing notes, they were seeing the same features, and the image slowly faded as his thoughts strayed from the novel. Shows prominent thoughts forming verifiable three-dimensional images in the field.

- **Dryer: three-dimensional movies** — usually in color but sometimes brown or tintype-like; stories can take five minutes to an hour to unfold and are incredibly detailed (how many plants in a room, leaves per plant, bricks in a wall). When she chooses an image it expands to fill the room — "sometimes I feel I can step right into it... as if I'm in a three-dimensional movie, a holographic movie, with the person."
- **Talbot's toy-closet incident (personal)** — at age five Talbot spent hours meticulously organizing his toys in a closet and admonished his mother not to touch anything; in his first reading Dryer described this incident in detail (chuckling as she watched it "unfold like a movie" in his field), along with many other events of his life.
- **Dryer: the unconscious's picture-show** — some of her holographic movies are written in the symbolic language of the unconscious (Fromm's "forgotten language" of dreams, myths, fairy tales, religious visions); she peels back the waking self and reads the river of unconscious imagery, which is why "Jungian psychologists love me." Her verification method: a misinterpreted image "doesn't go away. It just stays in the energy field," but once the person has been told everything they need to know it dissolves and disappears — she thinks the client's own unconscious chooses what to show her (like Ullman, she believes the psyche tries to teach the conscious self what it needs to grow). Talbot's own reading felt like being told a dream he had not yet dreamed. He wonders if this density of information is why Hunt's aura data produced such a pronounced chaos pattern.
- **Emanuel Swedenborg's portrayals** — nearly three hundred years ago the Swedish mystic reported seeing a "wave-substance" around people in which thoughts were visible as images he called "portrayals": "I could see solid concepts of thought as though they were surrounded by a kind of wave..."; thinking about a known person, "his image appeared... but all around, like something flowing in waves, was everything I had known and thought about him from boyhood." Shows the images-in-aura phenomenon is not new.
- **Ronald Wong Jue's Holographic Body Assessment** — California clinical psychologist, former president of the Association for Transpersonal Psychology, talented clairvoyant; finds an individual's history contained in the body's inherent "energy patterns" ("The body is a kind of microcosm, a universe unto itself"). Instead of seeing movies in the field, he conjures them in his mind's eye by laying hands on the person — psychometrizing the body — to quickly determine emotional scripts, core issues, and relationship patterns; used clinically to facilitate therapy and taught in seminars. Learned from psychiatrist colleague Ernest Pecci, who called it "body reading"; Jue renamed it via the holographic model.
- **Karagulla's "Diane": intestinal blockage case** — Diane, head of a corporation with X-ray vision ("For me as a psychiatrist to be meeting somebody who was reported to be able to 'see' right through me was a shattering reversal of my usual procedures," Karagulla wrote). X-rays revealed a blockage in the precise area, and three days later the woman had surgery to remove the life-threatening obstruction.
- **Diane: hospital outpatient tests** — Karagulla had Diane diagnose random patients at a large New York hospital's outpatient clinic, checking against records. For one unknown woman, Diane reported: pituitary gland missing, pancreas apparently not functioning properly, breasts affected but now missing, not enough energy through the spine from the waist down, trouble with her legs.
