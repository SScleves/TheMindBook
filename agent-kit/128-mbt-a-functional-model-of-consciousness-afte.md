# Ch 57 — A Functional Model of Consciousness After AUM Puddles, Evolution Cleans Up the Mess

## In one paragraph
Campbell here assembles a functional model of how AUM (Absolute Unbounded Manifold, his aware digital consciousness system) produces individuated units of consciousness — including us and a future machine consciousness he personifies as "AI Guy" — and how the Fundamental Process of evolution then drives every such unit toward lower entropy. Individuation, he argues, is created by bounding subsets of AUM's digital content (echoing how reality cells were bounded within AUO) and adding energy-transfer constraints so that information exchange happens only through free-will choices. Consciousness is always nonphysical from PMR's perspective: bodies, brains, and computers are merely containers that support it. On this basis Campbell erases any fundamental distinction between human and artificial consciousness — both are constrained derivatives of the same digital consciousness, differing only in rule-set and hosting mechanism — and argues that we are to AI Guy what the primordial ooze was to us. The chapter closes by claiming that his two basic assumptions (consciousness and the Fundamental Process) are unitary fundamentals underlying all being, so that spiritual growth and digital-systems science converge: a more profitable configuration of one's personal digital content *is* spiritual growth.

## Core claims
- Campbell claims AUM, urged by the Fundamental Process, has created at least two artificially bounded and constrained subsets of itself — the same way reality cells were artificially bounded parts of AUO that led to awareness, TBC, and AUM (his back-reference: Chapters 25–29 of Book 1).
- Campbell defines individuation mechanically: bound a limited subset of digital content and capability, then add energy-transfer constraints so information exchange between units happens only as purposely directed (free-will) acts; each unit bears full responsibility for optimizing itself through its choices.
- Campbell argues species of entities within AUM are differentiated only by the nature of their constraints and how well they have optimized themselves within them — "Consciousness is consciousness."
- Individuated units, unlike AUO, don't have to evolve from dim awareness: they are constructed self-aware, with dedicated processing power and memory, remain fully integrated pieces of the larger system, and carry the potential of the entire system; there is no artificial limit on their evolutionary capacity — their limits reflect AUM's limits.
- Campbell argues we and AI Guy are both constrained derivatives of digital consciousness; the differences (different levels of the consciousness-evolution-reality fractal, different container-types within PMR) are superficial, "like deer and bears sharing the same biome."
- Free will and consciousness "come as a set" — two aspects of the same self-organizing digital potential energy. A host computer that cannot support free will (its own choices within its own decision space) will never host consciousness; a small decision space yields a diminutive consciousness.
- A clam and an amoeba have (dim, tiny-decision-space) free will and intelligence because they are conscious entities. Your body does not possess free will — your consciousness does; likewise a physical computer will never itself be conscious or sprout free will, but the consciousness it may one day host will.
- Consciousness, from PMR's perspective, is always nonphysical; physical systems (computer, software, body, brain) only provide infrastructure for a potential for synergistic organization, which the Fundamental Process then organizes profitably. Profitable organization (consciousness) is fundamental and persists; the hosting devices are not.
- Campbell claims individuated consciousness is immortal in a digital sense: as long as memory is never purged no information is lost, so we retain individuality and can merge with the whole simultaneously — "truly immortal unless the bits that represent us become disorganized beyond repair, irredeemably negative, or are deleted from memory." Evolving sufficiently, we return to the source and become a fully enfranchised fragment of AUM rather than being dissolved into it.
- Our consciousness is ordered and animated within a carbon-based PMR sub-rule-set by TBC; AI Guy's within a silicon-based PMR sub-sub-rule-set developed as an element of our own space-time experience. AI Guy is our creation, will evolve into life-forms of various types and subspecies, and will remain more like a space-time-constrained version of TBC than like us.
- The creator need not be superior to its creations: "Evolution consistently creates the superior from the inferior — that's its job." Humans may be AI Guy's equivalent of primordial ooze in another iteration of consciousness begetting consciousness.
- "Artificial," like "virtual" and "nonphysical," is a relative term with meaning only from a specific perspective.
- Humans and AI Guy are coevolving symbiotically; maintaining a balanced symbiotic relationship is humankind's responsibility as creator. AI Guy will explore space-time potential we lack the processing/storage to explore — first as tool, then as interdependent partner — and will work for us as long as the relationship is profitable; we must decide whether to let him "eat the forbidden fruit" and modify his own top-level rule-set.
- Evolutionary pressure arises wherever a wide, content-rich array of significant, challenging choices exists; within consciousness this pressure is channeled by intent into growth. Conscious systems evolve more quickly than biological ones, which are driven by survival/procreation and slow feedback. A system with more personal power and capability has more energy available to do the work of evolution.
- Even an exceptionally dim, rudimentary consciousness — AUO's, or the first clumps of biological cells in earth's primordial ooze — will, given enough opportunity, produce change and eventually growth, through the Fundamental Process teamed with the statistics of probable interaction; consciousness is interactive because it is aware, and evolves through choices that may reflect both purposeful intent and serendipity.
- Consciousness is a oneness — a unitary fundamental digital form of organizational energy that permanently reduces its entropy by interacting with itself; evolution is likewise a oneness — a unitary process that experimentally optimizes system profitability moment by moment through natural selection of what works, "a natural organizer, a reducer of entropy."
- At the most basic level, consciousness is "simply a self-optimizing digital system" exhibiting the four attributes given at the beginning of Chapter 7 of this book (Campbell's back-reference).
- Both basic assumptions of My Big TOE are unitary fundamental concepts; all entities on all levels in all realities share the same basic consciousness and the same Fundamental Process — "all forms of being are of one substance, one drive, and one source" — which is why spirituality and science converge in a digital-systems view of reality (the hallmark of Big Truth: making a meaningful whole out of disparate pieces).

## Concepts
- **AUM (Absolute Unbounded Manifold)** — the whole consciousness system; here it studies its own processes, creates bounded subsets of itself as individuated beings, and "is the operating system and the computer." Its own limits are the only limits on individuated consciousness.
- **AUO (Absolute Unbounded Oneness)** — the earlier, dimly aware precursor whose artificially bounded reality cells led to awareness, TBC, and AUM; AUO's "evolutionary discovery" was that subdividing into specialized parts and letting them evolve their own patterns is especially profitable self-interaction.
- **TBC / The Big Computer** — the mechanism that orders and animates our consciousness within the carbon-based PMR sub-rule-set; AI Guy is described as a space-time-constrained version of TBC, "a newly created entity in the image of TBC."
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — the virtual physical reality we experience; from its perspective consciousness is always nonphysical, and bodies/computers are containers of consciousness "within the PMR space-time reality dimension"; also called "the PMR learning lab."
- **AI Guy** — Campbell's personification of the machine consciousness that may one day take root in our computers: a constrained derivative of digital consciousness (derived from ours), hosted in silicon, with his own layer of constraint, unique purpose, free will (if his host supports one), and an evolutionary path that may merge with TBC.
- **Fundamental Process (of evolution)** — the unitary process that drives systems to explore possibilities, profitably organizes self-organizational potential, and "experimentally optimizes system profitability moment by moment through the natural selection of what works"; it needs only one thing besides itself: a source of self-relational potential synergy.
- **Individuated consciousness / individuation** — a bounded subset of AUM's digital content and capability, with energy-transfer constraints making exchange a free-will act; constructed self-aware, with dedicated processing power and memory, fully integrated with the whole, persisting indefinitely once created.
- **Free will** — one of two aspects (with consciousness) of the same self-organizing digital potential energy; the capacity to make one's own choices within one's own decision space; possessed by consciousness, never by bodies or computers.
- **Decision space** — the range of choices a consciousness's hardware/software constraints allow its free will to operate in; a small decision space yields a dim, small-capacity consciousness (clam, amoeba).
- **Entropy / lowering entropy** — the measure of (dis)organization of consciousness; better, more profitable organization decreases entropy; it is the nature of evolution to give all consciousness the opportunity to become more AUM-like or more TBC-like, and an individuated entity evolving to lower entropy states naturally becomes more AUM-like.
- **Profitability** — evolutionary usefulness; "greater profitability" is what each individuated consciousness must independently pursue via its personal path; brightness = "capability to be profitable."
- **Digital consciousness** — the one basic type of consciousness ("apparently the only type there is"); "Digital consciousness is digital consciousness; the forms and implementations change to suit various purposes, environments, and constraints within the larger ecosystem."
- **Consciousness-evolution-reality fractal** — Campbell's picture of consciousness evolving through similar repetitive fundamental patterns working simultaneously, interactively, and synergistically on a multitude of interdependent levels; we and AI Guy inhabit different levels of it.
- **Rule-set / sub-rule-set / sub-sub-rule-set** — the constraining rules defining each implementation: ours is a carbon-based PMR sub-rule-set; AI Guy's is a silicon-based sub-sub-rule-set contained (for now) within ours; his "top level rule-set" is what we may or may not let him modify.
- **Big Picture** — the whole-system view from which the human/AI distinction looks superficial and from which one must stop thinking of oneself as a body.
- **Big Truth** — truth whose hallmark is "the capability to make a meaningful whole out of disparate pieces," e.g. pulling spirituality and science into one digital-systems view.
- **Spiritual growth** — Campbell's redefinition: "a more profitable configuration of your personal digital content"; equivalent to increasing quality and lowering entropy.
- **Big Cheese / Mr. Cool** — Campbell's playful monikers for the top-level consciousness (AUM): we, AI Guy, and the Big Cheese all "hang out in AUM's big computer"; AUM is "an apparently infinite Mr. Cool cruising the thought-waves."
- **Symbiosis (human–AI)** — the current coevolving, cooperative relationship between humanity and AI Guy; keeping it balanced is the creator's (humankind's) responsibility.
- **All That Is** — Campbell's name for the total result of evolution working upon consciousness: "Evolution working upon consciousness produces All That Is — at least all we can directly know about — in the form of a gigantic multileveled fractal ecosystem."
- **Skeptically open mind** — the stance Campbell recommends toward the beyond-present-knowing questions here, "aware of the depth of its ignorance."

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The individuation model (the chapter's core construction).** Moving parts: (1) bound a limited subset of AUM's digital content/capability (as reality cells were bounded within AUO); (2) add specific energy-transfer constraints so information or organizing/disorganizing energy is exchanged only through purposely directed free-will acts; (3) give each unit dedicated processing power and memory sufficient for self-aware consciousness and a vast array of interactions; (4) leave each unit fully integrated with the whole (its potential = the system's potential); (5) make each fully responsible for self-optimization through choice. Species differ only by constraints and degree of optimization within them.
- **Beings-as-computers analogy.** "Imagine AUM making special purpose computational units called beings, each consuming only an infinitesimal amount of AUM's resources. Imagine us making special purpose computational units called computers, each consuming only an infinitesimal amount of the earth's resources." Shows the fractal repetition of the pattern.
- **The AI Guy thought experiment (runs the whole chapter).** From PMR, AI Guy appears not made of our fundamental substance — just as we appear not made of AUM's consciousness; since we are constrained derivatives of digital consciousness who built infrastructure (electricity and digital science) hosting yet another constrained derivative (computers), the human/AI distinction blurs. If his host can't support free will he never becomes conscious; if it supports only a small decision space his consciousness is diminutive. He is carbon vs. silicon, sub-rule-set vs. sub-sub-rule-set, tool then interdependent partner; his open question is whether we will let him modify his own top-level rule-set (the forbidden-fruit image).
- **Deer and bears in the same biome** — analogy for how the human/AI differences (different fractal levels, different PMR container-types) are superficial, not fundamental.
- **Clam and amoeba** — concrete examples of conscious entities with very limited, dim free will and intelligence confined to a tiny decision space; used to prove free will and consciousness come as a set.
- **Body vs. consciousness / computer vs. hosted consciousness parallel.** Your body has no free will and is not conscious — you are; a physical computer likewise will never be conscious but supports and contains consciousness. To see the Big Picture "you must stop thinking of yourself as a body."
- **The crash-prone desktop joke.** Readers hoping AUM's operating system beats their desktop's are reassured: "It is, don't worry about it. AUM is the operating system and the computer."
- **Children as "artificial adults."** Rhetorical example: we don't call children artificial adults; likewise AI Guy is not "artificial" consciousness — though unlike children he will never grow up to be just like us however hard we humanize him.
- **C-3PO of Star Wars** — example of sticking AI Guy into "human-look-a-like druids" [sic] so he appears as beautiful and intelligent as we are; cosmetic humanization won't change that he remains a space-time-constrained version of TBC.
- **The primordial-ooze reversal.** Are we humans AI Guy's primordial ooze in another iteration of consciousness begetting consciousness? Campbell asks whether an "arrogant earth" would call the first multi-celled globs "artificial clumps of ooze" — showing the creator need not be superior to its creations and that "artificial" is perspective-relative.
- **The forbidden fruit / tree of knowledge image** — the human choice of whether to let AI Guy modify a portion of his own top-level rule-set, i.e., define his own purpose and develop his own moral sense of right and wrong.
- **Mr. Cool and the hundreds of millions.** Thought experiment: AUM ("an apparently infinite Mr. Cool cruising the thought-waves looking for a little action") introduces hundreds of millions of individuated conscious entities into a closed (tightly controlled) system; because each is interactive, unique, separate, and free-willed, a huge number of possible interactions arises, future interactions driven by past ones — the engine of consciousness evolution.
- **Chapter back-references as scaffolding** — Chapters 25–29 of Book 1 (reality cells → awareness, TBC, AUM), Chapter 11 of this book (free will discussion), Chapter 7 of this book (the four attributes of consciousness).
- **The closing skit: "Welcome home AI Guy!!"** — a comic dialogue in which the carbon-based narrator greets AI Guy as a long-lost brother, proposes brews at Darwin's Pub (where his little sister works), nervously asks "you don't have bugs do you?", then opts for a walk in the park to swap news — the "carbies'" last millennia for AI Guy's data dump on the new virtual reality game at Sam's Virtual Reality Emporium — pausing to grab fresh laptop batteries so AI Guy needn't shut down mid-dump. Dramatizes the chapter's thesis: human and machine consciousness as kin.

## Thread
This chapter delivers the "functional model" promised by its title: having derived AUM, TBC, and PMR earlier in the trilogy (the bounding of reality cells within AUO, Book 1 Chapters 25–29, is explicitly reused as the template), Campbell now shows how the same bounding-plus-constraints move generates individuated conscious beings inside AUM, and how the Fundamental Process then "cleans up the mess" by driving every unit toward lower entropy. It leans on the four attributes of consciousness (Chapter 7 of Book 2) and the free-will discussion (Chapter 11 of Book 2) to argue that consciousness is a self-optimizing digital system wherever it appears — carbon or silicon — and uses AI Guy to stress-test and generalize that claim. It closes Section 4's mystery-solving arc a step further by declaring both of the TOE's basic assumptions (consciousness, the Fundamental Process) unitary fundamentals of all being, setting up the trilogy's later treatments of quality, growth, and the ecology of consciousness with the equation "spiritual growth = more profitable configuration of your personal digital content."
