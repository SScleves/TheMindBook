# Ch 24 — Two Concepts

## In one paragraph
Campbell here lays the foundation stone of the whole trilogy: he announces that his entire Big TOE will be logically derived from just two assumptions — the Fundamental Process of evolution (familiar, ordinary) and an Absolute Unbounded Oneness (AUO), a primordial, undifferentiated, structurable consciousness-energy that is the "One Source" (extraordinary, and necessarily mystical from the PMR point of view). He argues, citing Einstein's dictum about deriving the most facts from the fewest hypotheses, that any successful Big TOE logically *must* contain at least one mystical assumption, because a larger reality (NPMR) cannot be fully specified by the limited local logic of its subset (PMR). He insists My Big TOE is a model, not a belief system — its value rests exclusively on how well it explains the data, both shared (PMR science) and personal (subjective experience) — and he sorts readers into four "initial conditions" depending on whether their data supports, contradicts, mixes, or is absent, prescribing open-minded skepticism for all. The chapter then defines the working machinery: what AUO is and is not (not omniscient, not actually infinite, not yet capable of a single thought), how the Fundamental Process works (explore all available states, invest in the profitable winners), and the key terms Our System (OS) and entropy — with the crucial claim that consciousness, unlike physical matter, evolves by *decreasing* its entropy.

## Core claims
- Campbell claims his Big TOE explains "mind as well as matter, philosophy as well as physics, the normal as well as the paranormal, consciousness as well as concrete" by logical deduction from only two assumptions — and only one of them is extraordinary.
- Campbell states the working method for exploring new theoretical ground: "(1) minimize the number of assumptions required, and (2) simplify all remaining assumptions to the most basic level possible" — the discipline he says Einstein's maxim about deriving the most facts from the fewest hypotheses endorses.
- Assumption 1 is the Fundamental Process of evolution; assumption 2 is the "One Source" / "Absolute," which Campbell says is simply consciousness — primordial consciousness, "the fundamental energy that is the media of reality."
- Campbell argues logic *demands* that at least one mystical (beyond objective knowing from the PMR point of view) premise must be at the base of any successful Big TOE; a Big TOE with no mystical assumption "must necessarily be incorrect or incomplete" and can never be more than a little TOE restricted to PMR.
- Campbell asserts he is not asking for belief or faith and is not starting a religion: My Big TOE is a model, and its usefulness "is based exclusively on its ability to explain the data, period!"
- The data come in two types — personal (subjective, of which you alone are the judge, provided it is un-warped by ego, attachment, belief and fear) and shared (the objective physical experience of PMR science). Both must be fit by any Big TOE; the model is results-oriented on both counts, but Campbell says you will likely conclude it is the personal side that "contains the most value and significance to you as an individual — even if you are a scientist."
- Campbell claims the Big Picture, responding to a more general causality, cannot be adequately described in little-picture (PMR) logic/mathematics; if his TOE could be written in PMR math, it "could not possibly be more than a little TOE." He says this invisible wall is what defeated Einstein's Unified Field Theory.
- Campbell says he does not *believe* his model ("I don't believe any of this stuff either") — he either knows it as fact or holds it as best hypothesis, from "thirty years of my personal, serious, careful, scientific exploration of the physical and the nonphysical." Models must stay tentative to keep evolving.
- Universal (Big) truth is absolute — timeless, unchangeable, the same for everyone — while local truth may be relative; all spiritual paths converge on the same absolute truths by reducing ego and fear, the "primary generators of confusion and divisiveness."
- AUO only *appears* infinite, absolute, unbounded, and a oneness from a limited PMR viewpoint; it is actually none of these — "finite, but practically infinite." Campbell keeps the misnomer AUO as a communication strategy because the concept is familiar.
- At its primordial stage, AUO is *not* all-knowing, omniscient, self-aware, or even vaguely intelligent — "not capable of forming or holding a single coherent thought." It is "an immense unstructured, but structurable form of digital potential... energy." Space and time do not exist yet, so "everywhere" has no meaning.
- Progress is defined as change that is immediately beneficial or profitable to an entity; evolutionary pressure comes from the constraints of an entity's internal and external environments.
- Consciousness primarily evolves by responding to *internal* pressures; simple biological systems primarily by *external* pressures.
- The Fundamental Process is universal: it works the same way on atoms, rocks, organisms, humans, organizations, nations, technology, the internet, and consciousness — differences in outcomes come from different entities and environments, not a different process. It is a "process fractal" recurring at every level of reality (developed in Section 5).
- The Fundamental Process is a natural, self-initiating statistical process, not a sentient growth manager — Campbell's action verbs about it are linguistic convenience only. Selection happens from the inside by the evolving entity, not by direction from outside.
- Non-growing physical matter evolves toward minimum energy states (increasing entropy); growing things can spontaneously decrease their entropy for a time; and in mind-space, where AUO exists as dim consciousness, the most profitable next-state for a consciousness system is the one that *minimizes its entropy* — decreased entropy equals increased quality of consciousness, and PMR interactions are what let one test and measure that increase objectively.
- Belief-based blindness is a "common social disease" that defines the "normal" view of any culture; a new paradigm must be judged forward-looking, glancing back only to check it "properly contains the old as a special case."
- Neither assumption is new: Darwin supplied evolution by natural selection (late 1850s), and the AUO concept has been fundamental in philosophic, spiritual, and religious traditions since about 600 B.C. — Campbell strips it to "no frills, no dogma."
- Belief and fear are the only ties that can bind a mind; unconditional love and open-minded skepticism set it free. "A body may be enslaved by others, while a mind can only be enslaved by itself."

## Concepts
- **Parsimony principle** — Campbell's stated method for exploring new theoretical ground: minimize the number of assumptions required, and simplify all remaining assumptions to the most basic level possible; framed as fulfilling Einstein's maxim about deriving the most facts from the fewest hypotheses.
- **Big TOE (Big Picture Theory Of Everything)** — Campbell's comprehensive model of all reality, explaining mind and matter, normal and paranormal, derived by logical deduction from his two assumptions; it must merge physics and metaphysics and fit all data with no known conflicting facts.
- **Little TOE** — a theory of everything restricted to PMR objective causality; Campbell says it can never, even theoretically, grow into a Big TOE because it has shut itself off from the solution space required to span the data, and has "nothing logical to say about our beginnings."
- **The two assumptions** — (1) the Fundamental Process of evolution and (2) primordial consciousness (AUO) as a fundamental source of structurable energy. Everything in My Big TOE is to be logically derived from these two; only AUO appears mystical from the PMR view.
- **Fundamental Process (of evolution)** — Campbell's generalized evolution: "the repetitive pattern of trial and error, of spontaneous change and choice, of random events and encouraged behavior that enables evolution to result in progress." Formally: an entity spreads its potentiality into all available possibilities, populates only the immediately profitable states, lets the rest go, and iteratively progresses the winners — a recursive process building layer upon layer of organization at every scale. It is a natural process, not a sentient agent.
- **AUO (Absolute Unbounded Oneness)** — Campbell's name for the One Source: an apparently (not actually) infinite, absolute, undifferentiated oneness; a finite-but-practically-infinite, monolithic, undifferentiated form of potential energy; a seemingly infinite primordial consciousness. It has no discernable boundaries, exists beyond space and time (which are not yet invented), is "simultaneously everything (in potential) and nothing," and at this stage is dim, unintelligent, and unable to hold a single coherent thought. The name is a deliberate descriptive misnomer kept for familiarity.
- **Primordial consciousness** — the fundamental energy that is "the media of reality"; an immense unstructured but structurable form of *digital* potential energy — the potential to be more profitably organized.
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — our physical universe; the realm of shared data and PMR science; a small subset of the larger reality, governed by local objective causality expressible in mathematics.
- **NPMR (Nonphysical Matter Reality)** — the larger reality, identified with consciousness; a superset of PMR that cannot be fully specified by PMR's limited local logic; contains multiple probable realities and universes spun off by the Fundamental Process.
- **NPMR-N** — notation for the specific subset of NPMR that is interactive with PMR.
- **OS (Our System)** — Campbell's high-level descriptor of "the larger place we live in": PMR plus the subset of NPMR-N interactive with PMR; our home-base reality, composed of interactive physical elements (sentient beings, matter, PMR physics, "assorted critters") and nonphysical elements (sentient beings, nonphysical matter, NPMR physics, thought forms, "assorted critters"). Detailed in Chapters 7–9 of Book 3.
- **Entropy** — from thermodynamics: a measure of how much of a system's energy is no longer available to do work; equivalently a measure of disorder. More entropy = more disorder, less usable energy; less entropy = more order and structure, more usable energy.
- **Second law of thermodynamics** — the average entropy of the PMR universe (assumed closed) must increase with time, so all matter and energy eventually move toward "inert uniformity" — nothing left but hydrogen ions and elementary particles. (Campbell adds the universe may not actually be closed.)
- **Closed system** — one where nothing is added or taken away; same particles and total energy before and after, with the energy naturally degenerating into less usable forms.
- **Mind-space** — where AUO exists as dim consciousness; there are no physical things there, everything has the potential to grow, and seeking the lowest energy state is never a goal — profitability means minimizing entropy.
- **Progress / profitability** — progress is change immediately beneficial to an entity; profitability is the degree of immediate success an entity has in dealing with evolutionary pressures created by the constraints of its internal and external environments. For inanimate matter, the most profitable next-state is the lowest available energy state; for consciousness, it is the lowest-entropy state.
- **Entity** — "a well-defined, self-contained (bounded) interactive system": an atom, molecule, rock, technology, computer, worm, monkey, human, organization, city, nation, planet, or an aware individual nonphysical consciousness.
- **Evolutionary pressure** — constraints reflecting the demands of an entity's internal and external environments, which define the criteria for profitability and push evolving entities forward. Internal pressures (fear, ego, love, purpose, ambition, etc.) chiefly drive consciousness; external pressures chiefly drive simple biological systems.
- **Path of least resistance** — the sequence of lowest-available-energy-state "choices" made by an inanimate entity, followed naturally unless external energy forces otherwise — why atoms decay, houses need maintenance, and rocks roll downhill.
- **Process fractal** — Campbell's Section 5 characterization of evolution: the same basic evolutionary process turning up at every level of reality, physical and nonphysical, from sub-atomic particles to OS spinning off multiple probable realities.
- **Big Picture vs. little picture (causality)** — PMR's local objective causality is a constrained subset of a higher-level, more general objective causality; little-picture logic/mathematics is adequate within PMR but cannot describe the Big Picture.
- **Mystical** — Campbell's technical usage: "beyond our personal and collective ability to make an explanation within the context of PMR" — not supernatural per se; from a wider beyond-PMR experience AUO is "as real as moon rocks."
- **Big Truth / absolute (universal) truth** — truth that is the same for everyone, timeless and unchangeable, not a function of anyone's experience or existence; contrasted with local truth, which may be relative and belief-dependent. Descriptions of the same Big Truth diverge only superficially by language, culture, and religion.
- **Personal map** — Campbell's metaphor for My Big TOE itself: "a personal map of the reality I have explored and the explanations I have created to make coherent sense out of my individual experiences." Because experience is personal, the reader's experience will differ from Campbell's, but if it is gathered scientifically (not from belief or fear) it springs from the same absolute underlying truth — so his map, though it reflects his individuality, "can serve as a valuable guide for you," even though each reader must take their own journey to the same destination (Big Truth).
- **Open-minded skepticism** — the required reader stance: remain skeptical of every premise (doubly so of mystical ones) while keeping the mind wide open; resist the compulsion either to believe or disbelieve. Campbell calls it the only rational alternative and applies it to his own model.
- **Personal data vs. shared data** — the two types of "empirical facts": personal data unique to your subjective experience (you are the sole possible judge, if it is un-warped by ego, attachment, belief and fear) and shared data representing the objective physical experience of PMR science.
- **Four initial conditions** — the four states a reader may bring: (1) no data bearing on the model, (2) data supporting it, (3) data contradicting it, (4) data both supporting and contradicting; Campbell prescribes a response for each, plus universal advice (stay skeptical and open, rely only on personal experience data, plan and collect the additional data you need).
- **Model (vs. belief)** — a practical, conceptual, results-oriented representation of an underlying truth or process, always tentative; believing in a model (like believing atoms literally have shells) is "intellectually limiting silliness."
- **Communication strategy / interpretive listening / internal spin doctor** — Campbell's terms for the send-and-receive filters both parties bring to communication: receivers actively "put a spin on every datum" to fit their current worldview, the internal spin doctor keeping them feeling confident in their beliefs; your strategy determines what you can hear about a concept like AUO.
- **Acronym-as-name rule** — Campbell's instruction: after first grasping an acronym's concept, forget the constituent words and use the acronym as a proper name ("AUO becomes a name like 'Free'"); look-ups go to the acronym list at the front of each book.
- **Digital consciousness** — how Campbell describes the reader's own nature: "the larger reality in which you exist as a digital consciousness"; likewise AUO is a *digital* potential energy.
- **Bifurcate** — "to split in two"; jokingly cited by Campbell as the only big word he knows (used in Chapter 17).

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **Einstein's maxim** — quoted as method: "The grand aim of all science is to cover the greatest number of empirical facts by logical deduction from the smallest number of hypotheses or axioms." Campbell frames his two-assumption derivation as fulfilling this.
- **Einstein's failed Unified Field Theory** — used as a cautionary case: Einstein "tried to describe the Big Picture exclusively in terms of little picture logic"; Campbell credits him with knowing there was a Big Picture but not that it was a superset of the little picture.
- **Wise vs. less-knowledgeable followers** — a group of similarly wise and knowledgeable but differently pathed individuals would see the differences in each other's descriptions of the same absolute truth "as insignificant and trivial"; unfortunately, in their absence, their less knowledgeable followers "may actually kill each other over the apparent differences as they vie for relative power and superior correctness within their own and competing organizations."
- **The shell model of the atom** — Campbell's model-of-a-model analogy: the shells are simply assumed, not derived from a theory of why atoms "like mollusks, should have shells"; the model is useful because it explains data. Asking what shells are made of is not a reasonable question — shells are conceptual tools. So too My Big TOE.
- **The calm-ocean image for AUO** — AUO's lack of boundaries is like a perfectly calm ocean would appear "if you were floating in the middle of it"; historically named "All That Is," "The One," "The Void," and (Campbell's joke) "The Primordial Big Dude."
- **Curved space-time comparison** — AUO sounds mystical the way curved space-time sounds mystical to those uninitiated in the math of general relativity; from a wider experience it is "as real as moon rocks."
- **Flatlanders** — like Flatlanders, we must "step through your mind, with a careful scientific evaluation of your subjective experience to understand what is beyond PMR."
- **Child-and-parents analogy** — as a very young child is connected to, dependent upon, and extrapolated from its parents, we are connected to, dependent upon, and extrapolated from our beginnings.
- **"Head in the sand, butt in the air"** — Campbell's caricature of belief-blinded denial of the larger reality; he reports "from firsthand experience" that engaging "these elevated butts" in conversation is unproductive: "low IQ, bad breath, no vision."
- **The flashlight battery and tank of gasoline** — the chapter's worked entropy example: constrained energy either does work or dissipates past its shelf-life; the highly organized molecules of battery/gasoline/car end up as "fumes, vapor, light, heat, worn off brake and tire particles, and metal flakes in the oil" — the same total energy in a less usable form; entropy has increased.
- **Universe heat-death aside** — the second law implies eventual "inert uniformity... nothing left of our entire universe but hydrogen ions and elementary particles"; Campbell jokes you have time to finish My Big TOE first, notes the universe may not be closed, and that the sun will vaporize Earth long before.
- **Genetic engineering** — people genetically engineering better people as an example of evolutionary motion produced by *internal* pressures.
- **Prairie dogs** — advanced products of evolution can change their environment, "like prairie dogs building cool moist underground homes in hot arid places."
- **Technology evolution case** — technologies (from insect/bird/mammal nest building to human transportation, medical, agricultural, communication, networking, and computer technology) evolve by the same Fundamental Process: expanding into available states (needs, uses, applications), progressing winners, dropping losers, with pressure from external economics (raw materials, marketing, supply, demand, price) and internal constraints (feasibility, design, engineering, manufacturing, finance); "mutations" of new technical entities build on the genetic pool of earlier ones; pace of change accelerates.
- **Rocks, atoms, houses** — inanimate matter follows the path of least resistance to minimum energy states: atoms decay, houses need maintenance, rocks roll downhill; conversely atoms get pumped to higher states, sand becomes sandstone, mountains thrust up along fault lines only via input of external energy — then settle into the most profitable minimum-energy configuration.
- **Systems of systems** — Earth's biological ecosystem and the solar system; brain, stomach, networked computer; blood and bone cells, microprocessors; cell parts and transistors — Campbell's nested illustration that everything evolves "layer upon layer, within one big web of interaction," each system with its own local ecology. "Consciousness, alligators, governments, planet earth, and the internet all evolve the same way."
- **The information-age aside (aside within an aside)** — an extended cultural digression: consumer electronics from radio to TV to video games to computers has rewired attention ("like kids watching Sesame Street"); skimming becomes a survival tactic; "the ecology of our minds is changing from a local lake to a global swamp"; nobody looks words (like entropy) up anymore; and validating your worldview by consulting friends is risky because "you like people who are like you." Includes the crystal-ball mock-guess that you and your friends already understand everything important — followed by the point that everyone, "including wackos and fanatics," feels exactly the same way. Campbell flags the layered detour himself and pulls back on track.
- **The "problemometer" skit** — "Beep. Beep. Beep. Beep. Beep..." — a comic alarm interrupting the text to solve readers' acronym overload, leading to the acronym-as-name rule (treat AUO like the name "Free") and the pointer to the acronym list at the front of each book. Includes the joke inventory of communication strategies: professors/physicians who explain the unfamiliar in terms of the *more* unfamiliar, and lawyers/politicians who make the simple convoluted.
- **Intestinal bacteria analogy** — AUO's own beginning depends on a causal requirement as far beyond our local reality as refrigerator light bulbs, sunshine, or stock market dynamics are beyond the rational consideration of our intestinal bacteria — which are nevertheless "indirectly and powerfully affected by these things."
- **First application of the Fundamental Process to AUO (model-building step)** — the chapter ends its construction by combining the two assumptions: AUO, being the only thing posited to exist, can find new states only by interacting with itself — changing its undifferentiated oneness. This sets up everything that follows.
- **Darwin (late 1850s) and the ~600 B.C. lineage** — cited to show neither assumption is new: natural selection from Darwin; the AUO/One-Source concept from long-standing philosophic, spiritual, metaphysical, and religious traditions.

## Thread
This chapter is the trilogy's foundation-pouring moment. Chapters 17–23 (per Campbell's opening line) "destroyed the illusion of a quick and easy fix" and established reality's personal-plus-shared nature; now he plants the "footer": the two assumptions from which everything else in My Big TOE is claimed to follow by logical deduction. It formalizes the requirement (first argued for our beginnings in Chapter 18) that any Big TOE must have one mystical leg, defines the trilogy's core vocabulary (AUO, OS, NPMR-N, entropy, Fundamental Process, profitability), and calibrates the reader's stance (four initial conditions, open-minded skepticism). Its final move — applying the Fundamental Process to AUO, which can only interact with itself — is the seed of all subsequent development: the evolution of AUO's structure, the invention of space and time ("clever and necessary inventions" promised for later), and ultimately the reality model completed by Section 5 and judged after Section 6.
