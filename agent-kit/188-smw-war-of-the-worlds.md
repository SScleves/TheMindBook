# War of the Worlds

## In one paragraph
In this chapter (numbered 4 in the book itself) Tom DeLonge & Peter Levenda braid together three postwar strands — Soviet espionage, the Nazi scientist pipeline into America, and the birth of official UFO secrecy — and argue they are one story. Opening with the theft, over the Fourth of July weekend of 1948, of 1,885 classified pages from Theodore von Kármán's safe at Columbia by his protégé and Soviet spy William Perl (photographed by the Rosenberg ring), the authors place von Kármán at the center of the emerging military-industrial complex he himself proposed, even as Paperclip Nazis like Dornberger arrived at Wright Field the same month as the Roswell wreckage. They then relay a claim from one of their unnamed high-level sources: the Roswell crash was a man-made flying disk of Nazi design, and work through what that would require — Kammler's disappearance, the Škoda Works SS think tank, ODESSA and the ratlines, a stateless "Nazi Nation." The chapter traces how Project Sign's suppressed "Estimate of the Situation" set the seventy-year template of ridicule and secrecy, examines the Antarctic operations (NANOOK, HIGHJUMP, Deep Freeze) and Forrestal's death, and closes on the green fireballs over Los Alamos and the question of what UFO-adjacent material Perl may have handed the KGB. Throughout, the authors distinguish what the documents show from what their source asserts, and press their thesis that Cold War secrecy and UFO secrecy became inseparable — and that neither terrestrial explanation fully works.

## Core claims
- The authors claim that the postwar UFO problem cannot be separated from the Cold War: "The UFO Phenomenon is as much a feature of the Cold War as NATO, mutually assured destruction (MAD), and the spy novels of John le Carré."
- They argue the WWII "foo fighters" could not have been Nazi or Japanese secret weapons because they appeared in both theaters at once; the only conclusion at the time was that "they were not of this Earth" — not of any known human civilization or technology.
- They claim Theodore von Kármán effectively proposed the military-industrial complex (a Peenemünde-model, centrally led weapons R&D center) and sat at its center — while his protégés (Parsons, Malina, Qian, and above all Perl) were purged or were actual Soviet agents, yet von Kármán himself was never touched and received the first National Medal of Science from JFK in 1963.
- They claim William Perl "could not have been any guiltier": VENONA identifies him as "Gnome"/"James"/"Brahms," he supplied the 1,885 pages from von Kármán's safe to the Rosenberg ring, but was only convicted of perjury because prosecuting espionage would have exposed VENONA.
- They argue the US government treated its own patriotic scientists (Parsons, Oppenheimer, Malina, Qian) worse than the more than one thousand committed Nazi enemies imported under Paperclip — "there was a sinister method to this insanity." Positions of influence shifted "from the clean hands and quirky genius of idealistic and patriotic Americans to the bloody hands of foreign-born Nazis and war criminals."
- They report — attributing it explicitly to one of their "otherwise impeccable and unimpeachable" sources — that the Roswell crash was a flying disk, man-made, of neither American nor Soviet manufacture: a craft designed by the Nazis. They stress their own initial doubt.
- They argue that the very fact UFOs crash implies human manufacture: "the UFOs that have crashed—all of them—were made by us."
- They claim the handling of Roswell (General Ramey's weather-balloon reversal) "would prefigure how the military and the government would handle, and mishandle, the UFO phenomenon for the next seventy years," with institutionalized science becoming "the sole arbiter of reality at the guidance of political and military expediency."
- They claim a vast Nazi infrastructure survived the war (ODESSA, ratlines, Schacht's networks, expatriated funds and corporate subsidiaries), aided by Western intelligence from 1946–47 onward, and could conceivably have sustained covert aircraft development — a stateless "Nazi Nation" analogous to modern nonstate actors.
- They argue Hans Kammler — who controlled all Reich scientific R&D at war's end, including the Škoda Works SS think tank that Dornberger and von Braun could not access — did not die but, per recently surfaced documentation, "wound up working for the Americans"; his disappearance may have been contrived to let the "lesser" Nordhausen secrets distract the Allies from the real prize.
- They claim Project Sign's suppressed pro-extraterrestrial "Estimate of the Situation" was rejected by General Vandenberg and all copies subsequently destroyed — whatever his motive (incredulity, focus on man-made enemies, or "he simply knew better") — and, separately, that the earlier official Project Sign statement dismissing the ETH as "more likely" a foreign nation's craft "formed the template for everything to follow": debunkery, ridicule, and misidentification claims.
- They note the logical bind the authors say defeats every neat explanation: the Arnold/Rhodes sightings match Horten-type Nazi designs, but no known human propulsion (jet, rocket, nuclear) matches the observed behavior — leaving the proposition that "the craft may have been built by human beings, but the technology derives from a nonhuman source."
- They argue UFO secrecy cannot be justified as keeping secrets from enemy states (every state sees the Phenomenon); the secrets are being kept from citizens, and the cleared form "a secret society entrusted . . . with secret knowledge."
- They claim the military's decision that UFOs posed no threat because they didn't shoot was a category error: PSYOP shows an enemy can be defeated "without firing a shot," and "there is a demonstrably uneven development of our bicameral minds, and it may be this very vulnerability that the Phenomenon exploits."
- They claim Operation HIGHJUMP "made no sense then, and makes no sense now" — a 13-ship, ~5,000-man Antarctic convoy amid drastic demobilization — "which means it was probably not what it was claimed to be," possibly triggered by whatever Operation NANOOK encountered, and coinciding with the ghost-rocket wave.

## Concepts
- **foo fighters** — The strange colored lights/craft sighted by experienced airmen on all sides in both the European and Pacific theaters of WWII; because they could be neither Nazi nor Japanese weapons (wrong theaters), the authors say the only conclusion was that they were not made by human beings. The authors note their physical properties were "impossible to reconcile with 'present world knowledge'" — unlike the later 1947–48 disk sightings.
- **the Phenomenon** — The authors' standing term for the UFO/UAP reality they assume is genuine as the project's operating premise ("we proceed on the assumption that the UFO Phenomenon is real"), while granting that not every sighting, document, or assertion is valid.
- **extraterrestrial hypothesis (ETH)** — The proposition that UFOs are craft from beyond Earth; Hermann Oberth was one of its most prominent supporters, Project Sign's team came to accept it, and Vandenberg ordered it abandoned. The authors treat its official suppression as the founding act of UFO secrecy.
- **military-industrial complex** — Eisenhower's phrase for the postwar military–science–industry alliance; the authors argue von Kármán proposed it (an advanced weapons R&D center "under one leadership," on the Peenemünde model) and that its unforeseen consequence was "the usurpation of the 'truth' by that complex; indeed, the usurpation of legitimacy, reality, and even culture."
- **Suicide Squad** — Von Kármán's Caltech rocketry circle — Jack Parsons, Frank Malina, Ed Forman, Qian Xuesen and others — who "created the American space program out of whole cloth"; several lost clearances or left the country under suspicion of Communist ties.
- **Kármán vortex street** — Von Kármán's theory of vortices describing what happens to a structure under vibrations of increasing magnitude; demonstrated by the 1940 Tacoma bridge collapse, believed to underlie hummingbird/insect flight, and — the authors note — paralleled by a German wartime vortex theory that "became the basis for the design of an actual flying saucer."
- **Operation Crossbow** — The British air campaign of three massive raids on Peenemünde in August 1943; the first raid deliberately targeted the living quarters of the very scientists later brought to America under Paperclip.
- **Operation Paperclip** — The program that brought more than one thousand Nazi scientists, engineers, and medical specialists to the United States; the authors stress it "was a military operation, not a civilian one," aimed at long-range ballistic missiles and war-fighting platforms.
- **Operation Alsos** — "The Manhattan Project's own Operation Paperclip–type search through Europe, sometimes behind enemy lines, to locate German scientists and their atomic secrets"; the authors note alsos is Greek for "grove" — a wink at General Leslie Groves.
- **VENONA** — The US Signal Intelligence Service's covert project (begun 1943) to analyze encrypted Soviet diplomatic traffic; it exposed Perl under the code names "Gnome," then (from September 1944) "James"/Yacov and possibly "Brahms." Protecting VENONA is why Perl escaped espionage charges.
- **the Roswell Deception** — The authors' name (section heading) for the Air Force's flying-disk-then-weather-balloon reversal at Roswell in 1947, a deception "that fooled very few people" and set the management template for the next seventy years.
- **lenticular craft** — The Horten Brothers' flying-wing design, "very nearly a disk—with a scooped out rear section, like the heel of a shoe"; the shape matches both the Rhodes photograph (Phoenix, 1948) and Kenneth Arnold's 1947 drawings.
- **Project Sign** — The Air Force's 1948 UFO investigation, whose "study requirements" stated present world knowledge was "probably adequate . . . for spaceship construction"; its final report (February 1949, classified) leaned terrestrial on Vandenberg's command while leaving the ET question open for future investigators.
- **"Estimate of the Situation"** — The suppressed Project Sign report (credited chiefly to Alfred Loedding) concluding the UFOs were extraterrestrial; Vandenberg rejected it and all copies were destroyed, "literally incinerated" (per Ruppelt, who had read it).
- **Project Grudge** — Project Sign's successor, "an appropriate name for a project that had no real support at the Air Force unless it was to dismiss all UFO reports as misidentifications or hoaxes"; SAG figures (Langmuir, Valley, Kaplan) and RAND assisted it.
- **ghost rockets** — The 1946 wave of rocket-like objects over Scandinavia, first blamed on Soviets using captured Germans at Peenemünde; the authors say it became clear "even to Vandenberg" they were not Soviet — and "in 1946 when you thought 'rockets' you could only come up with 'Nazi.'"
- **ratlines** — Covert escape routes (American and other intelligence agencies, with Vatican cooperation) moving war criminals from Europe to Latin America and the Middle East, running by autumn 1946 once priority flipped from hunting Nazis to hiring them.
- **ODESSA** — The Nazi underground network, "up and fully functional by late 1945," aided by Western intelligence from 1946–47; the authors say it takes on "a new and more sinister connotation" — moving not just war criminals but "some of the most lethal-weapons development under SS control."
- **Nazi Nation** — The authors' coinage for a nonstate-actor Germany after 1945: "a kind of 'country' whose borders are nowhere and whose center is everywhere . . . a network of loyalists bound by identical beliefs and goals, scattered throughout the world, influencing or controlling local governments." Their example: Reinhard Gehlen's anti-Soviet Nazi intelligence network was revived and became the foundation of West Germany's new intelligence service, the BND.
- **Mittelwerk** — Kammler's underground V2 factory near Nordhausen in the Harz Mountains — 125,000 square meters, the largest underground factory in the world, online September 11, 1943, worked by thousands of slave laborers from Mittelbau-Dora.
- **Škoda Works SS think tank** — The top-secret SS research operation at the Škoda Works in Czechoslovakia under Kammler's ultimate jurisdiction (coordinated with chairman Wilhelm Voss), holding superweapon data "above and beyond" Peenemünde/Nordhausen — inaccessible even to Dornberger and von Braun; its truckload of secrets was lost to the Red Army.
- **Operation NANOOK** — Task Force Sixty Eight's joint American-Danish 1946 mission (July–September, six ships) to set up a radio/weather station at Thule, Greenland; its fleet was then rushed south to form the core of HIGHJUMP. The authors ask: "Did NANOOK see something?"
- **Operation HIGHJUMP** — The Antarctic Developments Project, authorized late August 1946 by Navy Secretary Forrestal and commanded by Admiral Byrd: thirteen ships and nearly five thousand personnel raced to Antarctica by December 1946 amid savage defense cuts; seven lives lost, results murky — the authors call it "probably not what it was claimed to be."
- **Operation Deep Freeze / VXE-6** — The ongoing US Antarctic program since the 1950s; Deep Freeze I (Nov 1955–Apr 1956) preceded the International Geophysical Year, and Rear Admiral Dufek credited it as the source of his belief in UFOs. VXE-6 (Antarctic Development Squadron Six) is the phase in which the informant "Brian" served.
- **Thule Society (Thule Gesellschaft)** — An occult society (unlike the scantily evidenced Vril) that certainly existed and met in the same Munich hotel as the fledgling Nazi Party; a mélange of Kabbalah, yoga, rune magic, Masonic ideas, racism, and "Teutonic mystification," named for the mythical Aryan homeland beneath the pole star per its leader Baron Rudolf von Sebottendorf.
- **Gravity Research Foundation** — Founded in 1948 by Roger Babson "with the specific aim of finding a way to defeat gravity"; still awards annual prizes (past recipients include Hawking 1971, Penrose 1975, four Nobel laureates) but has produced no workable antigravity — leaving the Phenomenon's propulsion "a chimera for modern science." Babson, an MIT-trained engineer who applied Newton's laws to stock-market forecasting, had also founded Babson College (1919) and run unsuccessfully for president in 1940 on the Prohibition Party ticket against incumbent Franklin D. Roosevelt.
- **green fireballs** — The 1948 New Mexico phenomenon: horizontally flying green fireballs (hence not meteors, per meteor expert Dr. Lincoln LaPaz) on trajectories away from and then toward Los Alamos — "intelligently controlled chimera" that would not land, crash, or break up, only vanish.
- **Project RAND / World-Circling Spaceship report** — Hap Arnold's think-tank project (later the RAND Corporation) whose 340-page Preliminary Design of an Experimental World-Circling Spaceship (May 2, 1946, under Douglas Aircraft) proposed satellites eleven years before Sputnik. Arnold himself had been trained in aviation by the Wright Brothers, was the only officer ever to hold five-star rank in both the Army and the Air Force, and cofounded Pan American World Airways.
- **Scientific Advisory Group (AAF-SAG)** — The Armed Forces (Army Air Force) Scientific Advisory Group formed September 1944 under von Kármán: thirty-one hand-picked specialists hunting German scientists, documents, and prototypes; later "deeply embedded in the UFO studies."
- **Itek / CORONA** — Itek ("Information Technology"), founded 1957 by Richard Leghorn with Walkowicz and Laurance Rockefeller, was "essentially [a front] for the CIA" building camera systems for the CORONA spy satellites — the authors' example of civilians unknowingly employed by the defense establishment.
- **security-clearance secret society** — The authors' description of the cleared class: the "fluttered" (polygraphed) live "in a different world," comfortable only among their own, "members of a secret society entrusted (a key concept) with secret knowledge," treating the uncleared as ignorant or deluded.
- **PSYOP / mass-hysteria countermeasure** — The military's chief worry about UFOs was their use in psychological warfare to create mass hysteria; the "only effective countermeasure, therefore, was to ridicule the Phenomenon," with resulting insanity as "collateral damage." Conversely, PSYOP teaches an enemy can be defeated without a shot — which is exactly the kind of threat the authors say UFOs might pose.
- **Willcutts Report** — The recently declassified report on Forrestal's death showing severe depression, a sodium amytal regimen, rumors of surveillance, and evidence "that could be construed as proof he was conspired against and pushed out the window" — though nothing was proven.
- **DSM-1** — The first Diagnostic and Statistical Manual, "the brainchild of the Department of Defense" (needing a metric for combat stress); its foreword was written by Dr. George N. Raines — Forrestal's psychiatrist at Bethesda, an irony the authors flag.
- **uneven bicameral development** — The authors' closing diagnosis in the secrecy essay: after fifteen thousand years of civilization "we are still fighting over religion"; this unevenness of the bicameral mind may be "this very vulnerability that the Phenomenon exploits."

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The von Kármán safe theft (July 4 weekend, 1948)** — Nearly two thousand pages (1,885) of classified aerospace/weapons/atomic documents removed from von Kármán's Columbia office safe, photographed over seventeen hours with Leica cameras at 65 Morton Street, Greenwich Village, by four men (Sobell, Perl, Julius Rosenberg, one unidentified), then hand-delivered as 35mm canisters to KGB agents at a Long Island Railroad platform. Frames the whole chapter: the man at the center of American defense science was the leak point to Moscow.
- **William Perl's career** — NACA at Langley from 1940; draft avoidance via critical-industry deferments; VENONA code names Gnome→James/Brahms (the switch dated September 1944, same month AAF-SAG formed — a coincidence the authors say is "worth considering"); Pasadena July 1946; July 1947 correspondence with Wright Field on jet compressibility and with Sverdrup & Parcel on the Aswan Dam project (run under Egypt's King Farouk, a wartime Reich sympathizer and supporter of the pro-Nazi grand mufti of Jerusalem, al-Husseini); access to NACA, SAG, and a nuclear-powered-aircraft feasibility study; convicted only of perjury (five years) because espionage charges would burn VENONA. Rosenberg told David Greenglass he had word from "one of the boys" of US satellite and nuclear-aircraft plans — which the authors say could only have come from SAG via Perl.
- **Von Kármán's protégé record** — Three Caltech followers lost clearances as suspected Soviet agents; the Columbia protégé (Perl) was an actual one; two Chinese doctoral students (Qian Xuesen, Hu Ning) built Communist China's physics and missile programs; yet von Kármán got the first National Medal of Science from JFK in 1963 and died that year in Germany. The authors also note he helped Parsons negotiate Israel's missile-program deal.
- **Von Kármán's Göttingen years** — Born Budapest, May 11, 1881; studied under Ludwig Prandtl ("father of modern aerodynamics") and worked with Max Born, boarding at the same house as Born, an associate there of Heisenberg, Max Planck, Einstein, and Fritz Haber. The rise of the Nazi Party and the firing of Jewish professors split the circle: Einstein, von Kármán, and Oppenheimer left for America; Born took a post in Edinburgh; Heisenberg stayed in the Third Reich.
- **Jack Sverdrup and the military-industrial network** — CEO of Sverdrup & Parcel (Perl's Aswan Dam correspondence is part of the FBI's Rosenberg file), Sverdrup was asked by Truman to be his secretary of defense (declined to keep running the firm); a Corps of Engineers general and adviser to General MacArthur, his firm went on to build Cape Canaveral facilities and a top-secret Alaska oil pipeline.
- **Dornberger at Wright Field (July 1947)** — The Peenemünde chief arrived at Wright Field the same month the Roswell wreckage was dispatched there; 1945 CIC reports called him an extreme case of Nazi sentiment with "wishes for a Third World War" and urged internment — ignored; he rose to VP at Bell (until 1965). The authors append the Bell chain: helicopter inventor Arthur Young left for paranormal research; his son-in-law Michael Paine worked at Bell Aerospace in Texas; Michael's wife Ruth Paine got Lee Harvey Oswald his Texas Schoolbook Depository job in 1963.
- **The source's Roswell claim** — During the authors' investigation an "otherwise impeccable and unimpeachable" source calmly confirmed Roswell was a flying disk, man-made, neither American nor Soviet: "a craft designed by the Nazis." The authors describe their doubts and treat it as the hypothesis the chapter stress-tests.
- **Ramey's two debunkings** — General Roger M. Ramey killed the Roswell disk story in 1947 and then held the July 29, 1952 Washington, D.C. press conference debunking the capital overflights; his widow told multiple investigators he was upset at telling the weather-balloon story "when he knew that wasn't the case," and he became fast friends with Truman over the affair.
- **The Rhodes/Arnold/Horten match (1948)** — William Rhodes photographed a heel-shaped "lenticular" craft over Phoenix (printed in newspapers, never debunked, witness never seriously challenged) closely matching Kenneth Arnold's 1947 drawings and the Horten design — prompting Project Sign.
- **The McCoy memoranda (1947)** — Sept 24, 1947 "Flying Disc" memo cites a June 9, 1947 Moscow attaché report that the Soviets were building 1,800 aircraft based on the Horten VIII (six-engine pusher, 131-foot span, ~33,000 lbs, "jet propelled"); a same-day "Flying Saucers" memo notes a Japan sighting; a Nov 18, 1947 memo chases German scientists in Franco's Spain (Dornberger's Wright Field Germans insisted there were "no important scientists from Germany" there). The authors call this proof the Air Force linked flying discs to Horten designs and the Russians; Spain was an ODESSA transit node (Skorzeny's Madrid arms operation).
- **Vandenberg and the Estimate** — Sign personnel, reduced to the ETH as the only logical explanation, sent a delegation to Washington; Vandenberg refused, the "Estimate of the Situation" was destroyed, and Sign became Grudge. Loedding, the Estimate's principal author, filed his own "Low Aspect Ratio Aircraft" flying-saucer patent on August 25, 1948 (granted as US 2,619,302 on November 25, 1952 — the year of the D.C. flap and Puharich's contact with "the Nine"); the authors call the dates "suggestive" of reverse-engineering.
- **Project Sign report reasoning (Feb 1949)** — Key passages the authors quote: enemy craft ruled out ("It is doubtful whether a potential enemy would arouse our curiosity in so idle a fashion"); and the "Space Ships" section granting that an advanced ET civilization, alarmed by A-bombs and rockets, would visit now — even proposing correlating sighting times with A-bomb detonations to compute the craft's home base (a method the authors fault for its stacked assumptions, offering instead that the ships might already be here, under the ocean or at the South Pole). The report also floated UFOs as a PSYOP mass-hysteria test — which the authors say cannot explain worldwide, seventy-year persistence.
- **Kammler's disappearance** — SS Brigadier General Hans Kammler: built the camps and Auschwitz crematoria, demolished the Warsaw Ghetto, ran Mittelwerk (online 9/11/1943, Mittelbau-Dora slave labor), took over the V2 program in 1944, held orders to kill the ~500 scientists near Oberammergau — then vanished the day before execution; von Braun's brother bicycled to American lines to arrange surrender. Rumors: suicide; Soviet capture; or escape with blueprints for "alternate energy sources and an antigravity device." The authors cite recent documentation suggesting he "wound up working for the Americans," and researchers Tom Agoston (via Škoda chairman Wilhelm Voss interviews), Joseph Farrell, and Nick Cook.
- **Wilhelm Voss in Egypt** — From ~1951 Voss helped Naguib's Egypt build missile facilities with former SS/Wehrmacht officers and surviving technical data — revealing, the authors argue, because Voss had no V1/V2 background; his qualification was the Kammler-linked Škoda think tank.
- **Škoda Works handover** — The US Army arrived first at Plzeň but, since Czechoslovakia was assigned to Soviet control (Patton overruled by Eisenhower on Prague), was ordered to leave everything intact; a truckload of SS scientific secrets was lost to the Russians despite Voss's repeated pleas; later British/American inspection requests were stonewalled.
- **Bormann's trail** — Journalist Paul Manning (ex-CBS, Murrow colleague) documented fund transfers from Nazi Germany to South American banks tied to Bormann; a skull found in Berlin tested as his in 1999, but the authors relay the assertion that it was planted after his death in South America — a deception necessary only if his escape covered "a grander scheme."
- **U-234** — The U-boat dispatched to Japan with uranium-235, technical documents, two Japanese officers, an entire Me 262, and a Henschel Hs 293 guided bomb; intercepted and brought to Portsmouth, NH, its cargo seized by Manhattan Project officials — one "said to be Oppenheimer himself." The authors link this to speculation that Nazi data (also via Alsos) partly enabled the bomb's fast completion before Trinity (July 16, 1945).
- **Operation NANOOK → HIGHJUMP** — NANOOK (created late April 1946, run July–September, six ships, Thule, Greenland) fed directly into HIGHJUMP (authorized August 26, 1946, by Forrestal; Byrd organizing; Cruzen commanding both; 13 ships, ~5,000 personnel including 50+ scientists; departure December 2, 1946) while defense spending collapsed from 37% of GDP (1945) to under 20% (1946) to 5.5% (1947). Seven lives lost; poorly trained pilots/photographers for a supposedly cartographic mission; declassified orders demand exploration "pursued aggressively" with knowledge "limited to . . . those who need to know." Byrd's El Mercurio interview with Lee Van Atta warned of future attacks "by planes from the Poles" — aviones later mistranslated as "flying objects," feeding the legend. The authors note the timing beside the RAND spaceship report and the ghost rockets, and the resonance of the name "Thule."
- **Antarctic legends sorted** — The authors separate the documented (1938 German Antarctic expedition, one member later in Argentina; two U-boats surfacing off Argentina months after war's end) from the fabricated: Holocaust denier Ernst Zündel admitted his Hitler-Antarctic-UFO books were unserious fundraising bait, yet they were taken seriously by researchers ignorant of Antarctic conditions.
- **Admiral Dufek and "Brian" (Deep Freeze/VXE-6)** — Dufek (retired 1959) stated belief in UFOs from sightings "related to him by people who worked with him on Operation Deep Freeze." Decades later, "Brian" contacted Linda Moulton Howe: restricted airspace five miles from South Pole Station (cover story: air-sampling camp — though a scientist confirmed VXE-6 aircraft hydrocarbons do interfere with trace measurements); on overflights he saw a hole in the ice big enough to fly his LC-130F Hercules into and was ordered to silence; crews repeatedly saw "silver disks" at 25,000–35,000 feet over the Transantarctic Mountains near Beardmore Glacier; scientists near Marie Byrd Land went radio-silent two weeks, were found missing with working equipment, reappeared a week later frightened and mute, and were flown straight to New Zealand while their gear was boxed to the States. The authors verified Brian's posting and medal but stress no dates, no identity, and no corroboration of the strange events.
- **Forrestal's death** — HIGHJUMP's authorizer, first Secretary of Defense, fired by Truman March 31, 1949 (after Dewey cabinet talks leaked), dead less than two months later in a fall from Bethesda Naval Hospital; the Willcutts Report shows depression, sodium amytal, surveillance fears, and construable-but-unproven evidence of foul play. The authors find no smoking gun tying him to UFOs but "every reason to believe he was aware of them."
- **The green fireballs (December 1948)** — Dr. Lincoln LaPaz, meteor expert and Air Force consultant, driving with two Kirtland AFB officers near Bernal, NM, on December 12, 1948, saw a green fireball on a horizontal (non-meteoric) trajectory away from Los Alamos; a December 20 sighting ran toward Los Alamos. Kaplan met LaPaz, then von Kármán, who wrote to General Cabell urging formal study; but post-Grudge, Kaplan and von Kármán (April 13, 1949) "decided to toe the party line," reclassifying it as "upper atmosphere research" rather than intelligence — getting it off Cabell's desk "without actually answering any questions."
- **Peenemünde surrender bargaining** — Dornberger, von Braun, Rudolph, Debus et al. held "the discussion" and negotiated rather than surrendered: fourteen tons of hidden Peenemünde documents plus ~100 V2s were their trump card against the Soviets and British (who wanted their heads over the London buzz bombs and Dora's corpses); Dornberger had approached General Electric as early as 1944.
- **Zwicky and the interrogations** — Caltech's Fritz Zwicky (coiner of "supernova," first to predict dark matter — who nonetheless told Malina rockets couldn't work in space) served as scientist-translator in the Dornberger/von Braun interrogations and saw the Nazis exploiting the Allies' disorganized, cross-purposes questioning. Qian Xuesen was also present at a von Braun interrogation.
- **Laurance Rockefeller's disclosure push** — Venture capitalist behind McDonnell Aircraft, Eastern Airlines, Intel and Apple funding; founded PEAR at Princeton for ESP research; ran his own "Disclosure" project at the Clinton White House seeking Roswell declassification (the famous photo of Hillary Clinton leaving with a UFO book); the authors add Podesta — whom DeLonge and Levenda interviewed in 2015 — as the ongoing transparency advocate.

## Thread
This chapter is the book's deep-dive into how UFO secrecy was born — the hinge between the WWII/foo-fighter material and the later Cold War and disclosure chapters. It follows from earlier discussions of Dornberger, Peenemünde, the Horten Brothers, the Maury Island affair (Tacoma), and Operation HIGHJUMP's first mention in Chapter 3, and it converts them into an argument: the military-industrial complex von Kármán proposed absorbed the UFO question at birth, and Cold War secrecy regulations became a dual-use veil — protecting real secrets from the Soviets while burying whatever was known about the Phenomenon. The suppressed "Estimate of the Situation" and the ridicule countermeasure establish "the pattern . . . set in 1948" (the authors' own phrase) that the modern disclosure chapters push against; the Nazi-Roswell source claim and the Kammler thread keep the "made by us — but from what?" question open; and the closing tease ("All this before the UFO flights over Washington, D.C.") sets up the 1952 flap. The "Politics of Secrecy" essay is an early statement of the panopticon argument the Afterword ("The Alien in the Panopticon") completes: secrets kept not from enemies but from citizens.
