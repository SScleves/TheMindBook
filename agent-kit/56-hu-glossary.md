# Glossary

Chapter 0 = Introduction. Pointers give the chapters whose notes define or substantively develop each concept. Merged names in an entry title are treated as one concept in the notes.

- **Acausal connecting principle** — The new principle Jung proposed to account for synchronicities, since psyche-to-world causation was classically inconceivable; nonlocal effects are likewise correlations for which any causal linkage is forbidden, hence acausal. Chapters: 3. Related: synchronicity, nonlocality.
- **Active (objective) meaning** — Bohm's idea that meaning is simultaneously mental and physical (a thought is at once a neurophysiological, chemical, physical activity) and serves as the "bridge" between the two sides of reality; since electrons actively use information, responsiveness to meaning is a property of all matter. Chapters: 5. Related: resonance of meanings / super nonlocality, consciousness as a more subtle form of matter, psychokinesis, quantum potential.
- **Acupuncture & meridians** — The ancient Chinese art holding that every organ and bone connects to specific surface points (over a thousand, organized in imaginary lines called meridians); stimulating the points can alleviate disease in the connected parts. Chapters: 4. Related: acupuncture microsystems, aura / human energy field.
- **Acupuncture microsystems / auriculotherapy ("the little man in the ear")** — Nogier's term for the two smaller acupuncture systems on each ear, whose points map a miniature inverted human; Dale has evidence for eighteen such micro-acupuncture holograms across the body ("holographic reiterations of the gross anatomy"), suggesting every part of the body contains an image of the whole. Chapters: 4. Related: acupuncture & meridians, whole in every part, reflexology / iridology.
- **Aharonov–Bohm effect** — The discovery (Bohm with Aharonov, Bristol) that under the right circumstances an electron can "feel" a magnetic field in a region where there is zero probability of finding the electron — a nonlocal effect, still occasionally disputed despite numerous confirmations. Chapters: 2. Related: nonlocality, quantum potential.
- **Alam almithal** — The twelfth-century Persian Sufis' subtle matter of thought, which forms into idea-images on the adjacent template plane of being and determines the course of one's life; in ch. 8 it is also the stuff from which the afterlife realm ("the country of the hidden Imam") is created — including space itself. Chapters: 7, 8. Related: hadarat, himma, imaginal realm, land of nonwhere, thought as builder.
- **Altered / nonordinary states of consciousness** — The unusual states (with archetypal experiences and encounters with the collective unconscious) that Grof concluded only a holographic model of the brain can explain; in ch. 3 the frame for his LSD and holotropic findings. Chapters: 0, 3. Related: holotropic states / holotropic therapy, transpersonal experiences / transpersonal psychology.
- **Anomalon** — A recently discovered subatomic particle whose properties vary from laboratory to laboratory; Jahn and Dunne cite it as evidence physicists may be creating particles rather than discovering them, and Talbot reads it as a young, still-plastic reality-field. Chapters: 5. Related: reality-fields, neutrino mass, observer-created properties.
- **Anu / paramanu** — Ancient Hindu terms: matter is composed of anu, "atoms," while the subtle vibratory energies of the human energy field exist paramanu, literally "beyond the atom." Chapters: 6. Related: aura / human energy field, plenum / sea of energy.
- **Archetypes / archetypal experiences** — Jung's name for images bubbling up from the collective unconscious, so ancient it is as if each of us carries "the memory of a two-million-year-old man"; Grof models them with composite holograms (many exposures on one film yielding a Cosmic Man, Mother, Trickster, etc.). Chapters: 0, 3. Related: collective unconscious, personal resonance.
- **"As above, so below" / macrocosm is the microcosm** — The alchemists' and twelfth-century Sufis' formulas for the nonlocal whole-in-every-part organization of reality (Hermes Trismegistus: "the without is like the within of things; the small is like the large"; the Visvasara Tantra: "What is here is elsewhere"); embraced by Anaximenes, Pythagoras, Heraclitus, Plato, the Gnostics, Philo Judaeus, and Maimonides. Chapters: 9. Related: whole in every part, Indra's network of pearls, monads.
- **Associative memory** — The tendency for one thing to evoke a memory of an associated thing (Proust's madeleine); modeled by recording two objects together on one holographic film so illuminating one reconstructs the image of the other. Chapters: 1. Related: recognition holography, eidetic / photographic memory, optical memory / multiplexed holographic gratings.
- **Aumakua ("high self")** — Kahuna term for the unconscious portion of a person that sees the crystallized parts of the future and weaves one's destiny from thought forms; most people bombard it with contradictory wishes and fears, so their lives look haphazard. Chapters: 7. Related: kino mea, crystallizing possibilities, thought as builder.
- **Aura / human energy field (prana, ch'i, nefish)** — A subtle field of energy around the human body, known under 97 names in 97 cultures — prana in India, ch'i in China, nefish in the Kabbalah; first named in ch. 5 as the energy field Talbot spontaneously began seeing at seventeen, then developed in ch. 6 as an information-rich, nonlocal, holographically organized field measurable by Hunt's electromyograph, readable for medical and psychological diagnosis, and possibly more primary than the physical body. Chapters: 5, 6. Related: chakras, subtle bodies, higher sense perception, energy field as blueprint, mind in the field.
- **Autosuggestion** — Thurston's explanation of stigmata as self-induced: wound size and location vary between stigmatists, their visions are products of their own minds, and many suffered from hysteria. Chapters: 4. Related: stigmata, hypnosis, unconscious beliefs (belief strata).
- **Being of light** — The compassionate luminous being(s) encountered in the NDE who prompt the life review and teach rather than judge (only self-judgment occurs), stressing love and knowledge; their outward appearance (Christian figure, Hindu deity) is sculpted by the experiencer's beliefs, and their ultimate identity is left an open question. Chapters: 8. Related: life review, near-death experience, thought-forms.
- **Belief addiction** — Siegel's view, endorsed by Talbot, that people are addicted to their beliefs and act like addicts when someone tries to change them — why science, after centuries of disbelief, resists the paranormal. Chapters: 0. Related: scientific paradigm, belief strata, restructuring of science.
- **Belief strata (cultural beliefs, attitudes, will, unconscious beliefs, spiritual faith)** — Talbot's typology of how belief sculpts the body holographic: beliefs imposed by society (Trobriand non-pregnancy, TB death rates), passive attitudes correlating with immunity and survival, steely conscious will (Schwarz, Dajo), unconscious beliefs reached by placebo or hypnosis (the most important type), and spiritual faith (Michelli at Lourdes). Chapters: 4. Related: placebo effect, hypnosis, belief addiction, stigmata.
- **Between-life state / interlife** — The "dazzling, light-filled realm" without time or space reported by Whitton's hypnotically regressed subjects, where the important events of the next life are planned; in ch. 8 it shows all classic NDE features, with the life review serving to plan the next incarnation. Chapters: 7, 8. Related: metaconsciousness, life plan, reincarnation, soul mates.
- **Bilocation** — The ability, often associated with saints, to be in two places at once; Sai Baba reportedly goes one better, vanishing and instantly reappearing a hundred or more yards away — suggesting bodies are holographic projections. Chapters: 5. Related: dematerialization, out-of-body experience, materialization.
- **Birthmark carryover** — Stevenson's finding that injuries to a previous personality carry over as scars or birthmarks on the new body (a slit throat as a scarlike neck mark, a bullet's entry and exit points as aligned birthmarks), sometimes confirmed by hospital or autopsy records. Chapters: 7. Related: template body, reincarnation, energy field as blueprint.
- **Blind spot** — The spot in the middle of the retina where the optic nerve connects and there are no photoreceptors; the brain fills in the gap so seamlessly we never notice — evidence the brain "reweaves the tapestry of our visual reality." Chapters: 6. Related: world-out-there, frequency domain.
- **Book of Lives** — Swedenborg's name for the life-review process he watched newly deceased souls undergo; the record, he said, is stored in the nervous system of the spiritual body and evoked by an angel examining the whole body beginning with the fingers. Chapters: 8. Related: life review, wave-substance / portrayals.
- **Brahman** — The Hindu name for the implicate level of reality: formless yet the birthplace of all forms, which appear out of it and enfold back into it in endless flux; sometimes personified as pure consciousness, making consciousness more fundamental than matter. Chapters: 9. Related: implicate order, maya, void and nonvoid, veiling and projecting powers of consciousness.
- **Brocq's disease** — A hereditary condition in which the skin develops a thick, horny, reptile-scale covering; Mason's 1951 hypnotic cure of it implies the mind can override even "the masterplan, our DNA programming itself." Chapters: 4. Related: hypnosis, belief strata, stigmata.
- **Chair tests** — Croiset's precognition protocol: an experimenter randomly picks a chair from the seating plan of an upcoming unreserved-seating public event, and without knowing hall, city, or event Croiset describes the person who will sit there — almost always accurately, over twenty-five years. Chapters: 7. Related: precognition, crystallizing possibilities.
- **Chakras / crown chakra** — Special energy centers (Sanskrit "wheel") connected to endocrine glands and nerve centers but extending into the energy field, looking like spinning vortices; the crown chakra atop the head, associated with spiritual awakening, is the one Talbot himself clearly sees. Chapters: 6. Related: aura / human energy field, subtle bodies, himma, therapeutic touch.
- **Chaos, strange attractors & the chaos holograph pattern** — Chaos is the study of phenomena so haphazard they appear lawless; mathematical analysis converts such data into shapes — a straight line if no hidden pattern, a "strange attractor" if hidden regularities exist. Hunt's name for the never-repeating, highly dynamic pattern from only 3–4 seconds of the energy field's high frequencies is the "chaos holograph pattern," suggesting the field is far richer in information than the brain's electrical activity. Chapters: 6. Related: aura / human energy field, degrees of order.
- **Cities of light / halls of learning** — The vast, brilliantly luminous cities and knowledge-institutions consistently reported by NDEers, yogic adepts, and shamans; some NDEers say the buildings were literally "built out of knowledge" — perhaps clouds of pure information translated by the mind into architecture. Chapters: 8. Related: near-death experience, thought-forms, vision of total knowledge.
- **Collective unconscious** — Jung's concept of a shared source, common to all people, from which myths, dreams, hallucinations, and religious visions spring; the holographic model's infinite interconnectedness finally supplies it a mechanism ("we are beings without borders"), and in ch. 8 it becomes a candidate author of Marian visions and UFOs. Chapters: 0, 3, 8. Related: archetypes, personal resonance, Marian visions, UFO phenomenon.
- **Completeness of theories** — Bohm's argument (Causality and Chance in Modern Physics) that no theory can be complete because nature may be infinite; all theories are approximations, "finite maps" of infinite, indivisible territory. Chapters: 2. Related: infinite causality, superstring theory, information-organizing categories.
- **Conceptual pigeonholes** — Bohm's warning image: the categories we use to parse the universe are of our own making and do not exist "out there," for "out there" is only the indivisible totality; Zen likewise identifies confusing reality with the pigeonholes of language as "the basic ignorance." Chapters: 9. Related: fragmentation, undivided wholeness.
- **Consciousness as a more subtle form of matter** — Bohm's proposal that the basis of the mind-matter relationship lies in the implicate order; consciousness is present in various degrees of enfoldment in all matter — something "mindlike" exists already in the electron, and even a rock is in some way alive. Chapters: 2. Related: active meaning, Brahman, implicate order, quantum potential.
- **Convulsionaires** — The Jansenist worshipers at Abbé Paris's tomb at Saint-Médard who fell into contagious trancelike convulsions during which they displayed invulnerability to torture, clairvoyance, eyes-bandaged reading, levitation, and fire immunity; Talbot reads their feats as collective unconscious psychokinesis. Chapters: 5. Related: fire immunity / human salamanders, Jansenism, psychokinesis.
- **Cosmic motion picture projector (reprogramming the)** — Talbot's image for Grof's proposal that certain states of consciousness can intervene directly in the implicate order — modifying phenomena by influencing their generative matrix, bypassing even rules like inertia — explaining what psychokinesis alone cannot. Chapters: 5. Related: implicate order, laws of physics as habits, psychokinesis.
- **Creative prayer** — The Sufi practice of using visualization to alter and reshape the fabric of one's destiny, following from their conclusion that imagination is a faculty of perception. Chapters: 8. Related: mental imagery / visualization, himma, alam almithal.
- **Crystallizing possibilities** — Swann's phrase for the composition of the future; the kahunas likewise saw the future as fluid but "crystallizing," with great world events and major personal events crystallized furthest in advance (Cordero's image: a hurricane gathering momentum). Chapters: 7. Related: parallel universes / hololeaps, precognition, aumakua.
- **Degrees of order / hidden (enfolded) order** — Bohm's realization that there are different degrees of order, perhaps endless hierarchies; things that appear disordered may have order of an "indefinitely high degree," manifest (explicit) or hidden (implicit), as with the ink drop dispersed in glycerine. Chapters: 2. Related: implicate order, chaos, enfoldment and unfoldment.
- **Dematerialization** — The vanishing of matter — most bizarrely, parts of the medium Indridason's body fading out of existence while scientists watched — further evidence, for Talbot, of the body's holographic, immaterial nature. Chapters: 5. Related: materialization, bilocation.
- **Dermatoglyphics / palmistry / ulnar loop** — The study of hand patterns (and the 4,500-year-old Indian hand-reading science): an ulnar-loop fingerprint associates with Alzheimer's, and ten common genetic disabilities including Down's syndrome associate with hand patterns — read by Talbot as more evidence the body is holographic. Chapters: 4. Related: whole in every part, reflexology / iridology, acupuncture microsystems.
- **Dissipative structures** — Prigogine's name for spontaneously appearing ordered systems (some mixed chemicals develop into more ordered arrangements); he and others suggest they are evidence of the implicate aspects of reality becoming explicate. Chapters: 9. Related: implicate order, enfoldment and unfoldment.
- **Double-blind experiment** — A study in which one group receives real treatment and another a fake, and neither researchers nor subjects know who is in which group, so the real treatment's effects can be assessed accurately. Chapters: 4. Related: placebo effect.
- **Dream wisdom** — Ullman's observation that even patients mean and manipulative while awake have dreams that invariably depict their failings honestly, prodding them toward self-awareness; he suggests dreams may be a "natural transformation of the implicate into the explicate," striving to preserve species-connectedness. Chapters: 3. Related: ESP / ESP dreams, implicate order, forgotten language.
- **Dreamtime** — The transcendent reality the Australian aborigines regard as the true source of the mind, visited by shamans in trance and almost identical to Western afterlife descriptions; they also say the entire world was once dreamtime — a far more plastic reality — and will return to it. Chapters: 8, 9. Related: holographic plasticity, shamanizing of modern humanity, survival in infinity.
- **Eidetic / photographic memory** — The ability to scan a scene briefly then "project" a vivid mental image of it (Elizabeth's projected page of Faust); explained by Pollen and Tractenberg as access to unusually large regions of one's memory holograms. Chapters: 1. Related: associative memory, localized vs. distributed memory.
- **Ejection of psychophysical structures** — Dubrov and Pushkin's proposed proof of brain holograms: an instance of the brain projecting an image outside itself; Talbot counts skin images, heart imprints, and nail-like stigmata protuberances as examples. Chapters: 4. Related: stigmata, neural holograms.
- **Energy field as blueprint** — The idea that the physical body is one more level of density in the field, a hologram coalesced out of the aura's interference patterns; since illness can appear in the field weeks or months before the body, the field may be more primary — "the body's own version of an implicate order." Chapters: 6. Related: subtle bodies (etheric body), template body, ratchet effect, aura / human energy field.
- **Enfoldment and unfoldment** — The countless exchanges between the implicate and explicate orders: an electron is an ensemble enfolded throughout space, its apparent motion or detection a continuous series of unfoldments and enfoldments (like the ink drop in glycerine); the constant exchange also explains how quanta shapeshift between particle kinds. Chapters: 2. Related: implicate order, explicate order, holomovement.
- **Engram** — The hypothesized specific memory trace stored at a specific location in the brain cells, backed by Penfield's stimulation experiments; Lashley's excision work and Pribram's clinical evidence undermined it. Chapters: 1. Related: localized vs. distributed memory.
- **EPR paradox** — Einstein, Podolsky, and Rosen's 1935 argument that Bohr's view forces twin particles into instantaneous (faster-than-light) communication, which relativity forbids, so Bohr had to be wrong; Bohr's counter was that twin particles are an indivisible system. Bell made it testable, and Aspect resolved it in favor of nonlocal connection. Chapters: 2. Related: nonlocality, polarization, quantum potential.
- **ESP / ESP dreams** — Extrasensory perception, listed in the Introduction among elusive phenomena the model suddenly made sense of; the Maimonides dream experiments (volunteers dreaming the imagery of paintings concentrated on in another room) are presented as some of the best empirical evidence that in dreams we communicate in ways not presently explainable. Chapters: 0, 3. Related: telepathy, precognition, dream wisdom.
- **Etheric mucus** — Brennan's term for the sticky gray residue that drugs such as alcohol, marijuana, and cocaine create in the aura, dulling its healthy colors. Chapters: 6. Related: aura / human energy field.
- **Evolutionary thrust toward higher consciousness** — Ring's answer to why NDEs are increasing: an evolutionary movement affecting all humanity, possibly also expressed in Marian visions and UFO encounters. Chapters: 9. Related: shamanizing of modern humanity, near-death experience, Marian visions, UFO phenomenon.
- **Explicate order** — Our own level of existence, the unfolded and perceptible order — the hologram projected from the film of the implicate; in ch. 9 matched against each tradition's visible world (the nonvoid, maya). Chapters: 2, 3, 9. Related: implicate order, enfoldment and unfoldment, maya, void and nonvoid.
- **Eyeless sight** — The reported ability to see with areas of the body other than the eyes (armpits, nose, earlobe, fingertips); Talbot takes it as evidence the physical body's sensory limits are themselves a holographic construct of belief. Chapters: 8. Related: 360-degree vision, out-of-body experience.
- **Fairy-faith** — Evans-Wentz's collected testimony of fairies and spectral scenes at Celtic ruins, which he concluded were afterimages of past events — "Nature herself has a memory"; the Manx elder's remark that education ended fairy-seeing shows belief gates the ability. Chapters: 7. Related: nature's memory, retrocognition.
- **Feature detectors** — Highly specialized visual-cortex cells (from 1960s research) each geared to fire for a specific pattern (horizontal lines, vertical lines); the DeValoises showed the cells respond instead to Fourier translations of patterns. Chapters: 1. Related: Fourier transforms, frequency analyzer.
- **Fire immunity / human salamanders** — Immunity to fire displayed by convulsionaires (Marie Sonnet, Gabrielle Moler — named for the mythological lizard believed to live in fire), the Camisards, kahunas, Pentecostals, and fire-walkers; Talbot's proof case that the laws of physics are suspendable habits. Chapters: 5. Related: convulsionaires, laws of physics as habits, psychokinesis.
- **Forgotten language** — Fromm's name for the symbolic, metaphoric language of the unconscious found in dreams, myths, fairy tales, and religious visions — and, per Dryer, in some holographic movies in the aura. Chapters: 6. Related: movies in the aura, dream wisdom.
- **Fourier transforms** — Equations (from calculus invented by Fourier) that convert any pattern into a language of simple waves and back; Gabor built holography on them, and the DeValoises' experiments convinced Pribram the brain uses the same mathematics to convert sensory images into wave forms. Chapters: 1. Related: frequency domain, interference pattern / wave forms, holography.
- **Fragmentation** — Our almost universal tendency to break the world into parts and ignore dynamic interconnectedness, which Bohm believes underlies problems in science, life, and society and may lead to our extinction; multiple personality disorder is presented as its ultimate psychological example. Chapters: 2, 3. Related: undivided wholeness, multiple personality disorder, relatively independent subtotalities, conceptual pigeonholes.
- **Frequency analyzer** — A system that decomposes input into its frequency components; Pribram came to view the senses this way — the ear (Helmholtz), smell (osmic frequencies), skin and taste (Bekesy). Chapters: 1. Related: Fourier transforms, osmic frequencies, frequency domain.
- **Frequency domain (vs. image-object domain)** — Pribram's speculative notion that what is "out there" is really a domain of wave-form frequencies — "a vast, resonating symphony of wave forms" — transformed into the familiar world (the "image-object" domain) only after entering the senses. It develops across the book: in ch. 2 it becomes the ocean of waves the brain converts into a china cup; in ch. 6 mystics and aura-seers glimpse it; in chs. 8–9 the OB/ND realm and the deeper implicate are described as increasingly frequency-like. Chapters: 1, 2, 6, 8, 9. Related: implicate order, Fourier transforms, world-out-there, aura / human energy field.
- **Future-life progression** — Wambach's hypnotic technique of progressing subjects to future lives and specified future time periods, completed and published by Snow as Mass Dreams of the Future. Chapters: 7. Related: mass dreams of the future, past-life regression, parallel universes / hololeaps.
- **Gremlin effect** — The tendency of carefully tested equipment to inexplicably malfunction at absurdly inopportune moments, reported by pilots and military operators; Jahn and Dunne suggest it may be unconscious psychokinesis. Chapters: 5. Related: Pauli effect, psychokinesis.
- **Hadarat** — The Sufi series of subtler planes of being; the plane directly adjacent to ours is a template reality where thought forms into idea-images. Chapters: 7. Related: alam almithal, higher planes, himma.
- **Higher planes** — The still subtler, more formless levels beyond even the afterlife realm — Swedenborg's higher heaven appearing only as "a streaming of light," and Whitton's subject's report that "each one is lighter and brighter than the one before"; evidence the afterlife realm is only a stepping stone. Chapters: 9. Related: hadarat, superhologram, implicate order.
- **Higher sense perception (HSP)** — Karagulla's term for the faculty of seeing the human energy field, which she found secretly widespread among physicians. Chapters: 6. Related: aura / human energy field, internal vision / X-ray vision.
- **Himma (heart chakra)** — For the Sufis, the agent responsible for forming idea-images; controlling it is the prerequisite for controlling one's destiny. Chapters: 7. Related: chakras, alam almithal, creative prayer.
- **Hologram** — A three-dimensional picture made with the aid of a laser: the laser is split into two beams, one bounced off an object, and the interference pattern where the beams recollide is recorded on film; re-illuminating the film reconstructs the 3-D image. The book's founding metaphor, introduced via the Princess Leia projection in Star Wars. Chapters: 0, 1. Related: holography, interference pattern, whole in every part, virtual image vs. real image.
- **Holodeck** — Tiller's metaphor (from Star Trek: The Next Generation) for the universe as an environment created by the integration of all living things: "we've created the laws that govern it... we're also creating the physics as we go along." Chapters: 5. Related: reality-fields, participatory reality, laws of physics as habits.
- **Holographic body assessment ("body reading")** — Jue's name (reframing Pecci's "body reading" in holographic-model terms) for psychometrizing a person's body by laying on hands to read the emotional scripts, core issues, and relationship patterns held in its energy patterns. Chapters: 6. Related: psychometry, aura / human energy field.
- **Holographic brain model (holographic model of mind/body)** — Pribram's thesis that the brain physically operates on holographic principles — encoding information as interfering wave forms, storing memory and vision distributed throughout the whole — explaining memory capacity, recall, recognition, skill transfer, phantom limbs, and the projected "world-out-there." Ch. 4 extends it to the mind/body: since real and imagined experience reduce to the same wave-form language, images and beliefs produce dramatic, specific physiological change. Chapters: 1, 4. Related: holographic model, neural holograms, localized vs. distributed memory, frequency domain.
- **Holographic model (paradigm / analogy / metaphor / theory)** — The book's central idea: the universe is a kind of giant hologram, and the world and everything in it are ghostly images — projections from a level of reality beyond space and time. Naming caveat: because the idea is still a mosaic of viewpoints not yet integrated into a unified whole, researchers call it a paradigm, analogy, metaphor, model, or theory; Talbot uses all these labels interchangeably "for the sake of diversity" and expressly does not claim strict model/theory status. Built from two independently reached halves — Pribram's holographic brain and Bohm's holographic universe — and extended through the book to psychology, healing, miracles, perception, time, and space. Chapters: 0, 1, 2 (applied throughout 3–9). Related: holographic brain model, implicate order, hologram, superhologram.
- **Holographic plasticity / plasticity of reality** — The tendency of OB and ND forms, landscapes, and even the mode of entry to the afterlife to be molded by the experiencer's thoughts, expectations, and cultural symbols; ch. 9 extends it to the aboriginal prophecy that reality itself may become malleable again as we learn to manipulate the hologram. Chapters: 8, 9. Related: thought-forms, second body, dreamtime, reality-fields.
- **Holographic record of the past** — The persistence of the past in the implicate order — Bohm's "cosmic storehouse" — accessible to retrocognition because consciousness also has its source in the implicate, making mind and the record of the past "already neighbors." Chapters: 7. Related: retrocognition, nature's memory, multiple-image hologram, nonlocality.
- **Holography** — The technique of making holograms, conceived mathematically by Gabor in 1947 (to improve the electron microscope) and made practical only after the invention of the laser. Chapters: 1. Related: hologram, laser / coherent light, Fourier transforms.
- **Holomovement** — Bohm's preferred term for the universe, since "hologram" suggests a static image and does not convey the dynamic, ever-active enfoldings and unfoldings that create our universe moment by moment; Fa-Tsang made the same caveat about his mirror model. Chapters: 2, 9. Related: implicate order, enfoldment and unfoldment, undivided wholeness.
- **Holophonic sound** — Zuccarelli's recording technique creating holograms made of sound instead of light, based on the fact that human ears emit sound (the audio equivalent of the reference laser); it produces uncannily three-dimensional sound that survives through one headphone side. Chapters: 9. Related: holography, frequency analyzer.
- **Holotropic states / holotropic therapy** — Grof's nonordinary state in which one can access the holographic labyrinth connecting all aspects of existence, and the Grofs' nondrug technique for inducing it using only rapid controlled breathing, evocative music, and body work — producing experiences as spectacular as the LSD subjects'. Chapters: 3. Related: transpersonal experiences, altered / nonordinary states of consciousness.
- **Hypnosis** — The tool by which unconscious beliefs are reached "like a surgeon altering an internal organ": deeply hypnotized persons can control allergies, blood flow, heart rate, pain, and even a genetic disease (Brocq's); ch. 5 adds that waking consciousness itself may be a kind of hypnosis, since hypnosis shows no unusual EEG pattern. Chapters: 4. Related: belief strata, Brocq's disease, mutual hypnosis, placebo effect.
- **Imaginal realm** — Corbin's coinage for a world created by imagination yet ontologically no less real than physical reality — created by the imagination of many people but possessing its own corporeality, forests, mountains, and cities. Chapters: 8. Related: omnijective universe, alam almithal, land of nonwhere.
- **Implicate order** — Bohm's deeper, enfolded level of reality: a vaster, more primary level that gives birth to all objects and appearances of the physical world the way holographic film gives birth to a hologram. Its meaning accretes through the book: ground of consciousness and the collective psyche (ch. 3), source of intention and healing ("every action starts from an intention in the implicate order," ch. 4), the generative matrix miracles reprogram (ch. 5), the body's blueprint field (ch. 6), the atemporal home of past and future (ch. 7), the visitable realm of light (ch. 8), and the ancient void/Brahman rediscovered (ch. 9). Chapters: 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9. Related: explicate order, holomovement, enfoldment and unfoldment, frequency domain, plenum / sea of energy.
- **Indra's network of pearls** — The Avatamsaka Sutra's image of a net of pearls over the palace of the god Indra, "so arranged that if you look at one, you see all the others reflected in it" — each object in the world involves every other object and "is everything else." Chapters: 9. Related: whole in every part, monads, "as above, so below".
- **Inedia** — The supernormal ability to live without food and even water, displayed by Therese Neumann for thirty-five years under medical scrutiny — implying she regularly materialized the water and nutrients keeping her alive. Chapters: 5. Related: materialization, stigmata.
- **Infinite causality** — Bohm's view that an effect can have an infinite number of causes (the complete list of causes of Lincoln's death would include every step of human evolution that produced a hand able to hold a gun); no cause-and-effect relation is ever separate from the universe as a whole. Chapters: 2. Related: completeness of theories, undivided wholeness.
- **Information-organizing categories** — Jahn and Dunne's status for all descriptive concepts (electron, wavelength, consciousness, time): useful organizing tools with no independent existence — all theories, their own included, are only metaphors. Chapters: 5. Related: wave-particle duality of consciousness, completeness of theories.
- **Interconnectedness** — The quantum evidence Bohm was repeatedly struck by and mainstream physics ignored: the EPR twin-particle puzzle, the organism-like behavior of plasmas, the Aharonov-Bohm effect — the thread that led to the quantum potential and the implicate order. Chapters: 2. Related: nonlocality, undivided wholeness, plasma / plasmons.
- **Interface between consciousness and its environment** — Jahn and Dunne's phrase for what we are learning to tinker with; Talbot speculates that as we grow more adept, reality may become malleable again, fulfilling the aboriginal prophecy of a return to the dreamtime. Chapters: 9. Related: participatory reality, psychokinesis, dreamtime.
