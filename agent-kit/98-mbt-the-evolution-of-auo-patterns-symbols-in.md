# Ch 27 — The Evolution of AUO: Patterns, Symbols, Information, and Memory; Motivation and Evolutionary Constraints

## In one paragraph
Campbell continues his origin story of consciousness: AUO (Absolute Unbounded Oneness), having differentiated itself into "reality cells" in earlier chapters, now begins arranging those cells into patterns, patterns of patterns, and groups of patterns of patterns — the beginnings of symbols, information, and memory. To explain what drives this, Campbell first works through biological evolution: survival and propagation are the classic external pressures, but he argues they fail to explain either the earliest cell clumps (which had no enemies) or modern technologically-buffered humans; at both extremes the real driver is an internal pressure of self-improvement. Generalizing, he claims AUO — timeless, immortal, and free of survival/propagation constraints — evolves under exactly one imperative: self-improvement through entropy reduction, i.e., improving the quality of its consciousness. A long "aside" applies ecosystem thinking to humanity: we live inside a consciousness ecosystem (the "mother of all others," of which our physical universe is a small virtual habitat), and our species' critical problem is low quality of consciousness, not lack of intellectual capacity. Campbell also concedes AUO is only *relatively* infinite — actual finiteness would push it, as a self-modifying computational system (digital organism), toward rules, priorities, and perhaps "a big computer" to manage its internal gedanken experiments. The chapter ends by deriving a new criterion for life itself: living systems are those that can intentionally decrease their entropy.

## Core claims
- Campbell claims that AUO, with its "trillion billion gazillion reality cells," begins organizing local deformations (cells) into multi-level patterns — and that the Fundamental Process creates evolutionary pressure that delivers whatever is most profitable to the evolving organism, with "mega-memory on the way." Where many choices can be profitable, the outcome is great diversity.
- Campbell argues AUO's capacity is effectively limitless relative to us: storing every word ever written or spoken in any language in binary reality cells (distorted vs. not distorted, like computer 0s and 1s) would use only an infinitesimal fraction of available cells.
- Campbell warns against conceiving AUO in terms of space and time: applying space/time-constrained words to ideas beyond their reach creates confusion and paradoxes, though we 3D creatures must stretch the tools we have.
- Campbell argues that in biological systems diversification and specialization are driven by survival and propagation, but that these two pressures cannot explain either extreme of biological evolution: the first cell clumps had no enemies and weren't eating each other, and modern humans are largely buffered from survival pressure by technology (his glasses example).
- Campbell claims self-improvement — an *internal* evolutionary pressure created by the Fundamental Process — is the major driver of change both for contemporary humankind and for the original clumps of cells in the primordial sea; survival and propagation are not the only variables in the "profit calculation."
- Campbell asserts we participate in an evolving consciousness ecosystem — "the mother of all others," the largest and most complex ecosystem we can interact with — and that our physical universe is a small virtual habitat within it. The ecological lessons about balance and responsibility apply directly to our relationship with it: "Science is science, the principles are the same."
- Campbell claims natural complex systems must be self-balancing, self-sustaining, and self-correcting or they become self-eliminating; growth toward an out-of-balance condition is eventually eliminated (rebalance, regression, or self-destruction).
- Campbell argues humanity's fundamental problem is not intellectual capacity but *quality* of consciousness: "Quality is the issue, not quantity or capacity." A bigger brain cannot be the solution to the excesses of a bigger brain. Our only remaining natural enemy is ourselves.
- Campbell claims improving the quality of individual and collective consciousness (equivalently: spiritual evolution, entropy reduction of consciousness) is the most critical self-improvement facing humanity in the twenty-first century; the choices of the next half-century will dramatically affect the next half millennium.
- Campbell insists growing up is a personal, not group or political, activity: intelligence and education are attributes of individuals; groups improve only as individuals do; "Only individuals acting as individuals can make a difference."
- Campbell claims evolution is not a rational, cognitive process — winning strategies "just happen and persist," losers fade; no one performs an evaluation — though after sufficient awareness and tool use evolve, evolution can be influenced by individual intent. Humans are now "climbing out of the petri-dish and into the lab," becoming co-designers of their own evolution, following in AUO's footsteps.
- Campbell argues survival and propagation are non-issues to AUO; instead self-improvement — the reduction of system entropy, equivalently the increase of useful energy — provides the evolutionary pressure and profitability criteria guiding AUO's evolvement. Fewer constraints yield more diversity, which is why (he flags for later) the less-constrained NPMRs evolve more varied life-forms than the more constrained PMRs.
- Campbell concedes "infinite" and "unbounded" do not accurately describe AUO: any actual existence has limiting factors, so AUO is finite — but from the tiny perspective of our local reality system (OS) it *appears* infinite and unbounded (no edge effects), and a relatively infinite AUO is sufficient to generate all the reality we can be aware of "and much more."
- Campbell claims AUO's finiteness would lead it, as a self-modifying computational system (digital organism), to set priorities and develop rules optimizing resource utilization — and that maintaining a complex interactive virtual reality would require a good memory "or perhaps a big computer."
- Campbell asserts AUO's motivation flows down to us because we are manifestations of it: AUO develops awareness of its internal states and learns to modify them to decrease overall entropy — "similar to our mission, our reason for being."
- Campbell derives a general criterion for life: living systems are sentient, exhibit consciousness (however dim), have choices guided by intent and profitability, and can purposely decrease or maintain their entropy against the second law of thermodynamics. Non-living things (rivers, rocks, atoms) evolve toward increasing entropy by seeking the lowest energy state — a fact he says is ultimately responsible for the second law and is one of the rules in the space-time rule-set defining PMR physics.
- Campbell closes by warning against anthropomorphizing AUO: attribution flows from parent to child — "try thinking of humans in terms of AUO's attributes," not the reverse.

## Concepts
- **AUO (Absolute Unbounded Oneness)** — Campbell's primordial consciousness, here evolving by organizing its reality cells into multi-level patterns. In this chapter he qualifies the name: AUO must actually be finite (any real existence has limiting factors), but appears infinite and unbounded from our tiny local perspective; a "relatively infinite" AUO suffices to generate all reality we can perceive.
- **Reality cells** — AUO's local deformations (distorted vs. undistorted states), which AUO arranges into patterns, patterns of patterns, and groups of patterns of patterns. Used binarily, like a computer's 0s and 1s, they can store information — the basis of AUO's memory.
- **Fundamental Process (of evolution)** — Campbell's universal two-step evolution engine: first explore all possible states, then continue investing in the profitable/significant states while "letting the losers go." It applies identically to humans, critters, consciousness, ecosystems, technology, governments, and all sufficiently complex interactive systems; it requires no evaluation, management, or intellect ("accountants and lawyers are not required"), and grinds to a halt when no significant potential states remain.
- **Evolutionary pressure** — whatever energizes and guides implementation of the Fundamental Process. External pressures (survival, propagation) come from the outside environment as constraints; internal pressure (self-improvement) comes from the inside environment. Campbell stresses inside environments can be a significant player.
- **Evolutionary profitability** — the criterion by which explored states persist: "Optimal profitability, as it is constrained and prodded by internal and external environments, defines evolutionary success." It is dynamic — investment opportunities come and go as evolution modifies the system it operates on — and is not computed by anyone; winning strategies just happen and persist.
- **Self-improvement** — the internal evolutionary pressure created by the Fundamental Process; for a highly sentient being it becomes "the urge to grow (reduce entropy), the drive to increase profitability, and an inherent dissatisfaction with stagnation and decay." Campbell says it drives both AUO and contemporary humankind (whose external environment is essentially subdued).
- **Entropy / entropy reduction** — Campbell's master metric of consciousness evolution: self-improvement = reduction of system entropy = increase of useful energy = improvement of the quality of consciousness = spiritual growth. All these are treated as equivalent expressions. Growing systems evolve toward minimizing average entropy through more profitable levels of organization.
- **Quality of consciousness** — the maturity/wisdom dimension of consciousness, distinct from capacity: "Quality is the issue, not quantity or capacity." Low quality (ignorance, fear, desire, need, ego) is the consciousness-system analog of cancer — an unbalanced condition leading toward self-destruction. To a low-quality being, "feel-good appears more important than do-good which appears more important than be-good."
- **Spiritual evolution / spiritual growth** — Campbell's equivalent term for improving the quality of one's consciousness (lowering its entropy); he calls it humanity's main goal now that survival is subdued, and a personal (never group or political) activity.
- **Consciousness ecosystem** — the largest, most complex ecosystem we can interact with; "the mother of all others," supporting all other ecosystems. Our physical universe is "a small virtual habitat" within it. Campbell instructs the reader to reread his ecology aside with every observation reinterpreted as describing our interaction with this larger system.
- **Big Picture / little picture** — Campbell's contrast between broad systems-level understanding and self-centered, short-sighted perspective. "Low quality consciousness does not see the Big Picture and makes short-sighted decisions for near term gain"; humanity's 18th–20th-century belief that it stood alone above all else is "little picture self-centered arrogance."
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — our space-time-constrained physical reality; its words and concepts carry "implicit PMR conceptual limitations" that must not trap us into thinking reality is confined to three spatial dimensions. Its physics laws derive from the space-time rule-set.
- **NPMR (Nonphysical Matter Reality)** — the less-constrained reality frames; Campbell notes here (promising more later) that fewer constraints mean more diversity, so NPMRs should evolve a more varied set of life-forms than the more constrained PMRs.
- **OS (Our System)** — our local reality system; the "relatively tiny perspective" from which AUO appears infinite and unbounded with no directly experienced edge effects.
- **Digital organism / self-modifying computational system** — Campbell's characterization of finite AUO: limiting factors would inevitably lead such a system to set priorities and develop rules that optimize resource utilization.
- **The big computer** — introduced here as a possibility: maintaining and profitably organizing all the information required to support a complex interactive virtual reality "would require a good memory — or perhaps a big computer."
- **Gedanken experiments** — AUO's internal thought-experiments-in-progress; keeping track of them all is the potential capacity constraint on a finite consciousness system.
- **Memory / mega-memory** — the capability evolutionary pressure delivers to AUO because pattern-organization makes it profitable; binary reality cells serve as the storage medium.
- **Living vs. non-living systems** — Campbell's newly derived general criterion: living systems are sentient, exhibit consciousness however dim, have choices guided by intent and profitability, and can intentionally decrease or maintain their entropy against the second law of thermodynamics; non-living systems lack (or have too little of) choice, interaction, feedback, and self-modification, and evolve toward minimum energy states / increasing entropy.
- **Second law of thermodynamics** — the "constant tug" toward increasing entropy that living systems resist by choice; Campbell says the tendency of non-living systems to move toward minimum energy states along the path of least resistance is ultimately responsible for its truth, and that this fact is one of the rules in the space-time rule-set.
- **Space-time rule-set** — the rule set that defines the laws of physics in PMR (flagged here, developed later).
- **Self-balancing systems** — Mother Nature "always produces self-balancing systems": maximizing overall system profitability must be self-optimizing and self-balancing to achieve stability; "the whole must be self-balancing, self-sustaining, and self-correcting, or it becomes self-eliminating."
- **Intent** — what guides the choices of living systems; also the lever by which, once sufficient awareness and tool use evolve, evolution itself can be influenced by individuals.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **Shakespeare and the twenty-six letters** — Campbell's opening analogy for pattern power: what Shakespeare accomplished "by arranging and rearranging only twenty-six letters of the English alphabet," plus the Library of Congress and this very book, shows the tremendous output possible from grouping patterns of patterns — a glimmer of what AUO can do with its cells.
- **Binary reality cells as computer memory** — thought experiment: if every word ever written or spoken in any language were stored using distorted/not-distorted reality cells (like a computer's 0s and 1s), AUO would use "only an infinitesimal percentage" of its available cells — showing its capacity is, relative to ours, apparently infinite.
- **The John Donne misquote** — "No man or bacterium is an island": every individual is an autonomous entity *and* an integral part of a larger system; nothing stands alone.
- **The industrialization / "mugging Mother Nature" aside** — an extended ecological case study: 18th–20th-century Western humans as "little picture" arrogant; "the early looter gets the best TV" (and is least likely to be caught); mugging Mother Nature in her park as the easy route to quick cash; trading "an endless supply of golden eggs for a single goose dinner" as a stupid trade even if you're hungry. Campbell's point: ability to exploit must be balanced by responsibility to conserve — "force with caring, and fear with love" — and the whole aside is meant to be reread as a description of our relationship with the *consciousness* ecosystem.
- **Layered global ecosystems** — beyond nature, Campbell predicts we will discover global technological, political, social, and economic ecosystems, in which every player has a bit part yet absolutely affects the whole; larger impact carries commensurately larger responsibility.
- **Cancer vs. ego** — paired examples of unbalanced systems: in biological systems, a quickly growing cancer is the most obvious self-destructive imbalance; in consciousness systems, it is "ignorance, fear, desire, need, and ego — a lack of quality."
- **The first cell clumps in the primordial soup** — counter-example to survival/propagation as universal motivators: the first small groups of biological cells "had no enemies... and they weren't interested in eating each other — not yet. Life was simple and good." They were doing simple self-improvement — exploring possibilities for a more profitable existence — the same process, Campbell says, as AUO's, with different energy form and constraints.
- **Eyeglasses and the fifth of Westerners** — counter-example at the modern end: almost a fifth of Western people have a technology-correctable genetic vision impairment that would have been seriously anti-survival ten thousand years ago; evolution is not weeding them out, showing technology has intervened in survival pressure.
- **The big-headed futurist humans** — futurists picture us evolving big brainy heads with spindly limbs; Campbell argues this is illogical: a bigger brain as the solution to the excesses of a bigger brain doesn't compute, we already dominate all competition, and (he notes he has "read somewhere") we use only a fraction of existing capacity. The real deficit is quality, not capacity.
- **Accountants and lawyers joke** — evolution needs no evaluators: "accountants and lawyers are not required to implement the Fundamental Process — it just happens"; he jokes some lobbying organization is trying to legislate mandatory legal/accounting services into evolution and charge high fees.
- **Mother Nature personified** — robust with poisonous snakes, killer bees, vampire bats, volcanoes, tornados, and earthquakes, and abhorring bureaucracy ("even if she gets raped, she will not call the authorities"), but terrified of shortsighted lobbyists and self-serving politicians, whose combined destructive potential is "like a malignant tumor." If abuse goes too far she gets even, punishing all within reach regardless of culpability. "Greed kills!"
- **Locomotion and survival strategies** — example of constraints vs. diversity: because few constraints were placed on offensive/defensive survival strategies or modes of locomotion, Earth shows enormous variety of both; by extension, AUO's near-total lack of constraints grants it "great freedom of form and function in solving evolution's profitability equation."
- **The carbon atom in your brain** — perspective analogy: imagine how unbounded and infinite your brain would appear to a carbon atom stuck in the middle of it — that is how the actually-finite AUO appears to us.
- **Intestinal bacteria and the food cycle** — analogy for AUO's external environment being irrelevant to us: gut bacteria are so immersed in their limited reality they cannot appreciate jobs, money, farmers, sunshine, rain, transportation, or economic conditions in the food cycle — "They think food is manna from heaven. Bullpucky!" Likewise we cannot experience or understand AUO's outside environment, so Campbell ignores it.
- **Climbing out of the petri dish** — model of humanity's evolutionary present: genetic engineering and psychotropic drugs growing quickly, tough guys with big muscles having less real power than "brainy bespectacled nerds," and humans "climbing out of the petri-dish and into the lab" to become co-designers — partners in the evolutionary process, following in AUO's footsteps.
- **The living-systems criterion (model)** — the chapter's constructed model: a general test differentiating living from non-living systems by the ability to intentionally decrease entropy (moving parts: sentience, choice, intent, profitability, feedback, self-modification, the second law as opposing force). Campbell teases its application: "Does that make your government, the earth's ecosystem, a national or global economy, digital computers, and the Internet potential life-forms? Stay tuned." He notes the attributes of a consciousness system are given more precisely at the beginning of Chapter 7, Book 2.
- **Closing anthropomorphism warning** — the reader's expected questions (Does AUO like art, science, love, fun, investment strategy?) are dismissed as "hopelessly anthropomorphic"; attribution flows from parent to child — think of humans in terms of AUO's attributes, not vice versa. Questions pop up "like toadstools after a summer rain."

## Thread
This chapter is a step in Section 2's staged evolution of AUO: earlier chapters gave AUO its first dim awareness and reality cells (via the Fundamental Process and disturbances/distortions); here those cells become patterns, symbols, information, and memory, and — crucially — Campbell identifies *what* drives AUO's evolution once survival and propagation are ruled out: self-improvement as entropy reduction. He uses biological evolution as the familiar on-ramp, then generalizes ("the energy form and constraints are different, but the evolutionary process is exactly the same"). The chapter plants several seeds explicitly flagged for later: the big computer, why NPMRs host more varied life-forms than PMRs, the space-time rule-set as the source of PMR physics (including the second law), additional profitability terms to come in Sections 3–5, the precise attributes of a consciousness system at the start of Chapter 7 of Book 2, and the promise that "we are evolving consciousness" — an integral part of AUO's evolutionary process. The moral through-line (quality of consciousness as humanity's critical evolutionary task, growth as a strictly individual activity) previews the trilogy's ethical core, and the closing warning against anthropomorphizing AUO sets the direction of attribution — from AUO down to us — for everything that follows.
