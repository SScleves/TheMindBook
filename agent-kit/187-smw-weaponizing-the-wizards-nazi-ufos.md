# Weaponizing the Wizards: Nazi UFOs

## In one paragraph
In this chapter Tom DeLonge & Peter Levenda take the persistent rumor of "Nazi UFOs" seriously enough to test it against the documentary record — not as a general solution to the Phenomenon (which they say it cannot be, given millennia of sightings), but as a live possibility for some of the 1947-era sightings and as a window into how governments manage the Phenomenon. They reconstruct the postwar Nazi diaspora: Operation Paperclip and its Soviet twin Osoaviakhim, the Maison Rouge asset-expatriation plan, the ratlines (ODESSA, Die Spinne, Kameradenwerke, the monastery/Spanish/Nordic routes), and the Argentine institutions (CAPRI, Tucumán, the Huemul Project) that gave Nazi scientists sinecures worldwide. They argue this global corporate-scientific infrastructure made a secret postwar Nazi "flying saucer" project far less implausible than critics claim. The chapter then pivots from nuts-and-bolts to the consciousness side: the SS's occult culture (the Ahnenerbe, Wewelsburg, neo-paganism), Barney Hill's and Whitley Strieber's Nazi-tinged abduction memories, Jung's mandala reading of the saucer, and a dark thought experiment — that any Alien interest in humanity, then as now, would be predicated on technology and self-interest, not morality, so the Alien would have watched Peenemünde and Auschwitz with equal indifference.

## Core claims
- The authors argue that a Nazi/human origin for UFOs cannot be a general solution to the Phenomenon, because sightings have been recorded worldwide for millennia; the Nazi-saucer proposition reads as "a transparent attempt to dismiss all UFO sightings" as engineering plus fairy tales.
- Nonetheless, they claim US military documents of the immediate postwar years show the generals took Nazi "flying saucer" technology quite seriously — implying the Army accepted there was something to the phenomenon but "hoped it was a Nazi superweapon," because the alien alternative was unacceptable.
- The authors argue the standard objection — that a defeated Reich could not have financed secret postwar saucer development — is debatable: an entire financial and technological infrastructure was established abroad by German firms in the last months of the war ("a continuation of war by other means").
- They claim the Antarctica/Hitler-survival saucer mythology was largely a fictional device begun in the 1970s by Holocaust denier Ernst Zündel, boosted by a misleading Spanish-to-English translation of an Admiral Byrd interview in El Mercurio.
- They argue that focusing only on Operation Paperclip misses the bigger picture: thousands more German scientists worked for the Americans inside occupied Germany, thousands went to the Soviet Union under Operation Osoaviakhim, and in aggregate more than ten thousand Nazi scientists, engineers, and specialized workers were reemployed worldwide — "there was a postwar Nazi space program. Just not in the way most of us think about it."
- They claim the SS controlled both the secret-weapons research (Peenemünde, Nordhausen, under Hans Kammler) and the expatriation of Reich assets, so ODESSA-type networks moved not just war criminals but technical and scientific teams and resources around the world.
- They argue that in 1947 the prevailing US military view was that "flying saucers" were a national security problem — foreign (possibly Russian-built, Nazi-designed) weapons platforms, e.g., Horten flying wings — not extraterrestrial craft; "That would start to change."
- They claim the Cold War killed accountability: by 1946–47 SS officers were being recruited rather than hunted, Operation Safehaven collapsed against Spanish/Swiss/Portuguese non-cooperation, and Nazi assets were allowed to seed a global "exo-state."
- They argue the Phenomenon divides (somewhat arbitrarily) into a nuts-and-bolts aeronautical element and a psychological/consciousness element, and that in the Reich — especially the SS — science and occult ideology were inextricably cross-fertilized.
- On the "Alien/Nazi alliance" theory, the authors argue it is ridiculous on its face but diagnostic: any Alien interest in humans would be predicated on the Alien's self-interest and on machines/technology, not ideology or ethics — "The Nazis had the monopoly on Earth technology at the time," the US from 1945 to 1955, and "now, it's anyone's game."
- They claim the Alien's likeliest counter to human technological advance would not be destroying factories or scientists but misdirection — inserting seductive technological red herrings — or, in the brutal case, gradual surreptitious eradication exploiting human mutual hatred.
- They argue the UFO is ultimately a critique of sovereignty and a threat aimed at our bodies and identities: just as Nazis did not see themselves in Jews or Slavs, the "aliens" presumably do not see themselves in us, making human appeals even more futile than those of camp prisoners.
- They note the Barney Hill "Nazi" perception and the "Nordic" alien type as the rare points where experiencer reports touch Nazi imagery, while insisting experiencer/contactee reports otherwise "have nothing to do with Nazis."

## Concepts
- **nazi-ufo-hypothesis** — The rumor that the Nazis developed a flying saucer as a WWII superweapon, or manufactured saucers postwar at a secret base (e.g., Antarctica), explaining foo fighters, the Arnold sightings, and even Roswell. The authors treat it as no general solution but as partially plausible for specific 1947 sightings, given the expatriated Nazi infrastructure.
- **the-phenomenon** — The authors' umbrella term for the UFO/UAP mystery, which they divide "somewhat arbitrarily" into a nuts-and-bolts aeronautical element and a psychological/consciousness element that cannot be fully disentangled.
- **foo-fighters** — The WWII-era UFOs, described as translucent spheres or globes (not flying wings); the authors compare them to the modern Tic Tac phenomenon.
- **extraterrestrial-hypothesis (ETH)** — The view that UFOs are alien craft; defended by Hermann Oberth, who divided modern human antigravity R&D from the historical UFO record, for which he said only the ETH makes sense.
- **operation-highjump** — The expensive, oddly timed 1946–47 US Navy Antarctica expedition organized by Admiral Byrd, never satisfactorily completed; combined with the Nazi 1938–39 New Swabia Expedition it fed Zündel's fictional Antarctic-base plotline.
- **new-swabia-expedition** — Nazi Germany's 1938–39 expedition to Antarctica, the seed of the secret-South-Pole-base myth.
- **operation-paperclip** — The program that brought not hundreds but thousands of Nazi scientists, engineers, and medical professionals to the US to work for the Army, Air Force, and NACA/NASA; the authors stress that counting only Paperclip personnel "is to miss the bigger picture."
- **operation-osoaviakhim** — "The Russian version of Paperclip": more than two thousand Nazi scientists plus families (over six thousand persons total) taken to Soviet territory for rocket and weapons work.
- **operation-safehaven** — The official US investigation of Nazi asset expatriation abroad; it "came to an ignominious end" as Spain, Switzerland, Portugal, and South American states refused cooperation and the Cold War redirected priorities.
- **wonder-weapons** — The Reich's terror weapons (V1, V2, feared atomic bomb); postwar fear of them became confused with "flying saucer" sightings that some in intelligence read as new man-made weapons.
- **v1 / v2** — The "flying bomb" and the rocket; British scientific intelligence (per R. V. Jones's Most Secret War) initially considered German missiles a technical impossibility — a mindset the authors compare to today's establishment reaction to UAP reports.
- **v3-horten-ho-229** — The Horten brothers' flying-wing design, developed at Gothaer Waggonfabrik in Gotha, Thuringia (Soviet zone postwar); its appearance was "virtually identical" to what Kenneth Arnold drew, prompting 1947 conjecture that the saucers were Russian-made craft of Nazi invention.
- **v7-craft** — Per Oberth, a German helicopter-like craft with rotating blades containing ramjet engines that could hover and be mistaken for a spinning disc; per a 1952 France-Soir article, an actual circular disk built by Dr. Richard Miethe in 1944, linked to Giuseppe Belluzzo, Breslau, and Prague (a presumed Nazi Bell site).
- **mir-v7** — The Soviet craft of about 1955, "obviously based on the German design" of the V7.
- **levetzow-gravity-shielding-theory** — German scientist Walter Levetzow's early-twentieth-century theory of gravity and "gravity shielding" — antigravity propulsion — said to have been eclipsed by Einstein's relativity; publicized in the Frankfurter Illustrierte (Nov 14 and 21, 1953) and fleshed out in Oberth's notes.
- **tachyon** — Particles believed able to travel faster than light, essential to the Levetzow antigravity concept; mentioned in Nick Cook's The Hunt for Zero Point and an interest of Hal Puthoff (currently of To The Stars).
- **antigravity-propulsion** — The goal of the Levetzow-Pinkell line of research; per the rumor Oberth partially credits, tested at a secret Urals facility around 1938, with the Russians allegedly flying a saucer-shaped antigravity vehicle by 1947.
- **hollow-moon-theory** — The theory (from Vasin and Shcherbakov's 1970 Sputnik article "Is the Moon the Creation of Alien Intelligence?", citing Stanyukovich) that the Moon is a hollow, artificially created satellite, explaining its one-side-facing orbit.
- **polycultural-institution-of-america** — Schneiderov's very exclusive private school founded 1948 in Washington, D.C. (classes of at most five or six students), where he taught a class in "flying saucers" run like a congressional hearing.
- **maison-rouge-meeting** — The secret meeting at the Maison Rouge Hotel, Strasbourg, August 10, 1944, chaired by SS officers, that "set the postwar program for the Reich": German industrial leaders were told to expatriate wealth and know-how immediately through front companies and subsidiaries.
- **ratline** — Escape-and-transfer networks for Nazis and assets; the authors' chapter section "A Ratline for Technology" extends the concept from war criminals to technical, scientific, and engineering teams and resources.
- **monastery-route** — The infamous ratline out of Austria started by Croatian Monsignor Krunoslav Draganović, using Roman Catholic monasteries and safe houses across northern Italy and false papers, blessed by Vatican Nazi sympathizers like Bishop Alois Hudal.
- **spanish-route** — Carlos Fuldner's parallel rescue system running through Franco's Nazi-friendly Spain to Argentina.
- **nordic-route** — Carlos Schulz's network running fugitives through safe houses in Norway and Sweden, fronted by the "Evangelical Escape Organization."
- **odessa** — Umbrella term (popularized by Forsyth's novel; German acronym for Organization of Former SS Officers) for the underground SS networks; the authors call it convenient but misleading — many parallel groups existed and, they argue, the ODESSA focus obscures the larger corporate-scientific web.
- **die-spinne / kameradenwerke** — Two of the other underground networks: Skorzeny's "Spider" and Hans-Ulrich Rudel's Kameradenwerke; originally autonomous but with much crossover and shared facilities.
- **capri** — Compañia Argentina para Proyectos y Realizaciones Industriales, Fuldner's Argentine operation funded by Ludwig Freude, created solely to provide sinecures ("boondoggles") for Nazi scientists, engineers, and the odd war criminal on the run.
- **huemul-project** — Ronald Richter's attempt on remote Huemul Island (San Carlos de Bariloche) to build a nuclear fusion device called the Thermotron, aimed at nuclear-powered aircraft; cancelled when Perón fell in 1955, with Richter jailed for fraud.
- **thermotron** — The name of Richter's fusion device.
- **colonia-dignidad** — Chilean colony that sheltered Nazis on the run (e.g., Mengele), trained Chilean secret-police interrogation specialists, and was a node in Operation Condor; supported by Mertins's fundraising Circle Colonia Dignidad.
- **operation-condor** — A multinational anti-Communist terrorist and assassination program run by Southern Cone dictatorships with the assistance of Nazi intelligence specialists such as Barbie; performed assassinations and sabotage in the US, Latin America, and Europe.
- **merex-ag** — The arms-trading firm formed by Skorzeny and Gerhard Mertins, specializing in German-made weapons exports — in at least one case an F-86 Sabre jet sale to Pakistan.
- **cedade** — A Spanish-based neo-Nazi publishing and propaganda operation Skorzeny helped form.
- **gehlen-organization** — Former general Reinhard Gehlen's anti-Soviet spy network staffed with his old friends, for a time part of the CIA's own regional operation; the authors note debate over whether it was "simply a boondoggle" of pretend espionage — which is their point: "The postwar Nazi apparatus worked for itself."
- **fourth-reich** — What the authors say the underground Nazi intelligentsia of weapons scientists actually served while "pretending to work for a more innocent 'anti-Communist' league."
- **ss-ahnenerbe** — Himmler's "ancestral heritage research" division: professors and intellectuals unemployable in reputable academia pursuing ancient civilizations, paganism, occultism, astrology, runes, the Grail, the Spear of Destiny, reincarnation — the authors' prime exhibit for science/occult cross-fertilization in the Reich.
- **wewelsburg** — The SS castle indicating Himmler's serious preoccupation with occultism, Grail legends, and ritual magic.
- **totenkopf / allgemeine-ss** — The death's head insignia ubiquitous on SS caps (doubled on camp officers' collar patches) and the black-uniformed early SS branch; the authors call the skull-and-crossbones "a kind of psychological warfare technique" and use it to decode Barney Hill's "Nazi" perception.
- **race-science** — The Nazi pseudo-scientific racial doctrine; the authors stress it had a theological core (e.g., an SS man "polluted" by intercourse with a Jewish woman) with no pretense of scientific basis.
- **special-access-program** — A project embedded beyond bureaucratic and journalistic scrutiny; the authors suggest a Nazi saucer project could have masqueraded inside an American, Soviet, Australian, or Argentine platform this way, noting the CIA admitted some Area 51 spy aircraft were allowed to be identified as UFOs.
- **nuts-and-bolts-vs-consciousness-split** — The authors' two-element division of the Phenomenon: the aeronautical machine and the psychological/consciousness component of close encounters, which they say stubbornly cannot be disentangled.
- **ufo-as-mandala / individuation** — Jung's opinion that the "flying saucer" was a mandala, an externalization of humanity's yearning for psychic integration (individuation) — psychically real without denying possible physical reality.
- **alien-self-interest** — The authors' thesis that any Alien-human relationship is predicated on the Alien's self-interest and on technology (machines, energy, performance, threat), not morality: it would have collaborated with the Nazis, and would collaborate with any nation, if its interests warranted.
- **alien-nazi-alliance** — The theory the authors probe as a mirror: the Nazi as villain-with-acumen seems a "natural ally" of the Alien; the authors note the Nazis would share power only with a perceived racial equal or superior, and that any contact evidently "didn't amount to much" technologically — inspiration, at most.
- **misdirection-hypothesis** — The authors' speculation that the Alien could retard human technology by inserting seductive technological red herrings so scientists abandon more fruitful research for a chimera — "Possible, certainly. Even likely."
- **convergence** — The point (introduced in Sekret Machines: Man) where human science and technology become indistinguishable from the Phenomenon's; here illustrated by stealth triangles resembling triangle UFOs, with propulsion the only critical difference.
- **tic-tac** — The modern Navy-encountered object the authors use as the contemporary analog of the foo fighter and as the hinge of the chapter's closing meditation.
- **nazi-bell** — Mentioned in passing: Prague as "a presumed site of the infamous Nazi Bell" in the Miethe V7 story.
- **counter-intelligence-corps (CIC)** — US Army intelligence whose officers received postwar POW reports of wingless-disc experiments and who by 1946 registered the shift of enemy from Nazi to Soviet.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **Barney Hill's "Nazi" alien (1964)** — In a hypnosis session with Dr. Benjamin Simon, abductee Barney Hill described an abductor: "An evil face . . . He looks like a German Nazi." The authors — noting Hill was a Black man married to a white woman in 1960s New Hampshire — read this as the consciousness side of the Phenomenon: to Hill, the only comparable experiences were "a white cop with a German shepherd" or "a Nazi landing in a V3." Barney and Betty Hill were the first well-known, well-publicized abductees.
- **The Zündel Antarctica myth** — Canadian publisher and Holocaust denier Ernst Zündel began, in the 1970s, "as a fictional device," the story of Hitler taken by U-boat to an Antarctic base where engineers built saucers to attack the West; boosted by a Spanish-to-English mistranslation of an Admiral Byrd interview in Chile's El Mercurio ("planes flying from pole to pole" rendered as if Byrd had witnessed craft from the South Pole).
- **Operation HIGHJUMP (1946–47)** — Byrd's expensive, oddly timed US Navy armada to Antarctica, despite postwar budget pressure; projects never satisfactorily completed (bad weather, inadequate instruments, untrained personnel, fatal accidents). Ended February 1947 — four months before Arnold. Combined with the Nazi New Swabia Expedition (1938–39), it fed the fictional plotline of a US Navy battle with a Nazi garrison.
- **H. Keith Thompson** — A HIGHJUMP Navy participant who was a prewar Nazi sympathizer claiming Reich intelligence work, a postwar Nazi-underground player consorting with Skorzeny, Rudel, and American Nazi James Madole (National Renaissance Party) — and, "even more incredibly," for a while the publicity and literary agent of Marguerite Oswald, mother of Lee Harvey Oswald.
- **The 1947 cascade** — June 1947: Kenneth Arnold sighting, then the Maury Island affair, then Roswell; the National Security Act signed July 26, 1947 — "only weeks after" the Roswell "crashed disc" — creating the CIA, DoD, and USAF.
- **Dornberger and von Braun under Paperclip** — General Walter Dornberger assigned to Wright Field (aviation scientific/technical intelligence) in June 1947; "an unrepentant Nazi" responsible for slave labor at Peenemünde and Mittelwerk/Nordhausen, later a Bell Aerospace board member. His protégé von Braun — a dedicated Nazi and longtime SS officer — was whitewashed as a Hitler-hating rocket enthusiast; the truth emerged only long after his 1977 death.
- **Hermann Oberth (1894–1989)** — Peenemünde rocket engineer, awarded the War Merit Cross First Class with Swords for defending the site during the 1943 British Operation Crossbow raid; denazified, Italy, back to Germany 1953, eventually NASA/Huntsville under von Braun — yet never technically Paperclip, the authors' proof that counting only Paperclip misses the picture. An ETH defender who nonetheless lent credence to Soviet antigravity rumors.
- **The Levetzow-Pinkell-Goryev tachyon story** — Per the rumor (Frankfurter Illustrierte, Nov 14/21, 1953; Oberth's notes): Levetzow's student Horst Pinkell traveled to the USSR in 1928 (voluntarily or as prisoner — accounts vary), was employed to make Levetzow's tachyon concept workable; the theory (refined with Andrei Goryev) was allegedly tested at a secret Urals facility circa 1938, and by 1947 the Russians allegedly flew a saucer-shaped antigravity vehicle "with considerable success." Oberth doubted the theory was mature enough for actual machinery but reported the tests.
- **The V7 / Miethe / Belluzzo** — Oberth described the German V7 as a ramjet-bladed helicopter mistakable for a spinning disc; the Soviets built the Mir-V7 circa 1955. But France-Soir (June 7, 1952) claimed Dr. Richard Miethe built the V7 in 1944 as an actual disk, working with Italian ex-Deputy Minister and steam turbine expert Dr. Giuseppe Belluzzo (1876–1952), at sites including Breslau and Prague. Belluzzo really existed and claimed in Il Giornale d'Italia (March 24, 1950) that he had personally helped design a flying saucer prototype in Nazi Germany in 1942 — picked up days later by English-language media including the Mirror.
- **Anatol J. Schneiderov** — Russian-born (Yekaterinburg, 1894) seismologist; as a ten-year-old in Perm, April 1905, allegedly experienced a red globe/corona containing a short black humanoid (three to four feet, large round eyes) at a Russian Orthodox church — the being frightened him then floated up a staircase (sources unverified by the authors but consistent). Left Russia at the Revolution via Shanghai to the US; 1945 Philosophical Society member alongside J. Allen Hynek; Columbia PhD 1948; founded the Polycultural Institution of America (1948, Washington, D.C.) and taught a 17-week "flying saucers" class run "like a congressional committee hearing"; claimed metallic spheres were reported over the Himalayas in 1111 A.D. and again by 18th-century travelers in Tibet and Mongolia; said America could build saucers "through sound engineering."
- **Kirill Petrovich Stanyukovich (1916–1989)** — Soviet astronomer who collaborated with Schneiderov's group on alternative theories of gravity; published on the Tunguska explosion; was involved in a 1958 Soviet Academy of Sciences study "On the Study of the 'Snowman' Question" (Bigfoot-type humanoids); cited in the 1970 Sputnik hollow-Moon article by Vasin and Shcherbakov. The authors ask how much the two Russians shared on antigravity and the Phenomenon.
- **R. V. Jones and the V2 disbelief** — In Most Secret War, Jones recounts that British scientific intelligence considered German missiles carrying one-ton warheads into England "a technical impossibility" — he had to struggle to convince them and get Peenemünde bombed. The authors offer this as a mindset-parallel to today's establishment reaction to UAP.
- **White Sands** — More than sixty captured V2s fired from White Sands Proving Ground (about an hour from the Roswell site) in 1946–51, with up to thirty-nine former Nazi rocket engineers on-site until spring 1947; a UFO sighting there on May 15, 1947 was witnessed by scientists and technicians during a V2 test launch.
- **The Horten brothers and Arnold** — Walter (ace pilot) and Reimar (inventor) Horten, interviewed by the British then the Americans in 1945 under Paperclip auspices but not offered US positions (Walter stayed in Germany, Reimar went to Argentina). Arnold's "saucers skipping across the water" described motion, not shape; his drawing matched the Horten Ho 229/V3 flying wing "as to be virtually identical," so military intelligence revisited the Horten interviews and conjectured the craft were Soviet-built Horten designs — the prevailing 1947 wisdom among some US officers.
- **The Maison Rouge meeting and the gold** — Strasbourg, August 10, 1944: SS-chaired instruction to German industry to expatriate wealth and know-how. Trains of gold bullion went to Spain and Portugal; forty tons shipped from Portugal to Macau, of which twenty tons went to China and twenty to Indonesia; more gold to Swiss banks, some financing terror groups in North Africa and the Middle East and war criminals' flight.
- **The 1945 congressional report** — A summer/fall 1945 series of reports to Congress detailing nested "Russian doll" dummy corporations: Argentina had more than one hundred listed firms (Thyssen, AEG, Agfa, Mitsubishi, Siemens, Zeiss); Brazil more than one hundred including banks and shipping; tiny Guatemala more than two hundred German-associated firms including more than seventy "fincas." The State Department attested to plans "to support an eventual resurgence of German power" via industrial concerns (I.G. Farben, Siemens, Bosch, Telefunken), foreign research labs for new weapons, subsidized schools, and propaganda media.
- **The U-boat cargo for Japan** — A German submarine captured off Canada at war's end carried cases of uranium for a Japanese atomic weapon, a disassembled Messerschmitt jet, weapons blueprints, and engineers/technicians for Japanese "wonder weapons" — evidence both of how close the Nazis were to a bomb and of their scorched-earth intent.
- **Safehaven's collapse and the Cold War flip** — By end-1946/early-1947 SS officers were recruited, not hunted; Spain (the most infamous "neutral"), Switzerland, and Portugal stonewalled Safehaven; politicians pressing Argentina to divest were smeared as "soft on Communism when they were only hard on Nazism."
- **The networks in operation** — Barbie heading Bolivia's secret police; Colonia Dignidad sheltering Mengele, training interrogators, serving Operation Condor; Woomera, Australia, as an "Area 51–type" rocket site staffed by Peenemünde/Nordhausen veterans; Nazi rocketeers in Egypt building missiles against Israel; Osoaviakhim's 2,000+ scientists (6,000+ with families) in the USSR.
- **CAPRI and Tucumán** — Argentine-born SS officer Carlos Fuldner's Spanish-route rescue system, funded by Ludwig Freude (one of the ten richest men in Latin America, Perón intimate, wartime pro-Nazi intelligence player who tapped German industrialists for Perón's campaign chest). CAPRI's terminus: the National University of Tucumán, first residence of Armin Schoklitsch (hydrologist, wartime president of the University of Graz) and Adolf Eichmann himself. Among 300+ CAPRI "employees": Carlos Schulz (Nordic route, Evangelical Escape Organization front) and Dr. Hermann Beer, Hitler's favorite "bridge builder," who later resumed his European career undisturbed.
- **The Huemul Project** — Ronald Richter (1909–1991), who had worked with Max Steenbeck and Manfred von Ardenne (both later on the Soviet bomb), recruited by Kurt Tank to build the Thermotron fusion device on Huemul Island toward nuclear-powered aircraft. It alarmed Washington — coinciding with Argentine uranium discoveries and fears of a Perónist atomic superpower. Perón's 1955 fall killed the project; Richter was imprisoned for fraud.
- **Kurt Tank and August Siebrecht** — Focke-Wulf FW190 designer Tank entered Argentina in 1946 on a false passport as "Pedro Matthies" with many Focke-Wulf employees (also fake-papered); he was nearly done with an Argentine Focke-Wulf Ta183 jet when Perón fell, then went to India to help create the Indian space program. His contact Siebrecht — Chilean-born member of the Chilean Nazi Party (about 1,400 registered members at war's end), Perón intimate, wartime Nazi spy once extradited to the US — hired Richter and pushed Perón to fund Nazi-staffed aeronautical research.
- **Skorzeny's portfolio** — SS commando Otto Skorzeny: built American Air Force bases in Europe in the 1950s; ran Die Spinne; trained PLO commandos including a young Yasser Arafat; advised Nasser's Egypt on Nazi-staffed missile programs against Israel; CIA source/asset deemed "apolitical" while feted at SS reunions in Europe and Latin America; helped form CEDADE; co-founded Merex AG with Gerhard Mertins (F-86 Sabre to Pakistan); appeared with Rudel (also a US government aviation consultant) at Colonia Dignidad. The authors present him as "the more or less public face" of a network with its own agenda.
- **Gehlen and Schacht** — Gehlen ran West German intelligence and the CIA's early anti-Soviet effort with old Nazi friends (possibly a pretend-espionage boondoggle); Hitler's ex-finance minister Hjalmar Schacht consulted for governments as far away as Indonesia, urging an Islamic crescent against Mao's China — Skorzeny in Egypt and Schacht in Southeast Asia "manipulating religion" to build an international network of Islamic extremists.
- **Strughold and Strieber at Randolph AFB** — Paperclip's Hubertus Strughold, responsible for heinous concentration-camp human experimentation, created the USAF Aviation Medicine center at Randolph AFB (San Antonio) with dozens of his former scientists under him — a lifelong sinecure. Whitley Strieber, "probably the most famous alien abductee" (Communion; the Christopher Walken film), grew up there and wondered whether his abduction memories were implanted by a specific remembered individual — a Nazi or Nazi-connected man — via mind control (hypnosis or hallucinogens). Levenda made a possible identification and traced the man to a German educational institution in Latin America. The authors say this "does not prove anything" but shows how Nazi and UFO ideas intertwine.
- **SS theology in practice** — SS publications banned words like "Christmas"; new neo-pagan christening and marriage rituals; the "pollution" doctrine regarding Jewish women ("it was a theological issue"); gas chambers and ovens run "right up until the end of the war" at the expense of Berlin's defense — the authors' evidence that ideology, not utility, drove war planning. A neo-Nazi leader personally told Levenda the murdered Jews "should be thankful" as they'd be reincarnated into Aryan bodies.
- **Heisenberg and Hahn** — Both worked for the Nazis: Heisenberg (Uncertainty Principle, Nobel 1932 for quantum mechanics) and Hahn (invented nuclear fission, Nobel 1944 — the authors italicize the year twice for emphasis).
