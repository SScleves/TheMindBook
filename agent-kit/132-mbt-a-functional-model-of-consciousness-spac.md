# Ch 61 — A Functional Model of Consciousness Space-Time: The Design Solution for Optimal Consciousness Evolution

## In one paragraph
Campbell narrates the evolutionary step that, in his model, makes physical reality (PMR) necessary: trillions of individuated consciousness units interacting in NPMR eventually reproduced themselves, formed value-based groupings (the prototypes of good and evil, families, clans, societies), and lived under loose "cosmic law" constraints — but growth stagnated because these near-immortal digital beings could never start over, so their delusions, beliefs, and ruts only deepened. AUM therefore needed a new experimental protocol: a being that could begin with a clean slate (no accumulated delusion, but wisdom intact) inside a reality that unambiguously enforces causal links between intent, action, and consequence. Campbell says AUM solved this by evolving a space-time rule-set defining hybrid beings — simultaneously physical and nonphysical, evolving in two reality dimensions at once — of which mankind (PMRk-kind) is one instance. He then poses, and defers to the next chapter, the question of how a "physical" reality is built from nonphysical consciousness, pausing for a digression on humor and not taking oneself too seriously. The chapter closes with the claim that PMR and its inhabitants are a logical requirement of AUM's evolution — derived from the two starting assumptions (the Fundamental Process plus consciousness as digital potential energy) — while cautioning against feeling special about it.

## Core claims
- Campbell claims that NPMR did not offer the optimal environment and rule-set for evolving consciousness quality: despite every unit's potential to reach the quality of its source, average progress was poor.
- Campbell argues that consciousness reproduction became self-perpetuating: some bounded thought-form entities in NPMR developed the ability to create self-sustaining thought-forms of their own — "chips from the old AUM block," each able (to the limit of its capacities) to develop the potential of the whole.
- These derived consciousnesses started with at least a partial copy of their creators' content — new, independent beings that nonetheless carried some of the form and much of the (limited) potential of their source.
- Campbell claims that competing strategies coalesced around differing value sets: those focused on serving self and using others versus those focused on using themselves to serve others — forming "the prototypes for good and evil," along with families, clans, societies, and social/racial/political groupings.
- AUM (within TBC) set up rules — "cosmic law" — constraining mostly destructive interactions in NPMR_N; enforcement was relatively strict but not perfect, and there were no constraints on intent, only on the action following intent.
- Campbell claims individuated consciousness is essentially immortal: digital beings, if not deleted or damaged, last as long as the computer (AUM). Only disintegration into an unorganized high-entropy pile of reality cells could completely terminate a conscious being, and catastrophic disintegration "does not happen naturally." The mortality of your virtual physical body is merely a learning device.
- AUM later modified rules so that creators of a new entity were held responsible for its quality and its good start; Campbell says our innate desire that our offspring prosper reflects that top-level rule-set protocol (PMR is part of OS, which exists within NPMR_N).
- Growth in NPMR_N went asymptotic: because existence was continuous, beings could not accumulate growth through fresh starts; self-laid traps, fantasies, and beliefs deepened, and the rate of quality growth slowed to a "sub-optimal crawl."
- The design requirements for the fix: a being that starts over with a clean slate — without accumulated delusions but with accumulated knowledge and wisdom intact — inside a virtual reality that unambiguously tracks, maintains, and enforces straightforward causal relationships (responsibility, accountability, immediate feedback), which mind-space defined poorly.
- Campbell claims AUM solved this by evolving a space-time rule-set defining the physical experience of hybrid beings with simultaneous physical and nonphysical components, existing in two reality dimensions at once; our PMR space-time reality is one such constrained subset (a unique dimension) of NPMR_N.
- Campbell asserts you and PMR are "nothing other than a construct of consciousness"; the key to reconciling being a consciousness extant only in the mind of AUM with having a flesh-and-blood body is understanding the nature of experience (next chapter's subject).
- PMR inhabited by us is a *logical requirement* — the necessary result of AUO plus the Fundamental Process leading to AUM and onward — not an offhand experiment; but Campbell warns against getting "puffed up" over that, since wheels are logical requirements of an automobile without being superior to the transmission.
- Campbell claims there are multiple other PMRs fundamentally similar to ours (differing in implementation detail), and other AUM experiments with entirely different logical requirements; they exist on the periphery of our operational reality and matter little practically (to be discussed in Section 5).
- Interlude claim: humor "keeps our brains lubricated" and prevents self-referential stupidity; too much serious work without relaxed play stunts growth — done right, work and play merge into "one joyful long-lasting boogie."

## Concepts
- **AUM** — the evolved, aware apex consciousness in whose mind we exist; here it is "the computer" that digital beings last as long as, the rule-maker who designs experimental protocols and rule-sets, and the being whose goal of studying, understanding, improving and evolving itself drives every step from AUO to PMR. Also nicknamed "the Big Cheese" and "the Big Dude."
- **AUO** — the earlier, "plain dim consciousness (digital potential energy)" from which the progression starts; AUM is the logical consequence of AUO plus the Fundamental Process.
- **NPMR** — nonphysical-matter reality; the consciousness-space where individuated units first interacted, reproduced, and formed societies, but which proved suboptimal for evolving consciousness quality.
- **NPMR_N** — the particular NPMR sector containing us; has a policy prohibiting certain destructive interactions among "law-abiding citizens," broad behavioral constraints ("cosmic law"), and contains OS and PMR within it. Constraints exist on action, never on intent.
- **NPMRn (the diverse NPMRs)** — the plural family of independent reality systems inhabited by various groups of evolving beings.
- **OS (Our System)** — the reality system within NPMR_N of which PMR is a part; groups play out their evolution "within NPMR_N and OS."
- **PMR** — physical-matter reality, our space-time reality: a subset (unique dimension) of NPMR_N with additional, cleverer constraints, designed to link intent and action with consequence. Campbell calls it a logically required step in this series of consciousness experiments, and asserts many other fundamentally similar PMRs exist.
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — where AUM's rules were "set up (evolved)" to constrain destructive behavior within NPMR_N.
- **Consciousness units / individuated consciousness** — the trillions of bounded, individuated beings in NPMR, each with the potential to evolve its quality to the level of its source; "chips from the old AUM block."
- **Derived consciousness** — a new individuated being created by another bounded thought-form entity; starts with at least a partial copy of its creator's content, representing some of the form and much of the (limited) potential of its source.
- **Bounded thought-form entities / self-sustaining thought-forms** — NPMR beings capable of creating new self-sustaining consciousness — the mechanism by which consciousness reproduction became self-perpetuating.
- **Cosmic law** — the broad behavioral constraints under which consciousness and its progeny evolve in NPMR_N; experimental protocols enforced relatively strictly but not perfectly.
- **Experimental protocol / rule-set** — AUM's designed sets of constraints defining what interactions are possible in a given reality; the space-time rule-set is the new protocol that fixes NPMR's learning inefficiencies.
- **Space-time rule-set** — the rule-set AUM evolved that "defines the physical experience or interactions" of hybrid beings; the design solution of the chapter title.
- **Hybrid beings / PMRk-kind** — beings that simultaneously have both physical and nonphysical components and "exist, grow, and evolve in two reality dimensions simultaneously"; mankind is one instance, which Campbell calls "an exceptionally clever solution."
- **Entropy / entropy reduction** — lowering entropy is the goal of consciousness evolution; "increased personal responsibility in pursuit of the goal of entropy reduction was just what the doctor ordered for NPMR_N." Total disintegration into "an unorganized high-entropy pile of reality cells" is the only true death of a conscious being.
- **Reality cells** — the elementary constituents of a conscious being; disintegration into a disordered pile of them is the only complete termination. Short of that, damage can be "imparted or inflicted in a multitude of ways" but is usually self-repairable if not fatal, and catastrophic disintegration "does not happen naturally."
- **Fundamental Process** — the process of evolution, one of the two foundational assumptions (with consciousness as digital potential energy) from which Campbell says the whole progression AUO → AUM → PMR logically follows (assumptions given in Chapter 24, Book 1).
- **Digital potential energy / digital consciousness** — Campbell's characterization of consciousness as a nonphysical, low-entropy, digital energy form; aware consciousness "exists only in the form of a nonphysical, low-entropy, energy form."
- **Mind-space / consciousness-space** — the nonphysical arena of NPMR interaction, where responsibility, accountability, and immediate feedback were "poorly defined and subject to individual interpretation," making learning difficult.
- **Virtual physical body / apparent mortality** — the seemingly mortal body is "simply a device to facilitate your learning"; local mortality is irrelevant to the apparently endless continuation of your digital awareness.
- **Clean slate / fresh start** — the key design requirement: a being that starts over "without the accumulated delusions of the past but with its accumulated knowledge and wisdom intact."
- **Good and evil (prototypes)** — value-orientations that formed in NPMR: serving self and using others versus using self to serve others.
- **Free will** — what groups of beings exercise as they play out their evolution within NPMR_N and OS; intent is unconstrained even where action is constrained.
- **Immortality** — the default condition of digital consciousness beings ("immortal with a capital 'I'") — unless "you screw up and get zapped by the Big Cheese or mugged by some tough guy or mean-spirited anti-rat."
- **Anti-rat** — a mean-spirited being that could conceivably "mug" an individuated consciousness (used in Campbell's aside on the limits of immortality).
- **Experience** — flagged as the key to understanding how a consciousness-being and a flesh-and-blood body are the same thing; the announced subject of the next chapter.
- **Quality of consciousness** — the property being evolved throughout: NPMR's problem was that growth toward "an overall higher quality of consciousness" had slowed to a crawl.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The NPMR stagnation narrative (the chapter's central model)** — a staged just-so account with these moving parts: (1) trillions of consciousness units interact in NPMR; (2) some develop reproduction — creating derived consciousness with partial copies of the creator's content — causing a "creative free-for-all"; (3) values coalesce into self-serving vs. other-serving orientations, spawning the prototypes of good and evil plus families, clans, societies, and political groupings; (4) AUM imposes cosmic-law constraints via TBC (strict but imperfect enforcement; intent never constrained); (5) growth goes asymptotic because immortal beings without fresh starts sink into ever-deeper ruts, traps, fantasies, and cultural beliefs ("been there, done that"); (6) AUM designs the space-time rule-set producing hybrid, two-dimension beings. Campbell presents this as the derivation of why PMR exists.
- **The design-requirements specification** — Campbell frames AUM as an engineer writing requirements: a clean-slate being retaining wisdom; unambiguous, enforced causal relationships; immediate (relative) accountability linking intent, action, and consequence. PMR is the delivered "design solution."
- **"Chips from the old AUM block"** — analogy for reproduction of consciousness: every chip (including you) can, to the limit of its capacities, develop the potential of the whole.
- **Digital-being immortality / computer analogy** — "Digital beings, if not deleted or damaged, last as long as the computer (AUM)"; shows why continuous existence caused the learning stagnation, and reframes bodily death as a learning device.
- **The parental-responsibility protocol** — AUM's mid-course rule change making creators responsible for the quality and good start of new entities; Campbell says our innate wish that offspring prosper beyond mere survival is a reflection of this top-level rule-set.
- **The Yoda joke** — after teasing that the answer to "how do you build a physical reality from consciousness?" will come in "my next, even more expensive book due out at the end of the next millennium," Campbell asks why Yoda lives alone in a filthy mud hut crawling with snakes — a self-deprecating aside about the toll of writing/teaching this material.
- **The humor interlude** — free advice: never get too wound up over details (it makes you "insufferable and grumpy" with "tunnel-vision"); humor lubricates the brain against "self-referential stupidity"; balanced work and play merge into "one joyful long-lasting boogie."
- **"Many ways to skin a cat" / Muffy** — used (with a mock apology to Muffy, addressed directly as if present) to introduce the claim that AUM ("the Big Dude") runs other experiments with different logical requirements, and that multiple PMRs fundamentally similar to ours exist with widely differing implementation details.
- **The automobile-wheels analogy** — we are logical requirements of AUM's evolution the way wheels are logical requirements of an automobile: very important, but with no cause to feel superior to the transmission, brakes, engine, or body — or to airplanes, trains, and boats (other experiments) and their wings, propellers, rudders, or tracks. An anti-anthropocentric closing move.
- **Two-puddles recap** — Campbell reminds the reader the discussion "started with two puddles of differentiated consciousness" and has now progressed to AUM evolving a PMR space-time "populated with good looking sexy bodies like yours," each step forced by AUM's self-study goal plus the two foundational assumptions (pointer to Chapter 24, Book 1).

## Thread
This chapter is the pivot of Section 4's derivation of the physical from the nonphysical: it follows the developmental story that began with "two puddles of differentiated consciousness" (AUO differentiating, AUM evolving, individuated units populating NPMR) and supplies the *reason PMR exists* — NPMR's learning stagnated because immortal beings could not get fresh starts or clear causal feedback, so AUM engineered a space-time rule-set for hybrid clean-slate beings. Campbell explicitly grounds the whole progression in the two assumptions of Chapter 24, Book 1 (the Fundamental Process plus consciousness as digital potential energy), presenting PMR as a logically required step rather than a whim. It sets up the next chapter's subject — the nature of *experience* — as the key to reconciling being "nothing other than a construct of consciousness" with having a "flesh-and-blood body," and it defers the treatment of other PMRs and nonphysical realities to Section 5.
