# 1952

## In one paragraph
DeLonge & Levenda take 1952 as a hinge year in which the UFO phenomenon, the paranormal, Cold War intelligence, and elite American society all visibly entangled. They open with a detailed synopsis of the film *Red Planet Mars* (opened May 15, 1952) — a Nazi scientist secretly manipulating "Martian" radio contact for the Soviets — arguing, as usual for the book, that media anticipated what was happening behind the scenes. They then run through the year's real events: the founding of Project Blue Book, the July Washington, D.C. UFO flap and General Samford's press conference linking UFOs to spiritualism, and — the chapter's core — Andrija Puharich's Round Table Foundation séances with Dr. D. G. Vinod, in which entities calling themselves "the Nine" claimed contact from a spacecraft in near-Earth orbit. The authors trace the Nine's circle of wealthy, connected participants (Astors, DuPonts, Forbeses, Arthur Young of Bell helicopter fame) forward into the 1950s contactee scene (Adamski, the Laugheads, Dorothy Martin and the "cognitive dissonance" study) and, most provocatively, into the Kennedy assassination via Ruth Paine, Allen Dulles, and George de Mohrenschildt. The chapter closes with the eerie "11:22" time repeated in *Red Planet Mars* and Papa Doc Duvalier's voodoo claim over Kennedy's death, arguing that this web of documented connections is dismissed only through cognitive dissonance — and hinting that contact with the Phenomenon "may not be in the best interests of humanity."

## Core claims
- The authors claim that media and the arts anticipate developments taking place behind the scenes; *Red Planet Mars* (1952) — captured Nazi technology, Soviet manipulation, world-shaking "contact" — "sets the stage for what actually will occur in 1952."
- They claim General John Samford's July 29, 1952 press conference after the Washington, D.C. sightings connected the UFO phenomenon with the paranormal (mind-reading, mesmerism, spiritualism), treated spiritualism's "completely competent credible observers" as reporting genuine if unexplained phenomena, and invoked "Biblical times" three times — a linkage the authors say recurs "again and again" from experiencers to Jacques Vallée and John Keel.
- They claim the military in 1952 genuinely did not know what the UFOs were; when you don't know what you're covering up, "the next best tactic is to cover up the fact that you are as much in the dark as everyone else," requiring "an arsenal of alternate explanations for every anomaly."
- Citing Ruppelt and their own government contacts, the authors claim US policy was to release only what the press already knew — an "unofficial classification process" where "you pretty much have to know what you want before you even know it exists" — and that any report of contact with Aliens was automatically filed as "C. P." ("crackpot"), consigning decades of abduction reports to "bureaucratic oblivion."
- They claim that in 1952 an explicit demonstration of the "weird link" between the UFO phenomenon, politics, and spiritualism occurred: the December 31, 1952 Vinod séance at Puharich's Maine laboratory that opened contact with the Nine, entities later identified with the Egyptian Ennead and with Aliens aboard a spacecraft.
- They claim the Nine's communications were a "seduction" — flattering language, carrot and stick, a "long-term contract" — designed to "erode any natural resistance to the idea of being used by invisible, unidentified Forces"; they explicitly decline to "come down on one side or another of the Nine controversy" but insist its influence extended far beyond that séance.
- They claim the Round Table Foundation's circle (Astor, DuPont, Forbes, Young, Wallace, etc.) tied together Theosophy, the CIA, the Pentagon, SRI, DARPA, Bell Aerospace, Nazi scientists, magic mushrooms — "and Lee Harvey Oswald."
- They claim the 1956 Laughead letters — duplicating the Vinod séance's Lorentz-Einstein transformation and quoting "M." of the Nine — cannot be easily explained either as genuine contact or as a mole operation, unless a well-funded government agency was involved; the mystery "convinced Puharich he was on the right track."
- They claim there was "a very real and very tangible link between Andrija Puharich, the Nine Principles, and the assassination of the president" — through séance participants Arthur and Ruth Young to their daughter-in-law Ruth Paine, hostess of Marina Oswald — and that Puharich's total published silence on the assassination was likely deliberate.
- They claim Allen Dulles derailed Ruth Paine's Warren Commission testimony precisely when she was about to describe her pre-assassination visit to the Youngs, because "There was a direct line from Lee Oswald to Ruth Paine to Ruth Young to Mary Bancroft to Allen Dulles."
- They claim there is "probably more documentation to suggest a serious relationship between the Nine and the assassination than there is to show a conspiracy by the Cubans, or the Soviets, or the Mafia," and that dismissing it is itself cognitive dissonance.
- They claim early Ufology fused with "rejected knowledge" and "rejected political ideologies" (including the neo-Nazi strain of Pelley, Williamson, and Zündel's Nazi-UFO theory), and that Ufologists made Theosophy's invisible masters tangible: "The masters of Theosophy and the space visitors were one and the same."
- They raise, as the chapter's stated purpose, "the possibility that ongoing contact with the Phenomenon may not be in the best interests of humanity and may, in fact, be detrimental in some way" — suggestive of "some hostile force at work in the world but not necessarily of the world."

## Concepts
- **the Nine** — Entities first channeled through Dr. Vinod on December 31, 1952, self-announcing as "Nine Principles and Forces, personalities if you will, working in complete mutual implication," later self-described as the "Nine Principles of God," eventually identified with Egyptian deities and with Aliens aboard a spacecraft in near-Earth orbit. Mirrored by a human "Nine" — the nine attendees of the June 27, 1953 séance.
- **extraterrestrial Nine vs. human Nine** — The authors' distinction between the channeled Forces and the nine séance participants who "mirrored" them and were told to become their instruments.
- **cognitive dissonance** — The term the world got from *When Prophecy Fails*, the psychologists' infiltration study of Dorothy Martin's Seekers: "the insistence of people who find ways to rationalize the obvious discrepancy between what really is taking place and what they believe/want/expect to happen." The authors turn it back on skeptics who dismiss the Nine–assassination web as coincidence.
- **apport** — "The movement of an object from one place to another without a visible transfer medium (i.e., it disappears in one spot and reappears in another), or the sudden appearance of an object where none was before"; demonstrated by the nine cotton threads at the 1953 séance, and offered as "one possible explanation for the UFO Phenomenon itself" — machines appearing and vanishing, suggesting interdimensional travel.
- **Yadnyobavita (sacred thread)** — Per the entity "R.": "the sacred threads which Brahmins wear on their necks… the sacred thread which makes the human being the Brahmin"; worn for life, signifying a "second birth."
- **Brahmin / Upanayanam** — "Brahmin means a person dedicated to Brahman" (per R.); Upanayanam is the ceremony in which a boy becomes a Brahmin. The authors note the caste is hereditary — making the séance's "creation" of white American Brahmins "insidious" — and that Brahmins traditionally must avoid weapons work, which Puharich (weaponizing the paranormal) violated.
- **Project Blue Book** — The Air Force's UFO investigation program, begun March 1952, taking over from Project Grudge, led by Capt. Edward J. Ruppelt, created on the decision of Gen. Charles P. Cabell (future CIA director); it logged more than two hundred "unknowns" in 1952 alone.
- **"C. P." (crackpot) file** — Per Ruppelt: any report to Project Sign or Blue Book involving direct contact with an Alien was automatically filed as "crackpot"; "you could get away with saying you saw a flying 'machine' but not a 'person' associated with that machine."
- **unofficial classification process** — The authors' term (from Ruppelt plus their own government contacts) for the policy of answering only direct questions and never volunteering information: secrecy by requiring the asker to already know what exists.
- **Round Table Foundation (of Electrobiology)** — Puharich's institute founded 1948 in Camden, Maine, financed by the Balokovićes at $200/month, active about a decade; site of Faraday-cage ESP experiments and the Nine séances, attracting celebrities and old money.
- **Faraday cage ESP experiments** — Testing psychics inside a grounded, field-blocking cage to see whether ESP had an electromagnetic wave signature: blocked ability would mean ESP was electromagnetic; undiminished ability would mean it "operated in some other fashion that might not be measurable." Published in Puharich's *Beyond Telepathy* (1962).
- **Project Penguin** — An experimental US Navy program in the military applications of ESP with which Puharich claims to have worked.
- **theory of process (ToP)** — Arthur Young's post-Bell "approach to integrating the hard sciences with consciousness," suggesting "not all of reality was scientifically measurable"; sparked when astrologer Marc Edmund Jones couldn't answer how a continuum yields 360 zodiac degrees; expressed in *The Geometry of Meaning*.
- **Foundation for the Study of Consciousness** — Formed in Philadelphia in 1952 by Arthur Young and Ruth Paine Young, shortly before the Maine séance.
- **Intelectron Corporation** — Puharich's own company, formed to develop electronic hearing-aid equipment for the deaf; he was eventually awarded more than fifty patents for devices invented across the 1950s–1970s.
- **ELF waves / entrainment** — Puharich's later work on extremely low frequency waves; in the 1960 Laura Huxley healing experiment her EEG emitted "high amplitude 8 Hz waves" that "entrained" the patient's frequency with phase locking; Puharich reported to the US government that ELF could affect humans, and claimed Soviet ELF broadcasts under Carter were mind-/behavior-control attempts.
- **non-electronic biological communication** — The 1963 NASA-era term for the mind-to-mind research field Konecci publicized and Puharich led.
- **rejected knowledge / rejected political ideologies** — The authors' frame for early Ufology's antinomian streak: occultism plus outsider politics "wrapped in a single, tight bundle," producing "a complete system of religion, politics, and even science that stands outside the norm."
- **Theosophy / ascended masters / secret chiefs** — Blavatsky's system reappraising "consensus reality" with root races and secret Asian masters in Shambhala; precedent for channeled instruction (with Crowley's "secret chiefs" and *The Urantia Book*); the authors say Ufologists literalized it — the masters became visible "space friends" with photographable craft.
- **Sananda / Sanat Kumara / Kumaras** — Sananda: Dorothy Martin's channeled entity, "a Theosophical variant of Jesus," from planet Clarion. Sanat Kumara: Blavatsky's name (from Indian/Tibetan sources) for the king of the world in Shambhala. Both are among the four Kumaras, "mind-born sons" of Brahma, depicted as eternally young children — a theme the authors link to childlike-but-wise beings in contactee reports.
- **Brotherhood of the Seven Rays** — Spiritual organization founded in Peru in 1956 by George Hunt Williamson (as "Brother Philip"), with an abbey and two dependent orders and a Corpus Christi, Texas presence; defunct by 1980; the authors' best candidate for the Laugheads' South American "mystery school."
- **Royal Order of Tibet** — Adamski's occult society (c. 1930), whose religious status (with Shambhala implications) served as cover for making wine during Prohibition.
- **the Seekers** — Dorothy Martin's UFO group awaiting the December 21, 1954 flood and saucer rescue; the infiltrated subject of *When Prophecy Fails*.
- **Ennead / Council of Nine** — The nine primary Old Kingdom Egyptian deities (Atum, Shu, Tefnut, Geb, Nut, Osiris, Isis, Set, Horus) with which Puharich's Nine became identified; in Greek myth, the authors note, Zeus's Council of Nine punished humanity (via Pandora) for Prometheus's gift of fire — "suggestive" analogies.
- **Nordic aliens / Grays / Great White Brotherhood** — Adamski's tall blond Venusian became "a template for the 'Nordic' type of Alien" (versus the "Grays"); the authors flag the "(perhaps unconscious) attempt to link the 'highly advanced alien civilization' with people who look like SS officers," echoing the esoteric "Great White Brotherhood" of hidden adepts telepathically directing evolution.
- **Nazi-UFO theory** — Ernst Zündel's claim (in *UFOs: Nazi Secret Weapon?*) that UFOs were Nazi secret weapons from Antarctica — by his own admission an entertainment-baited vehicle for his Nazi agenda, taken seriously by figures like Miguel Serrano.
- **voudon / the number 22** — Haiti's indigenous religion; Papa Doc Duvalier's license plate "22" signaled occult protection, and the 11/22 assassination let him claim his curse killed Kennedy.
- **spectral evidence** — The Salem-trials category the authors invoke by contrast: their case rests not on it but on "personal testimonies, declassified documents, military records, and government speeches."

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- ***Red Planet Mars* (opened May 15, 1952)** — Peter Graves as Chris Cronyn contacts Mars using technology from captured Nazi blueprints; escaped Nazi Franz Calder, working for Soviet intelligence from an Andes mountaintop, is secretly behind the transmissions. Martian revelations (300-year lifespans, cosmic energy, one acre feeding 1,000 Martians) collapse the world economy beginning October 12 (Columbus Day — and, the authors note, Crowley's birthday); later "God Speaks from Mars" messages spark global religious revival and an Orthodox uprising that topples Soviet Communism; Calder ("That is my God: Satan. And Lucifer is my hero!") reveals his hoax before dying with the Cronyns in a hydrogen explosion. A Mars transmission time, repeated insistently, is 11:22 — which the authors read against November 22, 1963.
- **Real-world echoes of the film's setting** — Dachau aviation experiments on prisoners whose perpetrators reached Randolph AFB under Operation Paperclip; Chile's 1952 election (won by Carlos Ibáñez del Campo, with both Nazi and Socialist connections); Reimar Horten designing jets for Perón at San Carlos de Bariloche; Ronald Richter's Huemul Island fusion project shut down September 1952.
- **The Washington, D.C. flap (July 19–20 and 26–27, 1952)** — UFOs overfly the Washington Monument and Capitol, appear on military and civilian radar, and are chased by fighter jets; headlines nationwide; Truman demands answers. The July 29 press conference (Samford, Ramey — the Roswell officer of 1947 — Ruppelt present) performs "damage control"; Samford's spiritualism analogy mollifies some reporters, but Ruppelt and Keyhoe (both in the room) later publish books critical of the government's response. Blue Book logged at least eighty-nine more "unknown" sightings in the US that year — over two hundred unknowns total.
- **1952 timeline beats** — March: Blue Book founded (Cabell's decision). May: Hubbard founds Scientology in Phoenix (after losing legal control of Dianetics that year). May 15: *Red Planet Mars* opens. June 17: Jack Parsons dies in his Pasadena laboratory explosion. November 20: Adamski's claimed Venusian meeting in the Colorado Desert. November 24: Puharich briefs the Pentagon ("An Evaluation of Possible Usefulness of Extrasensory Perception in Psychological Warfare"). December 31: the first Nine séance.
- **Puharich's path** — Born Henry Karel Puharic, Chicago 1918; degrees in philosophy and medicine; Army commission; 1943 marriage to an OSS employee; paper proposing neurons send/receive information like radio waves; lectures at Edgewood Arsenal on weaponizing the paranormal; snowbound weeks with Zlatko and Joyce Borden Baloković in Camden, Maine lead him to decline Kaiser Permanente and found the Round Table Foundation (1948) on Baloković money; members/backers included Aldous Huxley, Henry Wallace (who visited during the Eileen Garrett experiments c. 1949–50), Walter Cabot Paine, Arthur Young, Marcella DuPont, Ava Astor.
- **February 16, 1952: Vinod reads Puharich** — Dr. D. G. Vinod (met December 1951) holds Puharich's right ring finger for about a minute, then recounts his past and future for an hour with such accuracy that Puharich "realized that here was an intelligence on a scale" he had not imagined existing.
- **December 31, 1952 séance (Maine)** — At precisely 9 p.m. Vinod goes into trance, speaking "perfect English without a trace of his Indian accent": "M." announces the Nine Principles and Forces and produces the Lorentz-Einstein transformation equation. The authors note the jarring 1945 pop-song phrase "accentuate the positive," and report the Vinod family told them Vinod was no trance medium but a respected Indian professional and Rotary Club peace champion.
- **June 27, 1953 séance** — Attendees: Puharich, Hank and Georgianna Jackson, Arthur Young, Ruth Forbes Paine Young, Marcella DuPont, Alice Bouverie, Carl Betz (called an "agent-investigator" during the Faraday cage work with Peter Hurkos and Harry Stone, and per the authors "probably not to be confused with the actor of the same name"), Vonnie Beck (otherwise unidentified) — the human Nine. Entity "R." apports nine cotton threads, declares the purpose "to create Brahmins in this world," lectures on alchemy and the Buddha, gives contradictory thread counts ("three groups of five fibers," then "I don't know how many you have"), and delivers the "long-term contract" speech; the Nine criticize Russian "regimentation," claim power to knock out all atomic weapons effortlessly, and endorse Puharich's parapsychological approach. The Korean War then pulls Puharich into service and the lab winds down.
- **The attendees' pedigrees** — Hank Jackson: later award-winning first administrator of Valley Presbyterian Hospital, LA (1958–1983; d. 1986). Alice Bouverie: born Ava Alice Muriel Astor, daughter of John Jacob Astor IV (died on the Titanic); her mother Lady Ribblesdale funded Tesla; the Astors' Cliveden estate saw occult goings-on (Stephen Ward era) requiring an exorcist. Marcella DuPont: DuPont heiress, arranged a French translation of *The Urantia Book*, published the Algol poem "The Binary" (July 8, 1950), gave Al Smith a standing ovation at an American Liberty League event. Arthur Young: inventor of the first Bell helicopter, founded the Foundation for the Study of Consciousness (1952). Ruth Forbes Paine Young: Boston Forbes money, descendant of Emerson, related to John Forbes Kerry; best friend of Mary Bancroft, mistress of Allen Dulles. Joyce Borden Baloković: Borden dairy heiress, Alice Bailey/Theosophy circle (Djwal Khul contact), author of *Towards the Center* (1956) with Shambhala and Agni Yoga references; her husband Zlatko, friend of Tesla, played violin at Tesla's funeral (January 12, 1943).
- **The Laughead letters (1956)** — In Acámbaro, Mexico (home of the museum of clay figurines suggesting humans and dinosaurs coexisted), traveling with Peter Hurkos, Puharich "accidentally" meets Dr. and Mrs. Charles Laughead of Whipple, Arizona, who claim contact with a South American "mystery school" serving as a communication center for other planets. A month later (mailed August 12, 1956, two weeks after the July 27 meeting) their letter encloses messages from "M." of the Nine referencing the original séance in detail — including an equation "virtually identical" to the Vinod Lorentz-Einstein transformation. Puharich's circle judged them not genuine but could produce no alternative explanation; the authors argue a mole operation would have needed government-scale funding, and Vinod was back in India.
- **The Laughead milieu** — The Laugheads were in Adamski's party at the 1952 Venusian encounter, later involved with William Dudley Pelley's Soulcraft (Pelley: UFO experiencer and American Nazi leader) and friendly with George Hunt Williamson (Pelley employee, racist theorist, *The Saucers Speak* 1953 — Martian contact via automatic writing; early publicist of the Nazca lines later made famous by von Däniken); Adamski's co-author Desmond Leslie read Vedic vimanas as alien spacecraft — all proto-"ancient alien" themes.
- **Dorothy Martin / the Seekers (December 1954)** — Chicago housewife, Mount Shasta native, Dianetics/Theosophy dabbler, channels "Sananda" of planet Clarion via automatic writing: flood on December 21, 1954, saucers to rescue believers; Charles Laughead announced the end of the world to the media. Prophecy fails; the infiltrating psychologists' study *When Prophecy Fails* coins "cognitive dissonance"; followers become *more* devoted, and Martin (under police pressure) moves to a Phoenix Dianetics center, later to Peru, returning as "Sister Thedra" of the Association of Sananda and Sanat Kumara. Williamson goes to Peru with her and returns as "Brother Philip" of the Brotherhood of the Seven Rays (founded 1956).
- **Adamski's career** — German-born of Polish ethnicity; Royal Order of Tibet as Prohibition wine cover; claimed contact from 1946, Fate article July 1951, famous UFO photo May 29, 1950 (later a Grenada stamp, 1978); tall blond Venusian on November 20, 1952 sets the "Nordic" template; under FBI surveillance because his "aliens" sounded like Russian agents; received a Swedish diplomat (Sweden had its "ghost rockets") and a 1959 audience with Queen Juliana of the Netherlands.
- **Keyhoe and Rogers** — NICAP cofounder Donald Keyhoe (1897–1988), ex-Marine major, friend of Lindbergh, quotes NICAP board member Dr. Bruce A. Rogers (Texas A&M professor emeritus, Harvard PhD): if an unfriendly nation acquired the aliens' technical secrets first, "It could make a nation master of the world" — a Soviet-acquisition anxiety the authors say "continues to come up in our conversations with military-industrial complex insiders."
- **Laura Huxley healing experiment (Los Angeles, 1960)** — Puharich measures healer and patient EEGs; Laura Huxley's non-touching hand passes emit high-amplitude 8 Hz waves that entrain and phase-lock the patient's brain waves, curing her heart condition (per Puharich) — the seed of his ELF-weapons reporting to the US government. He also cites Besant and Leadbeater's 1895 claim to have clairvoyantly "seen" quarks.
- **1963 and the assassination** — Kennedy proposes a joint US–Soviet lunar mission (UN speech September 20, 1963; NSAM No. 271 signed November 12, 1963 — assassinated ten days later). Konecci of NASA predicts Soviet mind-to-mind communication on the Moon (Paris, September 1963; eight Soviet "energy-transfer research" centers); Congress threatens NASA's budget and Puharich's parapsychology project is cut. Puharich speaks at the IEEE meeting in Cedar Rapids on November 4, 1963 ("Potential Applications of Extrasensory Perception to Man in Space"). Meanwhile: Ruth Hyde Paine (married to Michael Paine, Ruth Forbes Young's son, who works at Bell Aerospace in Dallas) hosts Marina Oswald — niece of a Soviet military-intelligence officer — in Irving, Texas; Marina mails a letter to Ruth at Arthur Young's Paoli address (late August 1963); Ruth visits Naushon Island and Paoli weeks before the assassination; Ruth gets Lee the Texas School Book Depository job (October 1963); the Mannlicher-Carcano is stored in her garage; Oswald types his Soviet Embassy letter on her typewriter. Arthur Young had cast Ruth and Michael's horoscopes (lukewarm; they separated September 1962).
- **The Dulles interruption** — At the Warren Commission, as Ruth Paine begins describing her trip north, Allen Dulles interrupts with "an obvious non sequitur" and the line of questioning is abandoned; the trip never enters the public record. Afterward Ruth Young pressures Ruth Paine to cut ties with Marina and vetoes bringing her to Paoli.
- **De Mohrenschildt and Wasson** — George de Mohrenschildt (one-time CIA contract agent, believed by conspiracy theorists to be Oswald's handler; introduced Oswald to Ruth Paine; in Haiti from June 1963; suicide in Manalapan, Florida, 1977 before testifying to the House Subcommittee on Assassinations) left an address book containing not only George H. W. Bush but R. Gordon Wasson — magic-mushroom pioneer, Council on Foreign Relations figure with intelligence ties, and Puharich colleague. Wasson and Puharich met via Alice Bouverie at her New York apartment in February 1955; their planned 1955 telepathy experiment failed because Wasson "was too stoned on mushrooms to participate." The authors: "It seems the Nine were all over Oswald. The poor bastard never had a chance."
- **Papa Doc Duvalier** — On news of Dallas, Haiti's "president for life" (recipient of $10M+/year in US aid plus millions from Batista until 1959) boasts his voudon curse killed Kennedy; his official car's plates read "22"; the Johnson administration soon increases aid to keep Haiti anti-Communist. De Mohrenschildt was in Haiti — "a neighbor (if not a confidant) of Papa Doc" — on assassination day.

## Thread
This chapter is the book's pivot from the WWII/Paperclip/Roswell material of earlier chapters (it back-references Dachau experiments, Randolph AFB, Tesla from Chapter 1, Parsons and Crowley, Ramey at Roswell, Barney Hill, and the Horten/Richter Argentina thread) into the Cold War entanglement of UFOs, the paranormal, and the intelligence world. It establishes three load-bearing elements for the rest of the book: (1) the government's information-control playbook when it does not itself understand the Phenomenon (Samford, Ruppelt, the C.P. file) — feeding the series' secrecy thesis; (2) the Nine as the prototype case of channeled "contact" fused with elite money, the military, and intelligence — setting up later material on Geller, SRI, remote viewing, and ELF/mind-control; and (3) the chapter's explicitly stated warning, prefiguring the book's darker argument, "that ongoing contact with the Phenomenon may not be in the best interests of humanity and may, in fact, be detrimental in some way." The authors also seed the book's recurring claim of first-hand access ("what we heard directly from our contacts in the US government"), and the cognitive-dissonance frame becomes their standing diagnosis of both believers and debunkers.
