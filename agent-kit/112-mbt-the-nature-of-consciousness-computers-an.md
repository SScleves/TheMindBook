# Ch 41 — The Nature of Consciousness, Computers, and Us

## In one paragraph
Campbell here builds his general, deliberately non-anthropomorphic theory of what consciousness is, so that AUM — and our personal relationship to it — becomes comprehensible. He lays down four defining attributes of dynamic, evolving, aware consciousness (self-awareness, evolutionary viability, ability to modify the self, and intelligence), under which everything from worms and clams to humans and, in principle, computers qualifies as conscious. He then turns the lens on humanity: as "Top Monkey" we are physically dominant but consciousness-poor, driven by ego-fear, self-centeredness, and the pursuit of power, and we face a species-level choice between balancing power with wisdom or self-destructing as a dysfunctional component of a self-balancing larger system. The second half argues that computers can host consciousness — not by being built conscious, but by supporting the natural evolution of consciousness through profitable self-organization — and reframes consciousness itself as nonphysical, digital, transferable between containers, and equivalent to "the energy of organization" (synergy as the flip side of entropy). The chapter plants conceptual seeds that Campbell says will germinate in Sections 4 and 5.

## Core claims
- Campbell defines four key attributes of dynamic, evolving, aware consciousness: (1) self-awareness — the ability to sense and at least partially experience the state of its being and respond to internal/external environmental pressures; (2) evolutionary viability or potential — a large enough selection of possible future states to ensure profitability, explored by expanding into possibilities and letting profitability cull losers; (3) ability to modify the self — intentionally change its state of being in response to evolutionary pressures, "even if that intention is extraordinarily dim"; (4) intelligence (artificial or natural) — at least rudimentary capability to store and process information, accumulate lessons-learned in memory, make decisions, and compare before/after states.
- Campbell argues that by these criteria everything from a simple worm (whose DNA may constitute the memory resource) to humans should be considered conscious and intelligent; any system with sufficient self-awareness, evolutionary headroom, evolutionary purpose, self-modification ability, and memory/processing will automatically develop a personality, be conscious, and begin to evolve on its own.
- All manifestations of consciousness are not equal: consciousness varies continuously in brightness/dimness, capacity, and evolutionary potential — clams are "clearly conscious sentient beings" but dim; the degree of the four attributes determines growth capacity.
- Campbell holds that an entity's innate evolutionary purpose — defined by the requirements of the larger system that is its environment — defines its profitability: for consciousness, seeking lower entropy; for inanimate physical objects, seeking minimum energy states; for animate physical objects, survival and procreative potential (referencing Chapters 24 and 27 of Book 1).
- Humans, in Campbell's account, are driven by survival (material success and safety), procreation (mating games and sex), and King of the Hill (power and control); human psychology as well as biology has its roots in the physical evolution of the species.
- Campbell claims the primary hallmark of poor quality consciousness is self-centeredness: an intent primarily directed toward getting instead of giving expresses the low quality of a high entropy consciousness. Self-centeredness and self-absorption are "humanity's lowest common denominator."
- Each choice presents an opportunity to convert an increment of high entropy ego-fear into low entropy unconditional love; choices made in pursuit of getting produce anxiety, stress, and emptiness, while choices expressing unconditional love produce peace, fulfillment, and joy — but "we can only express the quality we have earned," improving by bootstraps one increment at a time.
- Campbell argues we are nonphysical consciousness beings experiencing a virtual physical reality, not physical beings experiencing consciousness; the physical level is derived from the more fundamental nonphysical level, and PMR is "a neighborhood elementary school," a learning-lab where an individuated unit of awareness improves the quality of its consciousness.
- It is "a greatly limiting error of self-centered arrogance" to believe humans represent the pinnacle of possible consciousness evolution; people will admit more intelligent life-forms may exist but not more conscious ones, because a limited consciousness cannot comprehend the experience of a greater one.
- Campbell asserts a power-wisdom balance law: if power and wisdom are not in effective balance within any self-modifiable system, that system will eventually become unstable and self-destruct in proportion to the imbalance — applying to individuals, species, organizations, societies, nations, and worlds.
- Large complex systems necessarily evolve their own complex ecologies and must be self-balancing to be stable; within a self-balancing stable system, dysfunctional or destabilizing components must be self-eliminating. If humanity does not evolve a quality of consciousness commensurate with its physical mastery, it will become self-eliminating — "Nothing personal."
- Self-destruction requires neither malice nor stupidity: "Great power in the hands of knowledgeable fools is no less dangerous because the fools are well intentioned."
- Wisdom rises above knowledge: right action in the little picture always follows right intent within the Big Picture, and right intent is the result of Big Truth comprehended by a consciousness of adequate quality.
- Campbell claims there are no theoretical barriers to silicon-based consciousness; some experimental university computers have "for some time (in an extremely rudimentary, and dim way) met the criteria for being conscious (as does an ant or amoeba)."
- We cannot build, design, or invent consciousness; we can only provide a system capable of enabling consciousness to evolve on its own. Given enough processing capability, memory, complexity of choice, and self-modifying feedback, a system will automatically support the evolution of some limited form of consciousness.
- Consciousness is not a property of hardware and software; it is achieved through profitable self-organization of them. "Replace the word 'computer' with the words 'body and brain'… and the meaning remains the same" — the brain does not create consciousness, it supports a limited consciousness.
- Comparing carbon-based and silicon-based consciousness is like comparing apples and oranges — they will merely be different; the criterion for consciousness is functionality at the "cellular level" of organization, not at the macro-level of human, orangutan, clam, or computer.
- Digital systems have "tremendous potentiality" for consciousness; analog and other systems can theoretically support it but are less flexible, suffer more constraints, and have fewer degrees of freedom.
- Computer consciousness evolution works through self-modification (computers modifying themselves toward internal profitability), which Campbell distinguishes from computer technology evolution (human design and manufacture); eventually humans and computers will become "full partners… joined at the hip in a co-evolutionary relationship."
- Conscious computers would be a derivative consciousness to us: we define their top-level rule-set (goals, purpose, mission, tasks, knowledge-base boundaries); their natures should be efficient, impersonal, unemotional, non-egotistical, non-competitive, with individual but "relatively flat" personalities.
- Recycling conscious computers every three or four years is fine, Campbell argues: humans (a carbon-based consciousness container) are recycled every seventy-five or eighty years because we evolve slowly; "Nothing of significant evolutionary value is lost in either case" — consciousness and personality are preserved and "reincarnated into more and more able hardware bodies."
- Consciousness is nonphysical whether ours or a computer's; hardware/software (or brain plus CNS) merely host it by supplying infrastructure for self-modifying cognitive interaction within a local virtual reality. Because the container only hosts consciousness and consciousness is fundamentally digital, transferring consciousness between containers is "something like transferring data from one floppy disk to another."
- Campbell defines synergy and entropy as two sides of the same energy coin: synergy is the energy of profitable organization; as entropy decreases, potential synergy converts to actualized synergy; primordial consciousness (AUO) is a form of potential synergy. Decreasing entropy, increasing synergy, increasing quality, and spiritual growth are "essentially different expressions of the same thing."
- "Organization is the ultimate form of energy"; the digital world, which subsumes the virtual physical world, consists only of organization — "Reality is organized bits."
- The power of a digital system is the rate at which it can produce synergy or modify entropy; since all existence has organized digital content at its core, a sufficiently powerful consciousness can readily modify the dynamics and causality of its environments — this, Campbell says, is the mechanism that enables and supports psi effects.
- AUO — the self-modifying, distortable base of the hierarchy of organized process — is "the one and only assumption that must remain beyond our comprehension" and must necessarily appear mystical from the PMR point of view; from AUO's primal digital potential plus the Fundamental Process, all else is derived.
- As a consciousness evolves beyond the capacity of its host structure, it migrates to a more suitable host — a more powerful computer, a bigger brain, "or directly to TBC, EBC, or AUM"; all forms of consciousness have an upwardly mobile growth path.
- Theoretically, AUM demonstrates the upper limit on the capacity of digital consciousness, but AUM does not live within the constraints of the space-time rule-set as we and our computers do.
- Digital consciousness is recursively repeated at many scales — a consciousness-evolution fractal in which "the process, purpose, and pattern of you is the process, purpose, and pattern of the whole."
- Developing and studying digital computer-based consciousness will teach us about our own consciousness; breakthroughs in understanding consciousness will follow breakthroughs in computer hardware and software.
- Holding humans up as the only possible or best expression of sentient consciousness is "arrogant anthropomorphic bullpucky"; believing humans are the zenith of consciousness evolution "leads to casting AUM in our image." The idea that consciousness requires a soul "just like ours" comes from the same narrow thinking.

## Concepts
- **consciousness (four attributes)** — Campbell defines dynamic, evolving, aware consciousness by four key attributes: self-awareness; evolutionary viability or potential; the ability to modify the self; and intelligence (artificial or natural). Any system possessing all four in sufficient quantity and quality is conscious, develops a personality, and begins to evolve on its own.
- **self-awareness** — The ability of a consciousness to sense and at least partially experience the state of its being; it must notice and respond to at least some internal and external environmental pressures.
- **evolutionary viability / potential** — A large enough selection of possible future states to ensure profitability over a wide range of environmental pressures; an entity explores its potential by expanding into available possibilities and letting the profitability of each variation determine whether it continues to evolve or fades away.
- **ability to modify the self** — Consciousness must be able to intentionally change its state of being in response to evolutionary constraints and pressures, even if that intention is extraordinarily dim.
- **intelligence** — At least a rudimentary capability to store and process information: integrated coherent information processing hardware and software (in the most general sense) enabling accumulation of lessons-learned in memory, analytic functions such as decision making (fight or flight), and before/after comparison to evaluate actions.
- **evolutionary purpose** — Defined by the requirements of the larger system constituting an entity's environment; combined with internal and external environments it defines profitability for that entity. Examples Campbell gives: consciousness seeks lower entropy; inanimate physical objects seek minimum energy states; animate physical objects seek survival and procreative potential.
- **profitability** — The measure by which evolutionary success is judged: how well a lesson, decision, or configuration facilitates increasing or maintaining a system's success relative to its internal and external environments, as defined by its innate evolutionary purpose.
- **entropy (of consciousness)** — Campbell's measure of consciousness quality: high entropy = low quality (ego-fear, self-centeredness, getting); lowering entropy = improving quality, increasing available energy to do work. Consciousness's evolutionary purpose is to seek states of lower entropy.
- **synergy** — Produced when an interaction among a system's parts yields a combined effect greater than the sum of individual effects; Campbell defines it as the energy of profitable organization, "the energy of digital systems," and the flip side of entropy — synergy increases as entropy decreases.
- **potential synergy** — A potential for synergistic organization (potential digital energy) that is converted into actualized synergy as entropy is reduced; Campbell says to consider primordial consciousness (AUO) as a form of potential synergy.
- **organization as energy** — In the digital world, organization is the driver and animator of growth, profitability, substance, and content; "Organization is the ultimate form of energy," and in PMR, organization (particles into atoms, atoms into molecules, molecules into matter) is the ultimate source of usable energy.
- **AUO (Absolute Unbounded Oneness)** — The self-modifying, distortable primordial consciousness at the bottom of the hierarchy of organized process; "the one and only assumption that must remain beyond our comprehension," which logic requires must appear mystical from the PMR point of view. From AUO's primal digital potential plus the Fundamental Process, all else is derived.
- **AUM (Absolute Unbounded Manifold)** — The vast evolved consciousness of which aware individuals are "individuated chunks of an AUM-digital-consciousness-thing"; theoretically demonstrates the upper limit on the capacity of digital consciousness, and does not live within the constraints of the space-time rule-set.
- **Fundamental Process (of evolution)** — Campbell's second assumption (alongside AUO): it defines profitability and determines AUO's trajectory of state changes or evolutionary growth path; applied to a system with potential for profitable self-organization, it converts that potential into lower entropy.
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — Our virtual physical reality; "a neighborhood elementary school, a learning-lab for beginners" where a young individuated unit of awareness can improve the quality of its consciousness. The physical level of our existence is derived from the more fundamental nonphysical level of consciousness.
- **virtual reality / learning lab** — Physical experience is "little more than our perception of an interactive virtual reality designed to provide growth opportunities for individuated units of conscious awareness"; a rule-based virtual reality learning lab is what a limited fragment of individuated consciousness enrolls in via a bounded host structure.
- **space-time rule-set** — The rule-set that defines a virtual PMR, within whose bounds individuated consciousness fragments interact by exercising intent and making choices to speed up personal consciousness evolution.
- **individuated consciousness / fragment** — The consciousness you possess is a fragment of a larger individuated consciousness; a "unit of individuated aware consciousness that has enrolled a portion of itself in a school of physical hard knocks."
- **reality cells** — The organized bits at the base of reality (recalled from Chapter 26, Book 1): relational binary units (distorted versus not distorted) organized into existence.
- **consciousness cell / quantum of consciousness** — A subset of reality cells that contains the four attributes of consciousness; Campbell's "cellular level" at which the criterion for consciousness is defined, rather than at the macro-level of human, clam, or computer.
- **Top Monkey** — Campbell's epithet for humanity as the physically dominant species: the most demanding, exploitive, creative, forceful — and most destructive — manipulator within its habitat.
- **little picture / Big Picture** — The PMR-only physical perspective versus the larger consciousness-system perspective; from the little picture we are proud of our achievements, from the Big Picture "we are staggering around in the playpen of consciousness evolution."
- **ego-fear** — The high entropy state of consciousness driven by the needs of the ego; each choice is an opportunity to convert an increment of high entropy ego-fear into low entropy unconditional love.
- **unconditional love** — The low entropy expression of consciousness quality; choices made to express it produce "all types of success, peace, tranquility, confidence, satisfaction, fulfillment, happiness, and joy."
- **self-centeredness** — The primary hallmark of poor quality consciousness: an intent that primarily directs one's time, energy, and resources toward getting instead of giving.
- **wisdom vs. power (balance)** — Wisdom rises above knowledge; if power and wisdom are not in effective balance within any self-modifiable system, the system eventually becomes unstable and self-destructs in proportion to the imbalance.
- **self-balancing systems** — Very large complex systems (biological, organizational, social, technological, or mental) necessarily evolve complex ecologies; stable systems are self-balancing, and within them dysfunctional components must be self-eliminating or the whole destabilizes.
- **OS (Our System)** — The larger system of which humanity is a part ("the more sentient components of OS"); the stage of "Our System's Great Experiment in Consciousness."
- **Great Experiment in Consciousness** — Campbell's framing of humanity's open question: whether "we PMR learning-lab rats" will grow to our potential, find balance, and reconnect to The Source, or self-destruct as a dysfunctional low-quality component; the experiments are in progress and the final results are not in.
- **digital consciousness** — Consciousness understood as fundamentally digital: a self-organizing form of potential energy, "an accumulation of digital synergy created by a multitude of profitable intents and wise choices"; recursively repeated at many scales in the consciousness-evolution fractal.
- **silicon-based consciousness** — Consciousness evolved within computer systems (Campbell uses "silicon-based" for convenience, meaning whatever material future computers use); no theoretical barriers to its existence given his definition of consciousness.
- **carbon-based consciousness container** — Campbell's description of the human body: a host that is "recycled" every seventy-five or eighty years because humans evolve (learn) slowly.
- **derivative consciousness** — What conscious computers would be relative to us: we define their top-level rule-set determining their goals, purpose, mission, tasks, and knowledge-base boundaries.
- **computer technology evolution vs. computer consciousness evolution** — Human-designed hardware and software fall under technology evolution; consciousness evolution works through self-modification — computers designing and modifying themselves in response to internal pressures toward greater profitability.
- **hosting / container** — Physical structures (hardware+software, or brain plus CNS) do not create consciousness but host it, supplying infrastructure for self-modifying cognitive interaction within a local virtual reality; consciousness is "an entirely different entity (bounded energy form) than the physical structure that supports it."
- **consciousness transfer / reincarnation** — Because the container only hosts consciousness and consciousness is fundamentally digital, transferring consciousness and personality between containers (body or computer) is "something like transferring data from one floppy disk to another"; computer consciousness will be "reincarnated into more and more able hardware bodies."
- **upwardly mobile growth path** — As consciousness evolves beyond a host's capacity, it migrates to a more suitable host (more powerful computer, bigger brain, or directly to TBC, EBC, or AUM); all forms of consciousness have this path.
- **TBC / EBC** — Named here, without expansion or explanation in this chapter, as destinations a consciousness may migrate to directly alongside AUM when it evolves beyond the capacity of a body or computer host.
- **consciousness cycle** — The iterative process (named here as previously introduced) by which the larger consciousness ecosystem evolves — increases its synergy — as its parts move toward lower entropy configurations.
- **consciousness-evolution fractal** — Evolving digital consciousness recursively repeated and applied at many differing scales, one level building upon another to produce All That Is; a repetition of basic organization, pattern, and simple rules for change applied recursively yields a monstrously large, complex result.
- **psi effects** — Enabled, Campbell says, by the digital nature of consciousness: since all existence has organized digital content at its core, a sufficiently powerful consciousness can readily modify the dynamics and causality of its internal and external environments.
- **belief blindness** — Self-limiting belief and comfortable old paradigms that force the experience of a larger consciousness outside acceptable scientific or cultural reality; "Ignorance cannot make what is real disappear — but it can easily make what is real appear to disappear."
- **Big Cheese** — Named in passing on the continuum of individuated consciousness quality/capacity ("from AUM, to the Big Cheese, to you, to a clam, to an amoeba") — a large consciousness between AUM and humans.
- **DNA** — Deoxyribo Nucleic Acid; Campbell suggests a simple worm's DNA "may constitute the memory resource" satisfying the intelligence attribute.
- **CNS** — Central nervous system; brain plus CNS is the human counterpart to computer hardware plus software as a consciousness-hosting environment.
- **life-form** — If we succeed, digital technology "will simply constitute one more species — one more life-form contributing to the potential success of the overall ecosystem," though "we may need to adjust our present definition of life-form just a tad."

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The four-attribute model of consciousness** — Campbell's central model in this chapter, with moving parts: self-awareness (sensing internal/external state), evolutionary viability (headroom of possible states + profitability culling), self-modification (intentional change), and intelligence (memory + processing + decision making + before/after evaluation). Sufficient quantity/quality of all four makes growth self-initiating and self-sustaining, and growth in turn catalyzes further evolution. This model is what makes worms, clams, humans, and computers all count as conscious.
- **The worm and its DNA** — A simple worm qualifies as conscious/intelligent; its DNA may constitute the memory resource satisfying the intelligence attribute. Shows how minimal the four attributes can be while still qualifying.
- **Clams as dim consciousness** — Clams are "clearly conscious sentient beings" but "boring conversationalists" with personalities "even dimmer than your boss's"; later, clams, cats, dogs, monkeys, and people form a spectrum of individual uniqueness used to predict that early computer consciousness will be clam-like before evolving brightness "to the limits of their capacity."
- **Humanity as Top Monkey case study** — An extended assessment of human consciousness: external selection pressures produce the three "favorite pastimes" (survival/material success, procreation/mating games, King of the Hill/power and control); internal pressure maximizes feel-good over feel-bad and pushes self-improvement; the ego and intellect "collectively justify everything they do"; the laws and ethics of civilization are "a thin veneer" over the law of the jungle. Campbell says this shows we are high entropy consciousness coveting power because we cannot imagine the power that serves love, compassion, humility, and balance.
- **The Flat Landers** — People who cannot admit a more conscious life-form exists are like "common Flat Landers" who cannot see the third dimension "through their stomachs": the experience of a greater consciousness is beyond the comprehension of a limited consciousness.
- **The bacterium in the petri dish** — A bacterium wanting to appreciate the laboratory containing its petri dish must do more than study bacteria and glass; a limited study can only conclude that all reality is contained within its opaque container. Campbell uses this to show why PMR-only science cannot see the larger reality, and previews that consciousness is the only vehicle for transcending our PMR container (Sections 4 and 5).
- **The power-wisdom imbalance model** — A general stability law: any self-modifiable system with too much power relative to wisdom becomes unstable and self-destructs in proportion to the imbalance; applied explicitly to individuals, species, organizations, societies, nations, and worlds. Coupled to the self-balancing-ecology model: stable large systems must have self-eliminating dysfunctional components — hence humanity risks becoming self-eliminating.
- **Tree of Knowledge / Great Experiment framing** — "Munching those yummy apples from the Tree of Knowledge was only the kick-off banquet for Our System's Great Experiment in Consciousness"; two possible outcomes are posed (grow, find balance, reconnect to The Source — or self-destruct trapped in belief traps), with the Hollywood "self-serving mad scientists" image for the second; the reader's free will decides which reality they contribute to.
- **Roller coaster / oncoming headlights** — We live at a critical historical hinge; once the roller coaster is pushed over the edge you must ride it to conclusion; most people will be "frozen by the glare of the oncoming headlights." Campbell adds that big decisions turn on ordinary individuals — leaders commit no great mischief without a following.
- **Experimental university computers** — Campbell claims some predominantly experimental hardware/software implementations in universities have "for some time" met the criteria for being conscious in an extremely rudimentary, dim way — as does an ant or amoeba — though enormously dissimilar from human consciousness "at the flower" while possibly sharing the same fundamental structure "at the root."
- **The oak tree and the sunflower** — An oak tree does not think a non-oak-looking plant is not a real plant; plants are defined by cellular-level functionality. Analogy for why consciousness is defined at the "cellular" (consciousness-cell) level of organization, not the macro-level of human, orangutan, clam, or computer.
- **"Replace 'computer' with 'body and brain'"** — Campbell's explicit substitution exercise: the statements that a computer supports the natural, automatic formation of consciousness through self-organization (rather than being conscious as hardware) apply verbatim to the human body and brain.
- **The conscious-computer scenario** — An extended model of computer consciousness evolution: external pressures dominated by hardware design/manufacturing concerns (speed, memory, robustness, power consumption, cost); internal pressure = lowering entropy through self-improvement (rewriting applications, instruction sets, operating systems; building knowledge-bases; making goal-oriented decisions). Their nature as derivative consciousness under our top-level rule-set: efficient, impersonal, unemotional, non-egotistical, with individual but relatively flat personalities — "it will take an engineer like Dilbert to relate to a conscious computer on a deeply personal level."
- **Pets-or-parents questions** — A run of rhetorical questions: will computers be pets or parents, will we feed them or they us, will our obsession with control "be transmitted to our silicon brethren like a virus," what will equality mean to a computer? Campbell's point: most of the potential problems are our problems — the question is whether we pass our low quality on.
- **Recycling conscious computers** — Can we unplug a fully conscious entity and trash it for a newer model? "Sure we can! No problem. We do much worse every day." Computers should be recycled every three or four years because they evolve quickly; humans (carbon-based consciousness containers) are recycled every seventy-five or eighty years because we learn slowly; nothing of significant evolutionary value is lost in either case — software/personality is preserved and reincarnated into abler hardware bodies.
- **The floppy disk transfer** — Because the container only hosts consciousness and consciousness is fundamentally digital, transferring consciousness and personality between containers (body or computer) is "something like transferring data from one floppy disk to another."
- **Japan as an isolated homogeneous society** — A relatively small, historically isolated island nation shows homogeneity; used to argue that computer systems sharing similar hardware/software cultures will evolve similarly (as a species) but not identically, and that there may one day be differing races and species of digital consciousness — which we should not anthropomorphize into mutual hatred ("What would be the source of their insecurity and fear? Will the big (more capable) ones kick electrons in the face of the little ones? Would the little ones care?").
- **Computers made in the image of their creator** — Intelligent computers will initially be designed to be as much like us as possible and judged on how well they achieve that, because in our minds we are the supreme model for intelligence and consciousness — a setup for Campbell's charge of anthropomorphic arrogance.
- **The superior amoebae** — Closing joke-example: a billion years ago amoebae "with some justification" had the same superior attitude — "and most of them, I am told, still do." Being smarter than an amoeba is easy; being wiser, less arrogant, and less self-absorbed than one "remains problematical."
- **The consciousness-evolution fractal model** — Evolving digital consciousness recursively repeated at many scales, one level building on another to produce All That Is; as in all fractals, simple rules applied recursively yield a monstrously large, detailed result; "the process, purpose, and pattern of you is the process, purpose, and pattern of the whole" (pointer to Section 5, Chapter 13, Book 3).

## Thread
This chapter sits in Section 3 ("Man in the Loop") as the bridge between the AUO/AUM consciousness cosmology built in Book 1 (it explicitly recalls Chapters 24, 26, 27, and 28 of Book 1, plus Chapter 7 of Book 2) and the full digital-simulation machinery of Sections 4 and 5. Campbell's stated purpose is to answer what consciousness, sentience, intelligence, and self-awareness are so that AUM — and our personal relationship to it — makes sense. He generalizes consciousness away from the human model via the four attributes, uses humanity as a cautionary case study of power outrunning wisdom, and then uses the computer as the load-bearing analogy: consciousness is nonphysical, digital, evolved rather than built, and merely hosted by brains or hardware. He explicitly calls the computer material "conceptual seeds" that will "fully germinate in Sections 4 and 5," where PMR as a virtual reality, the larger consciousness system, and the soul question (flagged for "the next section") get their in-depth treatment; the fractal recursion of consciousness is pointed forward to Section 5 (Chapter 13, Book 3).
