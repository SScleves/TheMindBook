# Some Observations

## In one paragraph
This short chapter, following Section 5's introduction (Chapter 73) and its "Science, Truth, Knowledge, Reality, and You" and "Preliminaries" chapters, lays out thirteen terse, "unsupported" summary statements about the nature of PMR, NPMR, and the Reality Wide Web (RWW) that Thomas Campbell says were already established through the logical development in Sections 2, 3, and 4 of the trilogy. He frames them explicitly as a compressed review, not new argument, and tells the reader to treat them either as confirmed facts (if personal experience matches) or as working assumptions (if it doesn't), while urging a "skeptical open mind" over belief or disbelief. The observations cover the layered structure of reality (PMR as a subset of one NPMRN among many), the "netted" telepathic nature of consciousness (the RWW/LAN metaphor), how intent functions as both navigation tool and filter, and the existence of an energy body in NPMR that sustains the physical body and survives its death. The chapter functions as a compressed roadmap for the next nine chapters of the book.

## Core claims
- Thomas Campbell states that PMR (Physical Matter Reality) is "not the only, and not necessarily the primary, nor the most important reality," with each reality existing in its own calculation-space or dimension, and realities not necessarily independent of one another.
- He claims there are multiple, loosely related, weakly interacting reality systems within NPMR (Non-Physical Matter Reality), denoted NPMRN (n = 1, 2, 3...), each organized as a separate ongoing experiment in consciousness with its own objectives, protocols, conditions, timeline, and purpose (referencing "Figure 5-1: Reality Systems: The Big Picture").
- He asserts that one specific reality system, NPMRN, contains our PMR as a subset, is a "spiritual (quality of consciousness) system" experimenting with the evolutionary potential of consciousness, and also contains other reality subsets — some physical-universe-like (space-time-matter based, to whose inhabitants we would be nonphysical) and some entirely non-space-time-matter-based — each with beings following their own physics, psychology, and sociology; "Our System" (OS) is defined as PMR plus the portion of NPMRN connected to it.
- He argues the goal for each sentient being within NPMRN is to learn, grow, and evolve through free-will choices made while interacting with others, and that the quality (intent, ranging good to evil) of these choices alters the entropy of one's consciousness via a self-modifying feedback system, with personal growth required to make an entropy-reducing contribution to "the larger consciousness cycle."
- He poses (without resolving here) open questions about whether consciousness evolution converges toward a "love-like" optimal state, its opposite, chaos, or some stable equilibrium, and whether optimal growth conditions can be isolated and stabilized to optimize the consciousness cycle's efficiency.
- He claims all consciousness within NPMRN is "netted" — each entity has something like a unique URL in a giant but finite Reality Wide Web (RWW) — with PMR beings forming a further "Local Area Network (LAN)" subset, itself connected to the rest of OS and to other NPMRN residents, and (less importantly, per the text) to NPMR beyond that.
- He states an individual in PMR becomes aware of NPMR through the mind, described as "the nonphysical part of one's being" and "the doorway" to NPMR, and asserts NPMR is, "if anything, more real than PMR" because it is less constrained and more fundamental, with PMR as one of its subsets.
- He claims that once fully aware within NPMRN, an individual can access and filter the RWW using intent, which functions both as the means of navigation (requiring specific "URL" knowledge/identification of what is sought) and as a filter that manages the otherwise overwhelming flow of information; access is developed through firsthand, scientifically-filtered (repeatable, evidential) step-by-step exploration, analogous to how reading about the World Wide Web differs from interactively using it.
- He states that communication between sentient entities is telepathic, and that within a given PMR this typically operates in parallel, to some degree, with ordinary sensory communication (sight, sound, touch, smell, taste).
- He claims people in PMR who are unaware or mostly unaware of NPMR can still experience the net if connected with sufficient signal-to-noise and clear intent, producing "crosstalk or bleedthrough" typically perceived as intuition or precognitive dreams, with signal amplitude varying and most interfering noise self-generated.
- He asserts telepathic and other NPMRN-net communications are unaffected by apparent distance or volume between sender and receiver, are not limited by light speed, and cross dimensional barriers without difficulty.
- He claims beings in all PMRs within NPMRN have portions or extensions of themselves that simultaneously exist in NPMRN, that the PMR body is "essentially sustained" through its connection to this NPMRN energy body, and that manipulating energy (reorganizing bits) in NPMRN can affect the PMR body — the claimed mechanism behind effects "from mental or psychic healing to voodoo."
- He describes the NPMRN energy body as viewable from different "frequencies" (an analogy to X-ray, infrared, or ultraviolet light) revealing different aspects of itself, while apologizing to scientists/engineers for the metaphor, and states the energy body is easily manipulated by intention.
- He characterizes intent as having magnitude (intensity), frequency (tunability), and clarity (signal-to-noise ratio) as its qualities.
- He claims the NPMRN energy body does not die when the physical body dies, and can manifest another body in PMR or some other suitable reality if it needs or wants one.

## Concepts
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — the physical, space-time-matter reality we normally inhabit; per this chapter, described as merely one subset within a larger reality system (NPMRN), not necessarily primary or most important.
- **NPMR (Non-Physical Matter Reality)** — the broader nonphysical reality domain; described here as more fundamental and less constrained than PMR, accessed through the mind.
- **NPMRN** — Campbell's notation for one of multiple distinct reality systems within NPMR, where the subscript n (n = 1, 2, 3…) indexes a specific system; each is "organized as separate ongoing experiments in consciousness" with its own objectives, protocols, conditions, timeline, and purpose. NPMRN (capital N) denotes the specific system that contains our PMR.
- **Our System (OS)** — defined in this chapter as the combination of PMR and the portion of NPMRN connected to/containing it.
- **Reality Wide Web (RWW)** — Campbell's metaphor for the netted, telepathic interconnection of all consciousness within NPMRN, likened to the World Wide Web, in which each entity has something like a unique URL/web address within a "gigantic, but finite" network.
- **LAN (Local Area Network) analogy** — Campbell's term for the sub-network formed by all beings within a particular PMR, as a subset connected within the larger RWW/OS/NPMRN structure.
- **Entropy of consciousness / self-modifying feedback system** — the idea that the quality of free-will choices made in interactions with other sentient beings alters one's consciousness entropy, via a feedback loop that modifies itself based on those choices; personal growth is framed as needed to make an "entropy reducing contribution" to the larger consciousness cycle.
- **Consciousness cycle** — the larger process, referenced but not fully defined here, that individual entropy-reducing growth is said to feed into; the chapter poses open questions about its optimal end-state (love-like, opposite, chaotic, or equilibrium) and whether its efficiency can be optimized.
- **Intent** — described as the mechanism by which an aware individual navigates and filters the RWW; requires "unique knowledge and identification of the URL being sought"; also characterized as having magnitude (intensity), frequency (tunability), and clarity (signal-to-noise ratio).
- **Filter** — the function intent serves in managing/selecting from the otherwise overwhelming flow of information available on the RWW.
- **Telepathy** — the claimed default mode of communication between sentient entities, operating (within a PMR) typically in parallel with ordinary sensory communication.
- **Crosstalk / bleedthrough** — Campbell's term for signal leakage from the NPMRN net into the awareness of PMR individuals who are unaware or mostly unaware of NPMR, typically perceived as intuition or precognitive dreams; depends on signal-to-noise strength and clarity of intent, with most interfering noise self-generated by the individual.
- **Energy body** — the portion/extension of a PMR being that exists simultaneously in NPMRN; sustains the PMR physical body through its connection, can be manipulated by intention, can be "viewed" from different metaphorical "frequencies" revealing different aspects, and does not die with the physical body (able to manifest another body in PMR or another suitable reality).
- **Frequency metaphor** — Campbell's explicit analogy (with an apology to scientists and engineers) comparing viewing the energy body at different "frequencies" to viewing something under X-ray, infrared, or ultraviolet light, to reveal different aspects of the same underlying thing.
- **Calculation-space / dimension** — the terminology used for the distinct "space" each reality is said to exist within.
- **Evolutionary potential of consciousness** — the subject matter of the "experiment" that Campbell says NPMRN (the specific system containing PMR) is conducting, framed as a spiritual (quality-of-consciousness) system.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- The chapter references **"Figure 5-1: Reality Systems: The Big Picture"** as the visual support for observation 2 (multiple NPMRN systems) and observation 3 (our PMR nested within one specific NPMRN), noting the figure shows "only one small portion of the larger consciousness ecosystem." No further figure detail is given in the text itself.
- Campbell draws an explicit **analogy between the RWW and the World Wide Web (WWW)**: he argues that exploring the RWW requires the same kind of firsthand, interactive engagement that using the WWW does, as opposed to merely reading about it — used to justify why explorers "must learn their way around by exploring the RWW" via a scientific-method-like process of repeatable, evidential data collection.
- He offers **effects "from mental or psychic healing to voodoo"** as examples that follow from the claimed mechanism of manipulating energy (reorganizing bits) in NPMRN to affect the PMR body — presented as the general category of phenomena this mechanism is meant to explain, though no individual case is detailed here.
- He cites **intuition and precognitive dreams** as the typical subjective form that "crosstalk or bleedthrough" takes for PMR individuals who are unaware or only partly aware of NPMR.
