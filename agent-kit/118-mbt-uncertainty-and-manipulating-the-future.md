# Ch 47 — Uncertainty and Manipulating the Future With Intent

## In one paragraph
Campbell shifts from the AUM-level system view of previous chapters to a "more human perspective," arguing that uncertainty, constraints on consciousness, psi phenomena, and spiritual growth are so intertwined that they must be treated together. He builds the chapter around entities as constrained energy-forms: external constraints create and maintain an individuated consciousness, while self-imposed internal constraints (belief, fear, ego) raise its entropy and squander its potential. From this he derives what he names the **psi uncertainty principle**: uncertainty around an event leaves it a wider set of probable outcomes that a focused, low-ego mind can alter, whereas objectively verified events can only be changed by a "miracle" costing far more energy — which is why psi effects evaporate under scientific measurement without being fraudulent. Campbell insists this is not a cover-up but a design requirement that preserves the causal integrity of the PMR learning lab for beings not yet ready for bigger truth. Along the way he presents the three paths to consciousness quality (Knowledge, Service, Surrender), declares psi power and love to be twin artifacts of low-entropy consciousness, and closes with practical wisdom: psi ability is earned as a by-product of spiritual growth, should never be the goal, and should not be demonstrated.

## Core claims
- Campbell argues that any entity — physical mass or nonphysical consciousness — is created and maintained by constraints imposed on an energy-form; remove the constraints and the entity disintegrates. Inanimate objects' entropy tends to increase (his gloss on the second law of thermodynamics, which he calls "a natural artifact of the functionality of space-time").
- Conscious entities follow the same rules but can modify themselves with free will intent; external constraints (e.g., the PMR space-time rule-set) are imposed to create an environment facilitating evolutionary progress, but sentient entities also create internal constraints of their own.
- Campbell claims self-imposed internal constraints (belief, fear, ego, needs) are "negative digital potential energy": they cost organizational energy, increase the entropy of your consciousness, and must eventually all be removed to actualize full potential.
- Adding uncertainty and minimizing constraints within a virtual reality widens evolutionary potential, because greater freedom and more choices support more diverse outcomes.
- Entropy in a consciousness system is the unavailability of consciousness energy to do work; the primary work of consciousness is to organize itself, take charge of its own evolution, and grow. Low-entropy consciousness is powerful; the urge to help and care about others (love) is an innate property of low-entropy consciousness.
- Campbell claims the difficulty of paranormally removing a tumor depends on its "force of being" in PMR — quantitatively, the expectation value of the future event on the probable reality surface. The more firmly credible sentient beings hold and share certainty about an outcome, the "taller and denser its probability function becomes" and the harder it is to change from NPMR.
- As in quantum mechanics (his analogy), performing the measurement forces the result to pick a state compatible with PMR causality — typically the most likely state, or a random pick among equally likely ones. Once a malignancy is verified, "by definition, it takes a miracle, where before no miracle was required."
- The future is not a done deal: PMR is interactive, and free will can manipulate or strongly influence the probability of events manifesting physically — but there are rules and wisdom about the use of nonphysical energy (e.g., knowing when help becomes interference, since removing a challenge removes the opportunity).
- Campbell distinguishes his claim from "positive thinking" or faith-manifestation: those effects are real but weak, diffuse, and unreliable; he means directly and precisely altering the probable future with a focused, clear, coherent mind — like a magnifying glass or laser versus ambient sunlight.
- "Is fake" does not follow logically from "unverified"; conversely, existence is not logically dependent on physical measurement. Physically verified problems must have either a physically verifiable physical solution or a physically unverifiable nonphysical solution (per the psi uncertainty principle).
- Requiring PMR-objective proof of everything is itself a "mystical belief" (that PMR is all reality and consciousness derives from matter) — illogical, irrational, and limiting; knowledge constrained solely to PMR defines a limited perception of reality, not reality itself.
- Becoming sentient and operative in NPMR is not a new skill but an **unlearning**: the constraints are self-made (belief, ego, fear, culture), and their removal lets NPMR sentience occur spontaneously — though breaking ingrained habits is hard.
- Campbell divides spiritual growth into three converging paths matched to personality types — Knowledge, Service, Surrender — with "growth path" meaning exactly the same as decreasing the entropy of your consciousness.
- Psi effects and love are both artifacts of low-entropy consciousness: "psi-power is love-power is quality consciousness power." In the Big Picture the paranormal is perfectly normal, available to (not thrust upon) anyone who sufficiently lowers their entropy.
- The **psi uncertainty principle** makes objective measurement of psi problematical: as soon as outside observers reduce the uncertainty, the miracles diminish — and insiders can't be trusted to be objective. This is not fraud; it is by design.
- Manipulating events through PK or telepathic suggestion within existing randomness appears in PMR as luck, intuition, or statistical skew; such manipulations are "typically helpful and relatively common" and pose no causality problems as long as uncertainty hides the influence.
- The paranormal is not hidden from us; we hide from it — "your beliefs and fears... create an impenetrable smokescreen of objective obfuscation." The psi uncertainty principle's job is to maintain the integrity of the PMR learning lab.
- Overt information transfer from NPMR (e.g., blueprints for an antigravity belt) is generally forbidden; transfers must be small steps to a prepared receiver. Intuition is the permitted low-bandwidth, highly filtered channel that feeds inventors and creators.
- Psi-relevant growth sits in a positive feedback loop (bootstrapping) while physically-focused uses are damped by a negative feedback loop enforced by psi uncertainty. "Consciousness does not exist to promote physical evolution within PMR; physical evolution within PMR exists to promote consciousness."
- Psi effects, being not of PMR, cannot be forced to abide by, or explained by, PMR causality; researchers can document psi's existence (done "thousands of times by hundreds of fully credentialed scientific researchers," Campbell asserts) but not produce, reproduce, or control it — though they can easily retard it.
- Good objective protocol removes uncertainty and thereby interferes with the effect being studied; only enough uncertainty must remain "to ensure that the vast majority of PMR citizens will not have their cherished delusions forcibly perturbed."
- Psi realness must be personally experienced; publishing research is "totally useless and ineffective" for transmitting Big Truth, because believing such results conflicts with more deeply held beliefs. This learning is experiential (like a one-year-old learning to walk), not intellectual (like calculus).
- Demonstrating psi to average people accomplishes nothing important and may do harm (loss of credibility, denial, paranoia); wisdom says do not demonstrate psi effects.
- The law of psi uncertainty has the same source and scientific nature as the law of gravitation — both are natural results of the space-time rule-set, no more mystical or arbitrary than gravity.
- Psi ability should never be the end goal; it is a collateral benefit of a well-traveled path, and as capability increases, interest in wielding it decreases. Everyone must progress under their own power from wherever they are — "there are no bad seats in the house."

## Concepts
- **Psi uncertainty principle** — Campbell's coinage (formalized here) for the entanglement of uncertainty with the measurement of psi effects: the uncertainty surrounding an event allows it a larger selection of probable outcomes that may be more easily altered by a focused (low-noise, small-ego) mind with clear intent. It masks the causal mechanics and denies perfect repeatability of psi effects, maintaining the illusion of exclusive little-picture causality needed to optimize growth in the PMR learning lab. Its source is the space-time rule-set, same as gravity.
- **Psi / psi phenomena** — psychic, paranormal, or acausal phenomena (Campbell notes psi was defined earlier in Chapter 19, Book 1); natural results of a lower-entropy consciousness having more energy available to do work.
- **Constraints (external vs. internal)** — impositions on an energy-form that define and maintain an entity. External constraints individuate consciousness and (via the space-time rule-set) create the learning environment; internal constraints are self-created by sentient entities (belief, fear, ego, needs) and typically limit evolutionary potential — "negative digital potential energy."
- **Entropy (of consciousness)** — the unavailability of consciousness energy to do work; higher entropy = disorganization, fears, beliefs, dimness, self-centeredness, inability to see big pictures. Entropy reduction "is the name of the game" at all levels of the consciousness-evolution fractal.
- **Love** — how a low-entropy consciousness interacts with other entities; the innate drive to be helpful to and care about others; an artifact of low-entropy consciousness alongside psi.
- **AUM** — the evolving digital consciousness system of which we are parts; a larger consciousness organism dependent on all its parts pulling together.
- **PMR** — Physical Matter Reality; the interactive virtual reality / "learning lab" we inhabit, governed by the space-time rule-set, specifically designed to constrain psi effects to produce a better learning environment.
- **NPMR / NPMR_N** — Nonphysical Matter Reality (and its subscripted instance), from which nonphysical energy can be viewed and manipulated; where a knowledgeable individual can work with surprising effectiveness.
- **OS** — Our System; Campbell says belief traps retard the evolution of consciousness within OS, clouding psi in uncertainty.
- **Space-time rule-set** — the constraint set defining PMR; PMR physics is simply a subset of it (to be developed in Section 4). Both gravitation and psi uncertainty are its natural results.
- **Force of being** — the degree to which something (e.g., a tumor) is connected to PMR reality; quantitatively, the expectation value of the future event on the associated probable reality surface (formal definitions promised in Book 3, Chapters 7 and 11).
- **Probable reality surface** — the surface on which future events carry expectation values; interacts with psi uncertainty (Section 5) to let one affect the probability that a future possibility actualizes.
- **Miracle** — reversing the causal laws of space-time; requires much more energy than directly modifying the outcome of a still-uncertain event.
- **Belief trap** — a belief that acts as an added internal constraint limiting the capacity and function of consciousness; belief-trap circularity ("it can't be true because it can't be true") also underpins closed-minded skepticism.
- **Path of Knowledge** — the "warrior's path," requiring courage, determination, and struggle to gain spiritual knowledge and defeat the ego; suits linear Western intellect; where one learns firsthand about the larger reality and how to manipulate energy to put psi to work for spiritual growth.
- **Path of Service** — doing good deeds sincerely from the heart, not the intellect; ego transcended by focusing on others' needs (Mother Teresa his example).
- **Path of Surrender** — giving oneself up to a higher spiritual concept, being, or values; the traditional religious path transcending ego through devotion and emulation of the beloved master, teacher, guru, or god. All three paths converge, each containing the essential truths of the other two; individuals blend them to fit their personality.
- **Psycho-kinetics (PK) / telepathic suggestion** — subtle applications of focused consciousness to the uncertainty in physical events, steering them toward an outcome; appears in PMR as luck, intuition, or statistical skew.
- **Intuition** — the usual highly filtered, low-bandwidth connection between NPMR and the individual, designed to preserve psi uncertainty; feeds prepared inventors and creators small-step insights.
- **Experience packet** — an individual's current stock of PMR ideas "temporarily stored in the physical brain," with which any intuitive insight must integrate.
- **Positive / negative feedback (in consciousness development)** — borrowed from electrical engineering (snowball analogy): psi-relevant consciousness-quality growth runs on positive feedback (bootstrapping); physically focused uses of psi are damped by negative feedback enforced by psi uncertainty.
- **Big Picture / little picture** — the larger reality versus the PMR-only view; the little picture must be a subset of the Big Picture, never the reverse. Requiring Big Picture reality to be described exclusively in little-picture terms is, per Campbell, "an incredibly dumb idea."
- **Collective mind** — the socially supported PMR-only consensus; "always represents the lowest common denominator in concepts and understanding."
- **Open-minded vs. closed-minded skeptic** — the open-minded skeptic explores anything with demanding standards for converting experience into knowledge; the closed-minded skeptic is trapped by his own limits, in a self-referential Catch-22 in which bigger-picture experience is defined to be impossible.
- **Unlearning** — the removal of self-imposed constraints and habituated thought patterns; NPMR sentience "occurs spontaneously after self-imposed constraints are removed."
- **Consciousness-evolution fractal** — the repetition of the same growth pattern at every level and scale: teenagers, parents, computers (eventually), the Big Cheese, and AUM all evolve "in the same way in a thousand different forms."
- **Big Cheese** — one of the higher-level entities Campbell places on the fractal ladder of evolving consciousness (between humans/computers and AUM in his list).
- **Big Truth** — Big Picture knowledge that cannot be transferred by publication or media; it must be gained via personal firsthand experience.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The tumor-removal scenario (the chapter's central worked example)** — Campbell walks a lump through escalating stages of measurement: (1) owner and a few others mildly worried → high uncertainty in PMR, low energy needed to dissipate it from NPMR (to the nonphysical viewer the lump's nature may be perfectly clear); (2) four doctors and half the hospital residents read the CAT scan → removal energy rises; (3) biopsy confirms a fast-growing, always-deadly malignancy → the probability function grows "taller and denser," uncertainty dissipates, and now a miracle is required. He stresses that the patient's own intent and attitude has the greatest potential impact — but is often driven by fear and others' fears — and warns of being "drawn into a fatal dance of expectation"; the best time for intervention is long before "fatal" nears inevitability.
- **Quantum-measurement analogy** — as in quantum mechanics, performing the measurement forces the outcome to pick a state compatible with the PMR space-time rule-set; the most likely state is picked, or a random choice among equally probable ones. Shows why verification collapses the room for nonphysical manipulation.
- **Bricks on the highway analogy** — profitable external constraints built the highway (traffic confined to an efficient roadway); belief, fear, and ego are like scattering bricks and concrete blocks on it — dysfunctional internal constraints that reduce functionality and efficiency.
- **Sunlight / magnifying glass / laser analogy** — hope, prayer, and diffuse belief are the sun's gentle warmth through a window; focused intent is a large magnifying glass burning holes, or laser coherency versus "an ordinary red light." Same energy, different methodology; the sophisticated focusing techniques embody generations of accumulated knowledge and require skill and practice.
- **Flatland reference** — most people resemble Flatlanders criticizing the concept of a sphere because it isn't derivable from their two-dimensional science; without an open, capable mind "you are stuck, ignorant forever, in Flatland" (he points to Chapter 20, Book 1 for the book reference).
- **Dick, Jane, and Spot** — for the experienced few, My Big TOE reads as familiar and simple as a children's primer; for others it is "impenetrable as a wall of granite."
- **The analytical clutch / brain-drive aside** — a comic maintenance metaphor: the reader's slipping "analytical clutch" is caused by lack of personal high-quality data; scrape off "sticky belief residue and corrosive pseudo-knowledge" and get the condition repaired.
- **Teenagers and parents** — teens ignore parental guidance just as adults ignore outside (NPMR) guidance and "beneficent manipulation"; Campbell uses this to illustrate the fractal repetition of growth patterns: children ↔ you ↔ computers (when hardware/software allow) ↔ the Big Cheese ↔ AUM.
- **Buck Rogers antigravity belt / color TV to Alexander thought experiment** — why giant-step technology transfer from NPMR is effectively impossible: imagine describing color television or microprocessor chips to Alexander the Great or Alexander Graham Bell; even with a clear signal, the receiver lacks concepts, parts, materials, and tools. Only small-step, prepared-receiver transfers (intuition) work.
- **Snowball feedback analogy** — electrical-engineering feedback types: positive feedback = a snowball rolling down a smooth steep snowy mountainside; negative feedback = a snowball rolling up one; snowball size and snow depth = system characteristics/response, slope steepness = feedback impact. Maps to bootstrapped psi/quality growth vs. damped physical-gain uses.
- **Daycare playground analogy** — no one leaves knives, guns, power tools, or laptops on a daycare playground; the tools aren't hidden, but children must be unaware of how to access them until capable of productive use. Likewise psi tools become naturally available with growth — and most "consciousness kids," once grown, have little overt interest in them.
- **The Three Stooges "remote viewing" skit** — an extended original comic sketch: Campbell "remote views" Moe, Larry, and Curly as university scientists in lab coats trying to examine the nonphysical with a child's microscope and to prove telepathy by reading each other's minds ("I get two blanks"; "I got more nothing than you did!"). Punchlines carry his critique: "nobody can prove the nonphysical is physical, so it must not exist!", the futile hunt for a "nonphysical microscope," and Moe's warning that "Too much success will get us fired... That much truth is dangerous!" — satirizing both the category error of physically measuring the nonphysical and the career penalties of psi research.
- **Remote viewers as real-world case** — remote viewers cannot produce perfect high-resolution photographs for experimenters; there is always some uncertainty and, over an in-depth series, some inconsistency; uncertainty grows further in minds not controlling the protocol or not physically present.
- **Precognitive dream/vision example** — even a psi event without uncertainty will be objectively verifiable only by a group small enough to have no lasting societal impact.
- **Teleportation-to-China example** — demonstrating genuine psi to an average person (e.g., teleporting him to China and leaving him to come home) might change his beliefs, but beliefs are not what matters; his quality of being would be untouched, the world would not believe him, and he could suffer real harm (loss of credibility, denial, paranoia). Hence: do not demonstrate psi.
- **The dismissive-scientist mechanism** — a scientist viewing someone else's positive measured psi results who hasn't done the work can "imagine all sorts of uncertainties into the experiment" and dismiss it as sloppy science, keeping his little picture intact; even a proof beyond reasonable certainty impacts only those directly associated with the experiment, and psi researchers get relegated to the career-killing fringe.
- **Mother Teresa** — named example of the Path of Service (good deeds sincerely from the heart).
- **The evening news reality check** — watch the news or read a newspaper to gauge the general quality of human consciousness: the stories are basically the same month to month, "only the names of the victims and perpetrators are changed," a "good case... for finding the innocent guilty by association."
- **The twelve-year-old model** — thinking psi is "bullpucky" is not a failure but a developmental state, like being twelve: necessary and desirable, since you cannot be a teen before twelve or an adult before your teens; no one jumps ahead.
- **Big fish / little fish** — the biggest fish in the PMR farm pond may be among the smallest in the NPMR_N ocean, and vice versa: worldly status says nothing about consciousness quality.
- **Consciousness-ejection device** — Campbell asserts devices exist that can throw sentient consciousness into NPMR, but without an open mind and spiritual growth the person returns "a temporary stranger in a strange land" whose rational-self denies the experience — the technical solution is useless by itself.
- **Leeches quip** — "at least physicians are no longer bleeding us with leeches": his jab at scientific self-assurance across eras.

## Thread
This chapter is the pivot in Section 3 ("Man in the Loop") from system-level theory to personal application: it explicitly follows the preceding chapters on how AUM and we are related, and "ties together many of the ideas we have been talking about from a more personal perspective." It draws on machinery Campbell built earlier — entropy (the aside in Ch 24, Book 1), psi's definition (Ch 19, Book 1), Flatland (Ch 20, Book 1), the paths discussion (Ch 21, Book 1), and this book's "AUM: scientist at work" aside (Ch 9) and free-will aside (Ch 11), which he tells the reader to reread to see that PMR was designed to constrain psi. It introduces and names the psi uncertainty principle, which becomes standing equipment for the rest of the trilogy: Section 4 will develop PMR physics as a subset of the space-time rule-set, and Section 5 (plus Book 3, Chapters 7 and 11, where "force of being" and expectation values on probable reality surfaces are formalized) will show how psi uncertainty interacts with probable-reality surfaces to let intent shift which futures actualize. It also pre-answers the standard skeptical objection — why psi vanishes under measurement — before the trilogy leans harder on paranormal experience as data.
