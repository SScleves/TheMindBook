# Ch 8 — The Science of Altered States

## In one paragraph
Campbell recounts how he, Dennis, and Bob turned the study of altered states of consciousness into a hands-on laboratory science. In a lab building with a control room and three sensory-deprivation isolation chambers (one fully electromagnetically shielded), they assembled instrumentation — EEG, GSR tracker, electrostatic sensors, audio gear, a high input-impedance voltmeter — and began measuring one another. The key experimental target was a 4 Hz oscillation Bob had perceived in his body and consciousness just before exiting (and sometimes after returning to) his body during out-of-body experiences; on the instruments, that pulsation state showed up as unusually coherent, synchronized brainwaves modulated at 4 Hz, with GSR oscillating at 4 Hz too. Campbell describes their fearless "cut and try" methodology — always testing devices on themselves first — including a near-disastrous attempt to shake the pineal gland with a 250,000-volt, 4 Hz electric field. In parallel, Bob trained them to hover on the edge between waking and sleep — body asleep, mind awake — until they could willfully and repeatably enter a half dozen altered states, each of which, Campbell reports, had its own unique functionality.

## Core claims
- Campbell claims the group approached altered states with genuine scientific rigor: "We were careful about our science. Our methodology was good" — as serious, sober, and straight as university researchers working traditional problems, even if they were not cautious or conservative, even though he allows it "may seem a little like mad scientists toiling in their hilltop laboratory at the midnight hour."
- Campbell reports as a key datum Bob's perception of a 4 Hz oscillation within his body and consciousness just before exiting his body in an OOBE, and sometimes just after returning.
- Campbell claims this pulsation state was instrumentally verifiable: when a person was caught in it in the lab, EEG brainwaves from multiple electrode pairs were unusually coherent (in phase), collectively synchronized, and modulated at 4 Hz, and GSR readings oscillated at 4 Hz as well. Bob "intuitively knew" this pulsation state was a key artifact, and they set out to reproduce, capture, and hold it on demand and under their control.
- Campbell notes that out-of-body experience (also known as "astral projection") had already been seriously studied for decades — mostly around the turn of the last century — by a half dozen highly respected, well-credentialed medical and technical professionals; several of their books agreed the pineal gland was perhaps a key organ affecting the experience.
- Campbell states their experimental ethic: try anything at least once ("cut and try"), always apply unusual devices to themselves first, and only after establishing worth and safety extend to others (friends, visitors, passersby) to widen the sample — they were looking for something that would work with anybody and everybody.
- Campbell argues their fearlessness came from the risks being "totally invisible" to them — the naiveté and brash enthusiasm of youth had its advantages in the pursuit of real, verifiable, repeatable results.
- Campbell claims Bob's hypothesis that opportunity lay on the boundary between being awake and asleep proved out: by practicing hovering on that edge — body asleep, mind awake simultaneously — they developed willful repeatability of a half dozen altered states of consciousness.
- Campbell claims each mind-state had its own unique functionality — specific things you could do, abilities you had, while in that state.

## Concepts
- **altered states of consciousness** — Non-ordinary states of consciousness the group learned to define and enter with "willful repeatability"; Campbell says they established a basic competency in a half dozen of them and then explored the functionality of each. The states produced through Bob's relaxation/visualization exercises were similar to the meditation state Campbell had learned via TM.
- **OOBE (out-of-body experience)** — The experience of exiting one's body, which Bob had personally studied; also known in the older literature as "astral projection." The chapter's central experimental object, marked by the 4 Hz pulsation on exit and sometimes return.
- **astral projection** — The older scholarly name for out-of-body experience, used in the turn-of-the-century literature Campbell's literature search turned up.
- **4 Hz pulsation state** — Bob's perceived 4 Hz oscillation within body and consciousness just before exiting the body (and sometimes just after returning). Instrumented, it appeared as EEG brainwaves that were unusually coherent (in phase), collectively synchronized, and modulated at 4 Hz, with GSR also oscillating at 4 Hz. Bob considered it a key artifact; the team's goal was to capture and hold it steady, on demand, under control.
- **EEG (electroencephalograph)** — Brainwave-measuring setup Bob purchased for the lab; used with multiple pairs of electrodes to detect coherence, synchronization, and 4 Hz modulation, and to track subjects easing in and out of various brain-wave configurations.
- **GSR (Galvanic Skin Response)** — Skin-response measure; Campbell and Dennis designed and built a device to track it. In the pulsation state the GSR reading would begin oscillating at 4 Hz.
- **pineal gland** — The organ that the older astral-projection literature (Carrington & Muldoon; Fox; Crookall) suggested was "somehow involved" or "perhaps a key organ" in the out-of-body experience; target of the capacitor experiment that tried to "shake" it at 4 Hz.
- **sensory deprivation** — Design principle of the three isolation chambers: each was constructed to provide as much sensory deprivation as possible, connected to the control room by audio and measuring devices.
- **electromagnetic shielding** — Complete shielding built into one chamber so the earth's magnetic field and other stray radiation would not wash out or overpower the effects that carefully controlled electromagnetic fields might have on altered states.
- **TM** — The meditation practice Campbell had previously learned; the reference point for the states Bob's exercises produced ("similar to the meditation state I had learned to achieve with TM").
- **body asleep, mind awake** — Bob's technique of hovering on the boundary between being awake and asleep: put the body asleep and keep the mind awake simultaneously. Campbell says they eventually got good at it and could get there quickly; it was the gateway to repeatable altered states.
- **mind-state functionality** — Campbell's observation that each altered mind-state had its own unique functionality: distinct things you could do and abilities you had while in that state.
- **cut and try** — The team's mode of operation: try anything at least once, on themselves first, in fearless pursuit of honest, verifiable, repeatable results.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The lab itself** — A lab building with a control room and three isolation chambers, one with complete electromagnetic shielding (so earth's field and stray radiation would not wash out controlled electromagnetic-field effects); each chamber built for maximum sensory deprivation and connected to the control room by audio and measuring devices. Equipment: borrowed sophisticated electrostatic sensing equipment and audio signal generators (Campbell), a complete EEG setup and professional audio mixer (Bob), an exceptionally sensitive high input-impedance voltmeter (Bill Yost), and a home-built GSR tracker (Campbell and Dennis). "Solder splatters all over the floor."
- **Measuring the 4 Hz pulsation state** — "We measured Bob. He measured us." When an instrumented person was caught in the pulsation state, multiple electrode pairs showed unusually coherent, collectively synchronized brainwaves modulated at 4 Hz, and GSR oscillated at 4 Hz. Campbell presents this as instrumental corroboration of Bob's subjective pre-exit perception, and as the artifact they aimed to reproduce on demand.
- **Literature search on astral projection** — Old scholarly studies by half a dozen respected, credentialed medical and technical professionals, mostly around the turn of the last century: *The Projection of the Astral Body* by Dr. Hereward Carrington and Sylvan Muldoon (Samuel Weiser, New York, 1929), which suggested pineal-gland involvement; *Astral Projections: Out of the Body Experiences* by Oliver Fox; and *The Study and Practice of Astral Projection* by Dr. Robert Crookall (University Press, 1960) — the latter two agreeing the pineal gland was perhaps a key organ.
- **The pineal-shaking capacitor experiment** — They built a huge capacitor with 2-ft² plates to generate a uniform, strong electric field, fed roughly a 250,000 volt, 4 Hz AC signal to the plates, and Campbell stuck his head between them for about an hour, varying voltages and frequencies against different altered states, hoping for a resonant effect on his consciousness. He suddenly felt woozy, his head wobbled dangerously between the exposed metal plates, the experiment was stopped — and he had a terrible headache for about three weeks. Campbell offers it as evidence of their commitment and risk-blind fearlessness.
- **Ion and static-charge measurements** — Negative ion generators used to provide controlled backgrounds; ultra-high impedance input voltmeters used to study changing electrical potentials of a body in an altered vs. normal state; borrowed equipment measured the dynamic buildup of static charge around their heads as they eased in and out of EEG-measured brain-wave configurations.
- **Bob's training protocol** — While Campbell and Dennis worked on the lab, Bob was leading and teaching them to experience and explore the nonphysical: he led them into deep relaxation, then used visualization to focus thoughts, center attention, and let go of body and environment — producing states similar to Campbell's TM meditation state. Then the wake/sleep-boundary practice: body asleep, mind awake. Result: basic competency in willfully and repeatably establishing a half dozen altered states, followed by systematic exploration of each state's unique functionality.

## Thread
This chapter continues the autobiographical thread of Book 1, Section 1 ("Delusion or Knowledge"): having joined Bob's effort (and with his TM background from earlier chapters as the point of comparison), Campbell shows the reader that his engagement with altered states was instrumented, methodical, and repeatable — not credulous. It establishes three load-bearing pieces for the trilogy's later argument: (1) subjective altered-state phenomena can produce objective, measurable correlates (the 4 Hz EEG/GSR signature); (2) altered states can be made willfully repeatable by ordinary trained people, satisfying a basic scientific criterion; and (3) different mind-states carry different functional capabilities — a fact the coming chapters' explorations of nonphysical experience will build on. It thereby sets up the evidential ground Campbell will later ask readers to weigh when he generalizes from these experiences to his model of consciousness and reality.
