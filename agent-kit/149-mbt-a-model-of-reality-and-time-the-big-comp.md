# A Model of Reality and Time The Big Computer (TBC)

## In one paragraph
Thomas Campbell introduces TBC ("The Big Computer") as the next piece of his model of reality and time, presenting it as a small, dedicated part of AUM's computational and memory capacity that actualizes and maintains the reality of OS (Our System) and its offspring within NPMRN. He is careful to frame TBC as a metaphor rather than a literal machine — not "the big mainframe in the sky" — built from the generic functional building blocks of any computer (binary cells, memory, instruction-sets, processors, data flow, operating-system control) rather than any specific architecture. He stresses that TBC, though vast and fast, is fundamentally mundane: a huge but finite digital processor running excellent but not necessarily perfect software, with nothing mystical or magical about it. He then folds TBC back into the larger picture, arguing that AUM, EBC, TBC, PMR, and the individual person are not truly separate systems but functional differentiations of one continuous consciousness. The chapter closes by tying this model back to human purpose: exploring the reach of our understanding is part of the larger consciousness cycle of entropy reduction and our duty to become active partners in the evolution of ourselves and AUM.

## Core claims
- Campbell argues that awareness cannot grow, progress, learn, or evolve past a very rudimentary level without the organizing influence of time, because learning and growing imply a before-state and an after-state.
- He claims our discrete, quantized, time-based reality has evolved (been designed) specifically to give us an opportunity to learn and to give AUM optimized results from its gedanken experiments about optimizing the evolutionary process.
- He describes the "complex configuration of digital energy (organization) and self-organizing process we call consciousness" as forming the foundation of reality as an interactive consciousness-evolution fractal ecosystem simulation, where what is actual and what is simulated "are one and the same."
- He proposes TBC as a conceptual device: a tiny piece of AUM's computational and memory part used to actualize and maintain the reality of OS and its offspring.
- He states he evokes only those attributes of "this celestial computer... in NPMRN" that PMR computers also have, to explain consciousness, quantized time, time travel, free will, rule-sets, space-time, precognition, telepathic communication, remote viewing, ego, and love within this model.
- He argues TBC differs from a desktop computer only by size (memory), computational speed, reliability, and "reasonably good software" — nothing else.
- He suggests picturing TBC as fully netted, massively parallel arrays of clustered processors with integrated, shared, and stand-alone super-fast memory, running within a highly efficient self-evolving AUM-operating-system "beyond all imagination."
- He states that, operationally, AUM is simultaneously the operating system, the computer, and the application software — an almost infinite, living, evolving, conscious, sentient computer and operating system that develops its own software; at a minimum it inhabits, defines, and creates all the digital mind-space (reality) we have the potential to even vaguely comprehend, and "other than that, it is an inconsequential piss-ant."
- He insists TBC should not be thought of as a literal mainframe in the sky, nor as "the OS Department server within N Division," but as a metaphor for the fundamental nature of the process and functionality (computation, memory, and rules) that implements our reality.
- He claims TBC is "a real thing" that we relate to more through its process and functionality than through its construction and design, just as most people relate to their desktop computers through what they do rather than their chip-level instruction sets.
- He states the most surprising thing about TBC is that it represents "nothing mystical, no magic," just a huge but finite, fast, mundane number cruncher running excellent, but not necessarily perfect, software — and that this mundaneness is itself remarkable and interesting.
- He argues the software and operating system of TBC might be construed either as representing AUM's intent, or as the result of evolutionary pressure on a growing, experimenting AUM-consciousness-system exploring all its possible states.
- He claims TBC, dedicated to NPMRN, represents only one small part of AUM's computational capacity — other reality systems have their own computational and memory resources — yet all are interconnected because they are of one consciousness.
- He states AUM, EBC, and TBC express differing levels of digital functionality but are not, at root, separate parts; he offers the image of "a single evolving digital system serving multiple functions simultaneously."
- He argues that trying to differentiate between the larger consciousness system and oneself can be misleading, since AUM, TBC, PMR, and the individual are all manifestations of a single consciousness, each differing only by function and purpose while remaining "of one continuous consciousness — like the lumps in a sheet."
- He asserts that although the greater consciousness ecosystem is vast and extends far beyond our view, much lies within reach of our direct experience and understanding, and humans have barely taken the first step toward knowing what is within their ability to know.
- He states it is our right and duty, as part of the larger consciousness cycle of entropy reduction, to explore everything between where we are today and the far edge of our potential, becoming active participants in the Big Picture and full partners in the evolution of ourselves and AUM.

## Concepts
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — Campbell's name for a tiny piece of the computational and memory part of AUM that is used to actualize and maintain the reality of OS and its offspring; a metaphor for the process and functionality (computation, memory, rules) that implements our reality, not a literal machine.
- **AUO** — referenced as having, in earlier chapters (26-28 of Book 1), naturally evolved binary (distorted vs. non-distorted) reality cells to support organizing and exploring increasing complexity and developing awareness.
- **AUM** — the encompassing digital-consciousness system that further refined its computational and memory part to develop intention and move from dim to bright awareness; in this chapter described as simultaneously the operating system, computer, and application software — an almost infinite, living, evolving, conscious, sentient entity that develops its own software and defines/creates all the digital mind-space (reality) we can potentially comprehend.
- **NPMRN** — the reality system within which TBC operates; TBC is described as dedicated to NPMRN specifically, representing only one small part of AUM's total computational capacity (other reality systems have their own resources).
- **PMR** — Our (physical) reality, whose computers are used as the reference point: TBC is said to differ from PMR desktop computers only in size, speed, reliability, and software quality, not in fundamental building blocks.
- **OS (Our System)** — the reality that TBC actualizes and maintains, along with "its offspring."
- **EBC** — named alongside AUM and TBC as one of three terms used to express differing levels of digital functionality within the same underlying single evolving digital system; not separately defined in this chapter (construction and design discussed elsewhere, per the text, in Sections 2 and 4).
- **Reality cells (binary, distorted vs. non-distorted)** — the fundamental constituent of aware consciousness/mind that AUO evolved, referenced back to Book 1, Chapters 26-28, as the building blocks underlying this computational model.
- **Digital energy / self-organizing process (consciousness)** — Campbell's term for the complex configuration that forms the foundation of reality as an interactive consciousness-evolution fractal ecosystem simulation, in which the actual and the simulated are described as one and the same.
- **Gedanken experiments** — AUM's thought-experiments in consciousness, tracked and implemented via its computational and memory part, seeking optimized results concerning the optimization of evolutionary process; time is described as necessary for these experiments to yield learning/growth.
- **Quantized (discrete) time** — the organizing structure Campbell says our reality has evolved/been designed with, providing before- and after-states so that awareness (and any system) can learn or grow; presented as a prerequisite for progress past a rudimentary level.
- **"Lumps in a sheet" analogy** — Campbell's image for how AUM, TBC, PMR, and the individual, despite appearing as distinct entities differentiated by function and purpose, remain manifestations of one continuous consciousness.
- **Desktop-computer analogy** — the central didactic device of the chapter: TBC is to be understood via the generic functional building blocks common to any computer (binary cells, memory, instruction-sets, processors, data flow mechanics, operating-system control), the same way people relate to their own computers by function/process rather than by chip-level design.
- **"Big mainframe in the sky" / "OS Department server within N Division"** — images Campbell explicitly rejects as misreadings of TBC; he clarifies TBC is not meant literally as either.
- **Entropy reduction** — named as the larger consciousness cycle that humans play a part in, framed as the context for our right/duty to explore the reach of our potential.
- **The Big Picture** — the larger reality/context Campbell says we have a duty to become "aware and active participants in," as full partners in the evolution of ourselves and AUM.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- No new field experiments, personal out-of-body accounts, or named case studies appear in this chapter. Campbell instead uses two extended conceptual analogies as his "examples": (1) the desktop/PMR computer, repeatedly invoked to show that TBC should be understood purely in terms of generic functional building blocks (binary cells, memory, instruction sets, processors, data flow, OS control) rather than specific design or architecture — just as ordinary computer users relate to their machines through function rather than chip-level instruction sets; and (2) the explicit rejection of two literalizing images — "the big mainframe in the sky" and "the OS Department server within N Division" — used to demonstrate what TBC is NOT, reinforcing that TBC is a functional metaphor, not a physical object with a location or literal server-room existence. He also directly addresses "computer geeks in the reading audience... waving their hands in the air," inviting non-technical readers to skip ahead while he elaborates the parallel-processor/clustered-memory image for technically minded readers.

## Thread
This chapter builds directly on Section 2 (Chapters 26-28 of Book 1), where Campbell established how AUO evolved binary reality cells and how AUM refined its computational and memory functions toward intention and bright awareness; it explicitly carries that groundwork forward into Book 3's "Inner Workings" material. The chapter's stated purpose is to set up TBC as the working metaphor Campbell will use "in this model of reality" to explain a long list of topics — quantized time, time travel, free will, rule-sets, space-time, precognition, telepathic communication, remote viewing, ego, and love — signaling that subsequent chapters will apply the TBC framework to each of these in turn. It also explicitly defers TBC's "construction and design" to Sections 2 and 4, framing this chapter as concerned only with TBC's process and functionality. The closing turn — from the mechanics of TBC to the "lumps in a sheet" unity of AUM/TBC/PMR/self and the duty to participate in entropy reduction and the Big Picture — ties the computational model back to the trilogy's larger evolutionary and purpose-driven arc.
