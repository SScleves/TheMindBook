# Ch 11 — If This is Tuesday, I Must Be in Physical Matter Reality

## In one paragraph
This is a short autobiographical chapter in which Campbell recounts the period when his exploration of the "larger reality" became a continuous, integrated part of daily life rather than a lab-only activity. He describes an extreme regimen — fifteen to twenty hours a week at the lab with Dennis plus nightly practice at home, running on two or three hours of sleep for years — during which PMR (physical-matter reality) and NPMR (nonphysical-matter reality) blended into a continuum he could inhabit simultaneously. He recounts shared out-of-body adventures with his five-year-old son Eric, and the spontaneous onset of seeing "auras" or nonphysical energy patterns around living things and, to his astonishment, inanimate objects as well. The chapter culminates in Dennis's blind photograph test, in which Campbell correctly picks out a Soviet psychic researcher from a group photo by reading auras — leading Campbell to the conclusion that "conscious intent is everything — space and time are not fundamental." The chapter closes with Campbell humbled by the enormity of what remains unknown yet energized, as a physicist, to keep experimenting.

## Core claims
- Campbell claims he lived and was continuously aware, sentient, and conscious in both reality systems (PMR and NPMR) simultaneously and permanently (except when sleeping) — it stopped being a matter of leaving one reality for the other and became merely a matter of shifting and splitting his focus.
- Campbell claims both realities were "equally real, they were just different and had different functions"; only occasionally, for a few seconds, was he unsure which reality he was in until he forced himself to differentiate.
- Campbell claims that at about five years of age most children naturally and spontaneously have lots of out-of-body experiences (OOBEs), and that the typical parental reaction is to deny and discard these experiences as foolish dreams; he instead shaped and shared them with his son Eric.
- Campbell claims that time spent in altered states with a deeply relaxed body substituted for sleep — a natural nine-to-ten-hours-a-night sleeper, he got by on two or three hours per night, year after year.
- Campbell claims he could make the nonphysical light phenomena disappear and reappear by adjusting the state of his consciousness — and that years later he needed only to shift his intent.
- Campbell claims everything living has a fuzzy light around it, with strands of nonphysical "cotton candy" connecting everything to everything — he "could literally see that everything was connected."
- Campbell claims that inorganic and inanimate things (buildings, telephone poles, power-lines, a wall clock, a programmable calculator) also carry a smaller, more uniform light, structured and in motion — which forced him to throw out his initial hypothesis that the light represented "life energy."
- Campbell claims that the aura test with the Soviet photograph taught him that "conscious intent is everything — space and time are not fundamental," and that time and space are a subset of a larger reality.
- Campbell claims that other people could not see the light-foam he saw (passers-by showed no reaction to something "massive and beautiful"), i.e., the phenomenon was in his perception, tied to his state of consciousness, not in shared public sight.
- Campbell claims that after twenty-two years of continuous education he realized he had studied only one small subset of the natural world, and presents himself as a physicist applying experimentation and data-gathering (the photo test as "another data-point") to the larger reality.

## Concepts
- **PMR (physical-matter reality)** — Campbell's acronym for the ordinary physical reality of everyday life (work, family, driving a car); one of two reality systems he inhabits, with its own distinct function.
- **NPMR (nonphysical matter reality)** — Campbell's acronym for the nonphysical reality explored through altered states and out-of-body experience; the "wilds" he and Eric explored; equally real as PMR in his account, just different, with different functions.
- **OOBE (out-of-body experience)** — the experience of leaving the physical body to explore nonphysically; Campbell says it occurs frequently and spontaneously in most five-year-old children, and describes joining his son's OOBEs.
- **The larger reality** — Campbell's umbrella term for the reality that contains and exceeds PMR; he concludes here that time and space are "a subset of a larger reality."
- **Reality frame** — a term Campbell uses for each of the two reality systems (PMR and NPMR); he reports occasional momentary confusion "between reality frames" until forcing himself to differentiate.
- **Altered states** — non-ordinary states of consciousness practiced in the lab and in bed at night; with the body deeply relaxed, they substituted for most of his sleep.
- **Parallel processing** — Campbell's description of the mind's capacity to engage in NPMR and PMR at the same time (e.g., mentally working in NPMR while carrying on a conversation and driving a car or motorcycle).
- **Aura / energy body** — the fuzzy nonphysical light Campbell began seeing around living things (changing and scintillating as people talked about important things in their lives) and, in smaller more uniform form, around inanimate objects; the Soviet psychic had "a much more developed energy body — particularly around and above his head."
- **Life energy** — Campbell's initial hypothesis for what the light represented; he explicitly threw the idea out once he saw the same light around buildings, telephone poles, and wires.
- **Clairvoyance** — Campbell's term for perceiving such nonphysical information, e.g., reading auras from a photograph; he notes it was "still a new experience" whose significance he did not yet fully know.
- **Conscious intent** — the operative faculty behind the perception: focusing intent made auras "blossom up" even from a photograph; years later intent alone (no consciousness-state shift) toggled the perception. His exclamation: "Conscious intent is everything — space and time are not fundamental."
- **Reality check** — Campbell's habit of verifying which reality he was focused in (a "quick reality check indicated I was solidly focused in PMR" when the tree-foam appeared), establishing that the phenomenon appeared while awake in PMR.
- **Interconnectedness** — the visually obvious strands of nonphysical "cotton candy" connecting everything to everything; Campbell says he could literally see that everything was connected.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The work regimen** — Fifteen to twenty hours a week at the lab with Dennis, plus continued experiments in bed after coming home at two or three in the morning; roughly forty-five hours a week studying altered states on top of a fifty-hour day job and raising a family. Shows the intensity of practice Campbell says produced the later abilities, and that altered-state time replaced sleep for years.
- **Out-of-body outings with son Eric (the whale episode)** — Campbell would "go by and join" his five-year-old son's spontaneous OOBEs; exploring the oceans, their bodies "slipped easily through" a huge whale, but Eric's head bumped against each of its ribs, one after the next, frightening him slightly (they typically did not interact with surroundings); they returned immediately. Eric usually had total, clear recall and they discussed the adventures each morning. Eventually Eric "was no longer a natural" and the forays ended naturally. Campbell notes Eric now holds an advanced degree in aeronautical engineering and still clearly remembers bumping his head on the whalebones. Campbell uses this to show childhood OOBEs are natural and spontaneous, and to rebut the idea he was warping Eric — Eric "was going with or without me."
- **Living in both realities at once** — Progression from quick sequential switching between PMR and NPMR to engaging mentally in NPMR while conversing and driving a car or motorcycle; occasional few-second confusion about which reality he was in; his marvel at the mind's parallel processing. For about six months he spent more time in NPMR than PMR ("a space cadet" who "needed a keeper"), then experimented his way to an optimum balance — remaining eccentric, protected professionally by being a productive physicist with an "absent minded professor" reputation.
- **The golden-white foam over the trees** — One spring day, walking back from lunch, Campbell saw golden-white foam with the texture of cotton candy draped over park trees, connecting them into "one large luminescent mass" (reminding him of Gulf-coast cypress with glowing Spanish moss). A reality check confirmed he was solidly in PMR. He tested whether others could see it by conspicuously staring; passers-by looked and moved on unreacting, so he concluded they could not see it (a crowd should have formed). From his office he found he could make it disappear and reappear by adjusting his state of consciousness.
- **The inorganic-matter test** — Wondering about non-living things, he moved his attention to buildings, telephone poles and power-lines: all had a smaller, more uniform, close-cut off-white light; the light around power-lines was in motion and "bushier"; this falsified his "life energy" hypothesis. Wondering what he'd see around an electrical appliance, he asked himself whether indoor things would have an aura too, or whether the effect was related to sunlight; he then checked indoor/electrical objects: his wall clock's light was highly structured and in steady motion; his programmable calculator showed a finely structured complex pattern that "changed and scintillated" when he turned it on and set it to work.
- **Auras on people** — Within days, people's auras changed and scintillated as they talked about important things in their lives; a movie theater contained "rows of swirling colored forms." He could turn any or all of it on or off by shifting consciousness state — years later, by shifting intent alone.
- **Dennis's Soviet photograph test (blind test / data-point)** — Dennis dropped a group photo of five Soviets on Campbell's desk and asked which one was the man supposedly leading Soviet psychic research. With focused intent the pictured people's auras "blossomed up exactly as they did with flesh and blood people." Campbell identified the one with a much more developed energy body, particularly around and above the head — cautioning he didn't yet know what the difference meant. Dennis grinned: "That's the one." Campbell realized Dennis had known the answer and it was a test; he welcomed it as "another data-point" showing time and space to be a subset of a larger reality, since photographic paper carried aura information across space and time.
- **Dennis as contrast case** — Dennis did not have the aura-seeing experience; Campbell speculates Dennis did not immerse himself in NPMR exploration and its theory to the same extreme degree. He notes they often grew in different ways at different times but usually ended up, eventually, with similar experiences.

## Key quotes
- "PMR and NPMR seemed to blend into a continuum and I found I could live in both realities simultaneously; it was no longer a matter of leaving one and going to the other." — on the merging of the two reality frames.
- "Both were equally real, they were just different and had different functions." — his status assessment of PMR vs. NPMR.
- "I began to marvel at the mind's capacity for parallel processing." — after learning to work in NPMR while driving and conversing.
- "By now I was so used to being amazed by the larger reality that what was normally strange had become strangely normal." — on seeing the golden-white foam over the trees.
- "I discovered that I could make it disappear and reappear by adjusting the state of my consciousness." — the tree-foam under voluntary control.
- "Buildings, telephone poles, and wires with life energy? I knew I had to throw that idea out." — falsifying his own first hypothesis about the light.
- "Conscious intent is everything — space and time are not fundamental. Wow!" — his conclusion when auras appeared from a mere photograph.
- "I could literally see that everything was connected." — the interconnection strands between all things.
- "I didn't mind; actually, I was pleased, another data-point was in and I had learned something valuable and amazing about time and space being a subset of a larger reality." — his scientist's framing of Dennis's blind test.
- "I felt small, humbled by the enormity of my ignorance." — the chapter's closing register of humility before the larger reality.
- "I was energized to discover any truth that would yield to my experimentation." — the closing statement of his empirical stance.

## Thread
Chapter 11 continues the autobiographical narrative of Book 1, Section 1 ("Delusion or Knowledge"), following the earlier chapters' account of Campbell's altered-state training and lab work with Dennis. Its job in the argument is evidential and credibility-building: before presenting his Big TOE, Campbell is laying out first-person "data" — sustained dual-reality living, sleep substitution, shared OOBEs with independent recall (Eric), aura perception that survived his own attempts at disconfirmation, and a blind test with a verifiable answer (the Soviet photo). It also plants two conceptual seeds the trilogy will build on: that PMR and NPMR are co-equal reality frames with different functions, and that "conscious intent is everything — space and time are not fundamental," i.e., space-time is a subset of a larger reality. The closing humility-plus-determination sets up the continued exploration in the chapters that follow.
