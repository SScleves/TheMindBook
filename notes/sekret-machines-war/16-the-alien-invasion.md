# The Alien Invasion

## In one paragraph
This chapter argues that the UFO/UAP Phenomenon must be studied "intersectionally" — across science, technology, religion, race, and money — rather than through any single narrow lens, because that is how modern warfare itself has operated since World War II. DeLonge & Levenda trace a chain from Nazi "total war" doctrine through the atomic bomb, the Cold War, and the 1947 Roswell/Kenneth Arnold sightings, arguing these events are historically and thematically braided together. They then pivot to a long analogy: European colonization of Africa and the Americas as a preview of what an actual alien invasion of Earth might look like, arguing humanity would be undifferentiated prey regardless of race. The chapter argues humans currently have zero military intelligence on the Phenomenon (no known enemy base, technology, or intentions), making conventional warfare against it impossible, and suggests the Phenomenon may itself be a psychological warfare (PSYOP) operation — possibly one waged by a weaker, more vulnerable civilization to keep humanity off balance. It closes with the 2004 Nimitz "Tic Tac" encounter and the Ariel School (Zimbabwe) case as evidence that the Phenomenon exhibits both ghostlike unpredictability and a "well-oiled machine" sophistication rooted in a science humans do not yet understand.

## Core claims
- The authors argue TTS (To The Stars) was widely misread as promoting an "alien threat" narrative, when in fact there is equally little evidence for hostile or benevolent Aliens — the imbalance came from people wanting to see the Phenomenon as "beneficent... and nearly angelic."
- The authors argue the Phenomenon must be studied "intersectionally" (across military, scientific, technological, biological, and other disciplines) rather than through the narrow, DoD-integrated lens that currently dominates US study of it.
- The authors argue that since the 20th century, war itself is the "intersection of science, technology, religion, race and genetics, gender, and money," and that WWII was the culmination of all these factors at once (atomic weapons, Nazi racial extermination, war profiteering, Aryanized Christianity).
- The authors argue the modern national-security apparatus (and its massive budget) is a direct product of reacting first to Nazi "total war" doctrine and then to the perceived Soviet threat.
- The authors argue 1947 (Kenneth Arnold sightings, Roswell) sits at the temporal and thematic midpoint between Hiroshima/Nagasaki (1945) and the first Soviet atomic test (1949), making the birth of the modern UFO era "nearly identical" in significance to the dawn of the atomic age.
- The authors argue the US government and its intelligence organs (CIA, AFOSI) deliberately let the public conflate UFO sightings with fears of Soviet secret weapons, both to cover stealth-technology testing and to disguise their own ignorance of the true nature of the Phenomenon.
- The authors argue that the 20th century marked the first time humans invaded the Phenomenon's own territory (the skies and space) via missiles, supersonic flight, and bombs — and speculate the Phenomenon's teasing behavior (shutting down missile silos, evading fighter jets, posing for FLIR photos) is a reaction to this human incursion.
- The authors argue European colonization of Africa and the Americas is the best available real-world model for what an alien invasion and its cultural aftermath would look like — including forced religious conversion, destruction of oral/land-based spiritual traditions, and the creation of a rootless, "neither one thing nor another" post-slavery culture.
- The authors argue that because race is a social construct with no genetic basis, an alien invader would draw no distinction between human "races" — all humanity would be uniformly subject to colonization, abduction, enslavement, or genocide.
- The authors argue humanity currently possesses zero actionable intelligence on the Phenomenon (no known base, technology, supply lines, or seat of government), making any conventional preemptive or retaliatory military strategy against it impossible.
- The authors argue that in a hypothetical invasion, Earth's militaries would be reduced to the position of "guerrilla cells" waging only asymmetric warfare, drawing analogies to Vietnam, the American Revolution, and Afghanistan.
- The authors argue the Phenomenon may itself be best understood as a sophisticated PSYOP campaign targeting Earth's civilian, military, and intelligence sectors simultaneously, and float the possibility that it is waged by a weaker, more vulnerable civilization trying to keep humanity distracted and off-balance rather than pursuing aggressive countermeasures.
- The authors argue that contactee messages warning against human technological development are suspicious because historically, no such warnings accompanied earlier weapons revolutions (gunpowder, poison gas, germ warfare) — only appearing after Hiroshima/Nagasaki, in the era of "Ban the Bomb" activism, suggesting the messages may reflect human anxiety projected onto the Phenomenon rather than genuine alien communication.
- The authors argue that the pattern of "selective witnessing" (some observers see the Phenomenon, others standing beside them see nothing) is not a flaw undermining UFO testimony but a "feature" pointing to the true, still-unexplained nature of the Phenomenon.
- The authors argue the 2004 Nimitz "Tic Tac" incident is decisive real-world evidence (not hallucination) because it combined pilot testimony, radar, and FLIR video, and because the craft appeared to know classified CAP coordinates in advance — implying "something bordering on consciousness and precognition."
- The authors argue the Phenomenon's dual nature (ghostlike unpredictability plus "well-oiled machine" physical capability) is explained by a single, not-yet-understood science that has "crossed the mind-machine barrier," treating consciousness like a computer program and weaponizing quantum entanglement and wave-function collapse.
- The authors argue AI's built-in human biases could make human systems especially vulnerable to a nonterrestrial consciousness free of those same biases, which could exploit human "blind spots" as a form of advanced psychological warfare.

## Concepts
- **Intersectionality** — a term borrowed from academia; the authors use it to argue that the UAP Phenomenon must be considered across the "wide range of disciplines" that describe, categorize, and analyze it, rather than forced into one narrow category (machine, hallucination, alien, secret weapon, disinformation, angels/demons).
- **Total war** — the doctrine, attributed to WWI general and "pagan mystic" Erich Ludendorff, of mobilizing a country's entire resources and population (manufacturing, agriculture, science, technology, academia) onto a constant war footing; the authors say WWII solidified this concept and it produced the modern US national-security apparatus.
- **Weltanschauungskrieg ("worldview warfare")** — the authors' cited Nazi-era term for psychological operations, meaning war waged on a target population's worldview rather than its territory or army.
- **Mutually assured destruction (MAD)** — the Cold War strategic doctrine that the authors say became reflected in popular culture (Dr. Strangelove, Seven Days in May) as the balance of atomic terror between the US and USSR.
- **PSYOP (psychological operations)** — described as an often-ignored aspect of warfare beyond weapons and aircraft; ranges from simple propaganda leaflets to weaponizing a population's religious/cultural beliefs to "probe for vulnerable, sensitive psychological components in a target population's worldview."
- **Alien gaze** — the authors' phrase for the presumed observational/informational capability of the Others, against which even the most advanced human cryptographic and communications security is "as thin as parchment."
- **Anomalous Aerial Vehicles (AAV)** — the US Navy's official term (as used in the Nimitz incident) for what are more commonly called UFOs or UAPs.
- **ATFLIR (AN/ASQ-228 Advanced Targeting Forward-Looking Infrared)** — Raytheon's targeting/infrared sensor pod used by the F/A-18F Super Hornets to record the "FLIR video" of the Tic Tac craft.
- **CAP (Combat Air Patrol) coordinates** — highly classified, ship-computer-generated and encrypted staging-area coordinates; the authors emphasize the Tic Tac's foreknowledge of these coordinates as powerful evidence of an intelligence with precognitive or advanced-surveillance capability.
- **Selective/partial witnessing (the "feature not a bug")** — the authors' framing for cases (Ariel School; the Nimitz encounter) where only some observers among many perceive the Phenomenon; rather than discrediting testimony, the authors argue this pattern is itself diagnostic of the Phenomenon's real nature.
- **Blind men and the elephant metaphor** — extended here to argue that many great 20th-century minds (Einstein, Hawking, Bohr, Pauli, Heisenberg, plus cultural figures like Fanon, Breton, Tolstoy, Artaud, Cage, Stockhausen) each perceive only one part of "the elephant" (the underlying science/Phenomenon), which itself "keeps changing shape."
- **Sekret skrim** — the authors' coinage for the possibility that flying-saucer/bug-eyed-alien imagery is a projection screen for humanity's own anxieties about its technological future, rather than a literal external message.
- **The science of the Sekret Machine** — the book's overarching concept: a single, unified but not-yet-understood science underlying stealth/exotic craft behavior, quantum phenomena, consciousness, and the WWII-era physics/programs (Crossbow, Overcast, Alsos, Paperclip) — framed as the hidden through-line of the whole book series.
- **Fa / Ifa (Table of Ifa)** — described as a sophisticated, entirely memorized African divination/mathematical system that the authors say anticipated European binary mathematics by centuries, cited as an example of oral, non-textual African intellectual sophistication destroyed or suppressed by colonization.
- **Operation Mongoose** — named as the CIA's 1960s covert program against Castro's Cuba, cited for its contemplated PSYOP tactic of exploiting Cuban Catholicism.
- **Operation Phoenix** — named as a Vietnam War program exemplifying the weaponizing of local religious/cultural beliefs as psychological operation.
- **Race as social construct** — the authors' claim that "race" does not exist at the genetic level, used to argue an alien invader would perceive no meaningful distinction among human groups, making any invasion effectively total and indiscriminate.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **WWII as total-war culmination**: cited as the moment science, technology, religion, race/genetics, gender, and money intersected in the atomic bomb, alongside Nazi genocide of Jews, Roma, Slavs, and the disabled, and the Japanese occupation/racial policy in China — illustrating the authors' "intersectionality of war" thesis.
- **Erich Ludendorff and total war**: WWI general and self-described pagan mystic (1865–1937) credited with theorizing "total war" mobilization of an entire nation's resources, which the authors say the US national-security state inherited.
- **The Soviet atomic bomb test, August 29, 1949**: cited as four years after Hiroshima/Nagasaki (August 1945), marking a "dramatic change in global power dynamics."
- **The Day the Earth Stood Still (1951)**: analyzed as pop-culture evidence of the era's fusion of nuclear anxiety and UFO speculation — Klaatu (Michael Rennie) halts world electricity for 30 minutes to force disarmament, and Sam Jaffe's Einstein-analog character has his equations corrected by Klaatu as proof of alien superiority.
- **Einstein's historical role**: cited (not fictional) as having warned FDR about the Nazi atomic threat and having protested the US importation of Nazi scientists under Operation Paperclip.
- **1947 Kenneth Arnold sightings and the Roswell incident**: presented as the birth of the modern UFO era, occurring under jurisdiction of the 509th Bombardment Group (formerly the 509th Composite Group / Enola Gay's unit, renamed in 1946) — used to argue the UFO era and the atomic-bomb era are "nearly identical" in origin.
- **Robert H. Goddard's Roswell rocket lab (1930s)**: Goddard (1882–1945) is said to have chosen Roswell for its remoteness/security, hosted General Jimmy Doolittle (later NACA/NASA chairman) there, and had his research spied on by both Nazis and Soviets before its military value was recognized in WWII.
- **Churchill's Iron Curtain speech, March 5, 1946**: cited as marking the start of the Cold War less than a year after WWII ended.
- **Jack Parsons, William Dudley Pelley, Andrija Puharich**: named (referencing the authors' other writing) as examples of the "bizarre coincidences" linking occultism, Nazism, and paranormal/UFO belief to the American space program and Cold War military-intelligence circles; Puharich specifically is said to have believed himself in telepathic contact with extraterrestrials while advising the US military on paranormal weaponization.
- **The 1952 Washington, D.C. sightings**: cited as a flap that was "explained away... or not" and eventually faded from public attention except among "fanatics."
- **Colonization of Africa (roughly 500 years)**: extended case study — English, Spanish, French, German, Portuguese, Italian, Belgian, and Dutch powers invaded, colonized, and enslaved Africans, forcibly converting them to Christianity or Islam and suppressing oral, land-based indigenous religions; used as the authors' primary analogy for what an alien invasion's cultural aftermath could resemble.
- **Firearms and the Gatling gun in Africa**: cited as technological changes that removed the "hand-to-hand" heroic combat ethic and allowed anonymous mass killing "by a single person, without the gunner even knowing who it was he was killing."
- **The Middle Passage and New World slavery**: described in terms of cultural/spiritual uprooting, sexual exploitation ("rape and molestation"), forced separation of children from mothers, and the syncretic emergence of Afro-Caribbean religions (Vodoun, Santeria, Palo Mayombe, Candomblé) from fragmented African traditions.
- **Indigenous Americas parallel**: the Adena, Hopewell, and Woodlands cultures (Serpent Mound, Chillicothe and Moundsville earthworks) and South American civilizations (Aztec, Mayan, Incan) cited as comparably destroyed by Spanish/Portuguese/French/British/Dutch colonization; Tituba of the Salem witch trials cited as a specific historical figure — an Indigenous Arawak woman from what is now Guyana, enslaved and forced to convert.
- **CIA admission on UFO cover story**: the authors cite a (footnoted) CIA admission that it used the UFO Phenomenon as cover for its own Cold War spy-plane and stealth-fighter testing, used as direct evidence that PSYOP and UFO narratives have already historically intertwined.
- **Operation Mongoose "Cuban Christ" plan**: a contemplated CIA tactic to demonize Castro to Cuba's Catholic population via propaganda leaflets/radio, culminating in a staged fireworks display simulating Christ's appearance over Havana.
- **Proposed Baghdad hologram plan**: American forces reportedly considered (but rejected) projecting a holographic image of the Prophet Muhammad or Allah over Baghdad during the Iraq invasion — the authors call it thankfully nixed, noting it would have backfired given the Islamic prohibition on depicting the Prophet/God.
- **Oppenheimer and the Alamogordo test, July 1945**: quoted invoking the Bhagavad Gita ("Now I am become Death, the Destroyer of Worlds") as an epigraph framing the chapter's meditation on war-as-death.
- **The Ariel School case, Zimbabwe, 1994**: of 250 children present, only about 62 reported seeing a craft and "aliens," no adults saw anything, and none of the details matched perfectly across witnesses; when re-interviewed as adults years later, the children maintained their testimony — cited as the paradigm case of "selective witnessing" that the authors argue should not discredit an account.
- **The Nimitz "Tic Tac" incident, November 2004**: the Nimitz Carrier Strike Group off Southern California/Baja encountered "Anomalous Aerial Vehicles" November 10–16; on November 14 the USS Princeton (a Ticonderoga-class cruiser) detected AAVs and dispatched two F/A-18F Super Hornets; pilots (including Commander David Fravor) observed a wingless, white Tic Tac–shaped craft performing evasive, physically extreme maneuvers (dropping from 60,000 feet to near sea level, "bouncing like a Ping Pong ball"), associated with an ocean turbulence that the strike group's own submarine could not detect, recorded on ATFLIR/FLIR video; the craft was later found waiting at the Hornets' classified CAP coordinates before they arrived — cited as the decisive modern evidentiary case proving Navy-documented, instrument-and-testimony-corroborated UAP encounters.
- **Radar operator's "snowflakes" testimony**: an unnamed radar operator described AAVs "flowing down like snowflakes" on his screen during the Nimitz encounter, cited by the authors as evidence of a possible "armada" or hive-mind coordination among multiple craft.
- **Sekret Machines: Man (referenced prior volume)**: cited as the place where the authors previously discussed whether the Others might be organic, robotic, android, or cyborg — reintroduced here as newly urgent "what-ifs" for invasion scenarios.

## Thread
This chapter follows the book's broader historical through-line (WWII, Roswell, Cold War intelligence programs referenced as "previous chapter" material on Sun Tzu/Clausewitz) and pivots the series' running "science of the Sekret Machine" concept toward an explicitly military-strategic and psychological-warfare register: rather than asking what the Phenomenon is technologically, it asks what humanity's total unpreparedness for an "invasion" reveals, and reframes the entire Phenomenon as possibly itself a PSYOP. The colonization analogy sets up the book's ethical/political stakes (what would be lost, what strategies of the colonized might be relevant) as forward-looking material for how humanity might attempt to preserve itself. The Nimitz case functions as the chapter's evidentiary anchor, connecting back to earlier volumes (Sekret Machines: Man) on the organic/robotic nature question and forward to the "blind men and the elephant" framing of the Phenomenon as a single deep, cross-disciplinary science — a framing that likely recurs into the book's Afterword ("The Alien in the Panopticon") on secrecy, national security, and control.
