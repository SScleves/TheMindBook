# Of Golems and Grays

## In one paragraph
DeLonge and Levenda ask whether the humanoid beings associated with the Phenomenon are biological aliens, culturally projected liminal figures, or artificial entities comparable to angels, golems, robots, and androids. Their most specific comparison is the obscure traditional claim that angels have no knees and abductee descriptions of Small Grays with undifferentiated joints, along with shared reports of standing, passing through walls, lacking visible reproductive and digestive functions, communicating warnings, and enabling unusual pregnancies. The chapter then reverses the human–alien relation: Visitors reportedly treat people as machines to inspect, adjust, implant, and return, echoing myths in which humans themselves are made golems or worker devices. From that analogy, the authors move into documented human programs in electrical brain stimulation, remote behavior control, genetic engineering, MKULTRA, and Operation Paperclip, but label links among those programs, UFO research, MJ-12, and alien implants as questions or speculation. They close by testing hybrid claims against genetic compatibility and Garry Nolan's five-year finding that the Atacama skeleton was a deformed human child, then argue that the enduring mixture of spiritual elevation and devastation in experiencers is more complex than an ordinary nightmare.

## Core claims
- The authors explicitly connect the chapter to *Sekret Machines: Gods*, saying ancient flying objects in Ezekiel, the *Mahabharata*, and the *Enuma Elish* were associated with nearly human gods, demons, or angels who communicated wisdom, violence, or despair.
- They allow that humans may have projected their own image onto the Phenomenon throughout recorded history and suggest this projection may be a key to understanding it.
- In the Genesis analogy, humans are God's projected "image and likeness" and therefore the liminal beings between divine and earthly, while aliens remain wholly Other.
- The chapter's unusual textual comparison is that Jewish and Christian angels traditionally lack knees and do not sit, while experiencers describe Small Grays with no clear knees, elbows, ankles, wrists, musculature, or bone structure.
- Because the no-knees tradition is obscure outside religious studies, the authors regard matching lay reports as possible independent confirmation of an old observational motif; this is their inference, not a demonstrated historical transmission study.
- Angelic standing signified eternal praise and refusal to sit before God. The authors note that angels could still walk, imagining a stiff robotic gait, and had wings for longer travel.
- Angels and Grays are compared through appearing and disappearing, passing through walls, high intelligence, serenity, power, knowledge of the future, and a messenger role carrying warnings.
- Biblical pregnancies following angelic visits—Mary and Elizabeth—and the earlier "sons of God"/"daughters of men" episode are compared with abduction reports of artificial insemination.
- Neither angels nor Grays are described with reproductive organs; the authors therefore infer that any reported impregnation would require another mechanism, such as laboratories, semen collection, and artificial fertilization.
- Reported absence of food, drink, sleeping quarters, breathing, and ordinary anatomy leads one quoted audience member to say Grays sound more mechanical than biological.
- Oannes supplies another nonhuman model: a sea-arriving teacher who neither ate nor drank, wore unusual clothing, possessed a vehicle, expected attention, and appeared beyond human threat.
- Image-based alien communication is compared with the visual and sign-based thought of people born deaf. Because Small Grays are described without ears or functional mouths, the authors speculate that image transmission may be their naturally evolved communication rather than supernatural telepathy.
- Despite the high strangeness of contact, reported messages are characterized as mundane warnings of disaster rather than new technologies or scientific instruction, resembling prophetic and popular-culture warnings.
- The chapter reverses agency by saying Visitors treat humans as mechanical objects: they prod bodies and minds, tune or adjust people, release them, and retrieve them later, much as creation myths portray humans as manufactured devices.
- The golem is the key model for artificially created humanity. Traditions in the Talmud and Mesopotamian sources describe Adam as golem-like, while Prague legend activates a clay defender through a divine word inserted in its head.
- The authors call the golem's parchment "the first implant" and ask whether alleged alien implants activate or alter people rather than merely track them.
- Documented 1960s electrical-brain-stimulation work, CIA Office of Research and Development programs, remote-control electrodes, and genetic experimentation show that human agencies were pursuing behavior and gene manipulation while modern abduction narratives developed.
- Citing Jacques Vallée's idea of the Phenomenon as a control system, the authors speculate about a multidisciplinary system using physical implants and trance-inducing sightings; the proposal is explicitly framed as fantastic but discussable because human intelligence programs researched related methods.
- The suggested MJ/MK relation—Majestic-12's alleged MJ designation and CIA mind-control cryptonyms—is posed as a question. The authors do not establish that MJ-12 was authentic or that MKULTRA grew from UFO programs.
- Operation Paperclip, Hubertus Strughold's aviation-medicine group, Nazi consciousness research, and Horten flying-saucer-like prototypes are used to pose a possible nexus between behavior research and alternate propulsion, not to demonstrate one.
- Roger Leir's claimed implant removals produced no hard evidence because specialists outside his circle did not examine the objects; the chapter explicitly preserves experiencer sincerity while rejecting validation of the devices.
- The implant story may be metaphorical psychic penetration or may reflect either human attempts to copy alleged Visitor methods or human experimentation misidentified as alien.
- Alien-human hybrid claims imply very close genetic and reproductive compatibility—presumably more than 99 percent identity—raising serious questions about where the alleged aliens evolved.
- Garry Nolan's Atacama study is presented as a corrective model: five years of deep genomic work found a deformed human child, not the alien or hybrid many believers wanted, and the authors favor the evidence over ideology.
- Claims that hybrid fetuses are removed early from a human womb face additional viability and compatibility problems.
- The authors note that *Homo sapiens* and Neanderthals produced offspring and that descendants remain, showing hybridization is possible between closely related humans, not proving alien hybridization.
- Ray Kurzweil's DNA-engineering forecast supports the narrower conclusion that humans increasingly regard their own bodies as machines with software-like components open to revision.
- The authors say abduction stories may be a psychic "bleed-through" of secret government programs conducted through the 1970s, but present this as one possible argument rather than a documented causal account.
- Whitley Strieber's simultaneous sense of redemption and devastation is treated as evidence of high strangeness unlike familiar nightmares, whose emotions and imagery people can describe readily.

## Concepts
- **Projection / liminal figure** — the possibility that humans impose a nearly human face on an unknown machine or force; humans are themselves cast as God's projected image between earthly and divine.
- **Angels without knees** — the roughly fourth-century Jewish and Christian tradition that angels stand eternally and cannot sit; compared with modern descriptions of jointless Small Grays.
- **Small Grays** — reported frail humanoids with thin, uniform limbs; no obvious knees, elbows, ankles, wrists, musculature, genitals, digestive features, ears, or mouths; the chapter treats these as experiencer descriptions, not verified anatomy.
- **Merkava and hekhalot mysticism** — early Jewish ascent traditions in which a mystic rises through seven heavens and must distinguish the enthroned Metatron from God.
- **Metatron** — the transformed human Enoch, permitted to sit despite the rule that angels stand; mistaking him for a second divine power causes the mystic's fatal fall in the tradition recounted.
- **Image telepathy** — communication by transmitted images rather than spoken language, hypothesized as a natural modality for beings lacking ears or mouths.
- **Golem / homunculus** — an artificially made humanlike being serving a function; the authors use both terms for humanity treated as manufactured and programmable.
- **Implant as activation** — the proposal that a device inserted into a person may switch on or modify a capacity rather than simply identify location.
- **Electrical brain stimulation** — electrode-based research intended to influence or remotely control animal and human behavior, begun with John Lilly and continued under CIA ORD in the chapter's account.
- **Control system** — Jacques Vallée's characterization of the Phenomenon, interpreted literally here as a possible combination of implants, perception, hypnosis, and consciousness manipulation.
- **MKULTRA / mind control** — documented CIA behavior-control work involving implants, consciousness, and memory; its temporal overlap with postwar UFO reports motivates questions but does not prove a connection.
- **Majestic-12 / MJ** — alleged and possibly hoaxed UFO documents naming MJ-12; the authors speculate about its cryptonym's similarity to CIA's MK prefix.
- **Alien hybrid** — a purported human–alien fetus created through assisted reproduction; the chapter stresses that the scenario would require extreme biological similarity and early fetal viability.
- **Psychic penetration** — Ingo Swann's language for invasion of consciousness, offered as a metaphorical alternative to a literal physical implant.
- **High strangeness** — here, a simultaneous experience of spiritual elevation and emotional devastation for which ordinary nightmare categories seem inadequate.
- **Reverse-engineering humanity** — treating DNA, brain, body, and consciousness as components to inspect and improve; the authors ask whether humans rather than UFO machinery are the objects being reverse engineered.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **Elijah of Chelm, mid-sixteenth century** — Joshua Trachtenberg recounts a legend in which Elijah created a clay golem through the *Sefer Yezirah*, wrote God's name on its forehead, gave it life, and withheld speech. This epigraph establishes artificial animation by encoded sacred information.
- **Ancient piloted-object texts** — Ezekiel, the *Mahabharata*, and the *Enuma Elish* are named as records where flying objects accompany speaking, instructing, threatening, or despair-producing nonhumans. The authors refer back to their treatment in *Gods* rather than restating the cases.
- **Genesis "image and likeness"** — humanity is interpreted as a projection of God's mind and therefore an artificial, liminal creation; the analogy does not establish alien manufacture.
- **Genesis Rabbah, about 400 CE** — this midrash supplies one of the earliest statements that angels have no knees. Saint John Chrysostom's roughly contemporary homily on 1 Corinthians also discusses the image while denying literal angelic bones.
- **Merkava/hekhalot ascent** — after rising through seven heavens to the penultimate stage, a mystic sees Metatron seated. Because angels do not sit, an untrained observer might identify him as God; the tradition insists there are "no two powers in heaven," identifies Metatron as Enoch taken bodily upward, and says the mistaken mystic is cast down and dies.
- **Russian Orthodox Divine Liturgy** — worshippers stand through a three-hour service; churches have no pews and only a few wall chairs for elderly people. The authors ask whether this imitates standing angels around a seated God.
- **C. D. B. Bryan's Small Gray description** — *Close Encounters of the Fourth Kind* (Penguin, New York, 1996, pp. 28–29) describes thin, frail bodies with limbs of uniform diameter and no distinct knees, elbows, ankles, wrists, musculature, stomach pouch, or genitals. A quoted audience member says the description sounds like machinery rather than biology.
- **Angelic pregnancy parallels** — Mary becomes pregnant after angelic visitation; Elizabeth conceives John the Baptist despite advanced age in Luke 1:13–25; the "sons of God" and "daughters of men" were covered in *Gods*. The authors compare these stories with alleged artificial fertilization by Grays.
- **Oannes** — in the Mesopotamian story attributed through Berossus, Oannes emerges from the sea in a vehicle, dressed unusually, neither eats nor drinks, and teaches arts and sciences. His confidence that people will listen and his apparent immunity to attack are compared with abductees' helplessness before Visitors.
- **Deaf thought comparison** — hearing people are described as thinking through internal spoken language, while people born deaf think in signs and images. The authors use this as an analogy for Grays' reported image transmission in the absence of ears and mouths.
- **Prophetic warnings and *The Day the Earth Stood Still*** — contactees reportedly receive catastrophe warnings, not useful technologies. The authors compare them with Old Testament messengers and both film versions' familiar warning motif.
- **Reported human servicing** — abductees describe being poked, electrically or mechanically examined, mentally manipulated, fine-tuned, returned "to the field," and later retrieved; the authors interpret the pattern as treatment of humans as machines or marionettes.
- **Rabbi Judah Loew's Prague Golem** — sixteenth-century legend says Loew made a clay defender for the Jewish ghetto against Emperor Rudolph II's pogroms and activated it by placing a parchment bearing God's word in its head. The authors call this insertion the first implant while noting doubt about Loew's historical involvement.
- **Earlier golem traditions** — Eleazar of Worms (1165–1230) in the *Sodei Raza* provides an earlier example, and Talmud tractate Sanhedrin 38b describes Adam as created as a golem, consistent in the authors' view with Sumerian and Babylonian artificial-human traditions.
- **John Lilly and CIA ORD, 1960s** — electrical stimulation work begun with Lilly and continued under the CIA Office of Research and Development sought remote control first of animals and then humans through implanted brain electrodes; related genetic-engineering projects allegedly ran years ahead of private-sector work.
- **John Marks's 1979 report** — in *The Search for the "Manchurian Candidate"* (p. 211), a researcher says the program addressed gene manipulation and "gene splintering" in 1965, questions the wider world did not ask until 1976, and feared obedient super-soldiers and subservient society.
- **Vallée's control-system proposal** — his 1992 *Forbidden Science* journal is cited for treating the Phenomenon as a control system. The authors extend it speculatively to implants and UFOs used like hypnotic spinning disks from old science fiction or *Twilight Zone* credits.
- **MJ-12 / MKULTRA question** — the chapter asks whether the alleged MJ-12 designation was only one cryptographic step from CIA MK labels and whether MKULTRA grew from an MJ-like UFO program. The question mark and the parenthetical "hoaxed?" are material qualifications.
- **Operation Paperclip nexus** — mind/behavior programs are traced partly to Paperclip data and Hubertus Strughold's Nazi aviation-medicine team; Horten brothers' early "flying saucer" prototypes represent propulsion research. The authors ask whether Nazi consciousness and engineering projects intersected.
- **Roger Leir's claimed removals** — the California podiatric surgeon (1934–2014) said he removed alien devices from patients, but no specialist outside his circle examined them, so the chapter says no hard validating evidence resulted.
- **Ingo Swann's *Penetration* (1998)** — the artist and psychic's title supplies "penetration" as a description of mental invasion, allowing the implant account to function as metaphor for psychic manipulation.
- **Hybrid-program reproductive sequence** — alleged abductors artificially impregnate human women, create part-human/part-alien fetuses, and remove them before birth. The authors say this would require more than 99 percent genetic identity, Earth-like evolution, and unexplained early viability.
- **Atacama skeleton study, 2013–2018** — Garry Nolan and fourteen coauthors conducted five years of deep genomic analysis at Stanford and published "Whole-genome sequencing of Atacama skeleton shows novel mutations linked with dysplasia" in the peer-reviewed journal *Genome Research* in April 2018. The elongated Chilean skeleton was found to be a deformed human child, not alien or hybrid; invested believers reacted angrily.
- **Human–Neanderthal reproduction** — the chapter states that *Homo sapiens* and Neanderthals mated in prehistory and left living descendants. This is evidence only for reproduction among closely related humans.
- **Ray Kurzweil at SXSW, March 2018** — the futurist, inventor, and Google director of engineering said clinical biotechnology would transform medicine by improving DNA, characterized in the cited article as outdated software that can be reprogrammed.
- **Government-program "bleed-through"** — the authors suggest that abduction imagery may manifest secret federal and contractor programs involving psychiatrists, engineers, scientists, and US citizens through the 1970s. They offer it as an interpretive possibility, not documentary proof.
- **Whitley Strieber's paired responses** — despite painful encounters, Strieber reports both redemption/spiritual elevation and profound disturbance/emotional devastation. The authors say the combination differs from familiar chase or public-nudity nightmares and supports a distinctive high-strangeness category.
- **Cotton Mather, 1693** — *Wonders of the Invisible World* is quoted to say neighbors were genuinely tormented, murdered, and made aware of hidden matters later shown real. The epigraph supports the claim that mistaken explanations need not imply nonexistent suffering.

## Key quotes
- "...inscribing the name of God upon its forehead, and thus giving it life, but withholding the power of speech." — Trachtenberg's Elijah-of-Chelm legend.
- "Because aliens have no knees..." — the chapter's deliberately stark transition from angelology to abductee anatomy.
- "Everything you have described sounds more like machinery than biology..." — an audience response to Bryan's Gray description.
- "We may consider this the first implant." — the authors' reading of the Golem's activating parchment.
- "What if the implant is used to 'activate' a human being...?" — the alternative to a tracking-device interpretation.
- "Creating a subservient society was not out of sight." — the researcher quoted by John Marks on gene and behavior work.
- "What if his impression is literally true?" — the authors' extension of Vallée's control-system idea.
- "Presumably, genetic material of humans and 'aliens' would have to be more than ninety-nine percent identical..." — the compatibility constraint on hybrid claims.
- "Many scientists would have loved to demonstrate that the Atacama skeleton was actually an alien..." — the authors' rejection of the believer backlash.
- "...human beings can be regarded as machines that need tinkering, modification, and manipulation..." — the chapter's uncomfortable reversal.
- "The Phenomenon creates its own space and makes its own rules." — the claim that ordinary nightmare categories are insufficient.
- "Our dear neighbors are most really tormented..." — Cotton Mather's closing insistence on real suffering beneath disputed interpretation.

## Thread
Chapter 3 answers the previous chapter's final "Almost" by constructing an intermediate category between flesh-and-blood extraterrestrial and invented humanoid: an artificial messenger or control device. Ancient angelology, golem lore, and modern Gray descriptions establish the comparative image; human brain electrodes, genetic programs, and covert behavior research establish that activation and control are technologically imaginable. The authors repeatedly test their own speculative field against hard limits—Leir's objects were not independently validated, the Atacama body was human, and hybrid reproduction demands extreme compatibility—while preserving the broader question of whether human bodies and minds are being reverse engineered. This sets up Chapter 4's deeper move from manipulation of individuals to possible design of the genetic code itself.
