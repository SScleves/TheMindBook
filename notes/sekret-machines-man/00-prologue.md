# Prologue

## In one paragraph
DeLonge and Levenda introduce *Sekret Machines: Man* through former senator Harry Reid's December 16, 2017 statement that the UFO question concerns both science and national security, assigning this second volume the scientific side while reserving security for *War*. They argue that the spiritual, scientific, and security dimensions of the UFO Phenomenon cannot really be separated, but organize the trilogy that way because roughly two centuries of post-Newtonian training have compartmentalized human knowledge. The authors defend integrating contactee and abductee testimony with physical UFO/UAP evidence because they report that Garry Nolan's research finds observable biological effects and John Mack found trauma resembling PTSD. They then recast the usual claim that UFOs are technologically advanced by asking how far ahead they could be now that human genetics, physics, computing, and artificial intelligence have reached former science-fiction levels. Finally, they preview four linked lines of inquiry—genetics, consciousness, brain-computer interfaces, and telepathy—while stating that the scientific and engineering data will come from experts but the book's arguments are largely the authors' and advisers', leaving conclusions to the reader.

## Core claims
- DeLonge and Levenda take Harry Reid's formula—"science and national security"—as the organizing bridge from this volume to the next: *Man* examines scientific implications of UFOs, while *War* will examine national-security implications.
- The authors explicitly resist treating spirituality, science, and security as independent domains. They say human beings have been trained to compartmentalize knowledge during the "past two hundred years or so since Newton," reducing the chance that a genuine Renaissance Man or Woman can arise.
- They propose a multidisciplinary approach as the best available method for studying the UFO Phenomenon and claim it could advance global knowledge and security, perhaps even contribute to "a Renaissance of the human spirit."
- The authors argue that contactee and abductee reports should not be excluded as the UFO/UAP community's least credible material, because they say the reports combine physiological evidence—observable and biologically traceable effects reported by Garry Nolan and colleagues—with psychological evidence—PTSD-like trauma observed by John Mack in 1994.
- The book challenges the stock description of UFOs as technology "far in advance" of humanity's. Because human genetics, physics, computer technology, and artificial intelligence have advanced rapidly since World War II, the authors say the useful question is "advanced by how much?"
- They contrast ancient and modern interpretations of comparable superior capacities: ancient scientific records are now classified as religious, magical, or spiritual texts, whereas modern observers understand apparently superior capabilities as technology only a few levels beyond ours.
- The authors say ancient humans could not fly, communicate across long distances, or see the planets beyond Saturn, whereas humanity is now approaching capacities attributed to purported "ancient aliens"; they turn this into a question not only about the alien culture but about what humanity itself is becoming.
- They select consciousness, brain-computer interfaces, telepathy, and genetics from To The Stars Academy's proposed research areas because each could illuminate both human nature and the Phenomenon.
- On genetics, the authors preview Francis Crick's and others' proposal that RNA and/or DNA were deliberately designed and seeded on Earth, and say they will compare scientific genetic-code theories with anecdotal patterns in African and Chinese divinatory systems and ancient Egyptian and Indian religious systems.
- They describe consciousness studies as a new interdisciplinary field joining neurobiology, genetics, and quantum mechanics, and say its theories matter for both UFO/UAP interpretation and human–"alien" contact.
- The authors argue that advances in AI, robotics, and brain-computer interfaces will soon force questions about sentience and humanity. They specifically ask whether the reported UFO pilots are robots or cyborgs comparable to the androids humans may send beyond the solar system.
- They propose examining telepathy through remote-viewing research, consciousness theory, and brain-computer interfaces. Because abductees report telepathic contact during abduction or experimentation, they ask whether mental communication can be reversed and whether a microlevel, non-Newtonian mechanism could enable communication with humans or nonhuman actors.
- The authors distinguish evidence from argument: they promise that information and data will come exclusively from scientific and engineering experts, published work, and personal interviews, but say the proposed arguments will be largely their own and their advisers'. They reserve conclusions for the reader.

## Concepts
- **The Phenomenon** — the authors' umbrella term for the UFO/UAP subject in its linked physical, contact, consciousness, scientific, spiritual, and security dimensions; this volume treats those dimensions as analytically separable but fundamentally entangled.
- **Contact** — in the trilogy's framing, interaction between humanity and beings "from the heavens" or nonhuman actors. The prior volume traced cultural evidence for it; this one asks what its reported biological, psychological, and technological effects imply scientifically.
- **Compartmentalization of knowledge** — the post-Newtonian habit, said to have developed over roughly two hundred years, of separating spiritual, scientific, and security fields so thoroughly that a Renaissance-style knower has become unlikely.
- **Multidisciplinary approach** — the authors' proposed corrective: integrating disciplines and evidence types rather than isolating physical craft reports from religion, consciousness, contactee testimony, biology, or national security.
- **Contactees and abductees** — experiencers who report interaction with the nonhuman actors associated with the Phenomenon; the authors retain their reports because they say physical biological traces and PTSD-like psychological trauma make them relevant to UFO/UAP study.
- **Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)** — the form of trauma John Mack reportedly observed among abductees in 1994; here it is evidence that some psychological trauma occurred, not proof of the experiencers' interpretation of its cause.
- **Sekret machines** — the authors' name for UFOs understood as machines with capabilities beyond current human technology; the spelling emphasizes that their nature and degree of advancement remain unknown.
- **Advanced-technology gap** — the reformulated question of not merely whether UFO technology is ahead of ours, but by how much, given rapid human advances since World War II.
- **Spiritual technology** — the authors' present-day characterization of ancient Sumerian and Egyptian practices linking heavenly beings, monumental devices, divine-human contact, immortality, and altered consciousness.
- **Consciousness studies** — described as a new field combining neurobiology, genetics, and quantum mechanics, with implications for understanding both the Phenomenon and human–"alien" contact.
- **Brain-computer interface** — the research area connecting brains and machines and therefore implicating AI, robotics, cyborgs, sentience, and the possibility that reported UFO operators are manufactured rather than biological beings.
- **Artificial intelligence, robotics, and cyborgs** — converging technologies through which humans may build machines that seem sentient but lack human physical and emotional vulnerabilities; the authors use this trajectory as an analogy for possible nonhuman UFO pilots.
- **Genetics / directed seeding** — the inquiry prompted by Francis Crick's and others' proposal that RNA and/or DNA were designed and deliberately seeded on Earth, to be compared in later chapters with divinatory and religious systems.
- **Telepathy** — reported mind-to-mind communication between abductees and "aliens"; the authors ask whether remote-viewing research and a microlevel, non-Newtonian component of consciousness could make reverse communication possible.
- **Remote viewing** — the scientific research area the authors intend to bring into their treatment of telepathy and contact; no results are stated in the Prologue.
- **Non-Newtonian microlevel** — the authors' proposed level below ordinary classical description where an unknown communication component might operate; it is posed as a research question, not established as fact.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **Harry Reid's December 16, 2017 tweet (12:59 p.m.)** — on the day news spread nationwide that Tom DeLonge's To The Stars Academy of Arts & Science had facilitated release of two official Department of Defense UFO videos, former Nevada senator Reid acknowledged a secret DOD UFO-investigation program. His statement—"We don't know the answers but we have plenty of evidence to support asking the questions" and "This is about science and national security"—provides the trilogy's division of labor and warns that if America does not lead, others will.
- **The explicit trilogy plan** — the authors say *Sekret Machines: Gods* examined ancient cultural evidence for Contact, *Man* will examine scientific implications, and the third and final book, *War*, will examine national-security implications. They acknowledge this separation is methodologically artificial.
- **Ancient Sumerian and Egyptian "spiritual technology"** — the earlier volume's evidence is summarized as religious and spiritual texts about heavenly beings, civilizations built around architectural devices connected to gods and the heavens, divine-human contact, immortality, and altered states of consciousness. Sumer and Egypt are named as examples, with other cultures left unspecified here.
- **Garry Nolan and colleagues' contactee research** — the authors report that recent research suggests contactees show physical effects that can be observed and traced biologically. The Prologue gives no sample size, procedure, institution, or particular outcome beyond the claimed traceability.
- **John Mack's 1994 abductee observation** — Mack reportedly observed that abductees suffered a form of PTSD, which the authors treat as evidence of psychological trauma. They do not claim this observation by itself proves literal extraterrestrial abduction.
- **Human technological acceleration since World War II** — genetics, physics, computer technology, and AI are cited as fields that have reached levels once confined to science fiction, with no foreseeable sign of slowing; this development motivates replacing "more advanced" with the comparative question "advanced by how much?"
- **Ancient-to-modern capability comparison** — ancient people could not fly, communicate over long distances, or observe trans-Saturnian planets, so extraordinary capabilities appeared magical or otherworldly and were recorded in texts now read as religious or spiritual. Modern people possess those capacities and interpret superior performance technologically.
- **To The Stars Academy research agenda selected for this book** — from the Academy's proposed focus areas, the authors choose consciousness, brain-computer interfaces, telepathy, and genetics for investigation and for explanation of why those areas matter to the Phenomenon.
- **Francis Crick's genetic-seeding proposal** — Crick and unnamed others are reported to have proposed that RNA and/or DNA were deliberately designed and seeded onto Earth. The authors plan to place published scientific theories beside anecdotal evidence they identify in African and Chinese divination and ancient Egyptian and Indian religious systems; no validating result is claimed yet.
- **Consciousness-studies convergence** — neurobiology, genetics, and quantum mechanics are named as the disciplines being combined in the "hot new field" of consciousness studies. The authors promise to assess multiple theories for their relevance to UFO/UAPs and contact.
- **Prospective sentient machines and spacefaring androids** — the authors predict machines that seem as sentient as humans while lacking human physical and emotional vulnerabilities, and say human-created androids may pilot spacecraft beyond the solar system to distant stars. This near-future prospect is used to make robotic or cyborg "aliens" a serious hypothesis rather than a fantasy that would have sounded implausible thirty years earlier.
- **Reported telepathy during abductions** — many abductees are said to report telepathic contact with beings who abducted or experimented on them. The authors propose testing the conceptual reverse: whether humans can use mental communication techniques to reach nonhuman actors, potentially through a microlevel mechanism.

## Key quotes
- "If anyone says they have the answers, they're fooling themselves." — Harry Reid's warning against premature certainty.
- "We don't know the answers but we have plenty of evidence to support asking the questions." — Reid's evidentiary posture, emphasized by the authors.
- "This is about science and national security." — Reid's formula for the subjects of the second and third volumes.
- "...a multidisciplinary approach to the problem of the UFO Phenomenon will yield the best results..." — the authors' methodological commitment.
- "If the UFO Phenomenon represents a technology more advanced than our own, advanced by how much?" — the Prologue's reformulation of the technology question.
- "More important, what does that say about us?" — the turn from alien capacity to human development.
- "We are close to developing machines that will seem as sentient as human beings but with none of the physical or emotional vulnerabilities." — the premise behind the robot/cyborg hypothesis.
- "Is it possible for us to employ the techniques of mental communication in reverse?" — the proposed telepathy/contact research question.
- "The arguments proposed will come largely from ourselves and our advisers..." — the authors' attribution boundary between scientific data and their interpretation.
- "The conclusions will be exclusively your own." — the closing invitation to reader judgment.

## Thread
The Prologue transfers the trilogy from the ancient cultural and spiritual evidence discussed in *Gods* to the scientific questions of *Man*. It identifies the volume's evidentiary tension—physical craft data cannot simply be detached from contactee biology and abductee trauma—and establishes the human technological trajectory as a comparative instrument for thinking about the Visitors. Its four announced research lines organize what follows: genetics asks whether life's code was designed and seeded; consciousness asks what kind of thing can experience; brain-computer interfaces ask whether sentience can inhabit machines; and telepathy asks whether contact can occur outside ordinary communication. The authors also establish an important epistemic boundary for the volume: experts supply reported data, but DeLonge, Levenda, and their advisers construct much of the argument.

- **Explicit trilogy link:** The authors state that *Sekret Machines: Gods* examined ancient cultural evidence for Contact, including heavenly beings, sacred architecture, immortality, and altered consciousness; *Man* now examines scientific implications.
- **Explicit trilogy link:** The authors state that the third and final volume, *Sekret Machines: War*, will take up the national-security half of Harry Reid's formula.
- **Explicit forward links within this book:** The Prologue directly previews later treatments of genetics and deliberate seeding, consciousness theory, the brain-computer interface with AI/robotics/cyborgs, and telepathy through remote viewing.
- **Reader-side speculative connection:** The question whether human-built androids might become our own distant-space explorers provides a thematic comparison to other books about machine consciousness or posthuman identity, but the Prologue itself names no outside book or theory beyond its cited research areas.
