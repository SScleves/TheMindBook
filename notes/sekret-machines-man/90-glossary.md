# Glossary

Legend: unit pointers use each source note's frontmatter `chapter:` number (the knowledge-base unit number), which is not always the book's printed chapter number. Unit 0 is the Prologue. Section One (Genetics) = intro at unit 1 plus printed Ch. 1–5 at units 2–6. Section Two (Consciousness) = intro at unit 7 plus printed Ch. 6–10 at units 8–12. Section Three (Human-Machine Symbiosis) = intro at unit 13 plus printed Ch. 11–15 at units 14–18. Unit 19 = Appendix; unit 20 = Bibliography; unit 21 = Government Documents. All definitions are the book's own, attributed where apt; the authors are Tom DeLonge & Peter Levenda ("the authors"), writing *Sekret Machines: Man* (2018).

## Concepts

- **Advanced by how much?** — The authors' reframing of the stock claim that UFO technology is "far in advance" of ours: because human genetics, computing, and AI have reached former science-fiction levels, the useful question is the *degree* of advancement — and what that implies about what humanity is becoming. Units: 0. Related: Convergence, Singularity, sekret machine.
- **Anthropocentrism / the black box** — The human tendency to interpret the nonhuman through human motives and inherited categories, preventing thought outside familiar conceptual limits; "black box" also names consciousness as a system whose inputs and outputs are visible while its mechanism is unknown. Units: 7, 9, 13. Related: cultural projection, ultimate Other, hard problem.
- **Biological SETI** — shCherbak and Makukov's proposal (their 2013 *Icarus* "WOW! signal" paper) that positional-decimal patterns, a zero-like start/stop function, symmetries, and nucleon sums in the genetic code are statistically more consistent with deliberate construction than random evolution; the search for intelligence relocated from radio signals into genetics. Reported, not endorsed. Units: 5, 6. Related: directed panspermia, Kazakh configuration, genetic code as language.
- **Biomarkers ("the Man variable")** — Measurable neurological, cardiac, dermatological, oncological, or genetic traces that Green, Nolan, Puthoff, and colleagues reportedly investigate as physical "fingerprints" of contact — the volume's attempt to move from subjective report to instrumented evidence, with cause kept attributed to the researchers. Units: 2. Related: broad-band non-ionizing radiation, blue-shifting, Rendlesham, Cash-Landrum.
- **Blue-shifting** — Hal Puthoff's 2018 proposed mechanism whereby an engineered vacuum shifts infrared into visible light and visible light into ultraviolet or soft X-rays, explaining both a craft's brilliant luminosity and possible burns/radiation injury near a powered landed object. Units: 2. Related: biomarkers, broad-band non-ionizing radiation.
- **Convergence** — The publicized technological movement toward a machine-like endpoint (AI, robotics, human-machine integration); the authors ask whether humanity is building the very detached, sexless, machine-like beings that abductees report, and whether the Singularity would make humans "indistinguishable from the Grays." Units: 1, 14. Related: Singularity, human-machine symbiosis, cyborg.
- **Cyborg (original sense)** — Clynes and Kline's 1960 term (Manfred Clynes coined it): not a robot or android, but an *organic human* whose exogenous mechanisms and drugs are integrated with unconscious homeostatic regulation to survive beyond Earth — a "machine-derived unconscious." An artificial limb, pacemaker, or performance drug can qualify. Units: 15. Related: homeostasis, intelligence amplification, machine-derived unconscious.
- **Directed panspermia** — Crick and Orgel's proposal (presented 1971, published 1973) that terrestrial life's genetic material was deliberately seeded from elsewhere by an organic precursor intelligence; the molybdenum argument (an element rare on Earth's surface but essential to the code) is its stated support. The volume's scientific anchor for design. Units: 0, 5, 6. Related: panspermia, Biological SETI, molybdenum argument.
- **DNA consciousness** — Two distinct uses: (a) John Grandy's reported neurogenetic proposal that nucleotide/protein and quantum-scale effects give rise to cellular and higher consciousness; (b) the authors' own culminating *inference* (unit 10) that because DNA builds the neural system, "DNA must be where consciousness comes from" — explicitly their argument, not a cited result. Units: 8, 10. Related: neurogenetic correlates, radio-receiver model, genetic imperative.
- **Emergent property / strong AI** — The reductionist view that consciousness is a "secretion" of sufficiently complex neural computation, so a powerful enough computer will become conscious (the Singularity; Kurzweil). The authors' central diagnostic is that this school conflates *intelligence* with *consciousness*. Units: 11, 14. Related: intelligence-consciousness distinction, Singularity, Orch OR.
- **False-equivalence warning** — The authors' explicit self-check (unit 11) that quantum strangeness makes paranormal possibilities *discussable* but does not demonstrate them: labeling entanglement and astral projection both "spooky action at a distance" joins two labels, not two mechanisms. The key restraint on their own extrapolations. Units: 11. Related: quantum consciousness, quantum alien communication.
- **Generative adversarial network (GAN)** — Ian Goodfellow's 2014 architecture in which a generator and discriminator compete until synthetic output is indistinguishable from real data; used by the authors to show self-learning AI, but also deepfakes, fake reality, propaganda, and inherited algorithmic bias — "the first casualty of AI is trust." Units: 14. Related: deepfake, algorithmic bias, adversarial input.
- **Genetic imperative / selfish gene** — DNA's tendency to persist through reproduction, variation, and generational turnover rather than preserve any individual host, contrasted with the individual's desire for immortality; the conflict is the source of the "ghost in the sekret machine." Units: 6, 8. Related: ghost in the sekret machine, genetic immortality, immortality.
- **Ghost in the sekret machine** — The authors' recasting of the mind-body problem: the independent will that can inhibit or transcend instinct is the unplanned element in an otherwise programmed organism — the "ghost" in a machine whose "instinct" is genetic programming. Units: 6. Related: genetic imperative, instinct versus will, hard problem.
- **Hard problem of consciousness** — Chalmers's question of why physical processing is accompanied by subjective *feeling* at all, and what that felt dimension is for; the authors use it against a purely computational or neuronal account. Units: 9, 14. Related: qualia, black box, brain-as-receiver.
- **High strangeness** — Hynek's term for encounters whose emotional and physical quality exceeds ordinary danger (fear, dislocation, awe, bodily symptoms); in *Man* it also names Strieber's simultaneous redemption-and-devastation, which the authors say ordinary nightmare categories cannot hold. Units: 2, 4, 17. Related: alien abduction, experiencer, missing time.
- **Human-machine symbiosis** — Full integration in which humans gain machine efficiencies and spacefaring mobility but risk losing appetite, sexuality, vulnerability, culture, and recognizable human identity ("Human Being Rev. 2.0"); the section's governing endpoint and its warning. Units: 13, 14. Related: Convergence, cyborg, Singularity.
- **Integrated information theory (IIT)** — Tononi's model in which consciousness arises from the integration of sensory and internal information into one composite reality; used by the authors to ask whether a mute, earless, non-eating Gray would have a consciousness radically unlike, or less complete than, a human's. Units: 14. Related: sensory consciousness, plant consciousness, Gray.
- **Intelligence–consciousness distinction** — The section-three diagnostic: intelligent computation, convincing simulation, and information integration need not include subjective awareness. "Machines can be intelligent, but that is not the same thing as conscious." Units: 14, 16. Related: strong AI, ELIZA, machine Psi.
- **Intelligence amplification (IA)** — Licklider's 1960 alternative to AI *replacement*: computers and sensors extend human judgment while a human keeps the final decision (as in SAGE); the human-centered counterpart to the cyborg's machine-derived unconscious. Units: 15. Related: cyborg, SAGE, Intergalactic Computer Network.
- **Interpretation versus experience** — The volume's core epistemic distinction (via Hynek's "cardinal mistake"): an account's imagery, language, or extraterrestrial *explanation* can be culturally filtered or wrong while the underlying *event and trauma* remain real. Units: 2, 3. Related: cultural conditioning, high strangeness, biomarkers.
- **Kazakh configuration** — The authors' name for shCherbak and Makukov's claimed arithmetic/ideographic organization of the genetic code (decimal positional patterns, zero-like separators, symmetry, nucleon totals such as 666/777/888 under Gamow-derived groupings); the specific evidence behind "Biological SETI." Units: 5, 6. Related: Biological SETI, zero, Gamow arrangement.
- **Machine Psi** — The authors' conditional prediction (via Koestler's emergent-mind reasoning) that a sufficiently powerful artificial consciousness should also develop Psi; used to make "telepathic machine Grays" discussable. Not a reported result. Units: 16. Related: intelligence-consciousness distinction, Psi, false-equivalence warning.
- **Missing time** — An interval for which witnesses lack conscious memory (in the Hill case, ~2 hours and 35 miles); a recurring abduction feature the authors treat as a report to be explained, not proof of cause. Units: 2, 12, 17. Related: high strangeness, recovered memory, contextual memory.
- **Molybdenum argument** — Crick and Orgel's reasoning (as reported) that life's dependence on molybdenum — rare on Earth's surface, commoner in oceans — suggests the genetic code developed where the element was abundant, i.e., possibly off-planet. Units: 5. Related: directed panspermia, universal genetic code.
- **Orch OR (orchestrated objective reduction)** — Penrose and Hameroff's theory that discrete "objective reductions" of quantum states in neuronal microtubules produce conscious moments linked to fine-scale space-time geometry, making consciousness an intrinsic feature of the universe; the quantum school's flagship, defended after quantum-biology findings. Units: 11, 12. Related: microtubule, quantum biology, proto-consciousness.
- **Panspermia** — The possibility that life's seeds arrived from elsewhere (on a meteorite or scattered debris) without necessarily being aimed; distinguished from *directed* panspermia, which adds an intelligence and deliberate targeting. Units: 5. Related: directed panspermia, molybdenum argument.
- **Panpsychism** — The possibility that consciousness permeates the universe and is not confined to particular brains; the authors' alternative basis for reported alien telepathy (a common field to "tune" to) if the human brain is not the sole source. Units: 13, 14. Related: brain-as-receiver, universal consciousness, mental telepathy.
- **Paranormal-defense paradox** — The authors' formula for modern policy: because institutions recognize no defense against demons, witches, or UFO-related effects, they "solve" the problem by declaring the phenomena nonexistent. Units: 3. Related: reality as social construct, psychology as secular authority.
- **Psi** — The umbrella term (from Greek *psyche*) for anomalous capacities — ESP, telepathy, clairvoyance, precognition, psychokinesis, remote viewing — studied by parapsychology; naming it does not prove it, and no accepted physical theory of it exists (the 1974 CIA verdict). Units: 13, 16, 21. Related: remote viewing, STAR GATE, machine Psi.
- **Qualia** — The subjective qualities of experience (the felt quality of "red") as distinct from the measurable properties of a stimulus; the authors argue qualia are *situated* (an alien on a different world would have different qualia) and that measuring a stimulus cannot transfer what it is *like*. Units: 9, 14. Related: hard problem, alien qualia, brain-as-receiver.
- **Quantum alien communication / "tagging"** — The authors' speculation (not an Orch OR result) that human and alien microtubules could communicate via quantum entanglement, offering a mechanism for telepathy and for abductees' claims that abductors can always locate them, as though "tagged." Held in check by the false-equivalence warning. Units: 11. Related: Orch OR, entanglement, false-equivalence warning.
- **Quantum biology** — Evidence or theory that biological systems use quantum processes at ordinary temperatures — photosynthesis (widely accepted by 2014) and Rieper's 2010 DNA-entanglement proposal (entangled phonons binding the double helix) — used as the bridge that makes quantum consciousness "discussable." Units: 11. Related: Orch OR, DNA entanglement, phonon.
- **Radio-receiver model** — The recurring analogy that the brain may *receive* consciousness (as a radio receives a broadcast) rather than generate it, so brain damage impairs *access* without ending the source; the authors' device for keeping "consciousness comes from elsewhere" open against materialism. Units: 8, 9, 11. Related: brain-as-receiver, universal consciousness, panpsychism.
- **Remote viewing** — Mental acquisition and description of target information blocked by distance, shielding, or time; the operational core of the SRI/STAR GATE program (Swann, Price, McMoneagle, Puthoff, Targ), documented in declassified files that establish institutional interest, not efficacy. Units: 16, 21. Related: Psi, STAR GATE, SRI.
- **Sekret machine (in *Man*)** — UFOs understood as machines with unknown capabilities and provenance; extended in this volume to the *organism*-as-machine question — whether humans are gene-driven "survival machines," divinely "manufactured" creatures, or seeded automata (the "ghost in the sekret machine"). Units: 0, 1, 11. Related: survival machines, ghost in the sekret machine, Convergence.
- **Singularity** — The anticipated point at which machine capability and consciousness make a computer indistinguishable from a human and alter evolution; strong AI's endpoint, which the authors treat as a convergence with the reported machine-like Visitors rather than proof of their nature. Units: 11, 14. Related: strong AI, Convergence, human-machine symbiosis.
- **STAR GATE (and its lineage)** — The umbrella name for the multi-decade US remote-viewing effort (SCANATE 1970 → GONDOLA WISH → GRILL FLAME → CENTER LANE → SUN STREAK → STAR GATE 1991), run through SRI and multiple agencies; represented by four declassified records in unit 21. Units: 16, 21. Related: remote viewing, SRI, GRILL FLAME.
- **Survival machines** — Dawkins's description (from *The Selfish Gene*) of organisms as "robot vehicles blindly programmed" to preserve genes; the biological version of the volume's human-as-machine premise. Units: 1. Related: selfish gene, sekret machine, genetic imperative.
- **Symbiotic relationship** — The closing thesis of "Gray Anatomy" and "House Guests": humans and the alien possess different powers, limitations, and needs and therefore depend on each other; the reason (per the authors) the alien "cannot" simply invade. Asserted more than evidenced. Units: 17, 18. Related: organic cyborg model, failed-invasion question, ultimate Other.
- **Top-down causation** — The authors' speculative reversal (unit 9): the conventional bottom-up build of the universe from particles may be correct in mechanism but wrong in *direction* — causation may proceed top-down. Offered as a question, not a result. Units: 9. Related: universal consciousness, hard problem.
- **Ultimate Other** — The alien as the final object of human difference, anxiety, hope, and hostility; the authors argue it is socially constructed as either benevolent "space brother" or demon, and that humans mistake that projection for knowledge. Units: 13, 17. Related: cultural projection, anthropocentrism, encoded racism.

## People Index

- **David Chalmers** — Philosopher cited (via *The Conscious Mind*) for treating consciousness as fundamental and irreducible — "on the level of space, time, or mass" — and for the hard problem; the authors' main authority against a purely emergent account. Units: 9, 14.
- **Manfred Clynes / Nathan Kline** — Authors of "Cyborgs and Space" (1960); Clynes coined "cyborg." Their machine-derived homeostatic "unconscious," osmotic pumps, and hypnosis/vestibular experiments are the volume's documented cyborg history. Units: 15.
- **Francis Crick / Christof Koch** — Their 1990 neurobiological program (neural correlates, binding, awareness/attention) is the consciousness sections' strongest published framework; the authors adopt its distinctions while objecting to its exclusion of selfhood, dreams, and volition. Units: 8, 11.
- **Francis Crick / Leslie Orgel** — Authors of directed panspermia (the molybdenum argument); the volume's scientific bridge from terrestrial chemistry to deliberate seeding. Units: 5.
- **Stanislas Dehaene** — Neuroscientist whose subliminal-perception and TMS work supplies unit 12's evidence that unconscious inputs and targeted activation shape later conscious experience — with his explicit "correlation not causation" warning that the authors preserve. Units: 12.
- **John Goodfellow (Ian Goodfellow)** — Originator of the GAN (2014), the concrete AI mechanism behind the deepfake/fake-reality/"trust is the first casualty" discussion. Units: 14.
- **John K. Grandy** — Proposer of "neurogenetic correlates of consciousness" and "DNA consciousness," including claimed quantum-scale genomic effects and links to Alzheimer's; used in both the genetics and consciousness sections as reported theory. Units: 5, 8.
- **Kit Green / Garry Nolan** — Physician-analyst and geneticist who reportedly linked contact injuries (Burroughs at Rendlesham) to broad-band non-ionizing electromagnetic exposure and identified candidate biomarkers; Nolan also studied the Atacama skeleton (found human) and reported contactee immune effects. Units: 2, 4, 9.
- **Stuart Hameroff / Roger Penrose** — Authors of Orch OR, the quantum school's flagship, locating conscious moments in neuronal microtubules; Hameroff's pleasure-loop proposal extends it to reproduction and evolution. Units: 11, 12.
- **Sam Harris** — Author of the *Atheist Manifesto*, the Appendix's principal anti-religion case study; the authors criticize it for reducing violence to religion and for pairing rationality with atheism. Units: 19.
- **J. Allen Hynek** — Source of "high strangeness," the close-encounter scale, and the "cardinal mistake" of substituting interpretation for the Phenomenon — the volume's governing epistemic rule. Units: 2, 3.
- **Betty & Barney Hill** — The 1961 case that publicly established the frightening abduction narrative (missing time, medical examination), used to introduce experience-vs-interpretation and to rebut blanket "cultural contamination." Units: 2.
- **Budd Hopkins** — Painter-turned-investigator whose books (*Missing Time*, *Intruders*, *Witnessed*) helped create the modern abduction "meme"; reported consistent accounts of dispassionate non-human examination. Units: 2, 17.
- **Carl Jung / Wolfgang Pauli** — Cited for the UFO-as-mandala/collective-individuation reading and for "synchronicity" (the "acausal connecting principle"); the psychological alternative to an external-object account. Units: 12, 16, 18.
- **Ray Kurzweil** — The named proponent of strong AI and the Singularity (*The Age of Spiritual Machines*); the emergent/computational school's representative and the authors' main foil for the intelligence-vs-consciousness distinction. Units: 4, 11, 14.
- **J. C. R. Licklider** — Author of "Man-Computer Symbiosis" (1960) and the "Intergalactic Computer Network"; source of *intelligence amplification*, the human-centered counterpart to the cyborg. Units: 15.
- **John E. Mack** — Harvard psychiatrist whose ~200 abductee interviews found PTSD in otherwise healthy people; his deliberately limited conclusion ("a compelling powerful phenomenon here that I can't account for in any other way") is the volume's evidentiary keystone for real trauma. Units: 0, 2, 12, 16.
- **Harry Reid** — Former senator whose December 2017 "science and national security" statement divides the trilogy's remaining labor and charters *Man*'s scientific brief. Units: 0.
- **Hal Puthoff / Russell Targ / Ingo Swann** — The SRI remote-viewing figures behind the STAR GATE lineage; Puthoff also supplies the "blue-shifting" injury mechanism and (with Nolan) links consciousness research to the Phenomenon. Units: 2, 9, 16, 21.
- **Vladimir shCherbak / Maxim Makukov** — Kazakh researchers whose 2013 *Icarus* "WOW! signal" paper is the basis for "Biological SETI" — the claim that the genetic code carries a designed signature. Units: 5, 6.
- **Whitley Strieber** — Author of *Communion*; his sincerity, screen-memory speculation (Randolph AFB / Operation Paperclip), and simultaneous redemption/devastation are used for the experience-vs-interpretation argument and the "high strangeness" category. Units: 2, 4, 18.
- **Giulio Tononi** — Originator of integrated information theory, the authors' model for grading a sensory-poor Gray's consciousness. Units: 14.
- **Larry Vandervert / Karl Pribram** — Sources of the holonomic brain model and "holoscapes" (unit 12), the basis for the holographic-reconstruction step in the conditional contact model. Units: 12.
- **Jacques Vallée** — Cited (via Aimé Michel and the "control system" reading) as a bridge between UFO study and consciousness; the epigraph that UFO progress requires "changing man's brain" frames the neuroplasticity discussion. Units: 3, 16.
