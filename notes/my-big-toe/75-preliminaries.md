# Preliminaries

## In one paragraph
This chapter tightens up two terms Thomas Campbell says he has so far used only "in a general way": PMR (Physical-Matter Reality) and NPMR (Nonphysical-matter Reality). He argues the physical/nonphysical distinction is not absolute but relative to the observer — a "PMR-centric" convenience — and defends NPMR's reality by the same logic physicists use to accept unmeasurable-but-inferred entities like neutrinos, quarks, and wave packets. He then widens the lens: PMR and NPMR are both subsets of one reality (AUM), and the history of physics — Newton's absolute space/time giving way to Einstein's relativity, which itself calmed into a new "absolute" of space-time — is offered as a template for a further, still-unfinished series of relativities culminating in a many-leveled, evolving "consciousness-evolution process-fractal" that never bottoms out in a final absolute. The chapter closes by re-defining "sentient" and "beings, objects, and energy" identically for both PMR and NPMR, and by arguing that a generalized scientific method — rigorous but not restricted to direct measurement — is needed to take NPMR data seriously.

## Core claims
- Campbell states that PMR and NPMR are each "real physical places from the point of view of the consciousness beings that inhabit them," each operationally viable (effectively and purposefully manipulable) and each inhabited by sentient beings following their own consistent physics.
- He argues the physical/nonphysical label is observer-relative: "everything within that reality appears physical while everything outside of that reality appears nonphysical," so defining NPMR as nonphysical is "a parochial PMR-centric view."
- He claims the common (operational) definition of "physical" — what's measurable by the five senses and their instrumental extensions — already smuggles in a nonphysical camp containing both inferred-but-unmeasured entities (his example: neutrinos) and purely conceptual entities (wave packets, dark matter, strings, and also ego, love, ethics, justice).
- He argues that conceptual/inferred entities in physics are accepted as real because they are "operationally significant" (they preserve logical consistency and predictive power), not because they are directly measured — and claims mind, consciousness, and intuition qualify by the same standard.
- He states that NPMR beings, objects, and energy — and the ability to see energy forms with "the mind's eye" or communicate telepathically — are "directly inferred" the same way, and their reality/meaningfulness depends on effective use, application, interaction with measurable things, and fit with an overarching model.
- He asserts ontology is dismissed by philosophers and scientists as "beyond objective discourse," and that they are "half right" — a genuine Big Picture solution is beyond "little picture" causal logic but not therefore impossible; it requires a broader, more general view of causality.
- He claims Western culture has struggled with the "Big Picture" for "at least 2,700 years" without much success because a "materialistically-based belief system blocks genuine progress," and that the West "desperately needs a Big TOE."
- He argues a "living theory" must remain open to revision as scope grows; only theories narrow in scope can safely be "dead" (fixed) if correct, and treating uncertain ignorance as small relative to certain knowledge is "the signature assumption of fools."
- He restates, from the end of Book 2, that PMR and NPMR "are dimensions or subsets of one reality as seen from differing points of view," with no primary/fundamental reality — paralleling the absence of a primary inertial frame in relativity theory.
- He builds a five-level historical/conceptual analogy: Newton's absolute space and time → Einstein's relativity of multiple inertial frames → the (mistaken) treatment of space-time itself as newly absolute → the relativity of multiple PMRs within AUM/NPMRN → the still-higher relativity of the "interconnected relationships of the multiple levels and scales of a consciousness-evolution process-fractal."
- He claims this series of ever-more-general relativities does not terminate in a final absolute: "we don't" get to the bottom; "we only get to chase that white rabbit, not catch it."
- He warns that whenever a conceptualization of reality "rests upon the absolute, the infinite, or the perfect, one should question the underlying assumptions very closely and expect to find a belief at the core."
- He argues limitation is not deprivation but design: "our consciousness and our virtual PMR are both defined into existence by the constraints placed upon us," and omniscience "simply isn't required for you to fully achieve your present purpose."
- He redefines "sentient" (starting from a dictionary definition) as self-aware, environment-aware, and capable of deliberate choices with significant consequences, and states this applies identically in PMR and NPMR.
- He defines "beings, objects, and energy" as an exhaustive triad for both PMR and NPMR: beings are choice-makers, objects are everything else non-sentient, and energy is neither being nor object but can affect both; he adds that beings/objects/energy "sometimes have random components associated with their choices."
- He argues traditional science's rejection of NPMR data is not because NPMR phenomena are illegitimate but because the data-gathering/validation process for NPMR is "mostly incompatible" with PMR science's narrow methodology, which rests on "a mystical belief in the universality of PMR causality."
- He claims all major PMR scientific models/methods (classical mechanics, special relativity, flat-earth geometry, the "stable physical universe") have only "limited validity," and by extension so does the scientific method itself — so a generalized scientific method, still rigorous and testable, is needed to also cover medicine, psychology, sociology, ethics, cosmology, epistemology, and ontology.
- He argues the practical, PMR-viewpoint separation of physical/nonphysical mirrors the common Western mental/physical (mind/body) split — the two are "hopelessly entangled and integrated," and "PMR and NPMR are likewise entangled" — with mind, consciousness, thought, and soul falling into the nonphysical camp regardless of whether a physical basis is presumed.
- He uses the CDC's inability to run controlled experiments on deadly contagious disease (relying instead on indirect statistical inference) as a precedent for how nonphysical/subjective phenomena can be studied rigorously without violating scientific rigor.
- He tells the reader not to over-focus on precision of definitions in this and the next chapter, since "precision and clarity comes only with personal experience in the larger reality," while still requiring the reader be "scientifically... critical and skeptical," not belief- or fear-driven, or "your Big TOE will not grow."

## Concepts
- **PMR (Physical-Matter Reality)** — "The reality that your body lives in, and its properties, and laws (physics)." Includes the material universe, galaxies, solar systems, planets, and everything materially existing within them, known or unknown; is operationally viable (can be effectively/purposefully manipulated) and is inhabited by sentient beings.
- **NPMR (Nonphysical-matter Reality)** — "Everything other than PMR." The non-material (from PMR's viewpoint) superset of PMR, with its own unique properties, materials, dimensions, time, and physics, existing independently of PMR; also operationally viable and inhabited by sentient beings; may contain numerous unique local realities as subsets.
- **AUM** — named as the encompassing reality of which local realities (dimensions) are subsets; the chapter states "there is no primary physical reality" and that PMR and NPMR are both dimensions/subsets of one reality (AUM); "How can we claim completion when AUM is still learning and growing?"
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — named alongside AUM as the frame within which "a specific dimension" constitutes a local reality that appears physical from within and nonphysical from without.
- **NPMRN** — named as a "consciousness system" within which multiple PMRs and NPMRs exist as interconnected equivalent realities, one level up from the relativity of multiple space-times/multiple PMRs.
- **Operational definition of physical reality** — the common, PMR-centric convention that whatever is directly measurable by the five senses (and instruments extending them) counts as physical by definition, and everything else is labeled nonphysical.
- **Inferred entities** — things not directly measurable but accepted because measuring something else assumed to be causally related supports them; Campbell's named example is neutrinos.
- **Conceptual entities** — things "defined into existence" to preserve the consistency of the current worldview (his examples: wave packets, dark matter, strings, quarks, and also ego, love, ethics, justice); accepted as potentially physical because of their operational functionality and logical usefulness, not direct measurement.
- **Sentient** — starting from the dictionary sense ("having sense perception; conscious; experiencing sensation or feeling"), Campbell generalizes this to: self-aware, aware of one's environment, and capable of deliberate choices with significant consequences to oneself and possibly others.
- **Beings, objects, and energy** — Campbell's exhaustive triad for both PMR and NPMR: beings are the choice-makers (the "upper echelons" of sentient things, though "technically everything sentient is a being"); objects are all non-sentient things; energy is neither being nor object but has the ability to effect changes in both, and can have random components tied to choices.
- **Ontology** — glossed as "the branch of metaphysics that deals with the nature of being"; Campbell says it's not taken seriously by many philosophers/scientists because it's considered beyond objective discourse, a judgment he calls "half right."
- **Big Picture vs. little picture** — recurring framing contrasting narrow, causally-bounded ("little picture") models of reality against comprehensive ("Big Picture") models like the trilogy's own; Western techno-culture is described as trapped by a "little picture objectivity über alles delusion."
- **Big TOE** — shorthand used throughout for the trilogy's comprehensive model of reality/being; described here as something the West "desperately needs... to provide balance and stability," and as something that must keep growing ("your Big TOE will not grow" if approached only with belief or fear).
- **Relativity of perception** — the claim that "physical" and "nonphysical" have no intrinsic meaning, only meaning relative to a local, belief-based perceptual frame — explicitly likened to the relativity of inertial frames in physics ("no primary inertial frame in relativity theory").
- **Absolute vs. relative** — Campbell's recurring pattern: each historical "absolute" (Newton's space/time, then space-time itself) is eventually revealed as just the outer limit of contemporary vision, superseded by a broader relativity; he warns that any reliance on "the absolute, the infinite, or the perfect" conceals an unexamined belief.
- **Consciousness-evolution process-fractal** — the highest-order relativity described in the chapter's five-level analogy: appreciating the "interconnected relationships of the multiple levels and scales" of the evolving consciousness system we are part of, replacing "one monolithic set of hierarchical virtual realities based upon an absolute AUM."
- **Digital consciousness fractals** — phrase used to describe the "finite AUM system of evolving digital consciousness fractals" that the chapter's five-level relativity analogy builds toward.
- **Generalized scientific method** — a still-rigorous, testable method (not formally defined in this chapter, deferred to the previous chapter and Chapter 13 of Book 2) that would treat the traditional scientific method as a subset while also supporting medicine, psychology, sociology, ethics, cosmology, epistemology, and ontology, incorporating subjective input without sacrificing rigor.
- **Limited validity of scientific models** — Campbell's claim that even the most successful PMR models/methods (classical mechanics for large slow objects, special relativity for fast objects without gravity, flat-earth geometry for short distances, a "stable" universe for short times) hold only within bounded domains — and that the scientific method itself has this same bounded validity.
- **PMR-centric viewpoint** — the deliberately familiar, physical-reality-anchored angle from which this section/chapter is written "to enhance comprehension," which the reader is told to integrate with, not mistake for, their "biggest picture."
- **Indirect statistical inference** — the methodology Campbell says is used both for NPMR-like subjective/nonphysical phenomena and, by his CDC example, for PMR public-health science on diseases too dangerous to experiment on directly.
- **Living theory** — a theory that "embraces change and the possibility of new and contradictory knowledge," contrasted with "impossible to change, know-it-all dead dogma"; Campbell says broad-scope models must periodically redefine themselves or degenerate into dogma, while narrow-scope ("small") models can safely be fixed/"dead" if correct.
- **Free will choice / randomness in choice** — the claim that most significant changes between time increments in a reality are driven by beings' "interactive free will choices," while objects and energy events are highly predictable given full data, and that choices themselves sometimes carry a (usually small) random component.
- **Mind-body entanglement (PMR/NPMR entanglement)** — Campbell's claim that the practical physical/nonphysical split reflects the West's common mental/physical (mind/body) separation, even though "these two opposites are hopelessly entangled and integrated"; he states "PMR and NPMR are likewise entangled," and that mind, consciousness, thought, and soul are unmeasurable within PMR and so fall into the nonphysical camp regardless of any presumed physical basis.
- **Acausal energy/information transfer** — Campbell's example of successful operational experimentation with nonphysical phenomena unacceptable to traditional science: "energy and information transfers within NPMR or PMR that appear to the PMR observer to be acausal," studied using the same methodologies physicists use for inferred objects (neutrinos) and conceptual objects (wave packets, strings).

## Experiments, examples & case studies
This is a definitional/argumentative chapter rather than a field-report chapter, so its "concrete instances" are worked analogies and one real-world institutional example rather than personal OBE experiences:
- **Neutrinos and quarks as inferred/conceptual entities** — used repeatedly as the chapter's central analogy: physics accepted these before they were directly measurable because they preserved logical consistency and predictive power; Campbell argues NPMR beings/objects/energy, and mind/consciousness/intuition, deserve the same provisional acceptance for the same reason.
- **Wave packets, dark matter, strings** — cited alongside neutrinos/quarks as purely conceptual entities in physics, defined into existence to support a worldview, offered as further precedent for taking nonphysical (NPMR) entities seriously.
- **The dictionary definition of "sentient"** — quoted directly ("Having sense perception; conscious; experiencing sensation or feeling") and then generalized by Campbell into his own operational definition used across PMR and NPMR.
- **Newton-to-Einstein history of physics** — used as the chapter's main historical case: Newton's belief in absolute space and time; Einstein's transcendence of that absolute via special/general relativity (multiple equivalent inertial frames); then physicists after Einstein treating space-time itself as a new absolute — presented as a template/precedent for a further, still-incomplete series of relativities (multiple PMRs, then AUM/NPMRN, then the consciousness-evolution process-fractal).
- **"Eating a large box of cream filled doughnuts in one sitting"** — Campbell's self-deprecating analogy for the density of the paragraph describing five hierarchical levels of relativity theory in one go, flagged as "a rather big conceptual bite for one paragraph."
- **The "white rabbit" chase** — analogy for the never-ending series of ever-more-general relativities: "we only get to chase that white rabbit, not catch it," i.e., there is no final absolute bottom to reach.
- **"Fun-loving bacteria in the PMR Petri dish"** — recurring self-referential image for humanity's position within the larger system, used to frame the question of whether we ever reach a final, satisfying absolute (answer: no).
- **The CDC and infectious disease research** — Campbell's concrete institutional example: the CDC cannot ethically run a traditional controlled experiment with a highly contagious deadly disease, so it relies on indirect statistical inference instead — offered as precedent that rigorous science need not require direct experimental measurement, supporting his case for a generalized scientific method that can also validate NPMR-related and subjective data.
- **Acausal energy/information transfers within NPMR or PMR** — cited as the kind of nonphysical phenomenon, unacceptable to traditional science, that has nonetheless been the subject of successful operational experimentation using the same methodologies physicists apply to inferred objects like neutrinos and conceptual objects like wave packets or strings.

## Thread
Positioned early in Section 5 ("Inner Space, the Final Frontier"), as the third chapter of Book 3 following the section's introduction (Chapter 73) and its methodological preface (Chapter 74), this chapter does the definitional groundwork the section promised: tightening PMR/NPMR terminology before Book 3 moves into "OS reality mechanics" and its consequences (time travel, teleportation, telepathy, the EBC, etc., as previewed in Chapter 73). It explicitly reaches back to "the end of Book 2" for the claim that PMR and NPMR are subsets of one reality, and forward to "this section and the next chapter" for how these definitions will be used, and defers a full formal treatment of a generalized scientific method to "the previous chapter [74] and... Chapter 13, Book 2." Structurally, the chapter's real work is building the reader's tolerance for open-ended relativity (via the Newton/Einstein analogy and the "white rabbit" image) so that later, more exotic claims about NPMR mechanics can be received as extensions of an already-familiar scientific habit (accepting inferred/conceptual entities) rather than as a break from it.
