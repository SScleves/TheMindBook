# Ch 20 — Causality in Every Dimension can Potentially Transform a Mystic into a Scientist from Inner Space

## In one paragraph
Campbell argues that the scientific insistence that "everything must have an objective cause" illegitimately stretches PMR (Physical Matter Reality) causality beyond the bounded region where it applies; the fair statement is only that everything we can objectively understand *from our PMR base of knowledge* must have a cause. He builds a picture of nested causal systems: PMR's causality is a subset of NPMR's (Nonphysical Matter Reality's), NPMR's a subset of beyond-NPMR's, and so on — each larger dimension operating under a higher, more general, less restrictive causality, so that a higher dimension can interact with a lower one in ways that violate the lower one's causality (appearing "paranormal" there) while remaining perfectly lawful in its own. He introduces the parent–child model of dimensions, in which each dimension births and governs the beginnings of the dimensions it spawns, so every child reality logically requires a beginning that looks mystical from inside. The upshot, for Campbell, is that "physical vs. nonphysical" is relative to the observer, one dimension's mysticism is another's science, and a mystic could be "a scientist from a higher dimension" — or a delusional fool — the difference being testable by carefully gathering experience and tasting the pudding. He leans on Abbott's *Flatland* as the canonical illustration of why residents of a constrained reality cannot comprehend a less constrained one, and defers the origins of dimensionality itself to later sections.

## Core claims
- Campbell claims the belief that "everything must have an objective cause" is not fair or reasonable: it stretches PMR causality beyond the bounded intellectual/logical region to which it applies. The defensible limited statement is that everything we can objectively understand from our PMR base of knowledge — knowledge based on measurements made exclusively within PMR — must have an accessible cause.
- Campbell claims that what lies beyond PMR can, within the causality of its own dimension, logically violate our 3D objective causality and produce measurable PMR effects that we label "paranormal." Paranormal essentially means acausal — beyond the cause-and-effect relationships defined by our limited PMR physical science.
- Campbell claims that once PMR science's limitations are surmounted, the formerly paranormal becomes a normal part of a larger scientific understanding answering to a higher (more general) level of causality.
- Campbell claims the assumption that nothing exists beyond PMR is "a normal, self-fulfilling, self-perpetuating, illogical belief," which he intends to examine over the next four sections of My Big TOE, providing a rational alternative that more fully and consistently explains the available measured data.
- Campbell claims normal events within NPMR also obey a uniform causality — well-defined action and reaction, similar processes consistently producing similar results for all experimenters — but with a far less restricted range of possible causes. PMR and its causality is a subset of NPMR and its causality.
- Campbell claims the nesting continues upward: beyond-NPMR has its own unique, still less restrictive causality and can interact with NPMR in ways that violate NPMR's causality while maintaining its own; each larger dimension of existence supports, and is a super-set of, the next one down. Perhaps beyond-NPMR is the outermost layer, or perhaps beyond-beyond-NPMR is.
- Campbell claims that whether a reality appears physical or nonphysical is relative to the observer — merely a result of perspective with "no real significance of its own"; PMR/NPMR are for now just a useful conceptualization from the perspective of a PMR resident who has experienced no other reality.
- Campbell claims beginnings belong to, and are governed by, the causality of the next higher dimension: each dimension births and nurtures child dimensions; from the child's own local objective causal system, its beginning must appear mystical, while to the parent it is well understood and not the slightest bit mystical.
- Campbell claims any system of objective causality is insulated from other causal systems by the local logic through which it defines itself; comprehension flows only one way, from superset to subset — the subset cannot understand the superset without first becoming a member of it.
- Campbell claims each dimension of reality has rules defining its own objective science and experiences the next higher (less limited) dimension as subjective and mystical — "your mysticism may be another's science" — and the bigger-picture knowledge of a higher dimension is accessible to lower-dimensional beings only through the experience of their individual locally-subjective mind.
- Campbell claims a mystic could be a scientist from a higher dimension or a delusional fool caught in a distorted web of belief; distinguishing the two requires reading *Flatland* to grasp the problem, scientifically gathering one's own experience while raising the quality of one's consciousness, and to "taste the pudding" — and to some extent "it takes one to know one," so one may need to evolve one's consciousness before getting good at discrimination.
- Campbell claims (citing *Flatland*) that ordinary residents of a given reality can only observe and understand interactions within their own reality and the interactions of residents of realities more highly constrained than their own; residents of a more constrained reality cannot comprehend a less constrained reality because it lies beyond the limits of their normal perception.
- Campbell claims his TOE must unfold slowly and methodically, will introduce concepts that seem dubious and initially hard to fathom ("it can appear no other way"), and asks the reader to maintain open-minded skepticism until the end of Section 6 before applying personal data to verify the model.
- Campbell defers explaining what dimensionality actually is, how it is generated, where dimensions come from, and "what love, wisdom, and physics could possibly have to do with any of it" to later chapters — "the seed has been planted."

## Concepts
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — the physical reality in which the reader lives; its science is defined by measurements made exclusively within it, and its causality applies only to what can be objectively understood from that PMR base of knowledge. Campbell says PMR appears (from our viewpoint) to be "the final downhill stop for this inter-causal reality train."
- **NPMR (Nonphysical Matter Reality)** — a larger reality of which PMR and its causality is a subset. Events within NPMR obey their own uniform causality with well-defined action and reaction, but the range of possible causes is far less restricted; its rules and the interactions between NPMR beings are of a higher order (more general, less restrictive).
- **beyond-NPMR** — the next dimension of existence up from NPMR, with its own unique causality answering to yet higher, less restrictive rules; it can interact with NPMR in ways that violate NPMR's causality while maintaining its own. Campbell says it may be the outermost layer.
- **beyond-beyond-NPMR** — the possible layer beyond beyond-NPMR; Campbell says perhaps this is the outermost layer, promising to discuss both in great detail later.
- **causality / local causality** — the cause-and-effect rule-set defining objective science within a given dimension. Each dimension has its own; each is insulated from other causal systems by the local logic through which it defines itself. Higher dimensions have more general, less restrictive causality.
- **causal hierarchy / creational hierarchy** — the nesting of causal systems where each larger dimension supports and is a super-set of the next one down, and beginnings are governed by the rules of the next higher dimension. Campbell notes dimensionality has breadth as well as depth (like a family tree), but this chapter looks only at depth.
- **paranormal** — Campbell defines it as "essentially… acausal — beyond the normal cause and effect relationships defined by our limited PMR physical science"; measurable PMR effects produced by interactions that violate PMR causality while lawful in a higher dimension's causality.
- **mysticism** — how the next higher (less limited) dimension is experienced from a lower one: subjective and inaccessible to the lower dimension's objective science. "Your mysticism may be another's science."
- **mystic as scientist from inner space** — the chapter's title idea: a mystic could be a scientist operating under the causality of a higher dimension — or a delusional fool; discrimination requires developed consciousness quality.
- **dimension / dimensionality** — a level of existence with its own local reality, rules, and objective science; dimensions are birthed by parent dimensions and can spawn children. What dimensionality actually is and how it is generated is deferred to later.
- **parent–child dimensions** — Campbell's model: each dimension births and nurtures child dimensions; a child can (but need not) become a parent; one parent can birth many children; each child exists within its own dimension. The child's birth appears mystical from inside the child, well understood from the parent.
- **reality subsystems** — realities each with their own local causality, likened to software components and subroutines of a large complex simulation, all running interdependently within the same computer so long as they have self-consistent rule-sets defining their internal and external interactions.
- **subset–superset comprehension rule** — comprehension and understanding normally flows in only one direction, from superset to subset; the subset does not have what it takes to understand the superset, and to understand it one must first become a member of it.
- **physical/nonphysical relativity** — whether a reality appears physical or nonphysical is relative to the observer; the property is merely perspectival and "has no real significance of its own."
- **open-minded skepticism** — the attitude Campbell asks the reader to maintain until the end of Section 6, after which the reader can apply personal data and specific knowledge to verify the model's value.
- **Big Picture / Big TOE** — Campbell's overall model of the larger reality, which must be unfolded slowly and methodically; the current chapter plants seeds ("peeling the reality onion") for the conceptual foundation still to be built.
- **taste the pudding** — Campbell's test for separating the wise from the foolish (genuine higher-dimensional knowledge from delusion): carefully, scientifically gather experience while increasing the quality of your mind/consciousness/being, then judge; if your "taste buds" are uneducated, repeat as necessary — "it takes one to know one."

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- ***Flatland* by E. A. Abbott** — the chapter's central illustrative reference. Campbell cites the second revised edition (1884, currently available from Princeton University Press) and even gives an online URL (http://www.geom.uiuc.edu/~banchoff/Flatland/). He says it describes, light-heartedly and humorously, "the fundamental technical, epistemological, social, and political difficulty in expanding your awareness of reality beyond the dimensionality of your physical perceptions." Though Flatland deals only with geometric/spatial dimensions, Campbell says the difficulties of perceiving a dimensionality different from one's native perceptual construct are much the same: anyone can easily understand the limitations of dimensions below their perspective, while "looking upstream reveals nothing but mystical confusion." He jokes that Flatland would be the next dimensional stop below PMR "unless one counts the fictional Flatland," strongly urges the reader to read it (twice in the chapter), and notes "it is a hoot." He also draws a direct rule from it: "the ordinary residents of a given reality can only observe and understand interactions within their own reality and the interactions of residents of realities that are more highly constrained than their own" — a resident of a more constrained reality cannot comprehend a less constrained one, since it lies beyond the limits of their normal perception.
- **The simulation/software analogy** — reality subsystems, each with their own local causality, are "likened to the software components and subroutines of a large complex simulation — all run interdependently within the same computer as long as they have self-consistent rule-sets to define their internal and external interactions." This is the chapter's model for how multiple causal systems coexist and interact.
- **The family-tree / parent-and-child model of dimensions** — moving parts: parent dimensions birth and nurture child dimensions; a child may become a parent; one parent may have many children; dimensionality has breadth (siblings) and depth (the creational hierarchy). What it shows: the child's beginning is necessarily mystical from within the child's local causality but ordinary and well understood to the parent — grounding Campbell's claim that "any system of objective causality is insulated from other causal systems by the local logic through which it defines itself."
- **The nested-causality ladder (PMR ⊂ NPMR ⊂ beyond-NPMR ⊂ …)** — the chapter's structural model: each level maintains uniform causality internally (similar processes produce similar results for all experimenters) but can interact downward in ways that violate the lower level's causality, producing what the lower level calls paranormal activity. PMR is pictured as "the final downhill stop for this inter-causal reality train."
- **The "reality onion"** — Campbell's image for the layered structure to be peeled further in later chapters, once the conceptual foundation for the Big TOE is more thoroughly developed.
- **The pudding-tasting procedure for evaluating mystics** — a stepwise quasi-experimental protocol: (1) read Flatland to appreciate the problem of understanding higher dimensions; (2) carefully and scientifically gather your experience while progressing step by step in the quality and capability of your mind/consciousness/being; (3) "taste the pudding to separate the wise from the foolish" — if you can't tell a wise, loving high-quality consciousness from one that is not, repeat step two, since discrimination itself requires evolved consciousness.

## Thread
This chapter sits early in Section 2 ("Mysticism Demystified"), doing the work its section title promises: it converts "mystical" from a synonym for irrational into a relational, perspectival term — what is mystical to a lower dimension is science to a higher one. It follows directly on Section 2's own opening chapters (17–19), which laid groundwork on causality, belief, and PMR-bound knowledge, and generalizes the PMR/NPMR distinction into a full hierarchy of nested causal systems, each lawful in itself. It explicitly sets up future work: Campbell announces he will examine the nothing-beyond-PMR belief "over the next four sections," will explain what dimensionality is and how it is generated later, will "peel the reality onion" once the conceptual foundation is built, and points to Section 3 (especially Chapter 14 of Book 2) for the detailed process of differentiating genuine mystic-scientists from deluded fools. It also asks the reader to hold open-minded skepticism until the end of Section 6, framing the entire trilogy's slow paradigm-busting unfolding. The closing teaser — where dimensions come from and "what love, wisdom, and physics could possibly have to do with any of it" — flags the consciousness-evolution themes to come.
