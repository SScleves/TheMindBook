# Ramifications: Changing the Future, Changing the Past, and the Quantization of Time

## In one paragraph
This chapter is an explicit summary-and-synthesis unit: Thomas Campbell pulls together concepts built up over the preceding chapters (TBC, DELTA-t, NPMRN, probability surfaces) before pushing into the "ramifications" of living inside a virtual digital reality. He argues the future is a database of unactualized, statistically weighted probabilities (not a fixed thing), that the past is likewise a permanently stored, revisitable database rather than a closed record, and that both can be "visited" or modified experientially via NPMRN without ever altering the original OS data — because the system is digital and quantized rather than continuous. He revisits the "force of being" / tumor example from Book 2 to explain why some probable events resist change more than others, then dismantles the sci-fi trope that changing the past would instantly ripple into the present. The chapter closes by zooming out to a nested hierarchy of ever-larger systems — NPMR, further NPMRn levels, an inferred EBC ("Even Bigger Computer"), and ultimately AUM — that Campbell says he can only partially access from his current level of development.

## Core claims
- The future is not fixed or "done"; it exists only as a complete set of probable, un-actualized realities with varying probability densities ("peaks") that are recalculated after every DELTA-t.
- Because the future is dynamic and statistical while the past is a stored, actualized record, the two have "a fundamentally different nature" — which the author says explains why Eastern traditions attach karma only to past lives, not future ones.
- Individuals or groups can have their "probable reality surface" queried, but access to that data must be both earned by the querent and granted by "the system administrator," based on the probable use of the data (spiritual benefit for all affected), not on who is asking or retrieving it — and there is no way to trick or force the system into giving data that wouldn't produce a net positive benefit.
- Focused mental intent can raise or lower the expectation value of a future event (create or dissipate probability peaks), but this obeys the "psi uncertainty principle": fuzzy/broad peaks are easier to modify than tall, sharp ones, and events grow harder to change the closer they approach the present.
- "Force of being" (introduced in Book 2, ch. 13) is the cumulative amount of digital energy — the shared belief, certainty, and emotional investment of sentient beings — poured into a given expectation; the greater that investment, the more resistant the associated event is to change, up to requiring what the author calls "a miracle."
- Digital energy accumulates like physical energy (a battery charging, water filling a tank); an individual's ability to reorganize it is limited, permission-gated, and must compete against everyone else's simultaneous organizing intent.
- There is no real conflict between free will and predestination/precognition: precognitive "visions of the future" are simply accurate readings of high-expectation-value probable states, not perception of an already-fixed future.
- State vectors are not closed systems — outside energy, beings, and objects can interact at any time, and TBC simply keeps up with all the resulting changes and their effects; the author says this is precisely why our reality is dynamic, interactive, and uncertain, since that is the nature of individuated consciousness.
- You may be blocked from knowing the probable future by "information filters" tied to concern over the impact such knowledge could have on the evolving quality of your consciousness — comparable to an adult keeping a child from playing with fire or from inappropriate material on TV or the World Wide Web; the block may simply be your own ignorance of how to access the data, or a limit placed by wiser entities aware of your intrinsic personal limitations and looking out for your best interests, since seeking such knowledge to serve your ego, personal material gain, or other high-entropy intents can cause more harm than good.
- The past (all actualized and even unactualized past state vectors) remains permanently stored and alive in TBC, accessible via NPMRN; a person can project a fragment of consciousness into a past DELTA-t and, by choosing differently, generate a full, realistic experience of an alternate history-thread.
- Modifying a past or future state vector this way is, from TBC's perspective, not the creation of new stored data — it is merely running a query against the existing "everything-that-could-happen-does-happen" database; the original history thread continues completely unperturbed.
- Only free-will choices made inside one's actual "operational" present (in PMR or NPMR) change that home reality for oneself and others; what-if excursions into past/future data change nothing in OS except the quality of the explorer's own consciousness (via the learning/experience gained).
- The common science-fiction assumption that altering the past would instantly and automatically alter the present/future is, the author argues, a category error — it treats reality as continuous rather than digital, and it misunderstands that past, present, and future are separately stored, independently branching state-vector threads.
- AUM can copy any interesting state vector, modify its initial conditions, and set it running as the seed for a wholly new, independently evolving virtual reality populated with free-will beings — analogous, the author says, to isolating an interesting bacterial strain from a petri dish to found new experiments.
- Because time is quantized (in discrete DELTA-t units) rather than continuous, TBC/AUM can pause, speed up, slow down, or "rewind" any given PMRk relative to NPMRN time without producing any noticeable effect within that PMR — comparable to editing digital audio/video.
- Multiple parallel OS/PMR systems exist, each indexed by a subscript k, each running its own local DELTA-tk and accumulating its own elapsed time Tk = Kk·(DELTA-tk); different systems can share a common historical origin and branch apart.
- The author infers, from personal experience, a still-larger system — the EBC — of which TBC may be an emulated subset or outer loop, running on a time quantum smaller than the one that animates OS; he says he has not yet directly experienced this level well enough to describe its rules, comparing himself to a physicist who can infer atoms and electrons only through their effects.
- Reality is described as a nested "onion": one can only properly analyze the dynamics of a given level (PMR, NPMR, NPMRN, further NPMRn levels) from the vantage of the next higher level of generality; the author speculates, half-jokingly, that even AUM might be as unaware of its own containing context ("an AUMosaurus") as we are of ours.

## Concepts
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — the overarching simulating/storing system in which the past, present, and all probable futures of OS exist simultaneously; it stores every actualized and unactualized state vector at the end of each DELTA-t and can be queried, branched, paused, or sped up because it is digital.
- **OS (Our System)** — the specific reality-system (encompassing PMR) that we inhabit; described here as possibly just one of many "OSk" simulations running within TBC, each with its own PMRk.
- **PMR** — the "virtual physical reality" that runs on its own local, incrementing DELTA-t; treated in this chapter as a subroutine-like reality nested within larger simulations.
- **NPMR** — the non-physical reality that, relative to NPMRN, is described as itself lying beyond NPMRN's causality, requiring a still-higher perspective to be understood mechanically.
- **NPMRN** — the specific non-physical level of reality (with its own local time base) from which a sentient, focused observer can access TBC's database, view/experience stored past or probable-future state vectors, and run what-if analyses.
- **NPMRn** — the general/plural form used for other, less-familiar non-physical levels beyond NPMRN; the author suggests these may represent the outermost layer of our "practical" reality onion.
- **DELTA-t** — the fundamental, smallest discrete time-quantum increment of a given reality system (e.g., PMR); reality feels continuous only because DELTA-t is so small.
- **Probability density / probable reality surface / expectation value** — the future is modeled as a surface of "peaks" whose height, width, and shape represent how likely (and how time-precisely) a given potential event is to be actualized; this surface is recalculated after every DELTA-t as choices are made.
- **Psi uncertainty principle** — the rule governing how much a probable event can be changed by focused intent: fuzzy, low, broad peaks are easier to modify than tall, sharp ones, and any given event becomes progressively harder to change as it nears actualization in the present.
- **Force of being** — (recalled from Book 2, ch. 13) the cumulative digital energy of organization — collective certainty, belief, and emotional investment — bound up in a given future expectation; higher force of being means greater resistance to change, quantitatively tied to the event's expectation value.
- **Digital energy** — the currency by which minds reorganize bits/expectation values within the consciousness ecosystem; it accumulates and depletes analogously to physical energy (e.g., a charging battery, a filling water tank), and any individual's capacity to wield it is limited and contested by others' simultaneous intent.
- **System administrator** — the (unnamed, implicit) authority within the TBC/AUM hierarchy that grants or withholds access to probable-future or past data, based on whether the requester's use of that data would produce net spiritual benefit — access cannot be gamed by proxy or trickery.
- **Information filters** — access limits that may block a person from knowing the probable future, tied to concern over how that knowledge would affect the evolving quality of their consciousness; likened to an adult keeping a child from playing with fire or from inappropriate TV/WWW content, and attributed either to the querent's own ignorance of how to access the data or to a limit imposed by wiser entities protecting against the querent's own intrinsic limitations.
- **State vectors as open systems** — state vectors are explicitly not closed systems; outside energy, beings, and objects can interact with them at any time, with TBC simply keeping up with all resulting changes — the stated reason our reality is dynamic, interactive, and uncertain.
- **What-if analysis / dynamic query** — the practice, conducted from NPMRN, of projecting a fragment of consciousness into a past or future state vector, introducing new initial conditions or choices, and experiencing the resulting branch as a fully realistic reality — described as running a query against a "volatile scratch copy" of the original data, leaving no permanent trace on TBC's stored record.
- **State vector** — a single stored snapshot, for a given DELTA-t, of all the variables, beings, objects, and probabilities of a reality system; TBC stores every actualized and unactualized state vector since the beginning of OS.
- **Reality-wide-web (RWW)** — the term used for the accessible network/database through which new (unactualized) pasts and futures are always available for exploration.
- **Parallel realities** — divergent reality-threads that share a common originating state or history and then branch; described as coexisting simultaneously within NPMRN "as books all exist simultaneously in the library."
- **AUM** — the encompassing conscious entity/originating awareness described as running or designing TBC and EBC; said to operate on "the smallest time quanta," which is what puts it "in charge" as the source of the fundamental beat other systems must follow; can copy and modify any interesting state vector as a seed for new evolving realities.
- **EBC (Even Bigger Computer)** — a still-larger, higher-order system the author infers from personal experience, within which TBC may be either emulated in an outer loop or simply a subset; driven by a time quantum smaller than the one animating OS; not yet directly, clearly experienced by the author.
- **Space-Time N-Division Management Team** — the functional entity (within the AUM/EBC hierarchy) said to collect and manipulate experimental data, terminate unproductive scenarios, rerun interesting ones (with or without new input/random seeds), and update hardware/software or modify experimental design without disturbing ongoing experiments.
- **Reality onion** — Campbell's layered metaphor for consciousness levels (PMR, NPMR, NPMRN, further NPMRn, EBC, AUM): a level's dynamics, content, and structure can only be properly analyzed from the vantage point of the next higher level of generality.
- **AUMosaurus** — a speculative, self-deprecating image the author raises: the possibility that AUM itself is merely "a single cell populating the lower intestine" of some still-vaster being, illustrating the potentially open-ended regress of the reality-onion hierarchy and the limits of what any given level's residents can know.
- **OSk / PMRk / DELTA-tk / Tk / Kk notation** — the author's formal shorthand for describing multiple parallel OS-PMR systems: DELTA-tk is the local time quantum of PMRk, Kk is an integer count of elapsed quanta, and Tk = Kk·(DELTA-tk) is total elapsed local time; different PMRk are said to have roughly the same DELTA-tk size but different Kk and hence different Tk, and may share common historical branch-points.
- **Karma and past lives** — cited as consistent with the model: Eastern traditions, the author notes, talk only of past lives and attach karma only to past (actualized) events, never to the future, because the future has not yet been actualized/chosen.
- **Precognition** — explained as accurate perception of high-expectation-value peaks on the probable reality surface, not knowledge of a literally fixed future; many future events are "accurately predictable" for this reason.
- **Quantized time** — the discretization of time into DELTA-t units, which the author says is precisely what makes possible independent history-branching, permanent-but-revisitable storage of the past, pausing/speeding/slowing of any given reality, and the coexistence of multiple simultaneous projections of a single consciousness.
- **Volatile scratch copy** — the author's metaphor for what a what-if analysis actually operates on: a temporary working copy of a stored state file, so the explorer gets a full experiential "operational reality" while the original TBC data set remains unaltered and could be "reconstituted" identically at any time.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The tumor / cancer case (recalled from Book 2, Chapter 13's "force of being" discussion)** — a detailed worked example: if only the patient and a few others are mildly worried about a possible malignant lump, the energy needed to non-physically dissipate it is relatively low. If four doctors and half the hospital's residents examine a CAT scan and become fairly certain it's malignant, the removal energy rises. Once a biopsy confirms a fast-growing, incurable, always-fatal malignancy, the required energy increases further — "by definition, it takes a miracle, where before no miracle was required." The author likens the moment of diagnosis to a quantum measurement: it forces the probable-outcome set to collapse toward the state most compatible with PMR causality, actively decreasing the probability of alternatives (spontaneous remission, benign result). He warns the patient and loved ones can inadvertently worsen the odds by reinforcing fear-based expectation ("a fatal dance of expectation"), and states the best point for any intervention (physical or non-physical) is long before "fatal" becomes near-inevitable.
- **The child-and-fire / inappropriate-content analogy** — the author compares being blocked from probable-future knowledge by "information filters" to an adult preventing a child from playing with fire or from accessing inappropriate material on TV or the World Wide Web (WWW) — used to illustrate that such access limits protect the querent's own consciousness development rather than being arbitrary.
- **The computer/software/folders analogy** — probable future states are likened to the many software applications, simulations, and folders that coexist simultaneously on a computer: some are actively running (incrementing local time), others idle awaiting input, others just storing results — illustrating how all potential states can be simultaneously "extant" within TBC without being actualized.
- **The petri dish / bacteria analogy** — AUM selecting an interesting state vector to seed a new evolving reality is compared to a researcher isolating "the best or most interesting bacteria found growing in all the petri dishes" to found a new set of experiments.
- **The sci-fi time-travel critique** — the author explicitly targets the "common sci-fi plot element" in which changing the past instantly and automatically ripples forward to alter the present/future (via a "nefarious or bungling time traveler"); he argues this rests on an erroneous assumption of continuous (rather than digital, quantized) reality, and that in his model the old history thread runs on completely undisturbed by any what-if branch.
- **The PMR-scientist-and-atoms analogy** — the author compares his own inability to directly experience the EBC level to a physical scientist who can infer atoms and electrons only through their effects, never directly perceiving the particles themselves — used to characterize the limits of his thirty years of NPMR/NPMRN/OS/PMR exploration.
- **The audio/video editing analogy** — TBC's/AUM's ability to pause, fast-forward, reverse, or slow down a given PMR's "reality movie" (relative to NPMRN time) without noticeable effect is compared to the "magical things we do by manipulating digital audio and video in our computers."
