# Ch 37 — Cheer Up: Things Are Not as Bleak as You Might Think

## In one paragraph
Campbell opens with reassurance — the situation of the individual consciousness "is neither hopeless, nor random, nor beyond your control" — and then delivers the chapter's central technical point: in the evolution of consciousness, free will choices are primarily choices of motivation and intent, not of action. What you do is only the secondary result of why you do it, and this reverses the logic of biological evolution, where what you do is the key process driver. He anchors right and wrong in entropy: right intent decreases the entropy of your consciousness, wrong intent increases it, and this entropy measurement defines absolute right and wrong, valid for every individuated consciousness from you to Rover to AUM. He then builds a three-part model — choice of intent, action, result — with internal and external feedback loops, and argues that a wrong intent damages its creator regardless of outcome, while right intent always yields some benefit regardless of what is done. The chapter closes on its title note: most beings are making slow positive progress, conscious "eyes open" effort accelerates it immensely, and the reader should shake off self-pity and get on with the game.

## Core claims
- Campbell argues that what we do is not of primary importance; why we do it is what counts. "The intent or motivation is the choice" — free will choices are primarily choices of motivation and intent, and only secondarily choices of doing.
- Campbell contrasts this with biological evolution, where the key process driver is what you do. In the evolution of consciousness, motivation and intent are the key evolutionary drivers; action is a primary driver for physical-biological systems only.
- Campbell claims "the right choice for the wrong reason is an oxymoron and does not exist" — he is deliberately "tricky-picky" with semantics to stress that the choice he means is the selection of intent.
- Campbell defines absolute right and wrong by entropy effect: right motivation, intent, and action generally decrease the entropy of your consciousness; wrong ones generally increase it. Right and wrong are differentiated by how they affect your spiritual quality (equivalently, the quality of your consciousness).
- Relative (local) right and wrong, Campbell says, are largely derived from our sense of absolute right and wrong but also depend on fad, fashion, culture, and personal, social, and political circumstances. My Big TOE, "being science," is only concerned with absolute right and wrong, where an entropy measurement — not a personal PMR viewpoint — determines which is which.
- Campbell argues absolute right and wrong apply consistently to everyone all the time, because the process of entropy reduction is the same for all individuated consciousness — "you, me, Rover, and AUM."
- You do not evolve higher-quality consciousness through right action or right result, but only through right motivation and right intent.
- Personal quality is gained the way strength, endurance, or piano-playing are gained — by dedication and continual effort over a significant period of time. Campbell calls this incremental self-teaching process "bootstrapping": a coach can improve technique and clarify issues, but only you can accomplish self-improvement.
- Campbell distinguishes three parts: (1) choice of intent, (2) action (the animation of your intent), and (3) result (the effect of what you do) — and warns against confusing action with end result.
- Exercising free will interactions produces an internal result (always) and an external result (usually). The internal result immediately and most potently affects the quality of consciousness according to the quality of the intent; the external result affects others and generates feedback.
- It is not possible to achieve a right result if the intent is wrong: "A wrong intent damages its creator despite what else happens." Apparently right action in the service of wrong intent is eventually revealed as self-destructive for the doer and as a challenging opportunity for whomever it is directed toward.
- Good motivation usually produces mostly right action; perfect motivation always produces right action. Right intent almost always drives right action; wrong intent usually drives wrong action.
- Right growth and right learning are technically defined as growth or learning that decreases the entropy of your consciousness. Personal growth, growing the quality of your being, growing wisdom, growing-up of your being, increasing your capacity to love, spiritual growth, and evolution of your soul are all essentially equivalent expressions — choose the one that suits you. All increase your personal power as they decrease your entropy.
- We are not randomly buffeted souls like particles in Brownian motion. Our specific opportunities may arise through Brownian-like social and personal collisions with large random components, but the overall opportunity to learn and the choices we make contain only small amounts of randomness — at our elementary level of development, most choices are easily predictable.
- If intent is wrong, we suffer the consequences "in all realities we function in" irrespective of what we actually do; if intent is right, we always derive some benefit regardless of the resulting action.
- The external feedback system of rewards and punishments is imperfect but usually efficient; its imperfections and errors show up much more often in the short-term than the long-term. Internal results drive an internal feedback mechanism that increments or decrements consciousness quality according to the rightness of the original intent.
- The PMR virtual reality consciousness trainer provides simple, straightforward interaction and a results-oriented feedback mechanism reflecting immediate and cumulative quality of intent.
- For the typical being, intent lies primarily beneath the surface of awareness; motivations are a complex mixture of sometimes inconsistent and incompatible components — "as Byzantine and inconsistent as you are." Yet for the typical human quality of consciousness, the benefits of right intent are usually much greater than the costs of wrong intent.
- Spiritual capital can be lost repetitively without going broke — you simply don't get ahead — and "major messing-up often precedes major growing-up," because humans sometimes need to be hit repeatedly by the results of their errors before noticing a problem. Egos are so good at blaming others that even after great suffering we miss that our pain is primarily self-inflicted.
- Changing the quality of consciousness cannot be modeled or accurately approximated by a simple linear process; people who force-fit it into linear models via assumptions are inevitably led to erroneous conclusions.
- Choices are mostly mixed, neither completely right nor completely wrong. If right means 100% right and wrong means not right, the average person makes many more wrong choices than right ones, but gains more from an almost-right one than they lose from a so-so wrong one — so most beings are steadily making positive progress, ever so slowly.
- Consciously knowing what you are doing and making an "eyes open" honest effort can increase the rate of progress immensely; a little help in finding the bigger picture can make a significant difference.
- The optimal growth process is uniquely specific to each individual at a given time.
- Right being, profitable intent, and good choices are tremendously easy, simple, and straightforward without ego, and with the humility and compassion that comes with love; they only appear complex because we are complex beings with complex egos suspended between good and evil, love and fear.

## Concepts
- **free-will** — In Campbell's usage here, free will choices are primarily choices of motivation and intent (why we do what we do) and only secondarily choices of doing; the action taken is the first result of the choice of intent.
- **intent / motivation** — The actual object of choice: "The intent or motivation is the choice." Intent lies mostly beneath the surface of awareness and is a complex, sometimes inconsistent mixture of components reflecting the ego. Its quality (rightness) is what directly increments or decrements consciousness quality.
- **choice** — The development or selection of the intent (not the action); Campbell is explicitly "tricky-picky" that a right choice for a wrong reason does not exist.
- **entropy** — Campbell's measure of consciousness quality: right motivation/intent/action generally decreases the entropy within your consciousness; wrong ones generally increase it. Entropy reduction is the same process for all individuated consciousness.
- **absolute-right-and-wrong** — Right and wrong defined by their effect on the entropy (spiritual quality) of consciousness; unvarying, applying consistently to everyone all the time, and determined by an entropy measurement rather than a personal PMR viewpoint.
- **relative-right-and-wrong** — Local right and wrong, largely derived from our sense of the absolute kind but also dependent on fad, fashion, culture, and personal, social, and political circumstances. My Big TOE is not concerned with these.
- **quality-of-consciousness / spiritual-quality** — The property that right and wrong choices raise or lower; equivalently expressed as personal growth, growing wisdom, maturing of your being, capacity to love, spiritual growth, or evolution of your soul — all essentially equivalent and all increasing personal power as entropy decreases.
- **evolution-of-consciousness** — The evolutionary process in which motivation and intent are the key drivers, in contrast to biological evolution where action (what you do) drives the process.
- **biological-evolution** — Campbell's foil: the evolution of physical-biological systems, for which action is the primary driver.
- **PMR** — Acronym used but not expanded in this chapter; the reality whose personal viewpoint does not determine absolute right and wrong, and which functions as a "virtual reality consciousness trainer."
- **consciousness-trainer / virtual-reality** — Campbell's characterization of PMR as "the PMR virtual reality consciousness trainer": it provides simple, straightforward interaction and a results-oriented feedback mechanism that reflects immediate and cumulative quality of intent.
- **AUM** — Named (not expanded here), alongside you, me, and Rover, as one of the individuated consciousnesses for which the entropy-reduction process is identical: "you, me, Rover, and AUM."
- **individuated-consciousness** — Any individual unit of consciousness (from a pet to AUM) subject to the same entropy-reduction process and the same absolute right and wrong.
- **bootstrapping** — Campbell's name for the self-teaching incremental self-improvement process by which you gain personal quality through dedication and continual effort over time; a coach may improve technique and clarify issues, but only you can accomplish self-improvement.
- **big-truth** — What you can express within your life by making right choices based on right intent; your ability to do so depends on the quality of your consciousness.
- **internal-result** — The result every free will interaction always produces; it immediately and most potently affects the quality of your consciousness according to the quality of the intent, driving an internal feedback mechanism that increments or decrements consciousness quality.
- **external-result** — The result a free will interaction usually produces; it affects others as well as yourself and generates feedback or reaction, driving the external reward-and-punishment feedback system — imperfect but usually efficient, with errors showing up more in the short-term than the long-term.
- **feedback** — The interconnected loop (motivation → intent → action → result → feedback) that, if we pay attention, helps us modify our intents; accrued benefits and liabilities help us recognize rightness/wrongness and guide future choices.
- **ego** — The "cut-up in your classroom" that makes it hard to hear the lesson; the source of Byzantine, inconsistent motivations and of blaming others so that we miss that our pain is primarily self-inflicted. Without ego, right being becomes tremendously easy.
- **love / fear** — The opposites (alongside good and evil) between which complex beings exist; humility and compassion come with love, and growing your being increases your capacity to love.
- **brownian-motion** — Campbell's borrowed physics image for what we are NOT: randomly buffeted particles. Our opportunities may arise through Brownian-like collisions with large random components, but our learning opportunities and choices contain only small amounts of randomness.
- **personal-power** — What increases as personal growth decreases the entropy of your consciousness.
- **bigger-picture** — The larger perspective; a little help in finding it can significantly increase the rate of one's progress.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The piano/strength analogy for bootstrapping** — Campbell says you gain personal quality "the same way you gain strength and endurance or learn to play the piano — by dedication and continual effort applied over a significant period of time." A coach can improve technique and clarify issues, but only you can accomplish self-improvement. Shows that consciousness quality is trained, not granted.
- **The three-part choice model** — Campbell's explicit model with moving parts: (1) choice of intent, (2) action ("the animation of your intent"), (3) result ("the effect of your intent"), plus two feedback channels — an internal result (always produced, directly changing consciousness quality per quality of intent) and an external result (usually produced, affecting others and generating reward/punishment feedback). The model's punchline: a right result is impossible from wrong intent; wrong intent damages its creator regardless of outcome.
- **The PMR virtual reality consciousness trainer** — PMR is modeled as a training simulator providing "simple, straightforward interaction and a results-oriented feedback mechanism that reflects immediate and cumulative quality of intent." Shows how the interconnection of motivation, intent, action, and feedback drives right growth.
- **Rover and AUM** — Campbell's illustrative span of individuated consciousness ("you, me, Rover, and AUM") to show entropy reduction is one and the same process at every scale, making absolute right and wrong universal.
- **Brownian motion analogy** — We are not "randomly buffeted souls driven to and fro by external forces like particles exhibiting Brownian motion"; our specific opportunities may come through "Brownian-like social and personal collisions" with large random components, yet the overall learning opportunity and our choices contain only small randomness — most choices at our elementary level are easily predictable.
- **The classroom analogy** — All we need to do is be "serious students committed to making good grades" who take advantage of learning opportunities; the only complication is "a cut-up in your classroom (your ego)" who makes it difficult to hear or understand the lesson.
- **The investment/spiritual-capital analogy** — Actions "can often lose the same spiritual capital repetitively only to have it reappear as an investment in winning." For the "economists, investment advisors, and stockbrokers" he jokes are confused by that sentence, he restates it: you can make the same mistakes repetitively without going broke (seriously impoverishing your consciousness quality) — you simply do not get ahead. Illustrates why major messing-up often precedes major growing-up.
- **The linear-modeler jab** — Precise types who want everything reducible to a simple linear process make assumptions to force-fit consciousness change into such a model, and those assumptions "inevitably lead those apparently precise and logical minds into erroneous conclusions." Delivered with an aside: "I know this is not a problem of yours, but I thought I would mention it in the event you know of somebody to whom it might apply."
- **The 100%-right accounting example** — If right means 100% right and wrong means not right, the average person makes many more wrong choices than right ones, "but gets more gain from an almost right one than loss from a so-so wrong one" — hence slow but steady positive progress for most beings.
- **The closing pep talk** — Wipe the frown, throw the resignation and deep sigh in the trash, shake off self-pity and notions of not wanting to play the game: "Things are not bleak at all. Go get 'em, tiger-rat!"

## Thread
This chapter sits early in Book 2's Section 3, "Man in the Loop," and does exactly what the section title suggests: it puts the human chooser inside the feedback loop of consciousness evolution. Following the trilogy's earlier establishment of consciousness, entropy reduction as the point of the system, and PMR as a virtual training environment (Book 1's mysticism-demystified groundwork), Campbell here specifies the mechanics of participation: free will operates on intent, not action; absolute right and wrong are entropy measurements; and internal plus external feedback loops turn everyday interaction into a consciousness trainer. The chapter's pastoral register — its reassurance that progress is slow but real and that conscious effort accelerates it — sets the motivational stage for the rest of Section 3's examination of how the individual functions within the larger reality's learning process.
