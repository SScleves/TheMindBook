# Ch 59 — A Functional Model of Consciousness Damn! We Finally Find a Plentiful Source of Cheap Energy and It Turns Out to be Us!

## In one paragraph
Campbell argues that consciousness — self-motivated digital organization — is the fundamental and in fact the *only* energy, with mass being a construct *of* consciousness and everything familiar to us being virtual energy causing virtual change inside a virtual reality run under the space-time rule-set. He credits Einstein with showing that mass is structured energy and with nearly reaching this answer via the unified field, but says Einstein fell into belief traps because the culture wasn't ready for Big TOE discovery. Campbell then builds the functional side of the model: discrete thought packets carry energy between individuated conscious entities, interactions are governed by rule-sets, transfer efficiency varies with the sender's and receiver's focus, clarity, and knowledge, and intentional action entails responsibility. Because all beings and things are constructs of the same consciousness, everything is interconnected — everyone is "on the network," which he dubs the RWW (Reality Wide Web) — and paranormal events are just the normal activity of consciousness that violates PMR causality. The chapter closes with an extended "growing up" discussion: why personal growth typically stalls after age forty (belief traps born of ignorance and arrogance), why graduation from the space-time learning lab is automatic when you are ready, and why the answer to spiritual seeking is to "stop doing and start being."

## Core claims
- Campbell claims mass is a highly structured form of energy (crediting Einstein) and, further, that mass is "a construct of (as opposed to, 'is constructed of') consciousness" — a truth he says is as valid as mass-energy equivalence but less widely known.
- Campbell argues Einstein came within "one or two belief traps" of the right answer with his non-physical unified field, but failed because he could not derive the Big Picture from the little picture and the solution lay outside everyone's cultural reality.
- Campbell defines consciousness as "self-motivated digital organization" and asserts it is the fundamental energy — "actually, the only energy." All other energy is virtual energy causing virtual change within a virtual reality according to the space-time rule-set.
- He claims a hydrogen bomb releasing virtual energy that blows up a virtual city is no more problematic than our own war-game simulations — if we can compute such things, so can TBC (The Big Computer).
- Energy is defined as the capacity to make something happen — to produce change. A discrete thought packet both requires energy to send and itself contains energy, able to add new energy and new information to the receiver's consciousness.
- Within a digital consciousness, "energy" and "virtual energy" blend and become indistinguishable at the root; virtual vs. real, like nonphysical vs. physical, is relative to your point of view.
- Only organization (this versus that, rules, and patterns) and process (self-modification toward lower entropy, i.e., more effective organization) are "real"; everything else is digitally derived (virtual). The two basic assumptions (AUO as primordial consciousness potential, and the Fundamental Process of evolution) supply all the required fundamentals.
- Any communication or interaction requires an exchange of energy; energy transfer by discrete thought packets is always intentional, and intentional (directed, controlled, willful) action naturally entails responsibility for the intent and its immediate consequences.
- Energy transfer between consciousnesses is, digitally viewed, the ability to affect the arrangement of bits within another's organization; allowable interactions are defined by the operative rule-set, and transfer coefficients between sentient beings can run from near zero to near 100%.
- Theoretically, any being with a mind can affect any other being with a mind or anything that is a consciousness construct — "which is absolutely everything." Connectedness of all beings and things is an artifact of the fundamental nature of consciousness.
- Our individuation is about entropy and about constraints on our ability to interact, not about being disconnected from the whole; at the most fundamental level we are all part of the same consciousness.
- Thought-energy transfers take place in thought-space outside PMR, constrained by the NPMR_N rule-set rather than the space-time rule-set, yet their results can directly affect what is experienced in PMR: "mind can alter a reality created by and within mind."
- Mind-space is "a jungle" — beings and critters maximizing their situations — but not a free-for-all: NPMR_N has specific rules (experimental protocols) restricting energy transfer, particularly between separate reality constructs (experiments), and the rules are obeyed and enforced most of the time.
- A knowledgeable receiver can refuse, deflect, or return energy sent by another's intent; an unknowledgeable receiver is comparatively open and vulnerable.
- The possibilities for interaction within the larger reality "range many orders of magnitude beyond your wildest imagination," with few constraints on what you can do or where you can go; aware intentional experiences in NPMR_N often become part of an accelerated growth path as you learn to "operate, function, work, play, and make free will choices as a responsible interactive citizen of multiple reality systems."
- Everyone is hooked up to the RWW (Reality Wide Web); spontaneous paranormal experiences are the occasional random glimmers most people get of this connectedness. Paranormal events are natural artifacts of consciousness, sometimes violate PMR causality rules, and are therefore vehemently denied by those in scientific or cultural belief traps.
- Once you understand that we are consciousness constructs, "the paranormal becomes normal — an everyday fact of existence that is as accessible as gravity"; "mystic" and "paranormal" have meaning only relative to the normal level of ignorance within PMR.
- Campbell claims the pace of learning of an aware consciousness naturally accelerates and can far outlast physical bodies, yet most people stop growing after about forty because of belief traps: the "been there, done that" delusion of omniscience is ignorance and arrogance teaming up to produce artificial blindness — "the same result as simple stupidity."
- Culture helps the first two transition ages (around two and the young teens) out of their belief traps by example, but the over-forty group forms the majority, defines accepted PMR reality, and must figure it out alone — though the third transition is actually the easiest.
- Graduation from the space-time learning lab is automatic when you are ready; consciousness evolution "does not work like public school" — you are not passed until you master the material and don't graduate just for being old enough. If nothing is happening, you are not as ready as you think.
- Growth is bootstrapping: an iterative process of very many very small steps, exercising and conditioning your intent instead of muscles, which each unique entity must work out individually.
- Nonphysical help is always available and more or less automatically applied to those ready to make good use of it — "No one is ever overlooked" — even if it isn't what you want, expect, or think you are ready for. The prescription for the frustrated seeker: "Stop doing and start being."

## Concepts
- **consciousness** — Campbell defines it here as "self-motivated digital organization"; the most fundamental energy in our system and actually the only energy. Everything else, including mass, is a construct of consciousness.
- **energy** — the capacity or ability to make something happen — to produce change. Within digital consciousness, energy and virtual energy blend and become indistinguishable at the root.
- **virtual energy** — what all the energy we are familiar with actually is: energy causing virtual change within a virtual reality according to the space-time rule-set. Campbell says calling AUM and consciousness themselves "virtual energy forms" is fine — virtual vs. real is relative to point of view.
- **AUM** — the digital self-organizing system (mind) within which everything is virtual; the apparently (but not actually) infinite consciousness that we are all extant in, and of, and whose fundamental digital energy source we all share.
- **AUO** — Campbell's second basic assumption: the potential energy system (potential to organize), the "mysterious mental media" able to distort a portion of itself, which eventually begins to self-organize into consciousness. The primordial consciousness potential that satisfies the requirement that any successful Big TOE have at least one assumption that appears mystical from PMR's viewpoint.
- **Fundamental Process (of evolution)** — Campbell's first basic assumption: the process that drives the mental media to seek lower average entropy through improved self-organization.
- **reality cells** — the binary cells of which AUM is composed; they exist only in relation to something else (distorted versus non-distorted).
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — the computer that can accomplish "horrific calculations" like a virtual hydrogen bomb destroying a virtual city, just as our war-game simulations do.
- **PMR** — physical-matter reality; the local physical reality whose exclusivity underpins the scientific and cultural beliefs that deny the paranormal. Campbell also calls it "PMRville" and "the space-time learning lab."
- **NPMR / NPMR_N** — the nonphysical reality where thought-energy transfers take place (thought-space outside PMR); its rule-set — not the space-time rule-set — constrains those transfers, though their results can affect PMR. Different NPMR_n have somewhat different rules.
- **space-time rule-set** — the rule-set of the particular virtual reality our physical experience must remain exclusively connected to while we use it to improve the quality of our consciousness.
- **rule-set (operative)** — whatever set of rules defines the allowable interactions between entities in a given reality; in NPMR_N, specific rules ("experimental protocols") restrict energy transfer, particularly between separate reality constructs (experiments).
- **discrete thought packet** — a packet of consciousness energy that requires energy to send and itself contains energy; it can add new energy and new content (information) to the receiver's consciousness. Energy transfer by thought packet is always intentional.
- **action** — the condition of a sender intentionally (or unintentionally) impacting a receiver in an energy exchange; when intentional (directed, controlled, willful), responsibility naturally follows.
- **transfer coefficient (coupling coefficient)** — the efficiency of an energy transfer between consciousnesses, anywhere from near zero to near 100% depending on circumstances; relatively high coefficients between sentient beings are not unusual.
- **energy density** — the power and focus of a transmission; together with the transmission coefficient it determines how much energy passes from sender to receiver.
- **thought-space / mind-space** — where consciousness-energy transfers occur, outside PMR; "a jungle out there" filled with beings and critters trying to maximize their situations, but governed by rules that are obeyed and enforced most of the time.
- **individuation** — being an "individuated lump within, as well as part of, the same sheet" of consciousness; it is about entropy and constraints on interaction, not about disconnection from the whole.
- **RWW (Reality Wide Web)** — Campbell's coinage for the universal consciousness network: everyone is hooked up and has a home page; a giant, open, super-high-bandwidth network with few rules, some informal etiquette, a few power users, plenty of hackers, and no one particularly in control — and its "personal touch" can bonk you with an energy exchange that does more than pass information.
- **paranormal** — natural artifacts of the nature of consciousness; the normal activity of the nonphysical energy that is consciousness. Spontaneous paranormal experiences are most people's random glimmers of RWW connectedness. Like "mystic," the word has meaning only relative to the normal level of ignorance within PMR.
- **belief traps** — the cultural/conceptual snares that stopped Einstein short of the Big TOE and that freeze personal growth (especially after forty); they melt away, along with the fear and ignorance that created them, when you grow up enough to graduate.
- **transition ages** — roughly age two, the young teens, and over-forty: ages where the self is centered in a small, already-mastered reality awaiting the next major change of perspective, and feels it has "been there, done that."
- **artificial blindness** — what ignorance and arrogance team up to produce: a delusion of omniscience about one's local reality that delivers the same result as simple stupidity.
- **graduation** — leaving the space-time learning lab; automatic when you are ready, never awarded for age or desire alone. "When you are ready, graduation is automatic."
- **bootstrapping** — pulling yourself up by your own bootstraps: an iterative growth process of very many very small steps, operationally like a child growing up or an athlete conditioning — except you are exercising and conditioning your intent instead of muscles.
- **intent** — the willful focus by which a being moves thought energy, affects another's consciousness, and grows; the size of the "thought-energy-rock" one can throw depends on how much energy one can transfer with intent.
- **entropy** — the quantity consciousness evolution minimizes: the Fundamental Process drives the media toward lower average entropy through improved self-organization; personal consciousness evolving "in the positive direction" means toward lower entropy.
- **AI Guy** — invoked in the line "In the bigger picture, AI Guy is as real as you are," making the point that our sense of real and our sense of virtual are one and the same.
- **love / fear** — once belief traps melt away with graduation, "Without fear your existence will be filled with love, peace, and balance."

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **Einstein and the unified field** — Campbell's historical anchor: Einstein taught that mass is structured energy and tried to convince us all energy was contained within a non-physical unified field, but failed — he "made it to within one or two belief traps of the right answer," ahead of his time before the logical/conceptual ground disappeared beneath his feet. Shows why the Big TOE could not appear earlier: cultural readiness limits discovery.
- **The hydrogen-bomb objection** — a staged skeptic asks whether an H-bomb "releases virtual energy that blows up a virtual city full of virtual people." Campbell answers yes, by analogy with our own war games: we compute such horrific calculations all the time, "so why can't TBC do the same?" Shows that virtual energy in a simulation is fully adequate to produce all observed physical effects.
- **Thought-energy-rock analogy** — individuated conscious entities can "both throw and get hit by a figurative thought-energy-rock as well as send and receive data"; the size of the rock depends on how much thought energy a being can move with its intent. Illustrates that consciousness connections carry force, not just information.
- **The RWW (Reality Wide Web) model** — Campbell's internet analogy for universal connectedness: everyone hooked up with a home page, a few power users, plenty of hackers, no one in control, a giant open super-high-bandwidth network with few rules and informal etiquette — where you can "reach out and bonk someone with an energy exchange." Paranormal experiences are ordinary people's random glimmers of this network.
- **The functional energy-transfer model itself** — moving parts Campbell assembles: sender and receiver; discrete thought packets as carriers; the operative rule-set defining allowable interactions; transmission power/focus (energy density) plus transfer coefficient determining how much energy moves; the receiver's sensitivity, clarity (low noise), and knowledge-base determining awareness of the energy's origins, context, and content; the sender's focus, clarity, and knowledge-base determining how much, what type, and to what end energy is imparted; knowledgeable receivers can refuse, deflect, or return energy while unknowledgeable ones are vulnerable.
- **The jungle of mind-space** — the larger reality as a natural environment full of beings and critters maximizing their situations without regard to others, restrained by NPMR_N "experimental protocols" restricting energy transfer between separate reality constructs (experiments). Shows NPMR is lively and lawful, not a free-for-all.
- **The three-year-old and its parents** — to a three-year-old almost everything in a normal day seems mystical and miraculous; to the parents, with a larger perspective, the same day is mundane. Mature adults don't seek out ignorant children to impress unless they are manipulative or egotistical. Shows "mystical" is relative to ignorance.
- **Staying in your house** — restricting yourself to PMR is like never venturing beyond your front door: fine and relatively safe for an infant, rather limiting for an adult. "Growing up is what your existence is all about."
- **The life-arc case study** — kids can't wait to be 16 (drive: freedom and control), 18 (vote, marry, buy recreational drugs), 21 (contracts, more drugs); the twenties are cool; in the thirties the pendulum swings back toward wanting youth but not at the price of hard-won knowledge; another forty years on, most would gladly trade everything they've learned to turn back the clock a decade, since the rate of learning and growing "is asymptotically approaching the time axis"; by the mid-forties "the mental rocket fuel appears to have been used up" and we drift on "a long slow ballistic trajectory awaiting the inevitable death on impact" as our bodies literally return to earth, learning little "during the exploration of the back forty." Campbell uses this to ask why growth stalls exactly when (age forty to eighty) learning should be steeply accelerating in preparation for "the really important stuff."
- **The three transition ages** — two-year-olds, young teens, and people over forty all feel they've "been there, done that"; culture pulls the first two groups out of their traps by adult example, but the over-forty majority defines PMR reality and must self-rescue. The third transition is easiest yet presents the greatest challenge of self-imposed artificial blindness.
- **A world of fourteen-year-old adults** — thought experiment: imagine the great majority of adults acting fourteen; "Think of it ladies, everybody would be that way instead of just the men!" Shows the collective cost when people fail to grow up at the appropriate time.
- **Forest Gump's philosopher-mother** — "Stupid is as stupid does," offered as the criterion for pudding tasters; never be afraid of being stupid, only of remaining that way when you can change.
- **Public school vs. consciousness evolution** — consciousness evolution is not like public school: no social promotion, no graduation by age or desire; like childhood itself, adulthood "simply happens" when you're ready, and the space-time learning lab of PMR works the same way.
- **The bootstrapping athlete/child** — figuring out your own growth is operationally similar to a child growing up or an athlete incrementally building balance, strength, coordination, stamina, strategy, and skill — except the thing being exercised and conditioned is your intent.
- **The lecture-hall Q&A skit** — Campbell stages audience questions: first, where to get help for the "space-time challenge" (answer: ask a mature adult if you need advice, but mostly figure it out yourself — and beware, not all grown people are mature adults). Second, the "cute blond in the tight red sweater" gag — Campbell admits the asker is actually "a nerdy looking male engineer with oily hair, dual pocket protectors, and a bad complexion," pretended into a blond "to trick my male readers into paying closer attention."
- **The New Age super-seeker** — the questioner does everything: meditation, chanting, yoga, incense, crystals, warrior movement classes, Ti Mei Shu massage therapy, every New Age course/seminar/"hoe-down," every philosophy/religion/New-Age book read twice, gurus and self-proclaimed wise-persons consulted — yet little spiritual progress. She asks what else to do and why advanced nonphysical beings haven't come to help. Campbell's two-part answer: "1) Stop doing and start being. 2) No one is ever overlooked" — you get all the nonphysical help you can use and need, even if it isn't what you want or expect.
- **The wise horses' proverb** — the chapter's closing line: "You can lead a human to knowledge, but you cannot make him think or understand."

## Thread
This chapter falls partway through Section 4 ("Solving the Mystery"), building the *functional* half of Campbell's model of consciousness on the foundations laid in Book 1: it explicitly recalls the two basic assumptions (AUO as primordial consciousness potential and the Fundamental Process of evolution — Chapters 18, 20, and 24 of Book 1) and the Section 2 point that "mystic" and "paranormal" are relative to PMR ignorance (Chapter 20). Having established in prior Section 4 chapters that reality is digital and simulated (TBC, reality cells, rule-sets), Campbell here converts that ontology into mechanics: consciousness as the only energy, thought packets as the currency of interaction, transfer coefficients, intent, responsibility, and the RWW as the connective fabric. He promises that exactly how mass is experienced through a construct of consciousness "is explained later in this section," and defers the how-to of using intent to affect other beings (and filtering what is directed at you) to Section 5. The growing-up/belief-trap material continues the trilogy's running argument that Big Truth is gated by personal readiness, setting up the practical growth guidance to come.
