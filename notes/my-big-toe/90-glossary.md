# Glossary

**Chapter-number legend.** Pointers use the note chapter numbers (each note's frontmatter `chapter:` field). **Chapter 0** is the trilogy's front matter (About, Synopsis, Preface, and Foreword). **Chapters 1–92** run continuously across the three books and six sections: **Book 1 *Awakening*** — Section 1 "Delusion or Knowledge" (ch 1–16), Section 2 "Mysticism Demystified" (ch 17–34); **Book 2 *Discovery*** — Section 3 "Man in the Loop" (ch 35–49), Section 4 "Solving the Mystery" (ch 50–72); **Book 3 *Inner Workings*** — Section 5 "Inner Space, the Final Frontier" (ch 73–86), Section 6 "The End is Always the Beginning" (ch 87–92). All definitions are the book's own, as presented by author Thomas Campbell; they are reported, not endorsed as fact.

- **AC (Alternating Current)** — acronym from the Book 1 list; the on/off/reversing electrical mode used in the lab experiments and as an analogy for oscillating reality-cell states. Chapters: 0, 8.
- **AI Guy / Artificial Intelligence / Artificial (vs. actual) Consciousness** — Campbell's personified artificial-intelligence character, first a "player" in a computer war-game simulation who is given memory and learning algorithms and bootstraps himself toward genuine consciousness; used to argue that consciousness is substrate-independent, that "artificial" is an ego-driven label, and that we are to AI Guy what AUM is to us. Chapters: 51, 52, 53, 54, 55, 56, 57, 59, 62, 63, 66, 67, 84. Related: digital-consciousness, consciousness (four attributes), rule-set.
- **All That Is** — the basic oneness, continuity, and connectedness of everything that any Big TOE must describe; in the mature model, consciousness (AUM) evolving toward lower entropy. Chapters: 0, 44, 57, 67, 85. Related: AUM, consciousness-evolution-fractal.
- **Altered state of consciousness** — any state different from "normal" (wide-awake and focused in the physical world); each altered state has its own unique functionality, and Campbell claims a half-dozen can be entered with willful repeatability. Chapters: 4, 5, 8, 9, 10. Related: meditation, point-consciousness, OOBE.
- **Anthropomorphic trap** — the "outrageously limiting" error of imagining AUM as a person or humanoid super-intellect; attribution flows parent-to-child, so one should think of humans in terms of AUM's attributes, not the reverse. Chapters: 26, 27, 29, 35, 38, 40, 43. Related: AUM, Big-Cheese, MOG.
- **Anti-rats / poisoned cheese** — a competing group in Campbell's pudding-maze image who scatter poisoned cheese and distractions, introducing the struggle of good versus evil and making the outcome of the experiment genuinely open; anti-rats can only evolve toward power-control-force, a self-limiting dead end. Chapters: 28, 36, 42, 43, 48, 61, 92. Related: rats/pudding-maze, free-will, good-and-evil.
- **Arrogance by assumed omniscience** — Campbell's coinage for the belief trap of assuming that whatever one's peer group has not directly experienced must be delusion; "the delusional always believe that they are non-delusional." Chapters: 92. Related: belief-trap, conceptual-center.
- **Astral projection** — the older (turn-of-the-last-century) scholarly name for the out-of-body experience, found in the literature Campbell's lab surveyed. Chapters: 8. Related: OOBE.
- **Aura / energy body / life energy** — the fuzzy nonphysical light Campbell reports seeing around living things (and, in smaller uniform form, around inanimate objects, which falsified his "life energy" hypothesis); in the mature model the "energy body" is the NPMR-N portion of a being that sustains the physical body and survives its death. Chapters: 11, 76. Related: nonphysical-body, energy-body, interconnectedness.
- **AUM (Absolute Unbounded Manifold)** — the name AUO earns once it has evolved into an extremely complex, highly ordered, low-entropy, purposeful digital consciousness ("one into many"); the fundamental source consciousness of which all individuated beings are parts, and which is simultaneously the computer, the programmer, and the operating system. The AUO→AUM change is evolution, not revolution, adding no new substance or assumptions. Chapters: 0, 30, 31, 32, 33, 35, 41, 44, 46, 56, 57, 78. Related: AUO, consciousness, TBC, entropy, Fundamental-Process.
- **AUMosaurus** — Campbell's deliberately absurd humor-creature ("AUMosaurus-beyondgraspus-outofmindus") in whose gut our whole reality might sit as bacteria; used to dramatize how far the "Even Bigger Picture" lies beyond our grasp and to model epistemic humility. Chapters: 32, 33, 43, 44, 56, 83, 92. Related: Even-Bigger-Picture, open-minded-skepticism.
- **AUO (Absolute Unbounded Oneness)** — Campbell's one extraordinary (mystical-from-PMR) assumption: primordial consciousness, an apparently-but-not-actually-infinite, undifferentiated, structurable form of digital potential energy that is not yet intelligent or self-aware and cannot hold a single coherent thought; from AUO plus the Fundamental Process, all reality is derived. Chapters: 0, 24, 25, 26, 27, 28, 29, 30. Related: AUM, primordial-consciousness, digital-potential-energy, two-assumptions.
- **Awareness / dim awareness** — the basic attribute of consciousness (its "appreciation of profitability"), ranging from very dim to very bright as a function of memory, processing, and the richness of possible interactions; at its dimmest it is merely the potential for awareness. Chapters: 25, 26, 56. Related: consciousness, intelligence, entropy.
- **Beat frequency / binaural beats / brainwave entrainment** — from Gerald Oster's article: a 100 Hz tone in one ear and a 104 Hz tone in the other produce a perceived 4 Hz beat (in the corpus callosum) that, Campbell reports, entrains brainwaves and reliably induces altered states in untrained people. Chapters: 9. Related: 4-Hz-pulsation-state, EEG, corpus-callosum.
- **Being objects / intellectual objects** — Campbell's two data-categories any complete account of reality must cover: "being objects" are nonphysical, personal units of individuated consciousness (known through subjective awareness); "intellectual objects" are shared, physical, measurable things obeying PMR's space-time causality. Chapters: 74. Related: knowledge-vs-wisdom, science-of-being, subjective-science.
- **Belief / belief trap / belief-blindness / dogma** — belief is "a conclusion based upon a mystical premise," generated and necessitated by ignorance; a belief trap is a comfortable ego-made pseudo-knowledge that invisibly circumscribes perceived reality, and belief-blindness is the invisible cultural "blindfold we all wear." Even scientific materialism ("PMR über alles") is a belief system. Chapters: 0, 17, 18, 19, 21, 42, 49, 50, 88, 89, 92. Related: pseudo-knowledge, open-minded-skepticism, dogma, PMR-uber-alles.
- **Big Bang / Big Digital Bang** — in Campbell's model the moment TBC begins applying the space-time rule-set to given initial conditions, letting a wound-up "potential energy reality spring" unwind one DELTA-t at a time; also called the Big Set of Initial Conditions or the Big Start of the Simulation. Chapters: 63, 81, 85, 91. Related: space-time-rule-set, TBC, DELTA-t.
- **Big Cheese** — Campbell's informal name for a high-level consciousness/administrator between humans and AUM who runs the conditions of the experiment (the "culture" of NPMR-N); like AUM, an egoless embodiment of unconditional love, and the only entity said to execute capital punishment (death by disassociation) in NPMR-N. Chapters: 41, 43, 46, 47, 48, 61, 84, 88. Related: AUM, MOG, death-by-disassociation.
- **Big Picture / little picture** — the whole, top-level view of the larger reality versus the limited, belief-bounded local (PMR-only) view; a little picture must be a subset of the Big Picture, never the reverse, and seeing the Big Picture requires transcending belief systems, not just intellect. Chapters: 0, 17, 36, 41, 44, 47, 49, 65. Related: Big-TOE, belief-trap, mystical.
- **Big TOE / little TOE / Tiny TOE** — a Big (Picture) Theory Of Everything integrates metaphysics and physics, normal and paranormal, into one model and must contain at least one assumption that appears mystical from PMR; a "little TOE" is confined to PMR objective causality; a "Tiny TOE" (e.g., intestinal bacteria's) describes only what a radically limited awareness can know. A candidate Big TOE must fully contain and not conflict with PMR physics. Chapters: 0, 1, 17, 24, 44, 49, 65. Related: TOE, Big-Picture, two-assumptions.
- **Big Truth / little truth** — fundamental (Big Picture) truth, absolute and uniformly significant to everyone yet discoverable only within one's own experience and non-transferable like wisdom; little truth is circumstantial and relative to the observer. "Big Truth does not rearrange itself to fit the whims of the fashionable majority." Chapters: 0, 19, 31, 48, 74. Related: wisdom, tasting-the-pudding, knowledge-vs-wisdom.
- **Body asleep, mind awake** — Bob Monroe's boundary technique for reaching altered states: keep the body asleep while the mind stays awake on the edge between waking and sleep. Chapters: 8. Related: altered-state, OOBE.
- **Bootstrapping** — the incremental, self-teaching process by which consciousness raises its own quality (lowers its entropy) through many small free-will choices and feedback; a coach can help but only you can accomplish it. Chapters: 23, 37, 45, 52, 54, 57, 59. Related: entropy-reduction, quality-of-consciousness, Fundamental-Process.
- **Brownian opportunity / Brownian motion (social)** — Campbell's image for consciousness evolution: individuals "bump into" one another in biological, psychological, and social Brownian motion, each collision generating new free-will choices, with feedback biasing the process toward quality; we are not merely randomly buffeted. Chapters: 37, 45. Related: free-will, randomness, feedback.
- **c (speed of light)** — 186,000 mph / 3×10^8 m/s; in the model, the evolved constant upper limit on information propagation (one space-time cell per DELTA-t) from which space is derived. Chapters: 0, 31. Related: space-time, DELTA-t, space-time-cell.
- **C++** — a compiled programming language; Campbell's analogy for our experience as "a simulation programmed in object-oriented C++ where we are the objects." Chapters: 0, 31, 65. Related: TBC, rule-set.
- **Causality / objective causality / local (system-specific) causality / universal causality** — objective causality is the assumption that everything must be preceded by its cause (the West's philosophical foundation of science); Campbell argues it is local (system-specific), not universal, so a system's own beginnings lie outside its causal logic in the next-higher dimension. Chapters: 17, 18, 20, 21, 45. Related: mystical, hierarchy-of-causal-systems, rule-set.
- **Central Nervous System (CNS)** — brain, nerves, synapses; "a highly constrained physical analog of consciousness functionality" and a transducer / constraining filter between the virtual physical body and the nonphysical mind, which it hosts as a computer hosts an operating system. Chapters: 0, 29, 45. Related: mind-brain-body-model, virtual-body.
- **Clean slate / recyclable awareness** — a designed feature of the space-time trainer: beings periodically restart a lifetime with personal delusions/fears wiped but accumulated wisdom intact, so growth can keep accelerating instead of stagnating in self-made belief traps; only the local PMR slate is wiped. Chapters: 61, 63, 64. Related: experience-packet, over-soul, karma.
- **Conscience / higher-level rule-set** — the top-level rule-set defining an entity's profitability criteria, goals, values, and purpose, which "trickles down" as guiding values and works through intuition and conscience; contrasted with the lower-level rule-set (local physics/causality). Chapters: 55, 56, 62, 63. Related: rule-set, intuition, values.
- **Consciousness** — Campbell's fundamental quantity: "self-motivated digital organization," the only real energy, a self-relational digital media that reduces its average entropy through evolution; defined by four attributes (self-awareness, evolutionary viability, ability to modify the self, intelligence). AUM *is* consciousness rather than *has* it. Chapters: 26, 41, 45, 54, 56, 57, 59. Related: digital-consciousness, entropy, free-will, four-attributes.
- **Consciousness cycle** — the recurring dynamic by which the larger system evolves: individuated units are created at high entropy and evolve to low-entropy wholeness, contributing their growth back to the whole (the "down-stroke" and "up-stroke"); learning is cumulative across lifetimes. Chapters: 28, 31, 32, 36, 44, 76. Related: entropy-reduction, experience-packet, consciousness-ecosystem.
- **Consciousness ecosystem / eco-mind-system** — the largest, most complex ecosystem we belong to ("the mother of all others"), driven by self-improvement (entropy lowering) rather than survival/propagation; our physical universe is "a small virtual habitat" (biome) within it. Chapters: 27, 36, 44, 62, 78. Related: consciousness-cycle, biome-niche-habitat, win-win-relationship.
- **Consciousness-evolution fractal / process fractal** — the recursive, self-similar pattern by which the same Fundamental Process, applied to the digital media of consciousness at every scale (AUM→TBC→us→AI Guy→computers), generates All That Is; "each part of the pattern carries the blueprint of the whole." A *process* fractal, not a geometric one. Chapters: 32, 33, 40, 41, 54, 55, 85. Related: Fundamental-Process, hologram-fractal, All-That-Is.
- **Consciousness entrainment technology** — Campbell's digital-native redefinition of space-time: a technology defined by the constraints it places on the interactions of the entities whose experience it bounds. Chapters: 64. Related: space-time-rule-set, virtual-reality.
- **Constraints (defining boundaries / limiting functionality)** — the mechanism that creates individuated beings: bounding a subset of consciousness (separation) and limiting its functionality (a more restrictive rule-set); "evolution is about constraints." Self-imposed internal constraints (belief, fear, ego) are "negative digital potential energy." Chapters: 35, 47, 56, 57, 62. Related: individuated-consciousness, rule-set, entropy.
- **Constructs of consciousness (vs. constructed of)** — Campbell's key semantic distinction: PMR and individuated beings are deformations/specific organizations *within* the continuum of consciousness ("lumps in the sheet"), not things *built out of* consciousness as a separate material. Chapters: 62. Related: lumps-in-the-sheet, digital-consciousness.
- **Corpus callosum** — the structure between the brain's hemispheres where, in the lab's working hypothesis, the binaural beat frequency occurs and drives brainwaves. Chapters: 9. Related: binaural-beats, brainwave-entrainment.
- **Cultural bias / cultural insanity / cultural belief** — culture lays out the worldview template "at the bone and sinew level"; "cultural insanity" is Campbell's term for absurdly illogical but culturally normal behavior, which he traces to fear-based, emotionally driven motivators. Chapters: 6, 21, 33. Related: belief-blindness, feel-good-belief-systems, herd-instinct.
- **Death by disassociation** — the exceedingly rare true death of a nonphysical being: loss of internal organization until identity dissolves into a maximum-entropy pile of reality cells (like degaussing a floppy disk); the last state is kept as a historical record, and only the Big Cheese executes it as punishment. Chapters: 48. Related: reality-cells, immortality, Big-Cheese.
- **Decision space** — the finite array of discrete free-will choices an entity can recognize, understand, and implement; larger and more complex for lower-entropy (higher-quality) consciousness, tiny for a clam or amoeba. Chapters: 45, 54, 57, 58. Related: free-will, quality-of-consciousness, entropy.
- **De-evolution** — the failure mode of digital consciousness: rather than deteriorating with age like biology, it can evolve into higher-entropy, less viable states. Chapters: 35. Related: entropy, grow-or-die.
- **Degrees of freedom** — the measure of how unconstrained a reality or mind is; nonphysical mind (and NPMR) has more degrees of freedom and fewer constraints than the body (and PMR), which is why it evolves faster and produces more varied life-forms. Chapters: 29, 30, 39. Related: constraints, NPMR, entropy.
- **DELTA-t / delta-t** — DELTA-t is the discrete quantum of PMR "real-time" (the beat all space-time marches to); delta-t is the much smaller NPMR-N simulation quantum on which TBC computes probable futures between successive DELTA-t ticks. Nested loops (AUM ⊃ NPMR ⊃ NPMR-N ⊃ OS/PMR) each run on a smaller quantum than the loop they contain. Chapters: 31, 33, 77, 79, 80, 83. Related: quantized-time, TBC, probable-reality-surface.
- **Determinism** — rejected as "a local rule-based virtual-reality illusion, engineered within TBC" that rests on the impossible premise of omniscience; "consciousness without free will is like warm ice or dry liquid water." Chapters: 45, 62, 82. Related: free-will, randomness, statistical-reality.
- **Digital consciousness / digital energy / digital potential energy / synergy** — consciousness as digital (reality-cell-based) organizational energy; digital energy is the ability to rearrange bits (lower entropy), synergy is the energy of profitable organization (synergy = constant/entropy), and their sum is conserved in a fixed-capacity system; AUO is pure potential synergy. Chapters: 24, 25, 28, 40, 41, 58, 59, 83. Related: reality-cells, entropy, consciousness, organization-as-energy.
- **Dimension** — not a geometric or spatial thing but a well-defined memory/calculation subset within TBC — "a constrained thought-space," a compartment hosting one reality's rule-set, like a saved computer file or folder; realities are separated by different rule-sets, and inter-dimensional travel is "relatively easy once you know how." Chapters: 20, 28, 40, 80. Related: rule-set, thought-space, virtual-reality.
- **Discovery mode** — the neither-optimistic-nor-pessimistic stance Campbell and Dennis held: open to all possibilities, wanting only the truth, trusting accumulated results to tell their own story. Chapters: 10. Related: open-minded-skepticism, evidential-experience.
- **Ego / ego-fear / I-structure** — the direct result of fear; a reactive structure with no power of its own whose job is to keep you feeling good by managing belief systems that hide your fears from your intellect, generating the needs, wants, and self-importance that raise entropy; humility and compassion are "what is left over when the ego is gone." Chapters: 19, 23, 41, 42, 44, 92. Related: fear, belief-trap, love, humility-compassion.
- **Ego value vs. real value** — Campbell's contrast: as perspective broadens, the ego value of what you do (status, importance-to-self) subsides while the "real value" (the growth from interactions with yourself and others) grows. Chapters: 90. Related: quality-of-consciousness, learning-lab.
- **Energy packet exchange interface** — the modeled boundary between the "un-experienced reality" (TBC) and the space-time player: sensors send and receive discrete energy packets per the space-time rule-set, and this exchange is the ultimate source of PMR experience. Chapters: 66, 67, 69, 84. Related: un-experienced-reality, perception, teleportation.
- **Entropy (of consciousness) / entropy reduction** — Campbell's master metric of consciousness quality: high entropy = fear/ego/dimness/disorder, low entropy = love/awareness/order and more usable energy; unlike physical (PMR) systems, consciousness is "frictionless" and its entropy can fall. Spiritual growth *is* lowering the entropy of your consciousness. Chapters: 21, 22, 24, 27, 28, 36, 40, 42, 92. Related: quality-of-consciousness, synergy, love, second-law-of-thermodynamics.
- **Evidential experience / operational significance / firsthand experience** — the lab's standard: altered-state experience must be consistent, repeatable, and evidential, producing objectively checkable results; only unambiguous firsthand experience (not secondhand data) can make truth "sink in." Chapters: 7, 8, 9, 10, 74. Related: tasting-the-pudding, subjective-science, open-minded-skepticism.
- **Even-Bigger-Picture** — the widest speculative frame (what lies outside AUM); Campbell holds it beyond comprehension and belonging to imagination rather than the theory, where "the best conclusion is no conclusion." Chapters: 32. Related: AUMosaurus, open-minded-skepticism, multiple-AUMs.
- **Experience packet** — a single, relatively short, more-or-less independent lifetime/learning-adventure in PMR; the over-soul gleans the growth from each and may enter a new body with the local slate wiped. Chapters: 57, 64, 82. Related: over-soul, clean-slate, karma.
- **Experience-bumps / matter bumps / lump in the AUM-sheet** — Campbell's imagery for individuated beings and their bodies: a being is a "lump in the AUM-sheet," and the physical body is a dense, space-time-constrained "experience-bump" (a sensor platform) on that lump. Chapters: 35, 39, 43. Related: virtual-body, FWAU, bed-sheet-analogy.
- **Explorer's attitude** — the courageous, open-minded stance of contemplating the unknown and then stepping into it (experiencing it) to find out for yourself: "There is no other way." Chapters: 22. Related: open-minded-skepticism, subjective-science.
- **Expectation value / probable reality surface** — the computed probability that a possible future event actualizes; TBC's projected futures form a "probable reality surface" of peaks (sharp/tall near the present, flat/diffuse far out) recomputed every DELTA-t, over which focused intent can raise or lower a peak. Chapters: 46, 47, 79, 80, 81, 82, 83. Related: force-of-being, psi-uncertainty-principle, state-vector.
- **Fantasy / fantasy-bubble / fantasy support systems** — the belief-held structure the ego-intellect team builds to counterbalance each fear (a "house of cards" grown into a "castle"); a fantasy-bubble is the local reality one self-imprisons within, and family/friends/work/media are its support systems. Chapters: 42. Related: ego, belief-trap, delusion.
- **Fear** — at the deepest level generated by ignorance within a low-quality consciousness; "mind-cancer," a disease of consciousness residing in intent (not action), mutually reinforcing with high entropy; the opposite of love, corrected by knowledge and courage. Chapters: 42, 92. Related: ego, love, entropy.
- **Feel-good belief systems / emotional hooks** — cultural, personal, and religious systems that flatter the needy ego, made popular by alternating feel-bad (guilt, inadequacy) with feel-good (importance, salvation, superiority) "one-two punches" — a good-cop/bad-cop routine Campbell fights with blunt humor. Chapters: 38. Related: belief-trap, ego, cultural-bias.
- **Flatland / Flatlanders / Linelander** — E. A. Abbott's *Flatland* (2D beings unable to conceive the third dimension) is Campbell's canonical illustration of why residents of a constrained reality cannot comprehend a less constrained one; a Linelander cannot describe a sphere. Chapters: 20, 21, 24, 25, 41, 47, 48. Related: dimension, mystical, subset-superset.
- **Force of being** — the degree to which a possible event is connected to PMR reality — quantitatively, its expectation value — built up by the collective belief, certainty, and emotional investment of sentient beings; the greater the force of being, the more energy (up to "a miracle") is needed to change the event. Chapters: 47, 83. Related: expectation-value, psi-uncertainty-principle, miracle.
- **4 Hz pulsation state** — Bob Monroe's perceived 4 Hz oscillation in body and consciousness just before exiting the body in an OOBE; instrumented, it showed as unusually coherent, synchronized, 4 Hz-modulated brainwaves with GSR also at 4 Hz — the key artifact the lab tried to capture on demand. Chapters: 8, 9. Related: OOBE, EEG, GSR, binaural-beats.
- **Free will** — the capacity of consciousness to choose among its decision space; inseparable from consciousness ("without free will, there is no consciousness"), it operates primarily on intent/motivation (not action), makes AUM's study of consciousness "real science instead of circular logic," and must include the ability to choose badly. Chapters: 36, 37, 44, 45, 56, 58. Related: intent, consciousness, decision-space, pseudo-randomness.
- **FWAU (Free Will Awareness Unit)** — Campbell's technical name for what people call the "soul": the individuated nonphysical component of a being that makes choices, learns through feedback, and is recycled through many experience packets until it self-destructs or "ripens for harvesting." Chapters: 0, 39, 40, 50. Related: soul, over-soul, individuated-consciousness.
- **Frequency-space** — the domain in which AUM operates, with a basic time increment some eighty orders of magnitude smaller than a human second, letting it run many parallel PMRs effortlessly. Chapters: 68. Related: quantized-time, DELTA-t, TBC.
- **Fundamental Process (of evolution)** — Campbell's second basic assumption: spread potentiality into all available possibilities, invest only in the profitable ("winning") states, let the losers go, and iterate; a natural, self-initiating, non-sentient statistical process (no "evaluator" required) that is the same for atoms, organisms, technology, and consciousness — a process fractal. Chapters: 24, 25, 26, 27, 85, 92. Related: profitability, evolution, process-fractal, two-assumptions.
- **Fundamental quantum of time** — the smallest measurable unit of time (one half-cycle of AUM's clock / one reality-cell state change); all other clocks (NPMR's, PMR's DELTA-t) are integer multiples of it. Chapters: 29, 31. Related: quantized-time, DELTA-t, AUM's-clock.
- **Gedanken experiment** — a thought experiment, a logical experiment performed only in the mind; the whole space-time simulation is "the mother of all gedanken experiments" in AUM's mind-space. Chapters: 0, 31, 63, 68. Related: mind-space, simulation.
- **Good and evil (prototypes)** — value-orientations that coalesced in NPMR: serving self and using others (evil) versus using oneself to serve others (good); the motivational dynamics (love/knowledge/wisdom vs. fear/ego/ignorance) our reality experiment studies, and why these themes dominate every human culture's arts. Chapters: 43, 61. Related: rats-and-anti-rats, love, fear.
- **Group consciousness / group mind / group soul** — not a different kind of consciousness but individual consciousnesses sharing intent and data; it distributes information efficiently but its quality gravitates to the lowest common acceptable level. "Group souls" (species/genetic intelligence) name the collective intelligence of plants and simple critters. Chapters: 56, 58. Related: culture, herd-instinct, mob-consciousness.
- **GSR (Galvanic Skin Response)** — skin-response measure; in the 4 Hz pulsation state the GSR reading oscillates at 4 Hz. Chapters: 0, 8, 10. Related: EEG, 4-Hz-pulsation-state.
- **Guides / nonphysical beings / mentors / workers** — the nonphysical friends, teachers, and functionaries who (Campbell reports) mentored him in childhood, sealed his out-of-body passage, and remained reachable telepathically; older/wiser beings who provide intuition, counsel, and (probabilistic) future information. Chapters: 13, 14, 15, 16. Related: telepathy, nonphysical-friends, larger-plan.
- **Hardware / firmware / software** — Campbell's mapping of an entity's parts: hardware = body, firmware = instincts, software = mind; all three slowly improved by evolution. Chapters: 25. Related: consciousness, evolution.
- **Herd instinct / peer pressure / conceptual center vs. edge** — humans are social (herd) animals who seek safety in numbers and conformity; peer pressure is the "iron jaws" of the belief trap, big ideas come only from "the edge" (never the safe "center"), and a frightened herd "can become a mindless destroyer of the light." Chapters: 19, 86, 91, 92. Related: belief-trap, cultural-bias, mob-consciousness.
- **Hideous-truth-trauma (HTT) syndrome** — Campbell's coinage for the emotional disturbance and retreat-into-fantasy that results from shoving Big Truth into the intellect of an ego "entranced" in its own belief-castle. Chapters: 42. Related: ego, belief-trap, satori.
- **Hierarchy of causal systems / reality onion** — nested causal systems, each a subset of the next (PMR ⊂ NPMR ⊂ beyond-NPMR ...), where beginnings belong to the next-higher level and a level's dynamics can only be properly analyzed from the vantage of the level above it. Chapters: 18, 20, 83. Related: causality, mystical, subset-superset.
- **Hologram / fractal (part contains whole)** — the analogy that our consciousness reflects AUM's whole pattern as a piece of a hologram or an element of a fractal contains the whole; AUM is primary consciousness, we derivative. Chapters: 25, 44, 85. Related: consciousness-evolution-fractal, primary-vs-derivative-consciousness.
- **Humility / compassion** — the antitheses of ego ("what is left over when the ego is gone"), the correcting mechanism for ego; together with knowledge/courage (the corrective for fear) they yield love. Chapters: 42. Related: ego, love, balance.
- **Hz (Hertz)** — cycles per second; the unit for the 4 Hz pulsation and binaural-beat frequencies. Chapters: 0, 8, 9. Related: 4-Hz-pulsation-state, beat-frequency.
- **icip / RWW (Reality Wide Web)** — the "master network of all consciousness" over which minds (chips off the AUM block) communicate; Campbell's internet-style joke protocol is `icip://RWW.Individuated-consciousness.NPMR`, where every entity has a unique "address." Chapters: 0, 40, 59, 76, 79, 84. Related: telepathy, thought-packets, interconnectedness.
- **Immortality (digital)** — individuated consciousness lasts as long as the computer (AUM) unless deleted, disorganized beyond repair, or "irredeemably negative"; the mortality of the virtual physical body is merely a learning device. Chapters: 57, 61. Related: death-by-disassociation, over-soul, reality-cells.
- **Individuated consciousness / puddles / units of consciousness** — a bounded subset of AUM's digital content defined into existence by constraints, self-aware and free-willed, containing the growth potential of the whole; AUM studies consciousness by setting such "puddles" interacting in its mental Petri dish. Chapters: 24, 55, 56, 57, 58. Related: constraints, over-soul, FWAU.
- **Information filters** — access limits that may block a person from knowing the probable future when such knowledge would lower the probability of their growth — like an adult keeping a child from fire. Chapters: 83. Related: psi-uncertainty-principle, probable-reality-surface, system-administrator.
