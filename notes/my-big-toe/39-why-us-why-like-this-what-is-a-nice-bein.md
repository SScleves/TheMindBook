# Ch 39 — Why Us? Why Like This? What is a Nice Being Like You Doing in a Place Like This?

## In one paragraph
Campbell asks why AUM (the Absolute Unbounded Oneness / evolving consciousness system) built a 3D space-time reality for us rather than something stranger, such as hyperbolic 5D space-time, and answers that 3D is simpler and works better for the consciousness interactions and evolutionary possibilities AUM is exploring — and that if AUM wanted weirder realities, it would simply dedicate other groups of specialized reality cells to them in separate dimensions of its thought-space. He argues that AUM runs many parallel reality experiments (like a mainframe running multiple simulations, each with its own memory space, rule-set, and time loop) so that the Fundamental Process can statistically determine which sub-realities and rule-sets are most profitable, which explains the many PMR-like sub-realities Campbell says he has encountered in his own explorations. He then locates our reality within nested dimensions — NPMR, the NPMR-N set, and the PMR-k set — each governed by its own causality and rule-set, and asserts that PMR "physical" life-forms are just as much thought-forms within AUM as NPMR beings are; the physical/nonphysical distinction is relative to the observer's perspective. Finally, he derives what we are: individuated, constrained subsets of AUM consciousness whose bodies are "experience-bumps" carrying a low-end Free Will Awareness Unit (FWAU, colloquially the "soul"), designed to make choices, learn through feedback, and be recycled until the FWAU self-destructs or ripens for harvesting — a design shared, in dimmer form, by critters, since diversity of entities is a natural artifact of low-constraint evolution.

## Core claims
- Campbell claims AUM chose 3D space-time for our reality because 3D is simpler and works better for the consciousness interactions and evolutionary possibilities AUM is exploring — not because exotic alternatives (e.g., hyperbolic 5D space-time) are impossible.
- Campbell argues that if AUM wanted to explore a stranger reality, it would merely dedicate a group of specialized reality cells to it; each such reality would occupy its own dimension (its own section of memory) of AUM thought-space, mutually irrelevant to ours.
- Campbell claims it is reasonable to expect many ongoing parallel experiments within AUM to determine the most profitable set of constraints for designing the most effective learning labs.
- Campbell argues AUM needs a broad assortment of 3D implementations to get good statistical sampling — basing conclusions about 3D's profitability solely on our PMR's performance would be "risky and very bad science."
- Campbell reports (from his own explorations of the larger reality) that many PMR-like sub-realities exist, some with space-time similar to ours and others with obviously different rule-sets, and that the variety of life-forms, habitats, and reality dimensions within NPMR alone is immense.
- Campbell claims evolution explores all significant options within reach but progresses and maintains only the profitable ones; it "doesn't mourn the victims (failed experiments)." Our existence proves we are profitable to AUM — or at worst that our process's profitability is not yet determined.
- Campbell asserts a nested dimensional structure: NPMR in one part of AUM, the NPMR-N in another, the PMR-k in the space-time part — "dimension within dimension within dimension." Our PMR is one member of the set {PMR-k}.
- Campbell claims everything in our physical universe must behave and evolve within the constraints (physical causality) defined by our rule-set — cosmic, galactic, solar, and biological evolution on earth all obey the same rules — while other PMR-like realities may run different rule-sets (different physics), and NPMR evolution follows its own less restrictive causality.
- Campbell claims less constrained NPMRs evolve a more varied set of life-forms than more constrained PMRs — confirmed, he says, by his experience.
- Campbell asserts (promising demonstration in Section 4) that the difference between NPMR "thought-forms" and PMR "real life-forms" is only apparent: physical vs. nonphysical is relative to the observer's perspective, itself a function of the constraints and rule-sets defining his interaction with his local virtual reality. PMR life-forms are actually thought-forms within AUM too.
- Campbell claims AUM's overall purpose and nature trickle down to our purpose and nature: we are products of consciousness evolution, occupying a niche on the edge of an enormous consciousness ecosystem.
- Campbell defines us as individuated subsets of AUM consciousness: our awareness at its most fundamental level is constructed of a bounded group of reality cells constrained to perceive a specific set of causally related events under the space-time rule-set.
- Campbell claims the being we call ourselves does not depend on the physical body — it is the other way around: bodies are dense, space-time-constrained "experience-bumps" on the lumps in the AUM-sheet, equipped with a sensor platform (the body), a low-end FWAU, and "a lifetime guarantee."
- Campbell claims critters have consciousness and free will too — more limited and less individuated than ours — and souls as well, since they are constructs of consciousness like us; their nonphysical component is less unique, structured, and interactive, but calling them inferior is "little picture arrogance."
- Campbell claims FWAUs must be self-modifying learners, recycled again and again through challenging interactions until the FWAU self-destructs or ripens for harvesting; each FWAU's uniqueness and personal integrity (identity) is maintained to support cumulative accelerating growth.
- Campbell claims the fewer constraints limiting a consciousness, the lower its local entropy can go and the more effective and powerful it can become.
- Campbell claims that in the Big Picture it does not matter how mankind physically arrived on earth (simple evolution, rogue planet, UFOs, mystical eruption) — all physical processes and entities derive from the same source, the evolving AUM consciousness system; only superficial physical details differentiate the origin theories.

## Concepts
- **AUM (Absolute Unbounded Oneness)** — the evolving consciousness system whose thought-space contains all realities; it dedicates groups of specialized reality cells to particular reality experiments, seeks "profitable" evolutionary returns, and is the source from which all entities and physical processes derive. Its overall purpose and nature "trickle down" to ours.
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — our 3D space-time reality; one specific member of the set {PMR-k}. Everything within it must behave and evolve within the physical causality defined by its rule-set. Its "real" life-forms are actually thought-forms within AUM.
- **{PMR-k}** — Campbell's notation for the set of all PMR-like space-time realities (k = 1, 2, 3, …), each residing in the space-time part of AUM; different members may have different rule-sets (different physics).
- **NPMR (Nonphysical Matter Reality)** — the nonphysical part(s) of AUM's reality; its evolution follows its own less restrictive causality and unique rules, producing an immense variety of life-forms, habitats, and reality dimensions.
- **{NPMR-N}** — the set of specific NPMR realities (n = 1, 2, 3, … N, N+1, N+2, …), of which NPMR-N is one member; the NPMR-n occupy a different dimension of AUM than NPMR itself or the PMR-k — "dimension within dimension within dimension."
- **Reality cells** — the fundamental units of AUM consciousness; a bounded (individuated) group of them, constrained to perceive a specific set of causally related events, constitutes our conscious awareness at its most fundamental level (analogous to biological cells constituting the body).
- **Rule-set** — the set of constraints (e.g., physical causality, a given space-time's physics) that defines how a particular reality behaves and how observers within it interact with and perceive their local virtual reality. Each independent reality/simulation has its own.
- **Dimension** — in Campbell's usage, a separate section of memory within AUM thought-space in which an independent reality runs; multiple realities each exist in their own dimension.
- **Thought-space** — AUM's internal "space" of consciousness within which all reality dimensions exist.
- **The Fundamental Process (of evolution)** — the process that explores all significant options within reach and progresses/maintains only the profitable ones; working upon AUM, it discovers which sub-realities, rule-sets, and types of space are most functional and profitable. It doesn't mourn failed experiments.
- **Profitability** — the evolutionary return-on-investment criterion by which AUM keeps or discards reality experiments; "If we are here, we are profitable."
- **Learning lab** — Campbell's characterization of what realities like PMR are designed to be: environments whose constraint-sets are tuned (via parallel experiments) for effective consciousness learning.
- **FWAU (Free Will Awareness Unit)** — the individuated nonphysical component of a bump-being; makes choices, learns through evaluative feedback loops, is recycled through many challenging interactions until it self-destructs or ripens for harvesting; its uniqueness and identity are maintained for cumulative accelerating growth. People unfamiliar with "AUM's design drawings" call it a "soul," "spirit," or "the nonphysical component." Bodies come with a "low-end" FWAU. Its full workings and why its invention was a logical necessity are deferred to Section 4.
- **Experience-bumps / lump in the AUM-sheet** — Campbell's imagery for individuated beings: the being we call ourselves is a "lump in the AUM-sheet," and physical bodies are relatively dense, space-time-constrained, self-perpetuating, low-maintenance biological-matter "experience-bumps" on those lumps, serving as sensor platforms providing experience and immediate feedback.
- **Entropy of consciousness** — the quality-metric of a consciousness: the fewer constraints limiting it, the lower its local entropy can go and the more effective and powerful it becomes (fuller discussion deferred to Chapter 13 of Book 2).
- **Consciousness ecosystem / consciousness-evolution fractal ecosystem** — the enormous evolving system of consciousness in which we occupy a niche on the edge; different entity types occupy different niches and habitats within the same fractal ecosystem, all springing from the same source and processes.
- **Critters** — non-human sentient entities; they have consciousness, free will, and souls too, just dimmer, more limited, less individuated, with more restricted degrees of freedom for spiritual growth. Different, not inferior.
- **Little picture arrogance** — the proclivity to reinterpret differences (e.g., between humans and critters) as obvious human superiority.
- **Physical vs. nonphysical (relativity of)** — Campbell's claim that the distinction is only apparent, relative to an observer's perspective, which is a function of the constraints and rule-sets defining his interaction with his local virtual reality.
- **Virtual reality (local)** — the reality an observer interacts with and perceives as defined by his constraints and rule-sets; both PMR and NPMR are such local virtual realities within AUM.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **Hyperbolic 5D space-time thought experiment** — Campbell opens by asking why AUM didn't evolve hyperbolic 5D space-time (or something stranger) for us to experience. Answer: 3D is simpler and works better for AUM's purposes; a 5D reality would just be another dedicated group of reality cells — "the weird part of AUM" — in its own dimension, irrelevant to us. Shows that our reality's form is a pragmatic design choice, not a necessity.
- **Mainframe-running-multiple-simulations analogy** — multiple realities, each in its own dimension, are "roughly analogous" to a big mainframe computer running multiple similar and dissimilar simulations at once, each with its own memory space, rule-set, and time loop. "We do that all the time and it is no big deal." Grounds the multiple-realities claim in familiar computing practice.
- **Parallel experiments / statistical-sampling argument** — AUM assembles a broad assortment of 3D implementations to get good statistical sampling across 3D results; judging 3D's profitability from PMR alone would be "risky and very bad science." A science-methodology analogy justifying many PMR-like realities.
- **Campbell's own explorations of the larger reality** — he reports having "run into many PMR-like sub-realities" during his explorations: some with space-time similar to ours, others with obviously different rule-sets; and he cites his experience as confirming that less constrained NPMRs evolve more varied life-forms than constrained PMRs. Personal empirical claim supporting the parallel-experiments picture.
- **"We are here, therefore profitable" argument** — evolutionary inference: since evolution maintains only profitable options and we exist, AUM must be getting a significant return on its 3D investment (or at worst, the verdict is still out).
- **Set-notation model of nested realities** — NPMR-N as a member of {NPMR-N} (n = 1, 2, 3, … N, N+1, …) and PMR as a member of {PMR-k} (k = 1, 2, 3, …), housed in different dimensions of AUM: a formal model of "dimension within dimension within dimension."
- **Body-as-cells analogy** — just as the body is constructed of biological cells, our conscious awareness is constructed of an individuated/bounded group of reality cells constrained by the space-time rule-set.
- **The bump-consciousness design experiment** — Campbell walks through designing an experiment in "bump-consciousness": use a simple self-replicating design that evolves its own forms; give it limited awareness and functionality so it won't get stuck in intellectual endless loops thinking about interactions instead of experiencing them ("as did some of the less productive experiments AUM tried earlier"); make it self-modifying, capable of learning through multiple evaluative feedback loops; recycle it through many challenging interactions until the FWAU self-destructs or ripens for harvesting. Moving parts: self-replication, limited awareness, choice-making, feedback assessment, self-modification, recycling, maintained FWAU identity for cumulative growth. This is his model of why humans (and critters) are built the way we are.
- **Product-brochure imagery for the body** — biological-matter experience-bumps "come with a sensor platform called a 'body'…, a low-end Free Will Awareness Unit (FWAU), and a lifetime guarantee" — satirical marketing framing of the human design package.
- **"AUM's design drawings" nomenclature joke** — people unfamiliar with the nomenclature on AUM's design drawings call the FWAU a "soul," "spirit," or "nonphysical component"; leads into the claim that critters have souls too.
- **Origin-theories list** — however you believe mankind arrived on earth (simple evolution making the most of random events, a rogue planet in a highly elliptical orbit, UFOs, a mystical eruption), all are superficially different physical details; at root everything derives from the evolving AUM consciousness system.

## Thread
Sitting early in Section 3 ("Man in the Loop"), this chapter answers the section's title question: it places humans inside AUM's evolutionary loop. It follows from Book 2's preceding chapters establishing AUM as an evolving digital consciousness system running reality experiments, and applies that machinery downward — from AUM's choice of 3D space-time, through the nested dimensional structure of {NPMR-N} and {PMR-k}, to the derivation of the individual human as a constrained subset of AUM consciousness with a body-sensor-platform and an FWAU. It explicitly sets up later material: the demonstration that physical/nonphysical is observer-relative and that PMR life-forms are thought-forms (Section 4), the FWAU's workings and the logical necessity of its invention (Section 4), AUM's earlier less productive experiments (Sections 4 and 5), and the entropy of consciousness (Chapter 13 of Book 2). Its closing move — that all human-origin theories differ only in superficial physical detail — clears the ground for treating consciousness evolution, not physical history, as the Big Picture story.
