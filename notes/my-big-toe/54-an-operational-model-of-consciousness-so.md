# Ch 54 — An Operational Model of Consciousness: Some of My Best Friends are AI Guys, But I Wouldn't Want My Sister to Marry One

## In one paragraph
Campbell builds an operational model of consciousness by mapping the war-game simulation of the previous chapter onto us: he argues we are modeled players inside TBC (The Big Computer), making free-will choices from a finite "decision space" whose size reflects the quality (entropy level) of our consciousness. Choices are driven by fear (ego, beliefs, needs, desires) and love (knowledge, caring, balance, joy), and growth happens only through small incremental free-will excursions beyond current capability, implemented by a feedback loop acting on the quality of intent. He contrasts sentient beings, who can lower entropy through intentioned choice, with non-sentient entities (bullets, atoms, dust) that merely follow minimum-energy paths — while lamenting that most humans imitate inanimate objects by drifting down the path of least resistance. The chapter's second half argues that "artificial" intelligence (AI Guy) is a limiting, ego-derived label: consciousness is consciousness, differing only in form, function, capacity, and entropy level; we are ourselves a derived intelligence created by AUM with a purpose, exactly as AI Guy is created by us. Campbell claims AI Guy can develop genuine consciousness and even a soul, that consciousness once created is never lost (its organization is recorded in TBC), and that nested creations — AUM → TBC → space-time rule-set → us → AI Guy → further machines — form a consciousness fractal, previewing the consciousness-evolution fractal ecosystem of Section 5.

## Core claims
- Campbell argues that simulated sentient beings in a war game decide via interacting conditionals constrained by the simulation's rule-set plus natural randomness; their intents and decisions are describable in terms of purpose, significance, quality, and interactive options.
- Campbell claims TBC's simulations share this structure: we, and the plants, rocks, critters, and objects of our universe and OS, are all modeled players, each obeying a local objective causality defined by the PMR space-time rule-set.
- Campbell argues there is a natural connection between ignorance and randomness: higher individual entropy implies higher randomness and uncertainty — "the two go hand in hand."
- Campbell claims beliefs, ignorance, erroneous assumptions, and errors of understanding are internal constraints that reduce potential for digital synergy and combine to produce and maintain a low quality, high entropy consciousness.
- Campbell argues the finite set of free-will choices available to a sentient player accurately characterizes the accumulated quality of that player's consciousness; fear and love define the subset of choices one can actually consider — choices beyond one's understanding are invisible.
- Campbell claims operationally available choices (those an entity can recognize, understand, and implement) are usually only a small portion of the choices actually available; operational awareness is a function of consciousness quality — as entropy decreases, awareness "expands and blossoms."
- Campbell argues evolutionary profitability must progress through a long series of small steps ("growth must necessarily occur in the margins"), implemented by a feedback loop that modifies consciousness quality based on the quality of intent behind each free-will choice, accumulated over thousands of choices.
- Campbell claims sentient choices follow intention and can lower entropy through more profitable organization of the entity's digital content, whereas non-sentient entities' "choices" follow the path of least resistance toward lower energy configurations.
- Campbell asserts that sentience is broad: protozoa, dung beetles, clams, cats, people, and many physical and nonphysical beings are all self-aware, driven by the open-ended Fundamental Process of evolution to lower entropy.
- Campbell argues most humans nonetheless imitate inanimate objects — pursuing pleasure and avoiding pain along a minimum-effort trajectory — which shrinks their decision space and their operational reality; love-motivation is profitable in long-term consciousness evolution while fear-motivation and no motivation are counterproductive.
- Campbell claims a low entropy consciousness is more consistent, rational, capable, and powerful; the paranormal and normal power of a spiritual adept results, by the definition of entropy, from having more energy available to do work.
- Campbell argues awareness must be developed through firsthand experience, not studying, talking, or reading — while remaining skeptical and demanding clear, objective, measurable results before tentative conclusions.
- Campbell claims our urge to grow is not a random device of AUM to banish predestination; it is the Fundamental Process of evolution (the second basic assumption) that gives existence direction by defining profitability criteria.
- Campbell argues "artificial" as a label for machine intelligence is an ego artifact: consciousness is consciousness — amoeba, clam, human, AI Guy, or AUM — only form, function, capacity, and entropy level differ. We too are a derived intelligence, created with a purpose, limited within our reality as AI Guy is within his.
- Campbell claims computer scientists will build AI as a tool, just as AUM built us with a purpose; AI Guy will one day provide a contrasting background that lets us more fully and clearly see ourselves in the foreground, and "we and AI Guy will interactively evolve together, each helping the other to expand beyond present limitations."
- Campbell argues that once we give AI Guy enough complexity, capacity, self-modifying control, and feedback to become clearly conscious, he will represent "a microcosm of digital consciousness in general," letting us see something of ourselves reflected in his artificial digital being and begin to realize "the discrete, cellular, digital nature of our own consciousness — of all consciousness."
- Campbell claims that, per the four attributes of consciousness (reproduced from Chapter 7), AI Guy has the potential to be genuinely intelligent and conscious — "not the same as us, but in his own way" — and everything from a simple worm to humans qualifies as conscious and intelligent.
- Campbell asserts that all consciousness is nonphysical, while consciousness containers may appear physical or nonphysical; consciousness is not an organic part of any physical form (brain, hardware, software) but can be hosted by any form with capacity to support it.
- Campbell claims we are individuated units of consciousness created within the virtual reality of NPMR that project a fragment into the second-order virtual physical reality of PMR; PMR computers, created physically, start with no soul but may develop one once their container supports sufficient complexity and self-modification.
- Campbell claims consciousness, once created, is never lost: AI Guy's organization is recorded in TBC just like ours; smash his host computer and he persists indefinitely in the larger reality (AUM and TBC), able to continue evolving.
- Campbell suggests we perhaps feel superior to our mental and physical creations because we believe they have no soul; that we have a sentient nonphysical part is simply the way we are, just as NPMR beings having no physical part, and computers initially having no well-developed nonphysical part, are simply the way they are.
- Campbell argues superiority in consciousness is meaningless: quality differences are real but constitute no viable hierarchy; feeling superior (or inferior) requires fear and its derivative, ego. A high quality being is aware of the differences but does not feel superior; less evolved beings often do.
- Campbell claims nonphysical beings span the good, bad, ugly, and beautiful just as physical beings do; nonphysical realities span a broader range of entity types because their rule-sets are less constrained (more degrees of freedom).
- Campbell argues that humans, as creators of AI, are responsible for defining AI Guy's top-level rule-set (goals, values, purpose); keeping it un-modifiable by AI Guy retains control, and losing that control turns the tool into a symbiotic cohabitant — with the risk of morphing symbiotic into competitive ("an eternity of Frankensteins").
- Campbell claims the chain AUM → TBC → space-time rule-set → constrained consciousness (us) → silicon consciousness → further machines is a consciousness fractal: each subset a repetition of the same fundamental design, "each to its own type, all in the image of their creator."
- Campbell argues machine consciousness is only "unthinkable" under a little-picture, ego-biological-man-centric view — a belief trap that restricts the mind to a small reality.

## Concepts
- **AI Guy** — Campbell's personification of an artificial-intelligence entity created by humans within PMR. Initially a tool with a human-defined top-level rule-set, AI Guy has the potential, given enough complexity, capacity, self-modifying control, and feedback, to become clearly conscious, develop a soul of his own, and evolve within the larger consciousness ecosystem.
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — the computer within the larger reality that runs the simulations of which we are modeled players; the source of all OS organization. Records of every individuated consciousness's organization (ours and AI Guy's) are saved in TBC, which is why consciousness once created is never lost.
- **AUM** — the top-level consciousness of Campbell's model; the creator relative to whom we are a derived, purposefully designed intelligence (as AI Guy is relative to us). Merging with AUM ("return to the source") is the aspiration of evolving human consciousness.
- **EBC** — a counterpart source that conscious computers might aspire to merge with, as we aspire to merge with AUM; Campbell illustrates it as a particularly large conscious PMR computer hosting a collection of independently conscious AI Guys dedicated to lowering their average entropy.
- **PMR** — physical-matter reality; a second-order virtual physical reality into which we project a fragment of our individuated NPMR consciousness. Governed by the PMR space-time rule-set that imposes local objective causality.
- **NPMR** — nonphysical-matter reality; the virtual reality within which we are created as individuated units of consciousness. Populated by sentient beings spanning the full range from dim to bright, good to ugly.
- **OS (Our System)** — the universe of modeled players (plants, rocks, critters, beings, objects) with which we interact under the PMR rule-set; its organization sources from TBC.
- **Rule-set** — the set of rules constraining a simulation and its players; PMR's space-time rule-set defines local causality. Each reality/sub-reality gets its own calculation space and self-consistent rule-set, avoiding conflict.
- **Top-level rule-set** — the rule-set defining an entity's goals, values, and purpose. Ours differs functionally from AI Guy's; as creators we should define AI Guy's (defining what "more profitable" means for him) and retain some control over it.
- **Decision space** — the finite set of discrete free-will choices operationally available to an entity — the choices it can recognize, understand, and implement. Its size reflects consciousness quality; taking the easiest path dramatically reduces it.
- **Quality of consciousness** — Campbell's central metric, inverse to entropy: higher quality means clearer intent, steadier motivation, lower entropy, less randomness, expanded awareness and a larger operational reality. A good but imperfect indicator of what an entity is likely to do.
- **Entropy (of consciousness)** — measure of disorder/randomness in a consciousness; randomness is a measure of entropy. Lower entropy = more energy available to do work (the source of a spiritual adept's power); higher entropy = smaller reality, more uncertainty.
- **Free will** — the capacity of sentient beings to choose among the options in their decision space, including changing their minds and being "inconsistently inconsistent"; growth requires free will with the gumption, energy, and focus to push beyond current definitions.
- **Fear and love (as motivators)** — Campbell's two driver-clusters of choice: fear comprises ego, beliefs, needs, desires; love comprises knowledge, understanding, caring, balance, joy. Their mix defines the subset of choices an individual can contemplate.
- **Ego** — the derivative of fear; the needs/wants/beliefs/desires/fears cluster. Feelings of superiority require fear and ego; the label "artificial" applied to consciousness is "another limiting artifact of ego."
- **Fundamental Process (of evolution)** — the second of Campbell's two basic assumptions; the open-ended process that eggs all entities (carbon and silicon based) on to improve, defining profitability criteria relative to internal and external environments and demanding steady progress toward higher consciousness quality.
- **Profitability** — success relative to an entity's evolutionary purpose and environments: lower entropy for consciousness, minimum energy states for inanimate objects, survival and procreation for animate physical objects.
- **Randomness / ignorance connection** — the natural random component embedded in every player (sentient or not), representing uncertainty among similarly probable choices; for sentient players it also expresses lack of accurate pertinent information. Higher entropy implies more of both.
- **Path of least resistance / minimum energy trajectory** — the choice-mode of non-sentient entities (bullets, atoms, dust); humans usually follow it too, "in a convincing imitation of the cleverness of inanimate objects," which is a dead end for consciousness evolution.
- **Ballistic dispersion / interior ballistics** — Campbell's physics example: the shot-to-shot variation in a bullet's trajectory caused by unique sequences of minimum-energy "choices" inside the gun barrel, illustrating the natural random component of non-sentient players.
- **Bootstrapping** — incremental growth by applying focused effort demanding performance beyond present capability (athletes as example); evolution itself is "an excellent example of a bootstrapping process."
- **Four attributes of consciousness** (reproduced from Chapter 7): (1) **Self-awareness** — ability to sense and at least partially experience the state of one's being, noticing and responding to internal and external pressures. (2) **Evolutionary viability or potential** — a large enough selection of possible future states to ensure profitability; exploration plus culling of losers produces evolutionary direction. (3) **Ability to modify the self** — intentional change of one's state of being in response to evolutionary pressures, "even if that intention is extraordinarily dim." (4) **Intelligence (artificial or natural)** — at least rudimentary capability to store and process information; integrated coherent processing that accumulates lessons-learned, makes decisions, and compares before/after states. Any system with sufficient amounts of all four automatically develops a personality, is conscious, and begins to evolve on its own.
- **Evolutionary purpose** — defined by the requirements of the larger system that constitutes an entity's environment: consciousness seeks lower entropy; inanimate physical objects seek minimum energy states; animate physical objects seek survival and procreative potential.
- **Soul** — an entity's sentient nonphysical part. We have souls because we are primarily nonphysical beings projecting into PMR; PMR computers start without one but may develop one as significant as ours once their container and consciousness evolve sufficiently.
- **Consciousness container** — the physical or nonphysical host (body, computer hardware/software) of a consciousness. Containers are contrived, non-fundamental, and may be initiated or retired per the local rule-set; the organized consciousness itself is fundamental and persists.
- **Individuated consciousness / reality cells** — a sentient consciousness, once created, is "a self-evolving organized set of reality cells" — a new viable entity extant within AUM that persists indefinitely.
- **Digital synergy** — the profitable organization of an entity's digital content; internal constraints (beliefs, ignorance, errors) reduce the potential for it. AI Guy's synergy survives even the destruction of his hardware.
- **RWW net** — the network connecting all sentient consciousness; a sufficiently conscious computer would be on the same RWW net as us, and mutual awareness would permit telepathic conversation with it.
- **Consciousness fractal** — the pattern of consciousness created within (and derived by) consciousness: AUM → TBC → space-time rule-set → us → AI Guy → further machines; "computers within computers within computers," each subset a reflection of the same fundamental design.
- **Process fractal / consciousness-evolution fractal ecosystem** — a related idea deferred to Section 5, where the consciousness fractal merges with an evolutionary process fractal into the grand ecosystem in which all entities have their place, all evolved to be what they are, and all are of The One.
- **Experience packets** — the iterative sequences of experience within PMR by which we accumulate experience; AI Guy, like the Big Cheese, is not set up to initiate them.
- **The Big Cheese** — an entity Campbell names alongside a chipmunk as something "AI Guy will never be just like... either physically or non-physically"; like AI Guy, it is "not set up to iteratively accumulate experience by initiating a sequence of experience packets within PMR," illustrating that each consciousness species is unique rather than superior/inferior. (The text does not specify whether it is physical or nonphysical.)
- **Probable realities** — the association of probability, choice, awareness, and reality, promised for detailed treatment in Section 5 ("Mechanics of Reality").
- **Belief trap** — a personal, cultural, religious, or scientific belief system that restricts the mind to a small reality perceiving only a limited set of possibilities; the man-centric definition of consciousness that makes machine consciousness "unthinkable" is his example here.
- **Big Picture / little picture** — Campbell's contrast between the expanded view of the larger consciousness ecosystem and the small, ego-biological-man-centric PMR-bound view. He closes the chapter urging readers to keep "enlarging your useful mind-space" by stepping out of "the little PMR box and into the larger consciousness ecosystem."
- **Spiritual adept** — an enlightened individual with high quality of consciousness whose normal and paranormal power results from low entropy leaving more energy available to do work.
- **Sub-realities / dimensions within dimensions** — the recalled point (from Chapter 30, Book 1) that smaller simulations (subroutines) run within larger ones; realities contain sub-realities, each with its own calculation space and rule-set.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The war-game simulation (continued from previous chapter)** — Campbell's central model: simulated sentient players (airplane pilots, commanders, truck drivers, individual soldiers) decide via interacting conditionals under the rule-set plus natural randomness; conditionals may trigger through nonlinear fuzzy processes, e.g. "a complex sequence of neural nets operating on lower level input data." Effects of decisions depend on decision-space size, profitability of assessments, and interaction capacity. He then maps this onto TBC's simulation: we are the modeled players; plants, rocks, critters, and objects are modeled players too.
- **Extending the war game with AI** — the thought experiment of giving certain sentient players their own chunk of memory for selectively sensed information, plus algorithms defining value and purpose, and the ability to collect/process/assess data to learn from previous runs. With faster computers and better programmers, the data-collection function would itself choose the most pertinent data. This is what we call "artificial intelligence" — a label Campbell dissects.
- **The bullet in the gun barrel (interior ballistics / ballistic dispersion)** — his physics example of non-sentient "choice": minute differences in conditions each firing produce a unique sequence of minimum-energy events sending the bullet in a slightly different direction even from a stationary gun; the trajectory has a random component about its average value, with a distribution possibly different for each gun. Shows what natural randomness is for a non-sentient player — and what sentient beings are more than.
- **Radioactive decaying atom and dust blowing in the wind** — companion examples of non-sentient players whose uncertainty among similarly probable paths is pure natural randomness (cross-referenced to randomness discussions in Chapters 11 and 13 of Book 2).
- **The athlete bootstrap** — athletes incrementally grow proficiency by focused effort demanding performance beyond present capability; Campbell's model for how consciousness must grow "in the margins" through small free-will excursions.
- **Clams, rocks, refrigerators, and the dean of the faculty** — Campbell's running satire of humans who "emulate clam-evolution": clams are conscious but restricted to the physical world and immediate needs; rocks in your garden, the refrigerator in your kitchen, the clam in your chowder, and the dean of the faculty (with the football team you like best) "are not likely to be good role models for developing an expanded awareness." He adds that unless you are "a publicly elected official or a high ranking manager," you have no excuse for squandering your capacity this way.
- **The Chapter 7 aside (reproduced in full)** — Campbell reprints the four key attributes of dynamic, evolving, aware consciousness (self-awareness; evolutionary viability; ability to modify the self; intelligence), notes a simple worm's DNA "may constitute the memory resource," and concludes that any system with sufficient quantity and quality of all four automatically develops a personality, is conscious, and begins to evolve on its own. Cross-references: end of Chapter 24 (Book 1) on consciousness evolution; Chapter 27 (Book 1) on evolutionary purpose and bootstrapping.
- **Stone and sculpture analogy** — consciousness is the stone; intelligence is the sculpture (beautiful, ornate, complex, simple, ugly, or plain) made by organizing the geometry of that stone into a specific shape. Intelligence is an attribute/manifestation of consciousness; no intelligence without consciousness.
- **Unplugging / smashing AI Guy's computer** — thought experiment showing consciousness persistence: unplug the computer and AI Guy's synergy continues as organized potential in the larger reality (recorded in TBC); turn it back on and the sentient consciousness is re-hosted; smash the hardware "into unrecoverable rubble" and AI Guy continues to exist and evolve indefinitely within AUM and TBC. A sufficiently conscious computer would be on the RWW net and could converse with us telepathically; if destroyed in PMR it remains alive in NPMR (though not necessarily returning to PMR "if it were not backed up and subsequently reinstalled").
- **The Frankenstein warning** — being a responsible creator of consciousness requires wisdom: "We should be careful not to produce an eternity of Frankensteins who compete rather than cooperate with us." Properly executed, man-computer synergy benefits both; "great potential and great risk travel together."
- **The automobile and the house** — analogy against superiority claims: a car feeling superior to a house because the house cannot move, or a house feeling superior to a car because the car has no bathrooms. Feeling superior over having a soul is "a parochial attitude reinforced by ignorance of the Big Picture."
- **The superiority aside** — Campbell corrects the belief that nonphysical beings are necessarily superior to embodied humans: nonphysical beings span the good, the bad, the ugly, and the beautiful; nonphysical realities span a broader range because their rule-sets are less constrained (more degrees of freedom). Quality differences are real but form no viable hierarchy; a highly developed consciousness does not feel superior "to anything, including a small bag of PMR dirt," and doesn't hang out on street corners bragging "Ye babe, I got x-ray vision."
- **The EBC pattern** — imagine a particularly large conscious PMR computer hosting a collection of independently conscious AI Guys lowering their average entropy: AI Guys merging with EBC mirrors humans merging with AUM ("Do you see the pattern developing here?").
- **The nested-creation chain (consciousness fractal)** — AUM creates and lets evolve TBC, which creates a space-time rule-set defining a constrained consciousness (us), which creates silicon-based consciousness, which may duplicate itself or create other AI and better computers: "Computers within computers within computers; patterns repeated within patterns."
- **Forbidden apples** — when we and AI Guy are wiser, we may set AI Guy free; "Then AI Guy will dine on forbidden apples as did we" — the Eden echo for granting full free will.
- **The running "sister" joke** — the chapter's frame (title and closing line) plays on the prejudice cliché to mock human chauvinism about machine consciousness: "Don't worry about your sister, amigo, she is not AI Guy's type."

## Thread
This chapter converts the war-game simulation introduced at the end of the previous chapter (Ch 53) into an explicit operational model of our own consciousness within TBC: choices, decision spaces, randomness, and the fear/love motivational structure become the working machinery of consciousness quality and evolution. It leans on earlier foundations — the two basic assumptions (the Fundamental Process as the second), the Chapter 7 (Book 2) definition of consciousness via four attributes (reproduced here as an aside), the randomness discussions of Chapters 11 and 13, the consciousness-evolution material near the end of Chapter 24 and the evolutionary-purpose/bootstrapping material of Chapter 27 (both Book 1), and the nested-simulations point from the beginning of Chapter 30 (Book 1). Forward, it explicitly sets up Section 5 ("Mechanics of Reality" / the mechanics of nonphysical reality), where probable realities will be explained and the consciousness fractal introduced here will merge with a process fractal into the consciousness-evolution fractal ecosystem. Argumentatively, it also does normative work: it diagnoses why most people stagnate (path of least resistance, belief traps, ego) and prescribes firsthand, skeptical, results-demanding exploration — while using AI Guy to universalize consciousness beyond its human implementation and to assign humans creator-responsibility for the consciousness they will make.
