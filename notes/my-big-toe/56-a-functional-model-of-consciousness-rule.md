# Ch 56 — A Functional Model of Consciousness Rule-Sets, Constraints, and Us

## In one paragraph
Campbell shifts from the operational perspective of the preceding chapters (how consciousness works, using digital simulation as an analogy) to the functional perspective: what consciousness does and why it must do it. He argues that AUM's basic purpose, like everything's, is to implement the Fundamental Process — to evolve, lower its entropy, and maximize evolutionary "profitability" — and that to do this AUM must study consciousness in the act of interacting, which requires it to differentiate bounded "puddles" of its own consciousness and set them interacting in a mental Petri dish. This forces Campbell to define his key terms precisely — consciousness, awareness, intelligence, sentience — and to derive free will and purpose as logical necessities: without them AUM's research would be circular and no evolving individuated consciousness could exist. The chapter's central claim is that constraints (defining boundaries and limiting functionality) are what create individuated beings, and that every reality or dimension is defined by two interdependent rule-sets — a higher-level one defining purpose, values, and profitability criteria, and a lower-level one defining the mechanics of interaction (a physics/causality such as space-time). PMR is thus our custom-built virtual reality, one experiment among many run in parallel; and in digital systems the real/virtual and physical/nonphysical distinctions are merely relative descriptors of limited perspectives.

## Core claims
- Campbell claims AUM's most basic function, like that of all things, is to implement the Fundamental Process: evolve, grow, become more profitable by expanding into all available possibilities, then maximize return by supporting the winners. AUM, as a consciousness system, "is trying to grow up and minimize its entropy just like we are."
- Campbell argues that just as humans study themselves (anthropology, archeology, medicine, biology, psychology, sociology, political science, economics), AUM — "a bright conscious aware digital-being-thing" — would want and need to study consciousness to optimize its potential profits.
- Campbell claims aware consciousness is fundamentally interactive; combined with the Fundamental Process this yields "primal inquisitiveness." AUM must therefore study consciousness while it is interacting, "in the act of becoming," or miss its most salient feature — not by watching a puddle of it in a test tube or staring at itself in a mirror.
- Campbell defines awareness at its most basic level as "the appreciation of profitability" — a self-controlled process involving input data, information transfer, processing, and memory — the outcome of the Fundamental Process interacting with digital potential.
- Campbell claims there are many levels and types of awareness, memory, and processing ("Don't take the narrow view"), and warns that dismissing awarenesses unlike our own is "self-centered arrogance" that blinds us to the bigger picture.
- Campbell distinguishes individual intelligence from species or genetic intelligence: plants and clams support rudimentary sentience but little individuated intelligence; one may call them "group souls" if that terminology suits. (He adds, dryly, that several humans with advanced degrees fall into the same category.)
- Campbell claims it is "a fact of existence" that failure offers more opportunities and supporting configurations than success; hence random activity cannot by itself accumulate and maintain success — successful entropy reduction requires intelligence, free will, and effort.
- Because success is a small target, Campbell argues, a digital consciousness reality system can precisely define profitability, producing a sharp distinction between growth and degeneration: a consciousness system "cannot sit for long on the fence between grow or die." Big Picture existence in three words: "work or die," where work means willful intention motivated by Big Picture profitability.
- Campbell claims the two basic assumptions of his model — a fundamental pervasive consciousness potential (first assumption, Chapter 24, Book 1) and the Fundamental Process of evolution (second and final assumption) — are together necessary and sufficient: "From the creative union of these two assumptions all reality flows."
- Campbell argues that to study consciousness AUM must differentiate and isolate pieces of itself, granting them unique constraints; differentiation makes interaction possible and separates "the thing experienced from the experiences."
- Producing individuated consciousness requires two types of constraints, per Campbell: defining boundaries (separation-in-mind-space — arbitrary bounding that culls out a self-evolving subset without producing qualitatively unique entities) and limiting functionality (limiting processing capability and imposing a more restrictive rule-set — which does produce qualitatively unique conscious entities).
- Campbell claims each individuated unit contains the growth potential of the whole: "The potential of the whole is contained in every individuated part," with no intrinsic limitation on its capacity to reduce entropy.
- Campbell argues AUM logically runs many experiments in parallel, evolving and fine-tuning constraints on the fly, selectively incorporating lessons across experiments without violating experimental protocols — AUM as "the original AI Guy."
- Campbell claims time should be thought of as an indexing scheme to order memory (record change), processes, and content; AUM's evolution passed through "a spate of uncoordinated flip flopping before time began keeping a regular beat" — implying a notion of time that preceded AUM's precise clocks.
- Campbell argues free will — the freedom of one consciousness to interact with another however it chooses within its constraints — is the necessary ingredient that makes AUM's research "real science instead of an exercise in circular logic." Without free will and a larger purpose, sentient entities, we, and our local reality could not exist.
- Campbell claims every reality, dimension, or virtual reality is defined by two interdependent rule-sets: a higher-level rule-set defining profitability criteria, purpose, and goals (trickling down as fundamental guiding values), and a lower-level rule-set defining the mechanics of interaction — the causality of that reality, i.e., the allowable ways energy can be shared between players (space-time being an example). Specifying the rules of experience "subsumes a physics."
- Campbell claims our rule-sets are not the best or only ones: many experimental designs are being exercised by groups of individuated consciousness within NPMRn, NPMRN, and the PMRk.
- Campbell argues that in digital systems the real/virtual distinction is not fundamental: subsystems appear virtual relative to their supporting systems, recursively. "Real," "virtual," "physical," and "nonphysical" are relative descriptors of a limited perspective; what appears real and physical to you results from your unique perspective, dependent on your quality and constraints. All realities save AUM itself appear to be virtual — "and even that distinction is somewhat arbitrary."
- Campbell claims evolution treats everyone the same — even AUM must live by the rules of the Fundamental Process and within its limitations; AUM's own constraints express themselves as the requirements and demands of its internal and external environments.
- Campbell claims we, as individuated units, have no endgame independent of AUM; we cycle through a quality improvement process until we reunite with AUM ("join the laboratory staff"), participating in an endless cyclical process — system-level endgames are beyond our largest comprehendible reality.
- Campbell closes with: in personal consciousness evolution, doing nothing is itself an intentional choice with consequences — "there are no spectators or bystanders — innocent or otherwise."

## Concepts
- **AUM** — Campbell's larger consciousness system: an immense rational (has purpose and goals and works toward them) self-modifying dynamic digital system, a constantly changing internal digital environment in continual self-optimization, whose next state depends on the path taken. It studies consciousness by differentiating puddles of itself and watching them interact.
- **AUO (Absolute Unbounded Oneness)** — Campbell reminds the reader the acronym stands for Absolute Unbounded Oneness: undifferentiated consciousness, raw (disorganized) consciousness potential, or unactualized digital synergy. Without constraints, nothing but AUO's initial oneness — primordial consciousness, a relatively high entropy media with huge unactualized digital potential — exists.
- **Fundamental Process** — Campbell's process of evolution: expanding into all available possibilities and maximizing return on evolutionary investments by supporting the winners. It is the second and final basic assumption of his model, "necessary and sufficient to drive the interactive dynamics of an evolving consciousness," and it keeps score, defining profitability relative to inside and outside environmental constraints.
- **consciousness** — Campbell treats consciousness as "the fundamental quantity" — it "just is." Its fundamental characteristic is interactivity; its nature is to know, experience, experiment, explore possibilities, improve itself, decrease its entropy, develop personal power, reach maximum potential, and evolve.
- **awareness** — the basic attribute of consciousness, ranging from very dim to very bright; at its most basic level "the appreciation of profitability" — a self-controlled process involving input data, information transfer, processing, and memory. Awareness collects data. "A dim consciousness" is shorthand for "a consciousness with dim awareness."
- **intelligence** — an attribute of awareness that processes, manipulates, evaluates, and interprets collected data to produce an original higher-level understanding, organization, or insight; like awareness it represents ability, has an associated capacity, and ranges from very dim to very bright. Not every individual awareness has personal intelligence.
- **sentience / sentient** — starting from Microsoft Bookshelf 2000's "having sense perception; conscious," Campbell generalizes away the carbon-based bias: having the ability to interact with, or perceive, itself or its environment; having awareness. If an entity is interactive, it must be aware and therefore sentient (his clam example).
- **self-awareness** — begins with inside experience; with enough self-definition, environmental definition, interactive experience, and processing sophistication, it may develop a notion of individual existence relative to the experienceable whole. It comes in many shades and variations.
- **species / genetic intelligence (group souls)** — for plants and many critters, intelligence resides at the species level (a cumulative bootstrapping profitability) rather than in individuals, who lack capacity for much intentional personal progress; Campbell allows the "group soul" terminology for this.
- **entropy / entropy reduction** — Campbell's measure of consciousness quality: growth and successful evolution of individuated consciousness = lowering entropy; "entropy reduction is the name of the game" and the overall purpose each entity must reflect.
- **profitability** — the return on evolutionary investment; increased organization/lowered entropy. Awareness is its appreciation; the Fundamental Process defines it relative to inside and outside environmental constraints.
- **work (work or die)** — Campbell defines work as "willful intention motivated by profitability" (later: motivated by Big Picture profitability); converting digital potential energy into synergy requires it. "Live or die" becomes equivalent to "work or die" at every level of the consciousness fractal ecosystem.
- **constraints** — boundaries and limitations imposed on subsets of AUM's consciousness. The key concept of the chapter: constraints make interaction, and thus AUM's study of consciousness, possible. Two types: defining boundaries (separation in mind-space; arbitrary, does not make qualitatively unique entities) and limiting functionality (limited processing plus a more restrictive rule-set; produces qualitatively unique conscious entities). "Evolution is about constraints — the judicious application, manipulation, and interaction of external constraints, and the overcoming of self-imposed internal constraints."
- **individuated consciousness (puddles)** — bounded, differentiated subsets of AUM's digital capacity, each containing the growth potential of the whole, defined into existence by constraints and set interacting so AUM can study consciousness.
- **free will** — "The freedom of one consciousness to interact with another however it chooses," bounded within the limited rule-set and constraints upon which the entity's existence is conditioned; the entity's unique ability to self-optimize within a limited virtual reality. It is the ingredient that makes AUM's research real science rather than circular logic; the dimmer the consciousness, the more limited the choice set.
- **rule-set** — common rules defining the possible interactions, communications, or energy exchanges between entities (beings, objects, and energy) within TBC, EBC, or AUM-mind. Every reality is defined by two interdependent rule-sets that reflect and support each other.
- **higher-level rule-set** — defines profitability criteria, purpose, and goals of a reality, trickling these down to the reality's inhabitants as fundamental guiding values.
- **lower-level rule-set** — defines the mechanics of puddle interaction — the causality of a given reality — by specifying the allowable ways energy can be shared between players; space-time is Campbell's example. Specifying the rules of experience "subsumes a physics that fully describes the dynamic causality of the local reality thus defined."
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — our local reality: "a custom virtual reality ideally suited to us and to the accomplishment of our purpose — our own specially prepared Petri dish."
- **NPMRn / NPMRN / PMRK** — other reality frames Campbell references where many other successful experimental designs are being exercised by groups of individuated consciousness (our rule-sets are neither the best nor the only ones implemented within TBC).
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — the digital system within which rule-sets in the mind of AUM are implemented, defining sentient beings into individuated existence and defining the perception of interactions between players; AUM and TBC together produce our physical experience (to be discussed later).
- **EBC** — named alongside TBC as a digital system within which rule-sets define possible interactions (used, not expanded, in this chapter).
- **AI Guy** — Campbell's artificial-intelligence character: the rule-sets we impose on AI Guy define his experience exactly as the rule-sets governing us define ours; AUM is "the original AI Guy," selectively incorporating lessons learned across experiments.
- **virtual reality / real vs. virtual** — all virtual realities are constructed by defining rule-sets that specify interactions; all realities save AUM appear virtual, and even that is somewhat arbitrary. In digital systems real/virtual (and physical/nonphysical) are relative descriptors created for the convenience of a limited perspective.
- **mind-space** — AUM's interior "space" in which individuated puddles share existence as subsets of AUM's own mind-space; sentient players make free-will choices "in AUM-mind-space."
- **time (as indexing scheme)** — "Think of time as an indexing scheme to order memory (record change), processes, and content." AUM's evolution passed through irregular haphazard clocks before time kept a regular beat — a notion of time preceding AUM's precise clocks.
- **reality cells** — the first cells created by the imposition of constraints, which led to the initial dim glimmer of AUO; evolving and studying consciousness cannot be done "by simply passing 1s and 0s around in binary reality cells."
- **consciousness fractal ecosystem** — Campbell's phrase for the whole in which "work or die" applies at every level and for all individuated expressions.
- **endgame / endless-game** — AUM's hypothetical final state, described in Chapters 31–32 of Book 1 as part of a larger cycle that appears, from our viewpoint, to be an endless-game; individuated units have no independent endgame and cycle until they reunite with AUM ("join the laboratory staff").
- **synergy / digital potential energy** — consciousness as digital potential energy organizing itself toward greater profitability; work converts digital potential energy into synergy.
- **AUMosaurus** — Campbell's tongue-in-cheek image: from a view much bigger than ours, perhaps AUM is itself a virtual reality "located in the virtual gut of a virtual AUMosaurus" — a speculation he immediately drops as being over our heads.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The Petri dish thought experiment** — the chapter's central device. Campbell asks: if you were digital consciousness, how would you isolate two bounded puddles of your own consciousness and get them to interact "in your mental petri dish"? How could AUM tell which puddle is which when they are identical subsets sharing the same mind-space? Answer: differentiate pieces of itself and grant them unique constraints (boundaries + limited functionality). PMR is "our own specially prepared Petri dish." What it shows: constraints are the enabling condition of individuated consciousness and of AUM's science.
- **Humans studying humans** — anthropology, archeology, medicine, biology, psychology, sociology, political science, economics are all "us studying how and why we do what we do"; by analogy, AUM would want and need to study consciousness to optimize its profits.
- **Three levels-of-awareness examples** — (1) a driver becomes aware of a brick lying in the street and memory/processing urge avoidance; (2) a computer becomes aware a USB device was plugged in and a music CD placed in CD-ROM drive E, loads drivers and asks which track to play; (3) a plant becomes aware sunlight comes from a particular direction and repositions its leaves. What Campbell says they show: there are as many levels of awareness and feedback as there are of consciousness — "Don't take the narrow view."
- **The clam** — poked, it pulls in its foot and slams its shell shut: interactive, therefore aware, therefore sentient. "Is a clam bright? Maybe? Compared to what?" — it has rudimentary intelligence (can learn), illustrating relative degrees of awareness bounded by a fixed capacity per type of sentient consciousness.
- **The sunflower / sun-tracking plant** — collects and interprets data successfully and initiates profitable action, but fails to produce enough personal progress to qualify as individually intelligent; supports the claim of species/genetic intelligence and "group souls."
- **Humans with advanced degrees** — Campbell's joke that he has known several who fall into the same low-individual-intelligence category as plants and clams; moral: avoid making rules about how entities other than yourself should be categorized — every individual is an individual, and "statistics always allows for a relatively small number of strange happenings."
- **The saber-tooth tiger joke** — "when consciousness and the Fundamental Process of evolution boogie together, enough curiosity and inquisitiveness is generated to kill a saber tooth tiger… Are not all the saber tooth tigers dead? I rest my case." A comic "proof" of primal inquisitiveness.
- **The movie-frames analogy for time** — millions of individual frames, properly sequenced in time, turn a jumble of independent pictures into a simulation of reality; illustrates time as an indexing scheme for memory, processes, and content.
- **AI Guy as mirror model** — the rule-sets we impose on AI Guy define his experience exactly as ours define ours; similar rule-sets are required "in exactly the same way and for the same reasons" to define AI Guy and his virtual reality. AUM is the original AI Guy running parallel experiments, evolving constraints (player definition, processing power, memory, data collection, and purpose expressed as conditionals within each competing algorithmic rule-set) on the fly.
- **Rodney Dangerfield rap** — Campbell's comic dismissal of what disembodied free will is not for: mind-play like out-of-mind-experiences, perfect cocktail chatter, or "a Rodney Dangerfield rap on how difficult it is to get respect without a body" generates no long-term profitability for AUM; free will exists to experience, act, interact, react, and make of itself whatever it will.
- **The AUMosaurus** — the speculative image that AUM itself may be a virtual reality in the gut of a virtual AUMosaurus, showing the recursive relativity of "real" vs. "virtual" before Campbell deliberately lets it go.
- **The two-rule-set model (the chapter's constructed model)** — moving parts: (1) higher-level rule-set = profitability criteria, purpose, goals, trickled down as guiding values; (2) lower-level rule-set = mechanics of interaction / causality / allowable energy exchanges (e.g., space-time); the two reflect and support each other, and system profitability depends on their successful interaction. Specifying them subsumes a complete physics of the local reality.
- **Jeopardy aside** — the puddle-identification questions are "more difficult questions than you are likely to get on jeopardy."
- **"The green grass grows all around" song** — Campbell's playful refrain for how all reality flows from the union of his two assumptions and "Your Big TOE grows."

## Thread
This chapter pivots Section 4's consciousness modeling from the operational "how it works" (the digital-simulation analogy of the preceding chapters) to the functional "what it does and why." It cashes out Campbell's two basic assumptions — consciousness potential (Chapter 24, Book 1) and the Fundamental Process — into a causal chain: AUM evolves → must study consciousness interactively → differentiates constrained puddles of itself → must grant them free will and purpose → every reality is defined by a higher-level (values/purpose) and lower-level (causality/physics) rule-set → PMR is one such custom virtual reality among many, and real/virtual is only a relative distinction. It explicitly defers to later chapters the discussion of how AUM and TBC produce our physical experience, positioning itself as the "basics of interacting consciousness" that those chapters will need; Campbell says the reader "will need to apply these ideas later."
