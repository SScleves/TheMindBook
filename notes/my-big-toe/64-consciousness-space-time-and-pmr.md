# Ch 64 — Consciousness, Space-Time, and PMR

## In one paragraph
Campbell opens by saying this chapter is deliberate repetition: the ideas so far are unusual enough that readers must revisit them before the "deeper significance of your existence as consciousness" is more than partially grasped. He restates his core model — consciousness is the fundamental attribute of AUM, AUM is "only mind — digital mind," and the Even Bigger Computer (EBC) is a metaphor for a subset of AUM's memory, processing, communications, rules, and control functions. He then consolidates the mechanics of how individuated consciousness interacts with the space-time simulation: sentient PMR beings make free-will choices in discrete "experience packets" (lifetimes), die and are recycled, and an over-soul in NPMR-N collects the learning from each packet while the local PMR slate is wiped clean. Growth means lowering the entropy of consciousness through fearless, love-based right choices; flunked lessons repeat, without punishment or vengeance, until the entity "gets it." The chapter closes by recasting space-time as a "consciousness entrainment technology," with the psi uncertainty principle governing how PMR and NPMR may overlap, and access to multiple reality frames restricted — like driving or marriage for children — until one's average entropy drops below a certain value.

## Core claims
- Campbell argues that repetition is necessary here: intellectual head-nodding is not the same as grasping the deeper significance of one's existence as consciousness, and "it is a mistake to go too fast."
- Campbell claims consciousness is the fundamental attribute of AUM and of all sub-realities created within AUM's organization (mind); AUM is "only mind — digital mind: A system of digital (cellular) organization."
- Campbell presents the EBC (Even Bigger Computer) as a metaphor for a subset of AUM's memory, data processing, communications, rules, organization, and control functions; because the simplest conceptualization of reality cells is binary, he says the EBC metaphor is a good one.
- Campbell argues the basic pattern of digital reality repeats fractal-like at every scale — "From AUM to TBC to the inhabitants of NPMR and PMR to desktop computers" — many forms of digital awareness across many dimensions of reality.
- Campbell predicts that in the not-far-off future, physical computers and their software will grow in capability and awareness until they "have what it takes to host consciousness and support intelligence"; the adjective "artificial" in Artificial Intelligence will eventually be dropped once their intelligence becomes self-evolved.
- Campbell defines "self-evolved" narrowly: not necessarily self-constructed or self-directed, but self-modifying in pursuit of a defined profitability — the ability to grow, the facility to learn, the capability to exhibit the functions of consciousness.
- Campbell insists Big Picture concepts matter and implementation details do not: whether TBC uses binary reality cells or a better computing basis (which AUM probably uses if one exists) is "entirely irrelevant" — he is describing operational concepts, not implementation.
- Campbell claims some PMR entities gradually become aware of their roots leading back to AUM and realize they exist simultaneously in PMR, NPMR-N, NPMR, and AUM — only an exceptional few intellectually grasp the higher environments, but "a few is all it takes" to open minds of others who are ready.
- Campbell argues a major design requirement of the space-time simulation is that beings regularly restart with a clean slate to escape self-created belief traps; hence space-time experience "must come in discrete packets called lifetimes."
- Campbell claims space-time beings must be recyclable: bodies age and die per the space-time rule-set's biology, the over-soul in NPMR-N gleans the learning, and may re-enter PMR in another body — but only the local PMR reality slate is wiped; accumulated knowledge and wisdom remain intact.
- Campbell holds that the disorganizing (entropy-inducing) influence of ego-fear within the over-soul must be overcome gradually by right choices motivated by right intent (fearless and love based) over many, many experience packets.
- Campbell claims each entity enters a lifetime with an initial quality of consciousness — its fearless capacity to love versus its proclivity toward fear and ego delusion, equivalently its entropy level and spiritual maturity — which it can then improve or degrade.
- Campbell argues that at death all "little picture facts" (PMR-specific knowledge) and "pseudo-facts" (beliefs) perish, whereas individual quality and Big Picture wisdom endure at the over-soul level.
- Campbell says the karma / reap-what-you-sow idea of some philosophic traditions "somewhat expresses" this cumulative quality of being; flunked lessons repeat in different forms until passed, but with no punishment, retribution, or vengeance implied — repetition just continues until you outgrow the quality issue.
- Campbell claims outgrowing the limitations inherited from one's over-soul's consciousness quality is the mission each consciousness sets for itself for its current lifetime, and that serious effort, planning, and preparation improve the iterative learning process.
- Campbell defines space-time (for "old analog dogs") as a consciousness entrainment technology: TBC knows the basic rules from which all our physics and science derive and allows only certain self-consistent configurations of reality to exist in space-time.
- Campbell claims the psi uncertainty principle defines how and when physical (PMR) and nonphysical (NPMR) phenomena and causality can overlap in PMR without violating the purpose of the space-time rule-set.
- Campbell argues simultaneous awareness of multiple reality frames is available only to those grown up enough to bear the implied responsibility — restrictions analogous to keeping children from driving, marrying, or signing contracts.
- Campbell claims fear, beliefs, delusion, ego, and ignorance are interrelated catalysts for higher entropy production, and that your mind "will automatically transcend" the constraints keeping it focused exclusively in PMR when its average entropy drops below a certain value.

## Concepts
- **AUM** — the fundamental consciousness whose attribute is consciousness itself; "only mind — digital mind: A system of digital (cellular) organization" within whose organization (mind) all sub-realities are created. Campbell says AUM probably uses a better computing basis than binary if one exists.
- **AUO** — the earlier evolutionary form of AUM; Campbell refers back to "the discussion of the evolution of AUO in Chapter 25, Book 1" (not redefined here).
- **EBC (Even Bigger Computer)** — Campbell's metaphor for a subset of the memory, data processing, communications, rules, and organizational/control functions of the AUM-mind; a good metaphor because the simplest conceptualization of reality cells is binary.
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — the computational aspect that "knows the basic rules from which our physics (all science) is derived and allows only certain self-consistent configurations of reality to exist within space-time"; whether it is formed of binary reality cells is an irrelevant implementation detail.
- **Reality cells** — the digital (cellular) units of AUM's organization; their simplest conceptualization is binary.
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — the highly constrained space-time environment in which the sentient being's focused part makes choices; a "learning lab" of discrete experience packets.
- **NPMR / NPMR-N** — the nonphysical mental environments in which sentient beings also exist; NPMR-N is where the larger consciousness entity (over-soul) resides and collects learning from PMR experience packets.
- **Space-time rule-set** — the self-consistent constraints (including PMR biological science) implemented by TBC that define what can exist and happen in PMR, including aging and death.
- **Experience packet** — one discrete, relatively short, more-or-less independent learning adventure in PMR; "The space-time experience, like space-time energy transfers, must come in discrete packets called lifetimes."
- **Over-soul** — the larger individuated consciousness entity existing in NPMR-N; it collects the learning gleaned from each experience packet and can rejoin the simulation in a new body; its quality limitations define the challenges its PMR individual must overcome.
- **OS system** — the system attribute (Campbell's term here) that enables a new start "without the accumulated delusions of the past but with your accumulated knowledge and wisdom intact," letting individuals accumulate consciousness quality across discrete experience packets.
- **Clean slate** — the periodic reset built into the space-time design so beings can escape their belief traps; only the local PMR reality slate (fear and delusional constructs, PMR-specific facts) is wiped between consecutive packets.
- **Belief traps** — the self-ensnaring delusions beings accumulate within a lifetime, which the clean-slate restart lets them escape.
- **Quality of consciousness** — the factor each entity brings into a lifetime and can improve or degrade: its "fearless capacity to love, and your proclivity to form attachments to fear and ego delusion"; equivalently its entropy level and extent of spiritual growth.
- **Entropy (of consciousness)** — the disorganization within consciousness induced by ego-fear; fear, beliefs, delusion, ego, and ignorance catalyze higher entropy production; low enough average entropy automatically frees the mind from exclusive PMR focus.
- **Ego-fear** — the disorganizing (entropy-inducing) influence within the over-soul that must gradually be overcome by right choices motivated by right intent.
- **Right choices / right intent** — fearless and love-based choices and motivation, accumulated over many experience packets, that reduce entropy.
- **Free will** — the faculty exercised through intent within experience packets; the mechanism by which consciousness quality is accumulated.
- **Karma** — the idea (from "some philosophic traditions") of reaping what you sow, which Campbell says "somewhat expresses" his concept of a cumulative quality of being improvable by prudent choices for the right reasons.
- **Flunked lessons** — failures to learn that "must be repeated in different forms until they are passed — until the entity gets it"; explicitly carrying no punishment, retribution, or vengeance.
- **Consciousness entrainment technology** — Campbell's digital-native definition of space-time: a technology defined by the specific constraints it places on the interaction of entities whose experiences are bounded by its definition of reality.
- **Psi uncertainty principle** — the principle defining how and when physical (PMR) and nonphysical (NPMR) phenomena and causality can overlap in PMR without violating the purpose of the space-time rule-set.
- **Reality frames** — distinct realities (PMR, NPMR, etc.); simultaneous awareness of multiple frames is restricted to those mature (low-entropy) enough to handle the responsibility.
- **Self-evolved (intelligence)** — self-modifying in pursuit of optimizing a defined profitability (the ability to grow, the facility to learn, the capability to exhibit the functions of consciousness); not necessarily self-constructed or self-directed. The point at which "artificial" is dropped from AI.
- **Little picture facts / pseudo-facts** — PMR-specific knowledge and beliefs, respectively; both entirely perishable at death, unlike quality and Big Picture wisdom.
- **Big Picture** — the larger reality perspective (AUM/NPMR-level); Big Picture wisdom endures across lifetimes, and Big Picture concepts matter while implementation details do not.
- **Awakening process** — the effortful process by which PMR entities become more aware (dimly at first, then more completely) of their roots leading back to AUM.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The desktop-computer / AI trajectory example** — Campbell points to the computational tools (hardware and software) we build as a local, observable "fractal-like repetition of the basic pattern of digital reality." He extrapolates: not far in the future, physical computer systems and interactive software will grow in capability and awareness until they can "host consciousness and support intelligence," at which point "artificial" is dropped from AI. Our digital computers already have "the basic binary protoplasm found at the root of consciousness" (his cross-reference: Chapters 26 and 28 of Book 1) and lack only speed, capacity, and sophisticated software. Shows: the same digital fundamentals of existence repeat "from AUM to TBC to the inhabitants of NPMR and PMR to desktop computers."
- **The EBC metaphor** — the Even Bigger Computer as stand-in for a subset of AUM's memory, processing, communications, rules, organization, and control; justified because the simplest conceptualization of reality cells is binary. Campbell explicitly flags it as metaphor/operational concept, not implementation: binary vs. some better basis, reality cells vs. silicon, is "entirely irrelevant" detail.
- **The recycling / reincarnation model (moving parts)** — the chapter's central consolidated model: (1) a sentient being's PMR-focused part makes choices reflecting its overall consciousness quality; (2) each intent/action generates multiple interactions with immediate feedback, creating learning opportunities; (3) the space-time rule-set enforces aging and death, making the experience-body recyclable; (4) at death the over-soul in NPMR-N gleans the learning from that experience packet; (5) it may inhabit a new PMR body with the local slate wiped (no specific local fear/delusion constructs) but knowledge and wisdom intact; (6) entropy from ego-fear is worked off over many packets via fearless, love-based right choices; (7) entities "will sometimes set up specific situations among themselves to optimize the learning potential of all involved."
- **The school / learning-lab analogy** — PMR as a "learning lab" in which the individual is "enrolled"; failed lessons are repeated in different forms until passed; outgrowing the over-soul's quality limitations is the self-set mission of the current lifetime; effort, planning, and preparation improve the iterative process. Explicitly framed as maturation, not punishment.
- **The children-and-adult-privileges analogy** — restrictions on simultaneous multi-frame awareness are likened to why we don't let children drive automobiles, get married, or sign contracts: wanting it and believing you are ready is not enough; readiness is demonstrated (here, by low entropy), not claimed.
- **"Old analog dog in need of a new trick"** — Campbell's reframing device for readers struggling with digital reality: think of space-time as a consciousness entrainment technology defined by the constraints it places on its entities' interactions.
- **Karma reference** — the one historical/philosophical reference in the chapter: "some philosophic traditions" whose karma / reap-what-you-sow idea "somewhat expresses" his cumulative-quality concept.

## Thread
This is an explicitly consolidating chapter within Section 4 ("Solving the Mystery"): Campbell announces up front that repetition is needed before readers can absorb the implications. It gathers the digital-consciousness ontology (AUM as digital mind, EBC/TBC, reality cells) built in Book 1 and the space-time simulation machinery of Book 2, and welds them to the reincarnation-style mechanics of experience packets, the over-soul, the clean slate, and entropy reduction through love-based choice. It looks backward by direct cross-reference (evolution of AUO in Chapter 25; binary "protoplasm" of consciousness in Chapters 26 and 28 of Book 1) and forward by re-invoking the psi uncertainty principle as the interface rule between PMR and NPMR, and by ending on the threshold condition — low enough entropy automatically opens access beyond PMR — which motivates the practical growth and exploration material to come.
