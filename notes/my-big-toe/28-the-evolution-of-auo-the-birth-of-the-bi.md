# Ch 28 — The Evolution of AUO: The Birth of the Big Computer (TBC)

## In one paragraph
Campbell continues his evolutionary story of AUO (the primordial consciousness he introduced earlier): a "gazillion reality cells" randomly oscillating and changing state gradually evolve into patterns, patterns-of-patterns, and the rules that define and regulate them, driven by the Fundamental Process's evolutionary pressure toward self-improvement (lower entropy). Because the simplest reality cell is a binary unit, AUO can specialize part of itself into memory and processing — and this computational-memory function is what Campbell christens The Big Computer (TBC), a central metaphor for the rest of the trilogy. AUO thereby becomes a form of digital consciousness, and Campbell uses this to give a first non-geometric, non-temporal definition of "dimension": a well-defined memory-and-processing subset within TBC, like an independent simulation running in its own memory-space. Finally, Campbell argues that AUO must "invent" time — a digital indexing and sequencing technology — to unlock ordered process, cause-and-effect dynamics, and an exponential explosion of explorable possibilities. The chapter thus converts the amorphous consciousness-energy of earlier chapters into a rule-based digital computational system, setting up the derivation of realities, dimensions, and time to come.

## Core claims
- Campbell claims AUO consists of a gazillion reality cells more or less randomly changing state, which evolve into patterns and patterns-of-patterns as awareness and complexity increase; some patterns are "more interesting and profitable" than others.
- Campbell restates his definition of self-improvement in a consciousness system: higher levels of organization leading to lower average entropy; the Fundamental Process interacting with consciousness creates evolutionary pressure pushing all consciousness entities toward self-improvement.
- Campbell argues that rules are nothing other than knowledge-based or experience-based patterns for the reliable definition, repetition, and interaction of other patterns, and that rules evolve naturally from the pressure of self-improvement — bringing with them control and hierarchy.
- He claims patterns, patterns-of-patterns, and the rule-sets ordering them constitute the basic ingredients of almost everything we directly experience — brains, cities, economies, politics, culture, civilization, and consciousness itself.
- He suggests consciousness may usefully be described as made of discrete fundamental units (quanta): some minimum group of complex interactive reality cells constitutes a quantum of consciousness.
- Because reality cells are at the core of consciousness and the simplest reality cell is a binary unit, Campbell argues that "logic dictates that computers have the theoretical potential to become conscious devices" — though binary cells alone are not enough; they must interrelate, share information, and modify themselves around some purpose or intent.
- AUO could specialize part of itself into vast memory ("gazillions of tetra-tetra-tetra bytes"), leading it to invent its own mathematics — which Campbell says is nothing other than patterns, rules, operations, and relationships: a self-consistent system of logical process.
- This computational and memory function of AUO is the primordial Big Computer (TBC): memory, processing, rules, operations, and content — not a "Big Brain" in the biological organ sense, but a digital computational functionality based on discrete reality cells that evolved to improve organization and reduce entropy within AUO.
- AUO now represents digital consciousness based upon reality cells; digital logic and memory are needed to apply rules of interaction and coordinate the whole toward greater profitability, and purpose "differentiates success from failure."
- Campbell claims the same Fundamental Process applies universally to all systems complex enough for profitable self-induced change, but results differ according to each system's capabilities (intrinsic) and constraints (defined by internal and external environments); AUO evolves specialized cell groups "in the same way and for the same reasons" biological critters did.
- Whereas non-living, non-growing entities naturally move toward higher entropy, consciousness naturally moves toward lower entropy: dimness gives way to brightness, and the growth of quality of consciousness is what "we call... spiritual growth." Conversely, internal energy can sometimes drive consciousness toward greater entropy and lower quality (previewed via the Rats vs. Anti-Rats "Reality Bowl").
- Campbell rejects both time-as-a-fourth-dimension and geometric higher dimensions: relativity's 4D space-time has mathematical justification, but in his usage mixing time with position coordinates "does not constitute a new dimension," and "reality is not fundamentally geometric" — PMR's geometry comes from the local space-time rule-set, which does not apply to the larger reality.
- Humans, he says, directly experience a 3D time-ordered reality that appears to be the perception-limited product of a space-time universe requiring four coordinates per event relative to a reference frame, every reference frame being equally proper.
- His working definition: a "dimension" is a well-defined processing subset within TBC — a constrained thought-space or region of related content processing within the larger consciousness; a particular reality may be thought of as a digital simulation of a virtual world running in its own memory-space within TBC. Most realities exist simultaneously in their own dimension and as a subset of a larger reality, and travel between dimensions is possible — "relatively easy once you know how."
- AUO needs to "invent" time to exploit the lower entropy that comes with ordered process; time is a digital technology providing the indexing and sequencing scheme that supports the next level of organization, gives birth to dynamics (time-ordered causality), and makes explorable possibilities "explode exponentially."

## Concepts
- **AUO (Absolute Unbounded Oneness)** — the primordial consciousness entity of Campbell's model (acronym used throughout, not re-expanded here): here portrayed as a gazillion reality cells oscillating between states, evolving patterns, rules, memory, and processing until it "represents a form of digital consciousness based upon reality cells."
- **AUO's evolution** — the process whereby dim cellular awareness self-organizes into patterns-of-patterns, rules, and ordered processes under the Fundamental Process's pressure toward lower entropy.
- **Reality cells** — the discrete fundamental cells composing AUO; in their simplest form they are binary units ("uniform and non-uniform" for AUO, like 1s/0s or on/off), usable for arithmetic, information storage, and moving data between cell groups.
- **Binary cells** — reality cells employed in either of two states; Campbell likens them to transistors in a microprocessor or the 1s and 0s on a compact disk — "handy for creating memory, storing information, and supporting complex processing."
- **Patterns / patterns-of-patterns** — the self-organizing structures within AUO (and, Campbell says, within biology, civilization, and consciousness generally); he hints that "patterns of patterns" points toward "some sort of undefined fractal process as the ultimate creator of complex structure."
- **Rules / rule-sets** — "knowledge-based or experienced-based patterns for the reliable definition, repetition, and interaction of other patterns"; they evolve naturally from self-improvement pressure and introduce control and hierarchy.
- **Fundamental Process** — Campbell's universal evolutionary process (from earlier chapters), applied to all interactive or intra-active systems complex enough for profitable self-induced change; in interaction with consciousness it produces evolutionary pressure toward self-improvement.
- **Self-improvement / profitability** — within a consciousness system, higher levels of organization leading to lower average entropy; "profitable" configurations are those that reduce entropy and increase organization.
- **Entropy (in consciousness)** — Campbell's measure of disorder in consciousness: consciousness naturally moves toward lower entropy (brightening awareness), the opposite of non-living, non-growing entities which move toward higher entropy.
- **Quantum of consciousness** — a proposed discrete fundamental unit of consciousness: "some minimum group of complex interactive reality cells"; the cells must interrelate, share patterns of data, and modify themselves around some purpose or intent, however dimly perceived.
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — the computational and memory function of AUO; "memory, processing, rules, operations, and content"; not necessarily a biological "Big Brain," but a digital computational functionality based on discrete reality cells that naturally evolved to improve organization and reduce entropy within AUO. Campbell calls it "an important metaphor used throughout this model of reality" and describes it as "a special purpose digital processor and memory subset of a much larger digital consciousness."
- **Digital consciousness** — what AUO becomes: consciousness constituted by discrete binary reality cells organized by digital logic, memory, and rule-sets; mind-space as "a logical, rule-based, computational system — a generalized computer of some sort."
- **Mind-space / thought-space** — the metaphorical "space" in which the larger reality and our local reality exist, since both are constructs of consciousness.
- **Dimension** — Campbell's non-geometric, non-temporal definition: "a well-defined processing subset within The Big Computer, a constrained thought-space or region of related content processing within the larger consciousness"; equivalently, a reality is a digital simulation of a virtual world running in its own memory-space within TBC.
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — our local 3D time-ordered reality; geometrically constrained by the space-time rule-set, which Campbell says is only a local rule-set.
- **NPMR (Nonphysical Matter Reality)** — with PMR, one of the "dimensions" of the multidimensional larger reality (named here as context for the dimension discussion).
- **Space-time rule-set** — the local rule-set that geometrically constrains PMR; it does not apply to the larger reality.
- **3D time-ordered reality** — what humans directly experience: the perception-limited product of a space-time universe requiring four coordinates to specify events relative to a reference frame, each frame equally proper. Campbell accepts relativity's 4D mathematics but denies time is a "dimension" in his sense.
- **Time** — "a digital technology that enables consciousness to organize its content (thoughts) more effectively"; the indexing and sequencing scheme AUO must "invent" to gain ordered process and lower entropy. (Campbell notes in passing that time is "the technology and process ADO will eventually construct for ordering events.")
- **ADO** — the entity Campbell says will eventually construct time as a technology/process for ordering events (used here without expansion; developed in later chapters).
- **Ordered process / dynamics (time-ordered causality)** — what indexing and sequencing of interactive content makes possible; the birth of cause and effect, which makes the explorable possibilities for the Fundamental Process "explode exponentially."
- **Spiritual growth** — Campbell's name for the growth of quality of consciousness as entropy decreases; dimness giving way to brightness.
- **The Rats / Anti-Rats / Reality Bowl** — a teased upcoming device ("our favorite team, The Rats, against the Anti-Rats in the Reality Bowl") for discussing how internal energy can drive consciousness to greater entropy and lower quality; treated in the next section.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **Biological diversification list** — bacteria, guppies, alligators, kangaroos, people, arms, eyes, legs, breasts, brains — plus electric automobiles, the internet, and representative government: all examples of "the advanced biological world organizing itself into more profitable ensembles of patterns and patterns of patterns," paralleling AUO's self-organization.
- **Fractal hint** — Campbell says if "patterns of patterns" suggests "some sort of undefined fractal process as the ultimate creator of complex structure, you are on the right track."
- **Binary computing analogy** — binary patterns of 1s and 0s (for AUO: uniform and non-uniform) plus a small instruction-set evolved into today's computer technology; shows how simple binary cells + rules yield arithmetic, information storage, and data movement.
- **Everyday pattern examples** — a human brain's cellular organization and its neuron/electromagnetic activity patterns; the city you live in; the economy you work in; the political and cultural patterns ordering your life: "Civilization is about ordered patterns defined by profitability and constraints — so is everything else, including consciousness."
- **Conscious computers argument** — because computers are made of binary units like the reality cells that led to consciousness within AUO, Campbell argues they have theoretical potential to become conscious — with the caveat that mere binary cells are insufficient without interrelation, information sharing, and self-modification around purpose or intent.
- **Memory specialization model** — AUO specializing a small part of itself into "gazillions of tetra-tetra-tetra bytes of memory," resolving the previous chapter's constraint (evolving consciousness limited by accessible memory) and enabling AUO to invent its own mathematics.
- **TBC model (moving parts)** — memory + processing + rules + operations + content; a "special purpose digital processor and memory subset" of the larger digital consciousness; explicitly a metaphor to be elaborated in Section 4 (all) and Section 5 (Chapters 6 and 11 of Book 3).
- **Sandstone/crystals/fossil fuels/hydrogen fusion** — examples of external energy driving non-living, non-growing entities to temporarily decrease individual entropy (sand into sandstone, minerals into crystals, decomposing bio-mass into fossil fuels, hydrogen fusing into helium); Campbell uses the mirror image: internal energy can drive consciousness to greater entropy and lower quality.
- **Relativity discussion** — general relativity's 4D space-time continuum (three space coordinates that are functions of time, one time coordinate that is a function of the space coordinates): Campbell grants its mathematical justification but denies it makes time a "dimension" in his sense.
- **Sci-fi disclaimer** — Campbell warns that dimensionality does not work "as the screenwriters for Twilight Zone or the X-Files would have you believe," yet affirms that most realities exist in their own dimension within a larger reality and that inter-dimensional travel is "relatively easy once you know how."
- **Multitasking mainframe analogy (dimension model)** — commercial computers implement partitioning, multitasking, and multiprocessing; we can run multiple simulations, or multiple instances of one simulation with different initial conditions, simultaneously "within our PMR mainframes." Each simultaneously running simulation = an independent reality within its own dimension — Campbell's core picture of dimension within digital consciousness.
- **Transistors and compact disk analogy** — binary cells are like transistors in a microprocessor or the 1s and 0s on a compact disk: handy for memory, information storage, and complex processing.
- **Sea creatures inventing lungs and legs** — as sea creatures "invented" lungs and legs to crawl out of the oceans and exploit dry-land resources, AUO must "invent" time to exploit the increased order and organization (lower entropy) of ordered process.
- **Rats vs. Anti-Rats in the Reality Bowl** — teased extended example for the next section on consciousness moving toward higher entropy/lower quality.

## Thread
This chapter is the hinge in Section 2's genesis narrative: it takes AUO — introduced earlier as dim, oscillating primordial consciousness — and, following directly from the previous chapter's conclusion that evolving consciousness may be constrained by accessible memory, evolves it into a digital, rule-based, computational consciousness by specializing reality cells into memory and processing. It births the trilogy's central TBC metaphor, gives the first working (computational, non-geometric) definition of "dimension," and establishes the necessity of "inventing" time — setting up chapter 30 (AUO's evolution into "brilliant digital consciousness"), the next section's Rats vs. Anti-Rats treatment of entropy-increasing consciousness, and the full development of TBC's operation in Section 4 and Section 5 (Book 3 chapters 6 and 11, per Campbell's own pointers). It also quietly introduces ADO as the future constructor of time.
