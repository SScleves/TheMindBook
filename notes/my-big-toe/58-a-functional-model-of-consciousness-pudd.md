# Ch 58 — A Functional Model of Consciousness Puddle Evolution Starts a Revolution (Hey Jake, Your Great9°°° Grandmother Was a Retarded Puddle)

## In one paragraph
Campbell continues building his functional model of consciousness by following what happens after AUM individuates itself into bounded "puddles" of discrete conscious beings — the "lumps in the sheet" from Chapter 1 of Book 2. He argues that AUM discovers, via the Fundamental Process, that individuated consciousness naturally evolves from dim to bright when growth is mutually profitable, and that with brightness come values, intent, planning, and eventually intellectual self-awareness with free will — which brings both great evolutionary potential and personal responsibility, susceptibility to fear and delusion. From this Campbell derives why reality must be structured as it is: individuated units must have private, non-overlapping mind-spaces (so intent genuinely reflects quality), must start at the lowest practical level rather than the finish line (so entropy can actually be reduced), and must communicate through willfully controlled discrete thought packets (telepathy). He argues consciousness growth is strictly individual — no group can do it for you — and that group consciousness is real but degenerates to the lowest common acceptable quality level, which is why most of our "independent thought" is actually group-mind belief. The chapter closes with the realization that thought is energy and consciousness is energy: a bright, loving, aware consciousness is a very low-entropy energy form with great personal power, and unlike PMR systems, consciousness entropy is not doomed to increase.

## Core claims
- Campbell claims the bounded puddles of discrete conscious beings are the "lumps in the sheet" discussed in Chapter 1 of Book 2, and that AUM's first discovery is that individuated consciousness naturally evolves to greater awareness when its internal and external environments provide sufficient mutually profitable growth opportunities.
- Campbell argues that via the Fundamental Process consciousness naturally migrates "from dim to bright while following the carrot of profitability," and that brightness brings discrimination and values; values expressed by intent lead to actions reflecting individual quality and motivation.
- Campbell claims that by the time values appear in an individual consciousness energy system, entropy has been lowered enough for purposeful intention, premeditation, and planning to take root, from which an intellect that "thinks, or at least thinks it thinks" develops.
- Campbell argues that gaining the ability to think, reason, and exercise free will (or emulate those processes) is "the price for munching those tasty apples": great personal power and high growth rates come packaged with susceptibility to fear and delusion and responsibility for one's intents, actions, and personal quality.
- Campbell claims that, like AI Guys allowed to self-optimize part of their top-level rule-set, first AUO and then our puddle ancestors became intellectually self-aware entities responsible for their own evolution — and that evolution "knows no rank or privileged class"; all evolving consciousness, bright or dim, must follow the same process of entropy reduction.
- Campbell claims a free will in command of a sufficiently large decision space will eventually develop intellectual self-awareness capable of self-control, and must then deal with delusion, accept responsibility, and develop its own potential.
- Campbell argues that if AUM can design a virtual-reality environment that lets its individuated chunks of puddle consciousness optimize their evolution, it can develop a self-sustaining consciousness cycle to support its own existence (referencing Chapter 31 of Book 1).
- Campbell claims the range of values, intents, interaction strategies, cultures, and civilizations into which consciousness may evolve is exceptionally wide, with some more profitable and functional than others, creating a burning need to explore the evolution and application of values, their profitability, the brightness they encourage, and the rate of evolutionary growth under varying circumstances; because these variables are nonlinear functions of each other, they must be viewed from the holistic system perspective of an individuated consciousness with free will so that AUM can assess the evolutionary consequences of values, intention, motivation, and self-awareness.
- Campbell claims AUM must restrict individuated units from overlapping their processes and content — no intrusive thought-reading or thought-injection — because without control over one's own mind-space, genuine intentions and hence the quality of an individual consciousness would be impossible to ascertain.
- Campbell argues that without mental privacy interactions would evolve toward "stilted political correctness" and thoughtless conformity; the price of protecting individual integrity is "a huge black market in closet-depravity," and that is how AUM needs it to be so the quality of interaction directly reflects the quality of the being.
- Campbell claims individuated consciousness must start at the lowest practical level of profitable organization: nothing would be learned and no entropy reduced "if everyone started at the finish line" — this is also his answer to why a "digital-consciousness-love-being like AUM" populates our world with so many jerks: "This is simply how it must be to be at all."
- Campbell claims a free will able to make both good and bad choices is required for consciousness to be viable, useful, or sustainable, and that a mind needs a mechanism to sort self-generated thoughts from others' thoughts (otherwise blaming others would gain credibility).
- Campbell claims the sense of "I" versus "not I" is born from the need to isolate each consciousness unit so it can effectively evolve and so individual consciousness evolution can be efficiently studied, understood, and optimized by AUM.
- Campbell argues consciousness quality is strictly individual: "Only you can do it for you"; groups, and books like his own, can stimulate learning but "can effect no change in the quality of your being."
- Campbell claims group consciousness exists but is not a different form of consciousness — merely individual consciousnesses sharing intent and data; it distributes information efficiently but its quality gravitates to the lowest common denominator, because higher-quality consciousness can easily step down but lower quality cannot easily step up.
- Campbell claims culture itself is a form of group consciousness, that widely shared beliefs (cultural, religious, scientific, personal, social, political) are manifestations of group mind, and that much of what we hold as independent thought and unquestionable truth is "a knee-jerk expression of group mind."
- Campbell claims the subjective reality of higher-entropy individuals ("most of us") is defined by their beliefs, and that objective tidbits are usually used as "nails upon which we hang more important subjective interpretations."
- Campbell argues bounded consciousness units must evolve a non-overlapping communication mechanism — direct transfer of discrete thought packets, one form of which is telepathy — with incoming data willfully let in or blocked and outgoing data willfully pushed out and directed.
- Campbell claims thought is energy (thought organizes bits) and consciousness is energy (consciousness is organized bits); an active, aware, loving, bright consciousness system is a very low-entropy, highly organized energy form with the ability to do much work — considerable personal power.
- Campbell claims that unlike physical (PMR) systems, consciousness entropy does not have to increase with time — consciousness is a "frictionless system" whose entropy can rise or fall based on activity — and that AUM is experimenting with a methodology to optimally decrease its own entropy by encouraging subsets of itself to decrease their entropy through directed evolution.

## Concepts
- **AUM** — Campbell's absolute consciousness system ("digital-consciousness-love-being") that individuates puddles of consciousness, discovers the Fundamental Process's results, and studies/optimizes the evolution of finite individual consciousness to lower its own system entropy. As "a unique finite individual consciousness," AUM is most interested in the evolutionary dynamics of finite individual consciousness.
- **AUO** — the earlier consciousness entity that, before AUM, first became an intellectually self-aware entity with responsibility for its own evolution (compared to a self-optimizing AI Guy).
- **puddles / Puddledom** — Campbell's metaphor for bounded, individuated units of consciousness carved out of AUM; the "more retarded" (more limited and constrained) puddles develop the sense of "I" versus "not I." These puddles are our ancestors (hence the title's "retarded puddle" grandmother).
- **lumps in the sheet** — the image (from Chapter 1 of Book 2) that the bounded puddles of seemingly discrete conscious beings now represent.
- **Fundamental Process** — the evolutionary process AUM discovers, by which consciousness naturally migrates from dim to bright while following the "carrot of profitability" through a multitude of possibilities.
- **entropy (of consciousness)** — the inverse measure of consciousness quality and brightness: the brighter (more aware) a consciousness system, the less entropy it contains. Unlike PMR entropy, consciousness entropy is frictionless and can rise or fall based on activity within the system.
- **brightness / dim-to-bright** — Campbell's scale of consciousness awareness; brightness brings discrimination and values, and equates to lower entropy.
- **values** — what brightness produces; values create interactions with intent, and values expressed by intent lead to actions that reflect individual quality and motivation.
- **intent** — "the most important attribute of any conscious interaction"; intent reflects values and defines the quality of the consciousness doing the interacting.
- **quality of consciousness** — the individual measure defined by intent and values; improving it (spiritual growth) is strictly the individual's own responsibility and can be affected only by that individual.
- **free will** — the capacity to make both good and bad choices; without it consciousness "is not viable, useful, or sustainable." Firmly connected to motivation, intent, and quality.
- **decision space** — the range of choices commanded by a free will; a free will with a sufficiently large decision space eventually develops intellectual self-awareness able to control itself.
- **AI Guys** — Campbell's recurring artificial-intelligence analogy: entities allowed to self-optimize and change a limited portion of their top-level rule-set, ostensibly to optimize performance — the model for how AUO and the puddle ancestors became self-aware and responsible for their own evolution.
- **virtual reality** — the environment AUM can design to enable its individuated chunks of puddle consciousness to optimize their evolution.
- **self-sustaining consciousness cycle** — AUM's cyclical process (see Chapters 31–32, Book 1): generate low-quality individuated units with free will → let the Fundamental Process evolve them to higher quality/lower entropy → study and optimize the process → recycle unused/less productive bits until ecosystem entropy hits a minimum → upload results to a larger system and start over, or lower the minimum and continue entropy reduction.
- **self-optimization** — "the name of the evolutionary game at every level"; AUM must constantly assess and explore its potential for increasing profitability.
- **mind-space** — an individual's private mental territory; personal control of the thoughts residing in one's mind-space is what makes uniqueness, genuine intent, and responsibility possible. Campbell also uses left/right/middle "mind-space" satirically for political camps.
- **"I" versus "not I"** — the sense of separate personal identity and history, born from the need to isolate each consciousness unit so it can effectively evolve and be efficiently studied.
- **group consciousness / group mind** — not a different form of consciousness but a group of individual consciousnesses sharing intent and data in common; extremely efficient at distributing information, values, and content, but its quality gravitates to the lowest common acceptable level (sometimes degenerating into mob consciousness).
- **lowest common denominator (of group mind)** — the level group interaction sinks to, because higher quality can effortlessly step down to where it came from while lower quality cannot easily step up to expressions it has never attained.
- **culture** — itself a form of group consciousness; widely shared beliefs of any type are manifestations of group mind held together by common instincts, beliefs, needs, and traditions.
- **subjective reality / belief** — for higher-entropy individuals (most of us), subjective reality is defined by belief; much "independent thought" is knee-jerk recitation of shared group beliefs, easy to spot in other groups, hard to see in one's own — self-delusion being the easiest and hardest-to-see thing there is.
- **thought packets** — discrete units of information transferred directly between bounded consciousnesses; the simplest non-overlapping communication mechanism.
- **telepathy** — one form of information transfer by discrete thought packets; Campbell defines it as "adding data to (organizing bits within) someone else's calculation space." Incoming data must be willfully let in or blocked; outgoing data willfully pushed out and directed.
- **calculation space** — the computational mind-space of an individuated consciousness into which thought packets deposit organized bits.
- **thought is energy / consciousness is energy** — thought organizes bits, therefore thought is energy; consciousness is organized bits, therefore consciousness is energy whose potential is the potential to be better organized (creating synergy, improving quality, lowering entropy).
- **personal power** — the capacity of a highly structured, low-entropy consciousness energy form "to do much work."
- **PMR** — physical matter reality; contrasted here as the kind of system whose entropy must increase with time, unlike frictionless consciousness systems.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The puddle-evolution model (the chapter's central model)** — moving parts: AUM individuates bounded puddles (the lumps in the sheet); the Fundamental Process drives them dim→bright chasing profitability; brightness → values → intent → planning → an intellect that "thinks it thinks" → self-aware free will with exploded state-space and matching responsibility; AUM goes through this first, the puddles follow. Shows, per Campbell, that all consciousness — bright or dim — follows the same entropy-reduction process, without rank or privileged class.
- **The apples / tree-of-knowledge metaphor** — evolving consciousness past a certain point ("eating the metaphorical apples") exacts a price: power and growth come bundled with fear, delusion, and responsibility. Campbell's rework of the Eden trope into an evolutionary cost-benefit statement.
- **The AI Guys analogy** — self-optimizing AI entities permitted to modify a limited portion of their own top-level rule-set, used as the model for how AUO and puddle ancestors became responsible for their own evolution.
- **The self-sustaining consciousness cycle (flow model)** — generate low-quality free-will units → evolve them → study/optimize the process → recycle unproductive bits → drive ecosystem entropy to a minimum → upload results to a larger system and restart, or lower the minimum and continue. Campbell presents it as how AUM could support its own existence (cross-referenced to Book 1 Chapters 31–32). Punctuated by the joke: "Yo, being! Watch out for that first step — it's a big one. Oops!"
- **The privacy/political-correctness thought experiment** — if minds could intrusively read/inject thoughts, "pointy headed professors from the know-it-all left side of mind-space" would join "the 'I know best' dogma police of the right side" to turn the middle into "mindless-space"; honest individuality would give way to thoughtless conformity. Shows why AUM enforces mental privacy — at the price of "a huge black market in closet-depravity."
- **"Why so many jerks?"** — Campbell's answer to why a loving AUM populates our world with jerks: units must start at the lowest practical level of profitable organization; nothing would be learned and no entropy reduced if everyone started at the finish line. "This is simply how it must be to be at all."
- **Blame-shifting example** — without a mechanism to sort your own thoughts from others', "the common ploy of sidestepping responsibility by blaming others would take on a new sense of credibility"; supports the need for bounded, sorted mind-space.
- **Group-consciousness examples** — participants at a religious revival meeting, a group of vigilantes, a topnotch athletic team, a street gang, teenagers hanging out at the mall or high-school parking lot; mob consciousness generating "bizarre and brutal behavior"; the rock concert whose group consciousness "simply creates a better party atmosphere."
- **Social groupings as group mind** — herds, packs, flocks, colonies, pods, societies, nations, religions, terrorist cells — group consciousness focused on common goals (success, safety, anxiety reduction) held together by instincts, beliefs, needs, traditions; easy to see in groups we don't belong to (terrorist cells, political parties, other religions), hard to see in our own.
- **The nails metaphor** — the few objective tidbits that impose themselves on us are "used as nails upon which we hang more important subjective interpretations."
- **Telepathy as data transfer** — the communication model: discrete thought packets adding data to (organizing bits within) another's calculation space, with willful gating of both incoming and outgoing flows; grounds the "thought is energy / consciousness is energy" conclusion.
- **The "Jake" comic dialogue (closing skit)** — an unnamed speaker asks Jake to reread Campbell's "technoid" sentence about AUM decreasing its entropy through directed evolution, builds to a mock enlightenment ("I'm expanding ... expanding ... expanding"), then deflates it: "Nah! Just gas... Hey, toss me another chug-a-lug, pal! I think I'm running out of entropy." Campbell's self-deprecating counterweight to his own technical prose; Jake also appears in the chapter title as the addressee of the "retarded puddle" grandmother joke.

## Thread
This chapter sits in Book 2's Section 4 ("Solving the Mystery"), extending Campbell's functional model of consciousness from AUM's self-organization to the evolutionary dynamics of the individuated units ("puddles") it carves out of itself. It picks up the "lumps in the sheet" image from the opening of Book 2 (ch 35) and the self-sustaining consciousness cycle from Book 1 (chs 31–32), and derives from evolutionary necessity the features of our situation Campbell wants explained: private minds, the sense of "I," free will able to choose badly, starting at the bottom, and a world full of low-quality beings. It converts the model into two payloads used throughout the rest of the trilogy — consciousness as low-entropy organized energy (thought/telepathy as bit-organization in calculation space) and growth as strictly individual entropy reduction — while its group-mind analysis feeds Campbell's standing campaign against belief and self-delusion. It sets up the subsequent chapters' continued construction of the consciousness-computer model of reality.
