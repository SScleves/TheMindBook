# A Model of Reality and Time: Multiple Copies of You — Creating Your Reality

## In one paragraph
Campbell extends his "everything significant that can happen does happen" claim into a formal picture of how unactualized past and future states are populated: each is filled not by another free-willed you but by a probabilistic, non-sentient statistical model of you ("virtual you") maintained and continuously updated by TBC (The Big Computer). He explains that your true identity is not your PMR body but a fragment of an immortal over-soul, which can (rarely) project multiple simultaneous or serial fragments across PMRs and NPMRs, each fragment learning independently while its growth is shared back to the whole. He argues digital consciousness energy in NPMR is effectively unlimited, unlike PMR's scarce physical energy, so fragments never compete for the resources needed for growth. The chapter then describes the TBC database as a permanent, complete, and in-principle accessible record of every choice, thought, and unchosen alternative — the source, he says, of intuition, historical what-if speculation (WWII, Desert Storm, JFK/MLK/Gandhi/Jesus), and fiction-writing inspiration — while warning against over-investing time in it at the expense of physical-life growth. It closes by defining "significance" as whatever changes the entropy/quality of consciousness (illustrated by a marriage proposal versus scratching one's head), and by criticizing deterministic physics for misreading a fundamentally statistical, finite-precision reality.

## Core claims
- Campbell claims that for every choice you could have made but didn't (in the unactualized past) or could yet make (in the unactualized future), TBC populates the corresponding state vector with a "virtual you" — a high-fidelity but non-sentient probabilistic model of you, not a parallel free-willed consciousness.
- He argues the virtual you cannot learn, cannot change its own representation, and does not exercise free will; only "the actual you" — your individuated unit of consciousness in NPMR, not your PMR body — makes real choices.
- He claims each replicated/virtual you is a "predictive model of your primary energy in NPMR," accurate because it is built from your quality and choice history, but inert with respect to helping or hurting your growth.
- He states it is possible, though unlikely and rarely used, for your consciousness to inhabit more than one free-will-motivated entity simultaneously (parallel fragments), because serial learning is usually the better strategy given the strong coupling between past and present lessons.
- He argues your "totality" is the sum of all consciousness fragments that have ever exercised free will, and your "over-soul" (primary consciousness energy, "your main personal data folder") remains always conscious in NPMRN and is immortal (with rare exceptions), even though any single personal identity/fragment persists only as long as the over-soul finds it profitable.
- He claims the term "past lives" is a legitimate but too-narrow PMR-only label for what is really a broader set of physical and nonphysical fragments the over-soul may maintain.
- He argues digital consciousness energy in NPMR is effectively unlimited (unlike PMR's fuel-based, scarce energy concept), so fragments do not compete for energy; in practical terms your general access and use of nonphysical energy is limited only by your "personal quality." Separately, he says your ability to apply that energy into PMR to induce paranormal events is limited by your beliefs, ignorance, access privileges, and the psi uncertainty principle — but never by a shortage of basic energy itself.
- He claims each fragment is your over-soul's "team member," not its "CEO" — the proper perspective is developing the quality of the fragment you presently are, not serving as an agent optimizing the whole from above.
- He states personal growth normally proceeds by small, slow steps rather than great leaps, and that a "disadvantaged situation" is usually self-created by belief, fear, and ego rather than imposed from outside.
- He argues individual fragment experiences are kept separate (filed in their own "folder"), but the spiritual growth/quality gained is immediately shared to the whole over-soul.
- He claims all reality states — actualized and unactualized, past and future — are permanently and completely recorded in TBC, are not volatile, and remain "alive and kicking" as interactive simulations that can be re-run.
- He states that unlike the past, some unactualized future information is deliberately access-restricted when it would lower the probability of the individual's growth — analogous to protecting children from hazards.
- He argues intuition is the (often unconscious) product of accessing this probable-reality/historical database, and that a "developed intuition" is a trained skill for purposeful querying.
- He claims that with focused intent you can build a "query-filter" against this database and experience the result as an indistinguishable-from-real simulation ("a calculation space populated only by probability models... would be transparent and thus unnoticeable").
- He argues that the accuracy of any unactualized-state projection degrades the further out in time it runs, chiefly because of free-will unpredictability and near-equal-probability branch points.
- He states that the number of computed generations (M) is not fixed but governed by a "statistical reasonability criterion" — computation stops once probability distributions go low and flat (fuzzy) and continues only where they stay clear and crisp.
- He warns that spending too much energy/time exploring nonphysical/unactualized realities risks neglecting the physical-reality growth that matters most, and that believing you have no significant choices left ("spiritually dead-ended") can lead a person to unconsciously engineer their own exit (illness, accident) from the experience packet, though he says many other exits are usually available.
- He defines "significant" choices as those capable of path-altering, life-changing effects on quality of consciousness (illustrated by a marriage proposal versus scratching one's head with the "wrong" hand), and argues Big-Picture significance (changing entropy/quality of consciousness) differs from little-picture significance (satisfying ego, wants, desires).
- He argues that absolute deterministic precision is incompatible with a finite, statistics-based digital virtual reality, and that physicists (starting with Newton) have mistaken PMR's apparent causal order for literal determinism — including, he says, forcing quantum mechanics' statistical description into a "deterministic straight jacket" when the statistical description is actually the more fundamental reality and the collapsed physical state is the "virtual shadow."
- He argues that reducing the entropy of your individuated consciousness simultaneously reduces the entropy of the entire AUM consciousness system and of your own over-soul, per the Fundamental Process's directive to "evolve, grow, become more, seek profitability, reduce entropy, become motivated by love."
- He argues OS (and all of physical reality/humanity) is a minuscule subset of the larger reality, yet still significant — using the nested scale analogy of neutrino/hydrogen atom/virus-bacterium/human to argue smallness does not preclude significance.

## Concepts
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — the overarching digital-consciousness system in NPMRN that computes and stores all state vectors (actualized and unactualized, past and future); it populates every unactualized state with a probabilistic "virtual you" and retains all information as a permanent, non-volatile, queryable database.
- **DELTA-t** — the discrete time increment at which free-will choices are made and actualized, updating the state vector; used here as the unit across which "significant choices" and dead-end feelings are measured ("future increments of DELTA-t").
- **OS (Our System)** — the specific reality system (encompassing our PMR and its associated NPMR) within the larger AUM consciousness system; Campbell frames the whole chapter's model as being about "the OS file folder."
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — space-time physical reality, described here as a "learning lab called life"; a fragment's PMR identity ("brilliant, good-looking, lovable being") is only one possible fragment among many, not necessarily the most successful.
- **PMRk** — the general/plural notation for any of the (possibly many) physical matter realities across which parallel fragment-projections may be scattered.
- **NPMR (Non-Physical Matter Reality)** — the nonphysical reality where individuated consciousness (the "actual you") resides and where free will choices originate, as opposed to the PMR body.
- **NPMRN** — the specific nonphysical reality where TBC resides and where your over-soul remains always conscious; described as the "thought-space or mind-space" holding the OS database.
- **Over-soul** — "your main personal data folder": the immortal (with rare exceptions) primary consciousness energy that accumulates the results of all your projected fragments' efforts; the source from which fragments are projected and to which their growth (though not their raw experience) is shared back.
- **Virtual you / modeled you** — a non-sentient, statistically accurate, continuously updated representation of you that populates every unactualized (past or future) state vector; it simulates your likely choices but cannot learn, change, or exercise free will.
- **Unactualized states (past and future)** — the full set of state vectors representing choices that could have been made but weren't (past) or could yet be made (future); Campbell says these remain "alive and kicking" as interactive, re-runnable simulations, with future-state access sometimes restricted.
- **State vector** — the data structure representing a complete configuration of OS at a given DELTA-t, including all players' choices and conditions; unactualized state vectors are generated as "child states" from actualized ones.
- **M (generations)** — the number of successive generations of child/dependent states computed forward from a given state; not fixed, but limited by a statistical reasonability criterion (computation halts once probability distributions become "low and flat/fuzzy").
- **Statistical reasonability criterion** — the rule Campbell proposes for how many generations (M) of unactualized states TBC computes forward: continue while probability distributions are clear/crisp/peaked, stop once they go fuzzy.
- **Fundamental Process** — the system-wide imperative described as "Evolve! Grow! Become more! Seek out profitability! Reduce the entropy of your consciousness; become animated and motivated by love"; it promotes profitability and eliminates failure at every level of existence, including guiding whether the over-soul maintains a given personal identity.
- **AUM** — named here as the overarching consciousness system whose entropy is reduced when any individuated consciousness within it reduces its own entropy.
- **Entropy (of consciousness) / quality of consciousness** — Campbell's measure of spiritual development; "significant" choices are those capable of altering this quality, and the whole PMR "class of life" exists to reduce this entropy.
- **Expectation values** — the statistical parameters governing how each modeled/virtual player (including virtual you) behaves in an unactualized simulation; also invoked as the mechanism by which intent applied from NPMR modifies a virtual/physical reality.
- **Psi uncertainty principle** — a rule of the "parent reality" that constrains how much energy/intent can be applied to PMR from NPMR (i.e., limits paranormal effects) even though basic energy itself is never the limiting factor; affecting a virtual reality from the outside must still obey the rules (such as this principle) of the next larger parent reality, whereas affecting it from the inside means working within its own rule-set, "like putting gas in your tank."
- **Light workers** — Campbell's borrowed New Age term for people who have learned to manipulate consciousness energy between NPMRN and PMR, said to never run out of "light" (energy) to do so.
- **Team Earth** — Campbell's term for the collective of all sentient entities on earth, engaged in a "collective dance of creative interaction" whose shared reality is the vector sum of everyone's free-will choices; framed so that no one can cheat others without cheating themselves.
- **Co-conspirators** — Campbell's term for the other people significant to your existence or being, with whose help, advice, and cooperation you design your "path of maximum opportunity" and the reality in which your lessons, opportunities, and choices play out.
- **Experience packet** — the term (used earlier in the book and referenced here) for a bounded lifetime/experience a fragment undertakes; a person can "check out of" or "shed" one when spiritually dead-ended, though Campbell says other choices are usually available.
- **Spiritual dead end** — a state where a projected fragment believes it has no significant choices left to make, which can (in a minority of cases) lead to unconsciously engineered termination of the experience (illness, accident), even though other paths out typically exist but remain invisible to a belief/fear/ego-limited consciousness.
- **Query-filter (of intent)** — the mechanism by which a person consciously defines the conditions/parameters applied to the TBC database to generate an experienced result; described via the "querying a multidimensional database... result is a movie you both act in and watch" analogy, and the "3D holographic movie" analogy where lack of specificity in intent yields jumbled results.
- **Intuition** — Campbell's term for the (often unintentional) result of querying probable-reality data; a "developed intuition" is the trained skill of purposefully accessing this data.
- **Significance (Big Picture vs. little picture)** — Big-Picture significance = capacity to change the entropy/quality of consciousness (path-altering, life-changing); little-picture significance = satisfying ego's needs/wants/expectations; trivial choices with low probability of Big-Picture significance can be safely ignored by the reality's computation.
- **Determinism vs. statistical reality (critique)** — Campbell's argument that PMR is fundamentally a finite, statistics-based digital virtual reality incompatible with the absolute precision determinism requires, and that physicists (from Newton onward, including quantum mechanics practitioners) mistakenly force a statistical reality into a deterministic framework, inverting which layer (statistical vs. physical-collapsed) is actually fundamental.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The marriage-proposal vs. head-scratching example** — Campbell contrasts a man proposing marriage (which cascades choices onto her, her family, her roommate, her landlord, his mother, his other girlfriends) against the same man scratching his head with his right instead of left hand (his habit); he uses this pair to illustrate that "significant" choices are ones with cascading path-altering effects on others, while habitual/trivial choices need not be tracked by the reality system.
- **WWII alternate-outcome thread** — Campbell suggests one could compare the unactualized-state threads leading to the Allies winning versus losing WWII, "flipping through the state frames" to assess benefits/costs as a function of time, as an example of querying TBC's historical what-if database.
- **Desert Storm / Saddam Hussein example** — he posits a virtual-reality branch where Saddam Hussein and his army were "pursued and destroyed completely" during the 1991 Gulf War, asking what difference that might have made, as another illustration of accessible what-if threads.
- **JFK / MLK / Gandhi / Jesus example** — Campbell poses "what if John Kennedy, Martin Luther King, Mahatma Gandhi and Jesus had not been killed" as further illustrative what-if queries into the unactualized database, alongside smaller personal examples (a quarterback's fumble, forgetting an ex-wife's birthday five years running).
- **Neutrino/atom/virus/bacterium scale analogy** — used to argue that despite OS's minuscule size relative to the larger reality (the "Big Fractal Picture"), it remains significant — just as a virus or bacterium is small but vital to physical existence, a hydrogen atom small to a virus, and a neutrino small to an atom, yet each level remains "very significant."
- **The quantum-mechanics "joke"** — Campbell describes physicists treating the statistical description in quantum mechanics as merely a nebulous intermediate step before physical collapse, when (he argues) the reverse is true: the statistical description is the actual fundamental reality and the collapsed physical state is only a "virtual shadow" — offered as a concrete instance of the deterministic-bias critique.
- **The physician/dermatology specialization analogy** — Campbell notes that many sentient entities prefer to specialize in one, or a few, similar local realities to optimize learning efficiency by narrowing focus, "for much the same reason that a physician might specialize in dermatology"; most entities initially specialize in a single reality system before broadening as their consciousness develops.
- **The educational computer game / parametric analysis analogy** — interacting significantly with virtual players (mathematical models of yourself and other beings, objects, and energy) within parallel realities is, he says, "roughly analogous to playing against the computer in an educational computer game or performing parametric analysis within a simulation" — tools to be used, not become enamored of or overly dependent upon.
- **The "air on earth" analogy for Primal Energy** — Primal Energy in NPMR is likened to earth's air supply: finite in total but so abundant relative to any individual's use that there is effectively no competition, unlike PMR's scarcity-based concept of energy as fuel.
- **The large war-game energy analogy** — Campbell notes that the total energy consumed by hundreds of thousands of players in a large virtual war game is nothing more than organization, rules, memory, and digital processing power, used to argue that "energy" in a digital consciousness reality is not the scarce, fuel-like resource it appears to be in PMR.

## Thread
This unit follows directly from Campbell's earlier claim that "everything significant that can happen does happen," turning that abstract principle into an operational model: how TBC actually represents you across every unactualized branch, and what your over-soul/fragment structure means for identity, energy, and growth.
