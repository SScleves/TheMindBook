# The Mechanics of Perception and Perspective

## In one paragraph
Thomas Campbell closes out a mechanical account of perception: from the PMR point of view, our senses interact with an "un-experienced reality" through an energy-packet exchange interface, producing nervous-system data that consciousness interprets into experience. Because TBC's space-time rule-set defines PMR physics and governs the interface's output, and because our own quality of consciousness governs how we interpret what comes back, experience is sandwiched between two nonphysical processes at both ends — meaning the whole show, including "solid" physical reality, is created within consciousness. Campbell uses this to dissolve the mind-matter problem outright (there is no matter, only mind, in the Big Picture) and to argue that wave-particle duality, the uncertainty principle, and entangled-pair correlations stop being paradoxes once PMR is understood as a digital virtual reality computed by TBC. He restates why he uses the PMR/NPMR vocabulary at all (a communications aid to meet the reader where they are), reasserts that My Big TOE satisfies both of his stated nonnegotiable requirements for a Theory of Everything, and returns to the AI Guy analogy to explain why a being embedded in a simulation cannot perceive, or be argued into believing, the bigger reality that produced it.

## Core claims
- Campbell argues perception, from the "little picture" viewpoint, is just physics — biology and machines implementing an energy-packet exchange across an interface with an "un-experienced reality" — and that neither that source reality nor the interface itself is directly relevant to our PMR experience, just as a computer's power cord and processor chip are irrelevant to AI Guy's experience.
- He argues TBC's space-time rule-set defines all of PMR physics and controls the energy-packet interface's output, putting TBC "directly in charge" of what we perceive and of the limits/constraints of our interactions with other players.
- He argues that because our past experience and quality of consciousness determine how incoming data is interpreted, something nonphysical controls both ends of the perception process — meaning physical experience is created by two wholly nonphysical processes carried out within consciousness.
- He states directly: "the book you are reading in your mind is the book I wrote in my mind," and the paper, ink, hands, and eyes are effects required by the space-time rule-set that structures that interaction.
- He argues AUM and TBC are "in the driver's seat" for our sense of reality, and poses this as a run of rhetorical questions: how solid-seeming bodies, critters, rocks, and earthquakes can be constructs of consciousness; how PMR can be described as mind, a dream, a delusion, or as extant only "in the mind of God" (as an imaginative PMR poet or "hopelessly trapped" PMR scientist might put it); how our physical and nonphysical parts can be integrated into one being; how all of earth's beings, critters, objects, and energy are connected; and how all this can be one big communications net or part of a grand digital simulation.
- He claims everything began as uniform consciousness potential energy that, driven by the Fundamental Process (evolution), expanded into profitable states, consolidated profits, cut losses, and — through steadily decreasing system entropy — produced today's highly synergistic complex configurations.
- He claims the mind-matter problem's solution is "embarrassingly simple": in the Big Picture there is no matter, only mind; "physical" doesn't exist as a term except relative to a nonexistent physical reality, and once the delusion of local physical reality is left behind, the physical/nonphysical distinction vanishes entirely.
- He argues wave-particle duality, uncertainty principles, and instantaneous communication between entangled pairs become simple once PMR is understood as a virtual reality run by a digital simulation stepped forward in near-infinitesimal time increments obeying only its own rule-set — the paradoxes disappear along with the illusion of absolute space.
- He claims all that's needed to solve the "Big Questions" is a shift in perspective away from inherited erroneous scientific, cultural, and religious belief, and that progress is a bootstrapping process where each success builds on the last.
- He asserts that "dozens of top scientists" are currently approaching the discovery that PMR is a digital picture existing within a computed reality, and frames My Big TOE as the theoretical foundation underlying that phenomenology.
- He restates and claims to have met two "nonnegotiable requirements" of a correct Big Picture Theory of Everything: (1) it produces one consistent reality with no paradoxes, and (2) it subsumes existing knowledge as a special case of a more general understanding; he adds that My Big TOE's science not supporting traditional limited scientific views is a strength, not a weakness.
- He argues NPMR feels just as physical as PMR to those within it, and that PMR and NPMR differ only because each operates under a different rule-set with its own resulting causality — each reality dimension evolving a rule-set suited to its own use, function, and purpose (space-time serving human function/purpose within OS).
- He states there is "no significant distinction between physical and nonphysical realities" and that PMR/NPMR is an artificial, deliberately chosen terminology used as a communications aid to start from the reader's assumed starting point (belief in physical reality) and lead conceptually toward the Big Picture — comparable to the useful-but-incorrect billiard-ball-and-BBs model of an atom.
- He argues local reality is individuated mind interacting within a given causality's limits, and when multiple minds share a local reality there is a shared/public (objective) experience alongside a personal (subjective) one, both significant but functionally distinct.
- He defines the larger reality as All That Is: consciousness evolving toward greater profitability via entropy reduction. The fundamental digital potential energy underlying it is named (for reasons of conceptual familiarity) Absolute Unbounded Oneness, while the larger reality itself — a huge interactive digital consciousness system and consciousness-evolution fractal ecosystem — is named AUM.
- He claims physical/nonphysical, mind-matter, normal/paranormal, physics/metaphysics, and science/philosophy dichotomies are all illusions of perspective from a limited, locally-focused understanding; matter is a "simulated menial effect" experienced by mind because it makes a virtual environment that renders consciousness evolution more efficient and effective.
- He argues we, as constrained constructs of consciousness, carry an imperative to lower entropy (evolve consciousness) because that is the fundamental nature of the larger fractal ecosystem we are an infinitesimal part of, iterating recursively to generate All That Is.
- He extends the AI Guy analogy: we control AI Guy's simulated experience exactly as the space-time rule-set controls ours, defining what data he can access and the rules for processing it, to create an optimal simulation tool for our purpose.
- He argues AI Guy cannot perceive beyond his local reality (hardware, software, programmers, facilities, all of PMR/NPMR) because it is irrelevant to his mission, cognitively invisible/unthinkable, outside his local reality, and beyond his logical causality — and that, like his "human brothers," there is no way to convince him otherwise since he lacks the conceptual framework to expand his reality enough to grasp the Bigger Picture that derived him.
- He closes with a self-corrected analogy: telling a fish to dance an Irish jig is a poor comparison to telling a man he is nonphysical consciousness, because the fish has an excuse (no legs) while the man, theoretically, has a mind.

## Concepts
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — the local reality perceived by our senses; in this unit, the reality whose physics/science is fully defined by the space-time rule-set resident within TBC and delivered via the energy-packet exchange interface.
- **NPMR (Non-Physical Matter Reality)** — a reality dimension that "appears to be every bit as physical as PMR" to those within it, but operates under a different rule-set, producing different causality and interaction rules than PMR.
- **AUO (Absolute Unbounded Oneness)** — Campbell's name (chosen "for reasons of conceptual familiarity") for the fundamental potential energy whose digital organization creates aware consciousness.
- **AUM** — Campbell's name for the larger reality itself: a huge interactive digital consciousness system and consciousness-evolution fractal ecosystem, distinct from (but built from) the Absolute Unbounded Oneness potential energy; synonymous with All That Is in its evolving, organizing aspect.
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — the entity/system within which the space-time rule-set resides; it defines PMR physics (all science), controls the energy-packet interface's output, and is described (with AUM) as being "in the driver's seat" for our sense of reality.
- **Energy packet exchange interface** — the mechanism by which our sensors interact with an "un-experienced reality," producing data in the central nervous system that, once interpreted, becomes our experience of local reality; TBC controls its output/return packets.
- **Un-experienced reality** — the undefined source reality that our sensors interact with through the energy-packet interface; Campbell states it is not directly relevant to our PMR experience, paralleling the irrelevance of a computer's power cord to AI Guy.
- **Space-time rule-set** — the rule-set, resident within TBC, that defines PMR physics, establishes the limits/constraints of interactions with other players, and produces the required physical effects (paper, ink, hands, eyes) of any interaction within PMR; described as "simple, direct, slow, detailed, and relatively linear," suited to an "elementary school" level of experience.
- **AI Guy** — Campbell's recurring simulated-character analogy (established in earlier chapters, used here as the comparison case): a being whose simulated experience is controlled by its creators exactly as our experience is controlled by the space-time rule-set; his creators define what data he can access and how he processes it, and he cannot perceive anything beyond his local reality (hardware, software, programmers, facilities) because it is irrelevant to his mission and outside his logical causality.
- **Fundamental Process** — named here as the driver of evolution, by which consciousness potential energy expanded into profitable states, consolidated profits, and cut losses.
- **Entropy reduction** — the steady decrease in system entropy that Campbell says has produced today's "highly synergistic complex configurations"; framed as the fundamental developmental imperative of consciousness and of us as its constrained constructs.
- **All That Is** — Campbell's term for the larger reality: all of consciousness, evolving toward greater profitability by improving itself through entropy reduction.
- **Mind-matter problem** — the traditional philosophical puzzle of how mind and matter relate; Campbell claims its solution is that, in the Big Picture, there is no matter — everything is mind, and "physical"/"nonphysical" are terms that only have meaning relative to each other, collapsing once the local-reality delusion is left behind.
- **Physical/nonphysical dichotomy** — one of several (with mind-matter, normal-paranormal, physics-metaphysics, science-philosophy) dichotomies Campbell calls illusions of perspective, arising only from a limited understanding exclusively focused within its own local reality.
- **Digital simulation / virtual reality** — Campbell's description of PMR as a digital simulation implementing the space-time rule-set, stepped forward in time increments that appear infinitesimal to observers, obeying only its own rule-set's causality.
- **Wave-particle duality, uncertainty principle, entangled-pair communication** — named physics phenomena Campbell claims become simple to explain, and cease to be paradoxes, once PMR is understood as a computed digital virtual reality rather than an absolute physical space.
- **Big Picture vs. little picture** — Campbell's recurring contrast between the larger reality (Big Picture, consciousness/All That Is) and the constrained, local, individuated perspective (little picture) of beings such as humans or AI Guy.
- **Causality (rule-set-relative)** — Campbell's claim that each reality dimension (PMR, NPMR) evolves its own rule-set suited to its own use/function/purpose, and that causality itself is the product of whichever rule-set governs a given reality.
- **Subjective vs. objective experience** — where multiple individuated minds share a local reality, Campbell distinguishes a shared/public (objective) experience from a personal (subjective) one, both "extremely significant" but functionally different.
- **Individuated consciousness / thought-form-fragment-being** — Campbell's description of individual beings ("you and I") as "clumps of individuated consciousness" engaged in energy-packet exchanges, and as a "thought-form-fragment-being chip from the old block of AUM consciousness."
- **Bootstrapping process** — Campbell's description of how progress (and quality and ability) develops: each new success is built on previous successes, applied here to humanity's approach toward discovering the digital nature of the Big Picture.
- **Big TOE nonnegotiable requirements** — two criteria Campbell states a fully correct Big Picture Theory Of Everything must meet: (1) it produces one consistent reality with no paradoxes; (2) it subsumes existing knowledge as a special case of a more general understanding. He claims My Big TOE fully meets both.
- **OS (Our System)** — referenced here as the system whose function/purpose the space-time rule-set supports ("the space-time rule-set supports human function and purpose within OS").
- **Matter as simulated menial effect** — Campbell's characterization of matter as an effect simulated by/for mind, useful because it creates a virtual environment that makes the evolution of consciousness more efficient and effective.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The book-in-mind example**: Campbell states "the book you are reading in your mind is the book I wrote in my mind," with paper, ink, the reader's hand, and eyeballs cast as the required physical effects of the space-time rule-set structuring that shared interaction — illustrating that the physical medium is a rule-set-imposed byproduct of a consciousness-to-consciousness exchange, not the substance of the exchange itself.
- **The author's own writing constraint**: Campbell notes he "had to type the damn thing with just two stubby little physical fingers and a pail of drugstore glasses," offered as a personal, first-person illustration of how space-time constrains even the transmission of the book's own ideas compared to how "a more natural, less constrained mind" could transmit and absorb the same content in "a few quick big gulps."
- **AI Guy simulation-control example**: reiterated from earlier material — the creators of AI Guy define what data he can access and the rules by which he processes it, "to create the optimal... simulation tool," and deliberately withhold from him any perception of the hardware, software, programmers, or facilities behind his simulation because these are irrelevant to his mission and outside his logical causality.
- **Billiard-ball atom model**: Campbell cites the classic (his description: "patently incorrect, but useful at an elementary level") picture of an atom as a billiard ball with BBs zipping around it, as an analogy for why he continues using the PMR/NPMR distinction despite calling it ultimately artificial — a deliberately simplified conceptual stepping-stone.
- **"Dozens of top scientists" claim**: Campbell asserts, without naming individuals or studies, that many top scientists are currently approaching the discovery that PMR is a digital picture within a computed reality, positioning My Big TOE as providing the theoretical foundation for phenomenology and hypotheses "currently being investigated by scientists and philosophers worldwide."
- **Fish-and-Irish-jig analogy**: Campbell offers, then self-critiques, an analogy — explaining to a fish how to dance an Irish jig is like telling a man he is nonphysical consciousness — noting it's actually a poor analogy because the fish has an excuse (no legs) while the man, theoretically, has a mind, used to close the unit on the difficulty of communicating the Big Picture to a being still identifying with its local physical viewpoint.
