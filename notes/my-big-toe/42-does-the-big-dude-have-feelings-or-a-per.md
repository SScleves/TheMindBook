# Ch 42 — Does the Big Dude Have Feelings or a Personality? What Does Love Have to Do With Consciousness?

## In one paragraph
Campbell opens the question of whether AUM — a non-biological consciousness — has feelings, moods, or a personality (does it get bored or lonely "playing only with and by itself?"), and announces that this chapter and the next will juxtapose AUM's personality and feelings against the reader's. His stated key to that assessment is understanding ego, and the bulk of the chapter is an extended "ego digression": fear (born of ignorance and high entropy) generates ego; ego, in collusion with the intellect, builds belief-based fantasy structures to mask fear; and these fantasies eventually manifest the feared thing into physical reality. Campbell then gives "technical" definitions: love is the interaction style of a low-entropy consciousness (love capacity is a direct measure of entropy within consciousness), and spirituality is simply consciousness quality. He closes by defining ego-free emotions — joy, peace, and a compassionate sadness — whose combination composes love, and grounds love itself in the cooperative interaction of subsystems within a large digital consciousness system, declaring love the purpose and positive direction of consciousness evolution.

## Core claims
- Campbell argues that because AUM is not biological, questions about its emotions are mind-focused questions, and that the key to logically assessing personality and feelings (AUM's or ours) is understanding ego.
- Campbell claims fear, at the deepest level, is generated by ignorance within a low-quality consciousness; fear and high entropy are mutually supportive, each creating and encouraging the other. Fear also arises from incomplete knowledge — it "leaps up in dread of unknown possibilities."
- Campbell recalls his four major evolutionary motivators of humankind: 1) survival and material success, 2) male-female relationship and sex, 3) influence, control, and power, 4) self-improvement, love, and fear. The first three come from physical evolution; the fourth from consciousness evolution. Perceived serious lack of any of the first three generates fear, especially in low-quality consciousness.
- Campbell holds that fear resides in intent or motivation, not in action; it is a state of consciousness — "mind-cancer," a disease of consciousness, debilitating like biological cancer to the system it grows in.
- Campbell asserts that ego is the direct result of fear. Needs, wants, expectations, and desires are generated by the ego as a shortsighted strategy to reduce fear-anxiety. (Not all desire is fear-based: basic desires like sex and hunger, and rightly motivated self-improvement, can be positive.)
- Campbell describes the ego as living between fears/desires and the intellect — a "trusted advisor" whose job is neutralizing fear's effects; the intellect's job is justifying, approving, and "blessing" the ego's construction materials (beliefs, attitudes, pseudo-knowledge, strategies). Together they ensure the individual always feels good about him- or herself; no ploy or deceit is off limits if it can be justified.
- Of fear, ego, and intellect, Campbell says only fear is fundamentally powerful; the other two, like the Wizard of Oz, derive apparent power from illusion and trickery.
- Campbell claims humans are notoriously weak at logical process (a computer is better at it); we excel at data collection, interpretation, synthesis, and creative self-expression because those are what consciousness evolution needs. Mathematics braces our weak native logic — without it "our ascent to power would have stalled in the nineteenth century if not before." Being deeply logical "is not particularly important to our purpose."
- Campbell argues the intellect becomes part of the belief trap's trapping mechanism by making the ego's wants appear rational; in matters of importance it rarely has more than a tiny fraction of the truth-data needed for useful logical analysis, and spends most of its time serving the ego.
- Campbell claims we are "enrolled in a PMR kindergarten for young low quality consciousness," which is also why the psi uncertainty principle (Chapters 13–14 of Book 2) is required within PMR.
- Campbell holds that art, intuition, creativity, pattern recognition, and creative synthesis lie largely outside the intellect; and that the intellect cannot love or lower entropy — intent and will operate at a deeper level than the intellect's information storage and data analysis.
- Campbell defines Big-Picture personal power as the power to make right choices leading to right action, derived from quality of consciousness, not from forcing issues to resolve to your satisfaction.
- Campbell states humility and compassion are the antitheses of ego — "what is left over when the ego is gone" — and the correcting mechanism for ego; knowledge and courage (open-minded skepticism in the relentless pursuit of truth) are the correcting mechanism for fear; love is the result of the success of both corrections.
- Campbell gives a "technical definition of love": love capacity is a direct measure of entropy within consciousness — inversely related to the ego and fear a being contains. Love is how a low-entropy, high-quality consciousness interacts with other individuated consciousness and other sentient or non-sentient entities.
- Campbell defines spirituality technically as equivalent to consciousness quality: spiritual growth = lowering the entropy of your consciousness; spiritual quality is inversely related to the entropy the individuated consciousness contains.
- Campbell asks whether it's clear that "dogma, self-righteousness, ritual, and ego driven intolerance and superiority, which are generally exhibited by both organized religion and science, have nothing to do with spirituality, love, or the pursuit of Big Truth" — explicitly pairing science with organized religion as institutions whose dogmatism is irrelevant to (or opposed to) spiritual quality.
- Campbell argues spirituality and love must be personal achievements, never organizational ones; the notion that organized religion imbues spiritual quality is "wishful thinking." He claims the violence and meanness laid at the feet of organized religious fervor "dwarfs any other evil known to man," with power politics and cultural beliefs taking second and third place — and the "unholy triumvirate" of religious belief, cultural belief, and ego-driven power politics is the source of most of the world's evil.
- Campbell insists the reader verify this via open-minded skepticism, not belief — and that the process of coming to a conclusion matters more than the conclusion; seeking Big Truth is an iterative, lifetime process with tentative conclusions.
- Campbell claims delusion normal for a culture is invisible from inside it; the clash of cultures is a clash of egos, delusions, and needs as well as of values. Delusions form a continuum "between very little (like you and me) and a lot (like some people we know)."
- Campbell asserts the apparently happy egotist is deceiving: delusion, self-righteousness, arrogance, and egotism are directly proportional to unhappiness and dissatisfaction, because any fantasy world is in constant conflict with the real one; a poor quality of life inevitably results from poor quality of consciousness.
- Campbell rejects the psychologists' "healthy ego": "normal" merely means culturally average fear and desire. Low personal entropy, high personal power, fearlessness, love, and no ego "are all on the same team"; beings with low or no ego have the highest quality consciousness and the greatest personal power (subsuming, not redefining, the standard sense — including self-defense, leadership, and accomplishment). "The capacity and ability to master consciousness evolution accrues to the warrior, not the wimp."
- Campbell claims the unfettered ego always inadvertently manifests the fear into the being's reality — "your ego makes your fears come true" — turning imagined difficulties into in-your-face physical reality at a speed proportional to the energy invested. This makes the ego a powerful teacher within a "great educational feedback reality"; a "life sucks" existential/nihilist attitude is disastrous and dooms one to no-growth self-inflicted pain.
- Campbell warns never to shatter someone's worldview by collapsing their house of cards with logic: shoving Big Truth into an entranced ego risks "hideous-truth-trauma (HTT) syndrome" and a retreat deeper into fantasy. Still, satori may strike "the walking oblivious" at any time.
- Campbell defines balance as right being: minimum-entropy living on a continuum from highest-entropy consciousness (fearful, self-important, vengeful — artifacts of fear and ego) to lowest (fearless, egoless, compassionate, humble, expressive of unconditional love). Internal balance precedes and enables external balance, never the reverse; control creates only the appearance of balance.
- Campbell qualifies "detachment": it does not mean withdrawn, uninvolved, or uncaring — only no longer influenced by needs, wants, desires, expectations, and beliefs; detachment from ego enables balance.
- Campbell notes anti-rats (beings evolving through negative intention) also seek lower entropy via internal balance by total control of self and approximate external balance by controlling externals — but their potential is "dreadfully constrained"; control is "a poor substitute for love."
- Campbell defines pain (non-physical) as the awareness of not getting what you want — ego-attachment to unfulfilled requirements; it is the negative feedback stimulus ("the electric shock") in the PMR learning lab, with joy, love, and happiness as the positive feedback stimuli.
- Campbell defines joy (self-awareness unconditionally enjoying existence, without ego), peace (self-awareness unconditionally content, knowing right motivation), and a compassionate sadness (knowing that one's parts, creations, and loved ones could be better) — coexisting and mutually supporting — and says joy, peace, and sadness compose love.
- Campbell grounds love ontologically: within a large digital consciousness system, cooperative interaction among subsystems for long-term mutual profitability, at its lowest-entropy best, IS love — the wellspring of primordial love. All effectively growing personal Big TOEs must converge to love; "Love is your purpose."

## Concepts
- **AUM** — the "Big Dude" of the title: the non-biological, evolving consciousness whose personality and feelings this chapter and the next examine in juxtaposition to the reader's. Because AUM is not biological, questions of its joy, boredom, or loneliness are "mind-focused questions."
- **ego** — the direct result of fear; a reactive structure with no power of its own that lives between fears/desires and the intellect, generating needs, wants, expectations, and desires as a shortsighted strategy to reduce fear-anxiety, and building "feel good" fantasy barriers between the fear and the intellect.
- **fear** — at the deepest level, generated by ignorance within a low-quality consciousness; mutually supportive with high entropy; residing in intent/motivation, not action; "mind-cancer," a disease of consciousness. The only fundamentally powerful member of the fear-ego-intellect trio. Fear can also be a phobic reaction to real or imagined pain, discomfort, or disadvantage — producing an awareness of potential pain that creates additional related fears, a snowballing feedback process that quickly saturates to maximize anxiety and discomfort.
- **intellect** — the functions of information storage and data analysis; humanity's proudest evolutionary accomplishment and the source of our technical prowess, yet notoriously weak at logical process; spends most of its time justifying and rationalizing the ego's fantasy structures. Cannot by itself love, lower entropy, or direct intent or will.
- **entropy** — Campbell's measure of consciousness quality (lower entropy = higher quality); fear and high entropy feed each other; love capacity is a direct measure of the entropy within a consciousness.
- **love** — technically defined: the word for how a low-entropy, high-quality consciousness interacts with other individuated consciousness and other sentient or non-sentient entities; what remains after ego's wants, needs, desires, and expectations are removed; a continuum of quality; ontologically, the lowest-entropy cooperative interaction among subsystems of a large digital consciousness system ("primordial love"); the purpose and positive direction of consciousness evolution.
- **spirituality / consciousness quality** — technically defined as equivalent: one becomes more spiritual by lowering the entropy of one's consciousness; spiritual quality is inversely related to the entropy the individuated consciousness contains.
- **four major motivators** — 1) survival and material success, 2) male-female relationship and sex, 3) influence/control/power (all three from physical evolution), and 4) self-improvement, love, and fear (from consciousness evolution); their unique blend drives the bulk of our choices.
- **desire** — generated by wants and needs; when Campbell speaks of desire he generally means the desire arising in response to the ego's needs, though basic desires (sex, hunger) and rightly motivated self-improvement are not necessarily fear-based.
- **fantasy / delusion** — the belief-held structure the ego-intellect team builds to counterbalance each fear; a "system of finely tuned personal beliefs" legitimized by the intellect as a rational reality; sizes on a continuum; culturally normal delusion is invisible from inside the culture.
- **fantasy-bubble** — the local reality one creates and self-imprisons within with friends and family; floats within the larger reality interacting with compatible bubbles. "We do not change reality, or modify absolute truths; we merely create a fantasy-bubble to live in."
- **fantasy support systems** — social/cultural mutual-support structures for shared fantasy: family, friends, work, school, church, clubs, sports, and — one of the largest — the information and entertainment media.
- **belief trap** — the trap the intellect helps spring by making beliefs appear rational; the ego's castle-dweller is "caught like a rat in a belief trap" with "no easy or painless way out."
- **humility** — an ego-antithesis allowing confidence, certainty, purpose, and passion while recognizing limitations (inside: one's own ignorance; outside: the laws and requirements of reality); breeds compassion and vice versa.
- **compassion** — with humility, the antithesis of ego — "what is left over when the ego is gone"; together the correcting mechanism for ego.
- **knowledge and courage** — the correcting mechanism for fear; courage is glossed as "open minded skepticism in the relentless pursuit of truth."
- **open-minded skepticism** — Campbell's long-term epistemic tool: gather comparative data, come to your own tentative, iteratively revisable conclusions; loses out culturally to belief-based immediate gratification (the "feel-good belief-based pain pill").
- **personal power (Big Picture sense)** — the power to make right choices leading to right action, derived from quality of consciousness; subsumes, and exceeds, the standard definition (taking charge of your life, self-defense, leadership, lasting accomplishment).
- **intent / will** — intent is a reflection of consciousness quality; will is applied intent; both operate at a deeper level than the intellect and cannot be directed by analyzing data.
- **balance / right being** — living with optimal spiritual productivity and minimal consciousness entropy; the minimum entropy state for a system or entity at a given time; a continuum, not on-off; internal balance precedes and automatically produces external balance. A balanced individual is "a conscious part of the unified larger reality, a productive citizen of the larger consciousness ecosystem," aware of being interconnected to everything.
- **detachment** — no longer being influenced by needs, wants, desires, expectations, and beliefs; explicitly NOT withdrawal, aloofness, or not caring; detachment from ego enables balance and low entropy.
- **rats / anti-rats** — rats: entities growing toward the positive side (the chapter's rat-maze learning imagery: "A dumb rat gets zapped!"); anti-rats: those evolving through negative intention, seeking low entropy via total self-control and control of externals — dreadfully constrained, since control is a poor substitute for love.
- **pain (non-physical)** — the awareness of not getting what you want; created by ego out of attachment to unfulfilled requirements; the primary negative feedback stimulus in the PMR "intent-behavior modification trainer."
- **joy** — self-awareness without ego unconditionally enjoying its existence, its creations, and whatever else it is aware of.
- **peace** — self-awareness unconditionally content with itself and satisfied with its state of being, knowing it acted from right motivation; balance generates peace and vice versa.
- **sadness (compassionate)** — self-awareness knowing that it, its parts, its implementations of the Fundamental Process, its creations, and loved ones could be better (more optimized, more profitable); born of compassion, humility, and caring — not "boohoo" ego-induced sadness. Joy, peace, and sadness compose love.
- **hideous-truth-trauma (HTT) syndrome** — Campbell's coinage for what happens when Big Truth is shoved into the intellect of an entranced ego: an emotional disturbance leading to an angry, fearful retreat deeper into the fantasy jungle.
- **satori** — sudden awakening that "may strike the walking oblivious at any time like a brilliant bolt of rogue lightning."
- **PMR** — physical matter reality; here characterized as a "kindergarten for young low quality consciousness" and a "learning lab" / "local virtual space-time reality" delivering feedback stimuli.
- **psi uncertainty principle** — referenced forward (Chapters 13–14 of Book 2) as a requirement within PMR, needed because we are not ready to take effective charge of the important aspects of existence.
- **digital consciousness system** — the framing in which love is derived: a large complex system whose bit-groupings (subsystems) must interact cooperatively for long-term mutual profitability to evolve toward lower entropy.
- **Fundamental Process** — Campbell's evolution process, mentioned as something whose implementations a self-aware being may know "could be better" (the source of compassionate sadness).
- **consciousness cycle** — the cycle within which evolving consciousness works, whose goal is love.
- **Big TOE** — a personal Big Theory of Everything; all effectively growing personal Big TOEs must converge to love as they progress toward Big Truth.
- **ego-space** — Campbell's term for the ego's operating domain where "what you see is often not what you get — image is everything."

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **The AUM framing questions** — Does AUM ever annoy itself, have a bad day, get bored or lonely "playing only with and by itself?" Campbell poses these to open the two-chapter exploration of AUM's personality and feelings versus ours, noting AUM is not biological so these are mind-focused questions.
- **Loved one unexpectedly ill or missing** — everyday example of the degenerative process where ignorance fans fear into "a blaze of insecurities building one upon the other" when we don't know how a situation will turn out.
- **Avoiding trouble** — example showing fear resides in intent, not action: purposely avoiding trouble may be good judgment, not fear.
- **Fear as mind-cancer** — analogy: like biological cancer, fear is debilitating and destructive of the system in which it grows.
- **Fear-desire pair example** — fear of being inadequate coupled to a need to appear adequate; the ego-need is satisfied by any contrivance denying or compensating for the fear, however transparent to others.
- **The Wizard of Oz** — analogy for ego and intellect: only fear is fundamentally powerful; the other two derive apparent power from illusion and trickery.
- **Samson's hair** — analogy: our intellect is the source of our power individually and as a species, providing the evolutionary advantage to dominate other creatures and exploit the earth.
- **Human vs. digital computer** — the intellect performs deduction/induction roughly as a computer would, "except the human intellect is not nearly as good at logical process as a computer"; mathematics is the tool bracing our "naturally diminutive logical abilities," without which our ascent to power would have stalled by the nineteenth century.
- **The "low quality" ego-prick test** — Campbell asks whether the phrase "low quality consciousness" pricked the reader's ego and produced an emotional downer: "If it did and you did, I rest my case."
- **Ego-attributes as construction devices** — arrogance, self-importance, and self-righteousness compared to arches, vertical walls, and pitched roofs the ego uses to build its delusion; being judged "egotistical" means your fantasy is obvious to others.
- **Garbage under the rug** — recurring analogy: fantasies only appear to make the fear-garbage go away; it stinks increasingly with age, becomes harder to hide, and less likely ever to be cleaned up; most cultures require certain garbage be swept under the rug while everyone ignores the stench.
- **The unholy triumvirate (historical case)** — religious belief, cultural belief, and ego-driven power politics as history's top three evils ("history's Hall of Shame"); Campbell claims organized religious fervor's violence dwarfs any other evil known to man and that religion's potential to stimulate genuine spiritual growth remains "largely theoretical and non-actualized." He tells readers to pull out their old world-history textbook, gather comparative data, and "taste the pudding" themselves.
- **The happy egotist debunked** — observational exercise: consider the deluded, self-righteous, arrogant people you know; their delusion is directly proportional to their unhappiness, especially once old enough for consequences to catch up.
- **The power-trip question** — Campbell imagines the objection "Isn't ego about power trips and pushing others around?" and answers that internal control of your fears is the driver of ego, while a concerted effort to gain and maintain external control (fantasy management) is the function of ego; control of yourself and others is used to convince the individual the delusion is real. The use of external power — "bullying, dominating, manipulating, bribing, and threatening" — is how this control is exercised to get what you want, need, and desire.
- **The bully** — a bully on the outside is a typical ego response to feeling inadequate, powerless, and weak on the inside.
- **Forcing someone to be nice to you** — example of using external power to deny fear: feeling likeable based on coerced behavior is "self-delusion at its most obvious"; using power to pretend to be anything drives you further from being it — "That is how an abstract fear manifests itself into reality."
- **Image as costume** — we open with our image, tailor it, and wear it like a costume until "we cannot tell the costume from our skin."
- **The naked-at-work dream** — the common recurring dream of being naked or unprepared at work/school/church dramatizes a core fear: fear of exposure and inadequacy of our ego-fantasy "faux-being-construct" — why hiding behind a costume makes us feel secure.
- **The psychologists' "healthy ego"** — Campbell's counter-case: being culturally average in fear and desire earns the label "normal/healthy"; maintaining a healthy ego so you can be "normally dysfunctional in your culture is how mediocrity finds comfort in the security of the herd."
- **House of cards → castle model** — Campbell's central model of ego-structure: layers of fantasy camouflaging fantasy become a Gordian knot so complex the intellect can never trace any motivational strand to a particular fear ("Presto! Change-o! The fear has disappeared!"); with "rationalization-putty and justification-Bondo" it becomes a custom castle (with moat and drawbridge) where ego and intellect live and work — the apparent you — while the foundation remains a house of cards and the drawbridge stays up against any information exposing the delusion.
- **Fantasy-bubbles** — model: each bubble floats within the larger reality, interacting with compatible bubbles, defining a local reality for those self-imprisoned within it. "What a game! What a shame!"
- **Anti-rat balance note** — negative-path entities achieve internal balance by total self-control and approximate external balance by controlling externals; their potential is dreadfully constrained.
- **Ego manifests fear — the rat-maze feedback model** — the ego-intellect constructs encourage choices that actualize the fear, transforming imagined difficulties into physical reality; self-induced pain is "the electric shock you get in the PMR learning lab's amazing maze," the negative feedback in the "intent-behavior modification trainer" of local virtual space-time reality; "A dumb rat gets zapped!" This is an educational feedback reality, not a "life sucks" reality.
- **Cement block swimmer** — going through life with self-pity attitudes ("woe is me," "life is unfair," "I just can't do it") is like trying to swim in deep water with a cement block strapped to your back.
- **The head-nodding reader / HTT warning** — many will read the whole ego aside thinking it's about someone else; Campbell asks the reader to wake such people gently, never to collapse their house of cards with logic (risking HTT syndrome and a vicious cornered-critter reaction), but notes satori may still strike like rogue lightning.
- **Feel-good pain pill** — analogy for belief: humankind finds it quicker to pop a belief-based pain pill covering the symptom (fear from ignorance) than to struggle to triumph over the root cause.
- **Your body as a complex system** — cited as a PMR-constrained example of a large complex system with subsystems that interrelate, communicate, and share information — grounding the definition of love as lowest-entropy cooperative interaction within a digital consciousness system.

## Thread
This chapter opens a two-chapter unit (with Chapter 43) asking whether AUM has feelings and a personality — but spends most of its length building the prerequisite machinery: Campbell's model of fear → ego → intellect-justified fantasy, and its corrections (humility/compassion for ego, knowledge/courage for fear), so that human feelings can later be contrasted with AUM's. It explicitly builds on Chapter 29 (Book 1), where evolution organized consciousness into self-aware intelligence, then values, personality, and purpose. Its payoff is a set of technical definitions — love as inverse entropy, spirituality as consciousness quality, joy/peace/sadness as ego-free states composing love, and primordial love as lowest-entropy cooperative interaction inside a digital consciousness system — that convert the trilogy's moral vocabulary into its entropy-reduction physics of consciousness. It also points forward to the psi uncertainty principle (Chapters 13–14 of Book 2) as a requirement of the PMR "kindergarten," and its closing line ("Love is your purpose") states the trilogy's answer to the purpose of existence: become fearless, humble, and compassionate by lowering the entropy of your consciousness.
