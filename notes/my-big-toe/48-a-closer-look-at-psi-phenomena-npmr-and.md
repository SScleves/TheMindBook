# Ch 48 — A Closer Look at Psi Phenomena, NPMR, and You

## In one paragraph
Campbell argues that psi phenomena only look like "hocus-pocus" from a limited PMR-only perspective; at a higher level of awareness, psi and psi-uncertainty are "straightforward scientific concepts subservient to a higher level of causality." He warns against both anthropomorphic explanations (nonphysical entities "pulling strings," today's equivalent of angels towing the sun and moon) and belief-based denial, arguing that modern science covers its ignorance with the same devices as its predecessors. He claims demonstrating psi effects is pointless: the psi uncertainty principle is a designed-in feature of the PMR learning lab that forces you to gain real knowledge of NPMR only through personal experience and growth, and the only valid test of progress on the Path of Knowledge is measurable improvement in the quality (lowered entropy) of your consciousness — love, humility, and compassion increasing as ego, fear, and attachment decrease. The chapter then gives a concrete tour of NPMR-N — its varied inhabitants, its rules, death by disassociation, nonphysical mass, energy density, and perception as database queries of TBC — insisting that NPMR is a structured, rule-based, objective, causal reality whose physics is a superset of PMR physics, continuous and integrated with PMR rather than a separate place.

## Core claims
- Campbell claims psi and psi-uncertainty are not magic but "straightforward scientific concepts subservient to a higher level of causality" — the mystery is an artifact of a PMR-only viewpoint.
- Campbell argues we must not conclude the psi-uncertainty principle is enforced by nonphysical entities pulling strings from NPMR; such anthropomorphic concepts "compound the original ignorance," just like the once-serious theory of angels pulling the sun and moon.
- Campbell claims humans habitually handle undeniable ignorance by over-stretching old paradigms, invoking spooky science or faith, or denying the data — and that modern Western science and philosophy "use the exact same devices, with the exact same zeal" as the angel-theorists; our present science will one day look "incredibly silly — perhaps within this century."
- Campbell argues insecurity, ignorance, arrogance, and ego team up against us, and that fear of the unknown gives others "ready handles" to manipulate and control people; ego is "the origins of most, if not all, belief."
- Campbell claims there is no point demonstrating psi effects: the "gee-whiz effect" is useless, and natural psi experiences (precognitive dreams, telepathy experienced by hundreds of millions) generally lead nowhere in PMR, though they can catalyze an individual who is ready. Individuals not ready "do not usually have these experiences."
- Campbell asserts real evolutionary progress — improvement in quality of consciousness — "must come from the inside out."
- Campbell claims evidence of paranormal experience is valid only to the experiencer (and a few who trust them totally); the Path of Knowledge is not for the timid, fearful, or insecure, and requires "a warrior's strong mind, focused intent, and fearless attitude combined with a scientist's patient probing, high analytical standards, and fundamental inquisitiveness."
- Campbell argues sanity and progress on the Path of Knowledge are judged pragmatically by results: whether your understanding produces consistent, measurable growth in quality of consciousness and in your ability to help others grow spiritually. "The artifacts of spiritual growth are not esoteric or subtle" — if they are absent, "what you are experiencing is delusional."
- Campbell claims that if a spiritual teacher's activity is better described as "marketing" than as helping people grow, "delusional nut with delusional or naive insiders" may be accurate regardless of how real their paranormal experiences are.
- Campbell asserts all Big Truth and wisdom is personal truth flowing only from personal experience; outside judges without experience — "vitriolic scoffers and virulent closed-minded skeptics" — are "the most obviously delusional of all," like the emperor in his new clothes.
- Campbell claims the psi uncertainty principle is "a fundamental requirement of all virtual PMR space-time learning labs" — a designed-in feature providing an optimal opportunity to lower entropy, not a punishment; if you did not need it, "it would melt away."
- Campbell argues dropping ego and material attachment produces the high signal-to-noise ratios required for sentience in NPMR; spiritual knowledge and paranormal ability are not two sides of one coin but "mutually supportive and strongly related activities" — paranormal ability is an available byproduct of the Path of Knowledge that can be ignored, and may or may not appear on the Paths of Service and Surrender.
- Campbell claims attachment to paranormal abilities halts or degenerates spiritual progress and degrades the abilities themselves — misuse is self-correcting. The best psi performers are those uninterested or unwilling to perform, because the need to perform raises noise and produces "psychological psi-impotence"; this is why traditional science, demanding repeatable physical performance, gets poor results ("trying to trap nonphysical phenomena in a physical bottle").
- Campbell asserts PMR actions alone (studying psi, meditation techniques, reading books, believing anything) will not open NPMR to you without actual entropy-decreasing growth; forcing NPMR to conform to PMR forms is forcing "the proverbial NPMR camel through the eye of a PMR needle."
- Campbell claims NPMR-N is populated by varied life forms, sentient and elemental, good and bad, with strict rules regulating violence; death (by disassociation to a maximum-entropy state) is exceedingly rare, rule-breaking happens, only the Big Cheese can execute capital punishment, and all actions and motivations are transparent and stored in memory, so "lies are always counterproductive."
- Campbell asserts nonphysical bodies and objects are individuated consciousness energy whose perceived appearance is defined by the NPMR rule-set; "sensory views" are different query filters applied to subsets of the NPMR database within TBC, and denser appearance can mean (depending on view) dimmer awareness/higher entropy/higher ego-fear content, or higher probability of physical manifestation.
- Campbell claims F = ma and E = mc² are two rules within our space-time rule-set that give a rough idea of more general NPMR rules: focused mental energy applied by intent (force) modifies the inertia, density, coherence, or persistence (mass) of a thought-form.
- Campbell claims physical reality is "a secondary manifestation wholly dependent upon its primary nonphysical source" (to be shown in Section 4), so dissipating a tumor in the nonphysical dissipates it physically as well — but meddling with the learning lab's natural results is not always good: "great power must be tempered with great wisdom."
- Campbell asserts the form of NPMR data (how it is internalized as experience) is personal and unimportant, but the content is the same for everyone with a clear unbiased connection: "Big Truth is universal; one's experience of it is always personal."
- Campbell argues mathematical physics is the symbolic logic of the little picture and cannot lead to Big Picture understanding — "you cannot pull an elephant out of an acorn"; personal solutions must precede technical ones.
- Campbell claims NPMR-N is not a separate place: PMR is a dependent subset of NPMR-N, continuous and integrated with it; space and time are local constructs of the space-time rule-set; every being in our PMR is extant in NPMR-N (the converse is not true), and you already function in NPMR-N, unaware only because of constraints you place on your own awareness.

## Concepts
- **psi phenomena** — Paranormal effects and experiences (Campbell lists teleporting, healing, traveling, remote viewing, telepathic communication with physical or nonphysical beings, precognitive dreams). Campbell treats them as lawful phenomena of a larger causality, not magic, and as an optional byproduct of consciousness quality.
- **psi uncertainty principle** — Campbell's requirement that uncertainty must surround nonphysical-to-physical manifestations, so that real knowledge of NPMR can only be gained through personal experience and growth. He defines it as "a fundamental requirement of all virtual PMR space-time learning labs" and a designed-in feature providing optimal opportunity to lower entropy — not a punishment, and not enforced by meddling entities.
- **PMR (physical-matter reality)** — Our physical reality; a "virtual PMR space-time learning lab" and, per this chapter, a dependent subset of NPMR-N — a secondary manifestation wholly dependent on its primary nonphysical source. Its space-time is a local construct produced by the space-time rule-set.
- **NPMR (nonphysical-matter reality)** — The larger nonphysical reality. Campbell insists it is "a structured, rule-based, objective, causal reality" whose physics is "a superset of PMR physics" — more general, with more degrees of freedom and less constrained possibilities of existence.
- **NPMR-N (NPMRN)** — The specific part of NPMR that contains our PMR. Campbell describes it as more varied and abundant in life forms than PMR, relatively structured, peaceable, and safe compared to other NPMRs, continuous and integrated with PMR ("not a different place"). Every being in our PMR is extant in NPMR-N; the converse is not true.
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — Campbell's computer model of reality's substrate: NPMR perception is described as queries of "a subset of the NPMR database within TBC." All actions and events (information or energy transfers) are stored in its memory for post-event analysis.
- **EBC** — Referenced alongside TBC as holding data to which perception queries and filters are applied ("the set of queries and filters that can be applied to the data within TBC and EBC"); an even larger computing context in Campbell's model (used here without expansion).
- **rule-set** — The set of rules that "lays down the laws of being and interaction" within a reality — its physics and causality. Campbell says to think of rule-sets as "data filters that define your energetic interaction with the data," possibly granting read-only or read-write-modify privileges.
- **space-time rule-set** — The rule-set defining PMR: space and time are its direct artifacts, and equations like F = ma and E = mc² are two of its rules, special cases of more general NPMR rules.
- **NPMR physics** — The rule-set defining causality, perception, allowable energy transfers, and the form/content limits of transferable information within NPMR; a superset of PMR physics.
- **nonphysical mass / nonphysical matter** — Campbell's admittedly "oxymoronic"-sounding generalization of mass: the apparent density of nonphysical energy having "the combined qualitative properties of persistence and inertia — staying power, resistance to change, heaviness, and the ability to survive and persist under the duress of external forces."
- **energy density (in NPMR)** — The apparent denseness of nonphysical forms. In one sensory view, denser = dimmer awareness, higher entropy, higher ego and fear content; in another view (of the nonphysical-physical transition region), denser = higher expectation value (less uncertainty) of manifesting physically.
- **sensory view / query filter** — A specific query of a subset of the NPMR database within TBC; different views are like examining the same body with unaided eyes, X-ray, CAT scan, thermogram, or sonogram — each requiring skilled interpretation.
- **expectation value** — The probability of a nonphysical object manifesting (or maintaining manifestation) as part of physical reality; denser nonphysical objects have higher expectation values and require more energy to modify.
- **thought-form** — A nonphysical form whose inertia, density, coherence, or persistence (m) can be modified by sharply focused mental energy applied by intent (F), by constraining the dimension in which it can be extant as a function of time.
- **individuated consciousness** — What beings and objects in NPMR-N are made of: "individuated consciousness energy" with associated bodies or forms whose perception is defined by the NPMR rule-set.
- **death by disassociation** — Death in NPMR: loss of internal organization, identity dissolved when one's energy reaches "a maximum entropy state approximating the final state predicted by the second law of thermodynamics" — like degaussing a floppy disk. The defining code and memory are deleted from the simulation; the last state is held as a historical record ("last saved file") from which reconstitution is theoretically possible but "rarer than rare."
- **the Big Cheese** — Campbell's name for the authority in NPMR-N; as far as he knows, the only one who can execute capital punishment for specific offenses, and whose call a reconstitution from the historical record would be.
- **rats and anti-rats** — Beings in Campbell's maze metaphor; only in a few circumstances are they allowed by the rules to destroy each other permanently. The low-entropy consciousness finds the "amazing maze" a fun challenge delivering "an endless supply of exquisite cheese."
- **entropy (of consciousness)** — Campbell's measure of consciousness quality: lowering entropy = spiritual growth, whose effects become "as obvious as being hit by a truck"; maximum entropy = dissolution of identity.
- **quality of consciousness** — The real object of growth; measured pragmatically by increasing love, wisdom, humility, compassion, helpfulness, and balance as ego, fear, and material attachments decrease.
- **Path of Knowledge** — The warrior-scientist path: strong mind, focused intent, fearless attitude, plus patient probing, high analytical standards, and inquisitiveness. On it, spiritual knowledge and paranormal ability are often intertwined; not for the timid or insecure.
- **Paths of Service and Surrender** — Alternative spiritual paths on which paranormal abilities "may or may not be encountered."
- **Big Truth** — Truth about the larger reality; it cannot be given by demonstration, only learned as fact and believed — real knowledge of it requires personal experience. "Big Truth is universal; one's experience of it is always personal." All Big Truth and wisdom is personal truth.
- **Big Picture / little picture** — The larger reality versus the PMR-only view. Little-picture logic (including mathematical physics, "the logic (in symbolic form) of the little picture") can never derive Big Picture understanding.
- **signal-to-noise ratio** — Campbell's measure of clear consciousness function: dropping ego and material attachment produces the high signal-to-noise ratios required for being sentient in NPMR; need or desire to perform raises noise.
- **psychological psi-impotence** — Campbell's term for the failure to perform produced when performance demands tug at the subject's ego, increasing noise and lowering signal-to-noise.
- **ego / fear / belief** — The trio Campbell blames for denial and manipulation: creativity and "induced myopia" rise to justify what one needs to believe; ego is "the origins of most, if not all, belief"; fear and ego provide "ready handles" for others' control.
- **learning lab** — PMR's function: a virtual space-time environment whose natural results (e.g., illness) serve growth; purposely modifying expectation values (nonphysical healing) is meddling that "is not always a good thing."
- **paradigm transcendence** — Campbell's claim that new concepts can only be built by "stretching existing concepts and metaphors beyond their current use," combining creative synthesis of old ideas with inspired intuition — which is why transcending paradigms is exceptionally difficult.
- **Flatlander** — Used as a shorthand for a mind trying to force NPMR to conform to the form, function, and properties of PMR.
- **the PMRk** — Campbell's name for realities "where energy appears most dense": there, "change is relatively slow, steady, and smooth; existence is relatively simple, basic, and stable; and interactions between sentient beings are relatively inelastic, viscous and sticky."
- **belief trap** — Named in passing as the chapter closes the techie aside: "those belief traps are amazing — they can transmute simple ignorance and incompetence into blind stupidity in a flash."

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **Angels pulling the sun and moon** — Campbell's opening historical example: a "one-time very serious and popular theory strongly supported by the best and brightest" of Western science, philosophy, and theology. He uses it to show those believers were not stupid but covered ignorance with belief-consonant theories "exactly as we do," and predicts our present science will look equally silly to future generations, "perhaps within this century."
- **The three smug monologues** — Campbell ventriloquizes three paragraphs of contemporary attitude, from "We finally understand how the natural world works" in the first to "As a species, we have become incredibly smart and scientifically advanced — everyone knows that. You must be stupid if you don't know that" closing the second, and challenges the reader: "Does one or more of the preceding three paragraphs roughly represent your core beliefs? Dig deep and be honest."
- **Catalog of denial devices** — Ignoring the experience, claiming trickery or conspiracy, pleading temporary insanity, blaming witchcraft or the devil, hypnotic manipulation, or drugs slipped into the morning coffee — Campbell's inventory of how people deny anomalous experience.
- **Teleportation-to-China thought experiment** — Because of psi uncertainty, it would be easier to teleport someone to China *and back* than to leave them there (less provable), and easier still if they return with only memory; even souvenirs would be dismissed as bought at the local import shop. The traveler's choice: be "a delusional nut in the eyes of almost everyone" while secure in the truth, or deny the experience and "plead insanity." Shows evidence of psi is valid only to the experiencer.
- **The emperor's new clothes, revised** — Scoffers who judge without personal experience are like the emperor in socially acceptable new clothes — "the difference being… that the emperor and his most loyal subjects all use the same highly recommended tailor." Also "the proof of the pudding is in the tasting" for evaluating teachers.
- **Elephant in a pea patch** — A highly evolved individual "sticks out from the crowd like an elephant in a pea patch": beloved, gently and fearlessly effective. The visibility of real growth is Campbell's sanity test.
- **The uncooperative psi performer** — Campbell's model of why lab parapsychology fails: performance demands tug at ego, raising noise and causing psychological psi-impotence, so "the best performers (perhaps the only great performers) are those not particularly interested in performing, or those unwilling to perform"; a difficult performer is not necessarily faking. Experimenters err by "requiring nonphysical phenomena to be (behave as) physical phenomena" — trapping the nonphysical in a physical bottle.
- **Curly and Larry's nonphysical microscope** — Callback (twice) to the earlier bit of Curly and Larry trying to study the nonphysical with a physical microscope: "That doesn't work." Later: the nonphysical microscope is metaphorically your mind, and equations cannot help you find or evolve it.
- **NPMR camel through a PMR needle** — Forcing NPMR to conform to PMR science/philosophy/3D space-time is "haplessly and hopelessly trying to force the proverbial NPMR camel through the eye of a PMR needle."
- **College/graduate school and musicianship** — Campbell's scale for the commitment personal experimentation demands: like getting through graduate school plus becoming an accomplished musician; "Everybody can do it, but not everybody will do it."
- **The NPMR-N field guide (the chapter's central descriptive model)** — Life forms more varied and abundant than in PMR; some sentient, some elemental; "the good, the bad, and the ugly — and the beautiful"; violence and peace under strict rules; "lots of parasites, males, females, neuters, bullies, and social workers… unnatural death and employment, but no natural death or taxes"; no one grows old, only more or less loving, wise, fearful, greedy, etc.; a much greater range of quality among sentient beings than in PMR. No lawyers — you plead your own case, all actions and motivations transparent, everything stored in memory, so lies are counterproductive; only the Big Cheese executes capital punishment. Other NPMRs are generally less structured, peaceable, friendly, productive, and safe.
- **Degaussing a floppy disk / last saved file** — The model of NPMR death: identity dissolved at maximum entropy per the second law of thermodynamics; defining code and memory deleted from the simulation, held only as a historical record; reconstitution theoretically possible ("I have never seen that happen") and the Big Cheese's call.
- **Two-view density model with F = ma and E = mc²** — Campbell's model of nonphysical mass: in one query of TBC's NPMR database, denser forms = dimmer awareness/higher entropy/more ego-fear; in another (the transition region), denser = higher probability of physical manifestation, with more nonphysical "m" storing more potential nonphysical "E" (as in E = mc²) and requiring more Force — "focused mental energy with intent" — to modify (as in F = ma). The two equations are presented as two space-time rule-set rules approximating more general NPMR rules.
- **The brain tumor example** — If the nonphysical aspects of a brain tumor appear very dense, it has manifested or is manifesting physically; denser incipient tumors need more energy per unit time to dissipate. Since physical reality wholly depends on its nonphysical source (Section 4), tumors dissipated nonphysically "will automatically dissipate within the physical as well" — but such meddling with the learning lab is not always wise.
- **X-ray/CAT scan/thermogram/sonogram analogy** — Different NPMR "sensory views" are like imaging the same body part with different instruments, or "looking at the same database through several different query filters," each needing skilled interpretation.
- **Read/write/modify privileges** — Rule-sets as data filters granting differing access: "You may have read privileges only, or be allowed to read, write, and modify."
- **"We are among the densest beings within NPMR-N"** — Campbell's pun-payoff: physical beings are very dense in the energy-density sense, and "that sense of the word 'dense' fits like a glove."
- **The techie aside and ballistic trajectories** — Campbell pulls the "left-brainers" aside: he has purposely omitted breadth and detail, as introductory physics books omit air friction, non-uniform gravitation, Coriolis effects, and ballistic dispersion. Wanting a mathematical description of NPMR physics is "an error of misplaced emphasis": little-picture logic cannot yield the Big Picture — "You cannot pull an elephant out of an acorn." This is "a survey course at the 101 level, not a post-doc dissertation."
- **Crawl, walk, run — the eight-month-old** — Trying to run (technical mastery) before crawling (personal quality) causes a face-plant and the false conclusion "running is impossible… Anybody who tries to run is either an idiot or a fool." Babies keep trying because others' success is undeniable; committed intellectuals instead become "champions of denial" convincing themselves their ignorance, rather than their belief, is the delusion. Campbell closes the aside by naming the mechanism: "those belief traps are amazing — they can transmute simple ignorance and incompetence into blind stupidity in a flash."
- **Freudian reference** — Fear of the unknown described as "more subconscious than conscious in Freudian terminology," exploited by manipulators.

## Thread
This chapter closes out Section 3's treatment of psi (following the preceding discussion of psi phenomena and the psi uncertainty principle, which Campbell explicitly references: "Following our discussion about the relationship between psi phenomena and uncertainty…"). It converts the uncertainty requirement from a constraint into a purpose — psi uncertainty exists so that Big Truth can only be earned through personal experience and entropy reduction — and supplies the practical epistemology for the Path of Knowledge: how to tell growth from delusion in yourself and others. It then gives the trilogy's most concrete NPMR-N description so far (inhabitants, death, rules, nonphysical mass, density, TBC queries), deliberately at "101 level," setting up Section 4's detailed treatment of rule-sets and reality-as-experience ("In Section 4, rule-sets, and how they define the experience we perceive as reality… are discussed in detail") and deferring communications, time travel, teleportation, and multiple bodies to Chapter 12, Book 3. It also foreshadows Section 4's claim that physical reality is a secondary manifestation of its nonphysical source, and the signal-to-noise requirement for NPMR sentience treated in Chapter 4, Book 3.
