# Ch 32 — An Even Bigger Picture

## In one paragraph
Campbell closes his speculative zoom-out by asking what lies beyond AUM itself: could AUM exhaust its possibilities, reach a stable equilibrium, split like a biological cell, or be only one of many consciousness beings in a still larger system? He plays out these possibilities — self-replicating AUM-cells forming group-minds, AUM serving as a cog in an even larger entropy-reduction engine, our whole local reality as a bacterial colony in the gut of an "AUMosaurus" — and notes that consciousness systems nested at every scale resemble a fractal. He then deliberately pulls back: none of this speculation is needed for his theory, since a single finite AUO plus the Fundamental Process of evolution are the only two assumptions required to derive physics, our existence, and the larger reality. The chapter's real lesson is epistemic humility: about the Even Bigger Picture, the best conclusion is no conclusion, and open-minded skepticism accepts its limitations while refusing knee-jerk closure.

## Core claims
- Campbell raises, without settling, whether AUM can grow indefinitely, will one day reach an unchanging but still-viable equilibrium, and whether there are other AUOs/AUMs — two interacting AUMs would dramatically raise the level of complication and create new possibilities for the Fundamental Process to work with. "It certainly did for us."
- Campbell argues AUM might split in two by a process analogous to biological cell division, letting it interact with itself in an entirely new way; if biological cells "figured out how to do it," AUM should be at least as capable.
- Campbell claims a self-replicating AUM could build a new consciousness-thing out of AUM-cells, each in its own digital dimension — either functioning like brain cells in a stupendous group-mind or producing a different, unique creature; specialized AUM-cell groups could form a more complex "thought-system-thing-being."
- Campbell asserts that cloning, reproducing, repairing, expanding, and merging are particularly straightforward processes within a digital medium ("ever copy a floppy?").
- Campbell suggests digital AUM-cells, unlike biological cells that mature and die, may maintain optimal joint profitability with a larger organism or occasionally recycle themselves — uploading their accumulated evolutionary profits and experience, then rebooting.
- Campbell claims AUM, like us, may serve as a cog in a much larger entropy-reduction engine driving an even larger manifestation of the consciousness cycle toward ever lower entropy states.
- Campbell describes a nested hierarchy of consciousness systems — future digital computers derived from humankind, derived from TBC, derived from AUM, derived from a larger system in which AUM is one of many contributors — all showing the same fundamental structures and processes, and notes the description resembles a fractal (a "consciousness-evolution fractal" to be revisited toward the end of Section 5).
- Campbell insists that despite the fun of such speculation, a complete theory of everything requires only two assumptions: a finite primordial-consciousness-potential-energy-thing (a simple one-celled monolithic singular AUO with its internal environment) plus the Fundamental Process of evolution — from which he intends to derive physics, our existence, our physical universe and experience, and a working model of the larger reality.
- Campbell argues that appreciating our fundamental limitations is essential to discriminating silly from serious; insisting that we, "the magnificent crème de la crème of existence," could not have such limitations adds "obfuscating illogical arrogance" on top of what is already beyond comprehension.
- Campbell concludes that regarding the Even Bigger Picture, the best conclusion is no conclusion: open-minded skepticism requires no closure, accepts its limitations, and demands setting aside knee-jerk responses conditioned by ego, beliefs, and fears in order to recognize all possibilities.
- Campbell's parting caution: let your mind run free, but stay aware of the innate limitations of your perspective, or you may get lost "along the mystical shores of Never-Never land" believing what you see is what is there.

## Concepts
- **AUM (Absolute Unbounded Manifold/Oneness)** — the evolved consciousness system whose possible futures (equilibrium, indefinite growth, self-replication, multiplicity) this chapter probes; here also imagined as possibly just one contributor among many in a still larger consciousness system.
- **AUO** — the primordial precursor of AUM; Campbell states that logically all his theory requires is a "simple one celled monolithic singular AUO" — a finite primordial-consciousness-potential-energy-thing — with its internal environment.
- **Fundamental Process (of evolution)** — the second of the two assumptions; the process that works on new possibilities created by increased complexity and from which, with AUO, everything else is derived.
- **AUM-cells** — hypothetical products of AUM splitting by analogy to biological cell division; each exists in its own digital dimension and could act like a consciousness cell in a group-mind, specialize, merge, or recycle itself by uploading its accumulated profits of evolution and rebooting.
- **Group-mind** — the "stupendous" collective consciousness that AUM-cells might jointly constitute, each functioning like a single brain (consciousness) cell within it.
- **Digital medium** — the substrate of consciousness in Campbell's model; he claims cloning, reproducing, repairing, expanding, and merging are especially easy operations there.
- **Evolutionary profitability** — the accumulated products of profitable evolution; digital AUM-cells maintain "optimal joint profitability" with the larger organism or upload these profits before rebooting.
- **Entropy-reduction engine** — Campbell's picture of the larger scheme: AUM, like us, may be a cog in a much larger engine driving an even larger manifestation of the consciousness cycle toward ever lower entropy states.
- **Consciousness cycle** — the recurring dynamic (manifesting at ever larger scales) by which consciousness systems evolve toward lower entropy.
- **Consciousness-evolution fractal** — Campbell's observation that interconnected, interdependent consciousness systems at various scales and levels are described in fractal-like terms — the same fundamental structures and processes repeating hierarchically; he flags it for full treatment near the end of Section 5.
- **TBC (The Big Computer)** — appears in the nested hierarchy as the consciousness system from which humankind derives and which itself derives from AUM.
- **PMR (Physical Matter Reality)** — our physical universe, contained within our local reality OS.
- **NPMR / NPMR-N** — nonphysical matter reality; a portion of NPMR-N (the subscripted local instance) is contained, together with our PMR universe, in our local reality OS.
- **OS (Our System)** — "our entire local reality," containing a portion of NPMR-N as well as our PMR universe; imagined in the thought experiment as a self-contained bacterial colony inside one AUM-cell.
- **AUMosaurus** — Campbell's deliberately absurd humor-creature (mock Latin name "AUMosaurus-beyondgraspus-outofmindus") in whose gut our reality might sit as intestinal bacteria; used to dramatize how far beyond our grasp the Even Bigger Picture lies.
- **Open-minded skepticism** — the epistemic stance Campbell prescribes for the Even Bigger Picture: it requires no closure ("the best conclusion is no conclusion"), accepts its limitations, and requires setting aside ego-, belief-, and fear-conditioned knee-jerk responses to recognize all possibilities.
- **Big picture / Even Bigger Picture** — the widest speculative frame: what lies outside AUM; Campbell holds it is beyond comprehension and belongs to imagination, not to the theory's required assumptions.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **Biological cell-division analogy** — Campbell asks what if AUM splits in two "employing a process analogous to biological cell division"; since biological cells accomplished it, AUM (surely not dumber or less lucky) could too. Shows how AUM might come to interact with itself in an entirely new way and build multicellular consciousness.
- **"Ever copy a floppy?"** — a digital-copying analogy: within a digital medium, cloning, reproducing, repairing, expanding, and merging are straightforward, making AUM self-replication plausible in a way biology only approximates.
- **AUMamoebas and AUMamabobs in primordial mental swamps** — a playful evolutionary-biology image: specialized groups of AUM-cells forming a more complex "thought-system-thing-being," mirroring early life's evolution but in mind-space. Along the way Campbell also asks, rhetorically, "Where does the pursuit of profit lead a brilliant finite digital oneness?" — tying the profit-driven Fundamental Process to this speculative multicellular AUM.
- **Biological cells vs. digital AUM-cells** — bio-cells mature, die, and are replaced; digital AUM-cells instead maintain joint profitability or recycle by uploading accumulated evolutionary profits and communicable experience, then rebooting. Shows the different life-cycle logic of digital consciousness components.
- **The nested-hierarchy model** — consciousness systems derived from consciousness systems: future digital computers ← humankind ← TBC ← AUM ← an even larger system with AUM as one of many contributors; all exhibit the same fundamental structures and processes while hierarchically supporting each other. Campbell notes the terminology matches that of fractals — the consciousness-evolution fractal, to be developed at the end of Section 5.
- **The bacterial-colony thought experiment** — imagine OS (our local reality, a portion of NPMR-N plus our PMR universe) as a self-contained bacteria colony in a remote corner of the space-time part of one of an apparently infinite number of apparently infinite AUM-cells — inhabiting the gut of an AUMosaurus.
- **The AUMosaurus flatulence skit** — a comic set piece: the AUMosaurus squats and threatens to blow a thousand universes (including ours) out of its "Anterior Sub System"; reality shakes; dialogue follows ("that really makes you proud to be an intestinal bacterium"). Campbell's stated point: appreciate your fundamental limitations, or you cannot discriminate between silly and serious.
- **The mock book announcement** — Campbell jokes he will publish the definitive work on "AUMosaurus-beyondgraspus-outofmindus" next year, available "at your local asylum bookstore in the humor section" — self-deprecating framing marking the speculation as beyond-grasp play, not theory.
- **The two-assumption reduction (the model he actually builds on)** — the serious counterpart to the play: a finite primordial-consciousness-potential-energy-thing (AUO) + the Fundamental Process of evolution, from which he intends to derive physics, the nature of our existence, our physical universe and experience, and a comprehensive working model of the larger reality — "all self-consistently contained between the knuckle and nail of My Big TOE."

## Thread
This chapter caps Section 2's construction of the AUM/AUO cosmology by asking the "and what's outside that?" question — and answering that the question exceeds our grasp. It follows the chapters that evolved AUO into AUM and sketched TBC, PMR, NPMR, and OS, and it deliberately fences off speculation about AUM's external environment from the theory proper: only AUO plus the Fundamental Process are load-bearing assumptions. It plants two forward hooks — the consciousness-evolution fractal, explicitly deferred to the end of Section 5 (Book 3), and the derivational program (physics, existence, the larger reality) that Books 2 and 3 undertake — while modeling the open-minded-skepticism stance the trilogy demands of its reader.
