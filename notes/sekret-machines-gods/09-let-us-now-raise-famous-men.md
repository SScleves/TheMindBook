# Let Us Now Raise Famous Men

## In one paragraph
DeLonge and Levenda ask whether ancient contact matters if aliens themselves die, then shift the desired outcome from bodily immortality to continuity of identity and read Egyptian funerary religion as an attempted technology for that problem. Documentary Egyptian texts, tombs, mummies, stellar alignments, ritual implements, and concepts of *ka*, *ba*, *akh*, shadow, body, heart, and name establish an extraordinarily detailed after-death system; the authors interpret it as a Cargo Cult imitation of paleocontact and call the pyramid a resurrection machine, while clearly labeling their suspended-animation visitor story as a fanciful scenario. They contrast textual evidence with weak monument-decoding, surveying Cagliostro and pre-Champollion projection, von Däniken and Pelley, Bauval's Orion Correlation controversy, Robert Temple's Dogon/Sirius work, NASA reactions, Peter Tompkins's intelligence background, and *Hamlet's Mill* to show how personality, stigma, politics, and evidence quality become entangled. Modern parallels include Lenin's Djoser-styled mausoleum, Soviet extraterrestrial messaging, DNA preservation, astronaut torpor, relativity, Tibetan death guidance, robot and drone telepresence, and digital doubles, all used to ask whether humanity is independently reinventing the same consciousness-and-space technology. The chapter's strongest conclusion is not that aliens built pyramids, which the authors explicitly resist, but that Egyptian direct testimony describes death as a dangerous, staged, celestially navigated transformation and that this deserves analysis as technology rather than dismissal as superstition.

## Core claims
- **[Programmatic question]** Death and fear of death organize religion by driving the search for meaning, God, and relevance. Karl Marx's opium critique captures religion's political and psychological function but only from a materialist perspective.
- **[Author corrective]** If religion began in alien contact, contact has not demonstrated literal immortality. Seventy years of modern records contain no case of an experiencer made immortal, and alleged corpses at Roswell would imply alien mortality.
- **[Documentary cultural claims with caveat]** Stories of alien healing or revival are distinct from immortality. In the 1951 film *The Day the Earth Stood Still*, Gort revives Klaatu after a soldier shoots him, but Klaatu says the restoration is temporary; fiction is used as conceptual illustration, not evidence.
- **[Reported observation]** Anne Strieber, wife of experiencer Whitley Strieber, noticed that dead persons sometimes appear alongside aliens in contact accounts. The chapter says this suggests post-death existence but does not establish immortal identity.
- **[Cargo Cult analogy]** South Pacific peoples saw aircraft, engines, radios, clothes, food, medicine, war, and invasion but no cure for death. The authors infer that paleocontact's most important misunderstood "cargo" may instead have concerned identity continuity.
- **[Scientific possibility as reported]** Human lifespans may reach 100, 125, or more through changing genomic obsolescence programs; aliens might routinely live 300 years, as imagined by the 1952 film *Red Planet Mars*. Longevity still leaves a final death and a larger archive of identity to lose.
- **[Author hypothesis]** Space travel may connect longevity and post-death identity, explaining ancient monumental architecture and funerary systems. The chapter proposes fear of death as the human desperation beneath scriptures from Egypt, Genesis, Gospels, Qur'an, Bhagavad-Gita, Lotus Sutra, and Zend Avesta.
- **[Historical epistemology]** European knowledge of Egypt long depended on Greek reports and architectural speculation because hieroglyphs were not deciphered until Champollion used the Rosetta Stone in the 1820s. This allowed centuries of unconstrained occult projection.
- **[Historical case]** Count Cagliostro (1743-1795) founded an Egyptian form of Freemasonry in 1784 based on imagined mysteries. Joseph Smith Jr. later claimed "Reformed Egyptian" translation for the *Book of Mormon*; the authors state no such language exists.
- **[Hellenistic history]** Alexander invaded Egypt in 323 BCE and began Alexandria's library; Romans under Julius Caesar destroyed it around 48 BCE. The Ptolemaic third-to-first centuries BCE produced the Greek Septuagint and collected Hermetic texts.
- **[Conceptual history]** Greek Hermes corresponds to Egyptian Thoth/Tahuti, god of writing, mathematics, and wisdom. The Emerald Tablet's Arabic version appeared around the seventh or eighth century CE and became a foundational alchemical text through its above/below formula.
- **[Etymological/intellectual history]** Alchemy was long derived from Arabic *al khemia*, Egyptian matter, and early Western alchemical authors include Maria the Jewess. Alchemy transforms metals, seeks universal medicine, and can describe psychobiological transformation.
- **[Interpretive history]** Carl Jung foregrounded psychological individuation in alchemy and neglected its chemical side; Asian systems joined chemistry and spirituality. The authors say all aspects belong to one transformation project.
- **[Documentary Egyptian religion]** Egyptian afterlife is transformation in worlds or dimensions other than ordinary Earth. Osiris is simultaneously historical/local and eternally present, travels to stars through levels, uses a ship, and requires mummification.
- **[Textual corpus claim]** Pyramid Texts, Coffin Texts, and *Book of Going Forth by Day* integrate astronomy, biology, psychology, obstacles, gateways, names, and ritual action more systematically than the Babylonian/Jewish ascent systems.
- **[Author methodological claim]** Looking at these documents through possible non-terrestrial context reveals a proposed practical meaning. R. T. Rundle Clark's claim that Egyptians knew what they were doing when mythologizing authorizes treating myth as deliberate technology, though paleocontact remains the authors' inference.
- **[Cargo Cult psychology]** Humans and children imitate meaningful action without understanding it. The authors apply this to Egyptian funerary texts: detailed imitation of an original event might preserve efficacy or reveal its essence even if the imitators lack mechanism.
- **[Author thesis]** Egyptians did not regard myth as two separate systems of science/politics and consciousness/religion; they regarded it as one technology, a machine. The pyramid is therefore framed as an interstellar or resurrection device, not merely a symbolic tomb.
- **[Documentary Egyptological source]** Toby Wilkinson describes Djoser's north-aligned Step Pyramid as a resurrection machine designed to propel its royal Horus to the undying circumpolar stars. The authors treat this mainstream wording as direct support for function, not alien construction.
- **[Archaeological technology caution]** Pyramid construction by a Bronze Age culture with stone/copper tools remains debated, but copper hardened with arsenic could be highly effective and Egyptians possessed arsenic compounds such as orpiment. The example warns against premature extraterrestrial engineering claims.
- **[Critique of alternative theory]** Ancient-astronaut arguments overreach by assigning pyramid construction to aliens. As ordinary construction questions are solved, false claims unfairly devalue the broader paleocontact question; the authors prefer Egyptian texts as direct testimony.
- **[Author premise]** Starting from the unproven premise that paleocontact occurred makes Egyptian ritual purpose appear coherent. The chapter admits it is using that premise to reinterpret evidence rather than deriving contact deductively from architecture.
- **[Historical development]** Egyptian funerary practice changed by dynasty. Earlier documents may preserve clearer memory; later ones show ongoing interpretation and possibly new intermittent contact. Pantheism may reflect diverse encounters, but this is speculation.
- **[Author historical argument]** Egypt's sudden complex civilization, and civilization's wider appearance after more than 100,000 years of Homo sapiens, is presented as evidence suggestive of an external civilizing source called alien for lack of a better term.
- **[Documentary architectural function]** Djoser's pyramid, earliest known, aligns north to circumpolar stars. Tomb, prepared body, stellar destination, and ritual operation combine into Wilkinson's resurrection-machine function.
- **[Egyptian ontology]** The afterlife is not a vague postscript but one event in continuing identity. Life originates "elsewhere," the Doubleworld of gods and *ka*, which touches this world through a statue, image, mummy, or false door in a *ka* chapel.
- **[Myth/text summary]** Atum creates life by masturbation and scatters seed through space, first producing a divine couple who then generate others. Indian Shiva similarly ejaculates into cosmos after interruption during intercourse with Uma/Parvati.
- **[Author directed-panspermia comparison]** Solo divine seeding differs from primitive imitation of ordinary sexual reproduction and is compared with directed panspermia. The authors argue one god's fluid produces a non-divine external world, whereas divine coupling would produce another god.
- **[Comparative theology]** Ptah offers another solo-creation version; Genesis's single God creates in six days; *Sefer Yetzirah* describes emanations later identified with ten Kabbalistic spheres. Trained sensory withdrawal makes invisible realms perceptible and is compared with astronaut isolation training.
- **[Philological/conceptual claim]** Egyptian *ka* appears in words for magic, sorcerer, testicles, vagina, pregnancy, copulation, thought, and intention. The authors infer a unifying power in which invisible will extends into visible action or the Doubleworld.
- **[Historical-esoteric comparison]** Ficino and Bruno also linked occult power, sexuality, romantic love, and intention. The authors use them to show that mind, fertility, and magical extension remained connected in Renaissance thought.
- **[Egyptian anthropology]** Every person has a *ka*, often a perfect youthful double represented by raised arms/horns, the life force and immaterial psycho-spiritual aspect. Resurrection requires union with *ba*.
- **[Egyptian anthropology]** *Ba* approximates soul, initially human-shaped and later a human-headed bird. Coffin Texts identify it with Osiris and sometimes treat it as physically enabling the corpse to walk and copulate.
- **[Textual inference]** Coffin Texts repeatedly describe immortal dead returning to sex with humans. The authors compare them with Genesis's sons of God, but this is a cross-text interpretation rather than proof of identical beings.
- **[Egyptian anthropology]** Union of energized *ba* and *ka* manifests *akh*, effective agency in this world possessed by living leaders or ritually activated dead. *Sheut* is shadow/essence; *ha* body; *ib/ab* heart; *ren* name.
- **[Ritual interdependence]** Correct rites make the dead able to walk, breathe, eat, speak, and copulate. Monumental scale, stellar alignment, and ongoing offerings show an intense dependence between living humans, dead rulers, and stars.
- **[Opening of the Mouth]** The rite animated mummies, coffin faces, and statues, borrowing childbirth implements such as umbilical scissors and serpent knife. Its most prominent instrument is the hook-shaped Great Adze/Thigh of Set, modeled on the Big Dipper.
- **[Material fact and inference]** The adze was made from meteoric iron and could include magnetite; the Dipper points toward north and immortality. The authors acknowledge that evidence does not show Egyptians knew magnetism or navigation compasses, making the material-symbol match curious rather than conclusive.
- **[Astronomical theology]** Orion, with Sirius, rises and sets and represents Osiris's Underworld passage; the circumpolar Dipper never sets and marks final immortality. Djoser's orientation and later Great Pyramid shafts join both systems.
- **[Source limitation]** Earliest writing survives mainly in tombs; Fifth Dynasty papyri concern mortuary administration; later mathematical and medical papyri retain mythology. Therefore modern observers may overread Egypt as exclusively death-centered because the surviving sample is biased.
- **[Author inference]** The intensity of pyramid and mummification technology implies Egyptians had some evidence of viability. Calling it wish fulfillment or superstition marginalizes rather than explains the investment.
- **[Modern analogy]** Global space programs consume money, engineering, science, public faith, and entire institutional systems despite few people flying. The authors call them modern pyramid building and imagine descendants judging present space belief as we judge Egypt.
- **[Historical publishing case]** Swiss hotelier Erich von Däniken (born 1935) published *Chariots of the Gods?* in 1968, arguing aliens built monuments and reading Ezekiel literally. The authors describe their own book as revisiting the general question with deeper texts while rejecting arbitrary monument decoding.
- **[Extremist misuse case]** William Dudley Pelley led the Silver Legion/Silver Shirts in 1930s America, embraced UFOs after federal imprisonment, and, following British Israelite David Davidson, used pyramid dimensions to predict Jewish destruction on September 17, 2001. The chapter notes the uncanny proximity to September 11 without endorsing the prediction.
- **[Publishing history]** Von Däniken's editor Wilhelm Utermann was a Nazi author/editor who worked for the NSDAP and *Völkischer Beobachter*. The authors say extraterrestrial and hidden-master ideas can combine with racial purification and domination.
- **[Mainstream critique]** Egyptologists call unsupported alternative theorists "pyramidiots." The authors concede that scorn is often justified by faulty evidence, channeling, contrary-data suppression, and cover-up accusations, while maintaining mainstream knowledge is itself incomplete.
- **[Documentary controversy]** Robert Bauval's Orion Correlation Theory aligns Gizeh's three pyramids with Orion's belt and dates them by declination to about 10,500 BCE rather than approximately 2550 BCE. Djoser's Step Pyramid is placed around 2630 BCE, eighty years earlier than the standard Great Pyramid date.
- **[Institutional conflict]** Zahi Hawass accused Egypt-born Belgian Catholic Bauval of Zionist motives, arguing an older date displaced Egyptian builders. Hawass controlled archaeological access and combined antiquities protection with nationalism and gatekeeping.
- **[Public event]** In April 2015 in Cairo, Hawass left Graham Hancock's presentation within minutes after Bauval's name was mentioned; Hancock's attempt to reason with him failed, and video went viral. The authors use the outcome as evidence that personality and politics distort critique.
- **[Scientific dispute]** Griffith Observatory astronomer E. C. Krupp attacked Bauval's alignment/math, briefly appearing to debunk it; other astronomers called Krupp's critique unfounded and said he forced ancient belief into modern science. The BBC edited a broadcast segment involving his criticism.
- **[Documentary scholarly case]** Robert Temple's 1976 *The Sirius Mystery* argued that Mali's Dogon possessed knowledge of Sirius's binary nature before nineteenth-century telescopes. Temple was a Royal Astronomical Society Fellow, Sanskrit/Oriental Studies scholar, and Joseph Needham colleague.
- **[Critique of critics]** Alternatives said European astronomers conveyed the knowledge during an 1893 eclipse expedition and Dogon instantly integrated it, or that lack of equal knowledge about other stars invalidated Sirius knowledge. The authors consider these objections weak but do not prove Temple's extraterrestrial explanation.
- **[Intellectual lineage]** Temple's conversations with Arthur Young inspired the book. Young was one of the original human "Nine" communicating with Andrija Puharich in 1952-1953 and later identifying as the Egyptian Ennead; Temple served Young's Foundation for the Study of Consciousness in 1966 after knowing him five years and studied at University of Pennsylvania from 1961-1967 before moving to England in October 1966.
- **[NASA reception case]** Captain Robert Freitag (1920-1998), NASA Advanced Programs assistant director, Saturn development manager, and participant in Mercury, Gemini, Apollo-Soyuz, and Spacelab from 1963-1986, encouraged Temple and introduced Jesco von Puttkamer.
- **[NASA conflict]** German-born Jesco von Puttkamer (1933-2012), recruited by Wernher von Braun and distinct from his identically named Nazi relative who fought with a Freikorps brigade in Munich in 1919-1920 before becoming Hitler's liaison officer, attacked Temple and "little green men" on NASA stationery, urged publishers to reject him, and later partially recanted to the German publisher. He was also a *Star Trek* film consultant and convention regular.
- **[Intelligence interest]** Temple reports US intelligence attention to his Dogon/Sirius/Egypt work without explanation. The authors then connect him with Peter Tompkins, an OSS agent during World War II who declined CIA recruitment.
- **[Documentary publishing case]** Tompkins's *Secrets of the Great Pyramid* (1972) argued that pyramid dimensions encode Earth's size and site/stellar relationship. Temple praised it; the authors say if deliberate, the structure is Earth model, celestial alignment, and transformation module.
- **[Intelligence/consciousness case]** Tompkins coauthored *The Secret Life of Plants* (1973) with Christopher Bird, a CIA-linked Vietnam veteran and Rand Corporation worker. The book claims plants communicate at a distance and have consciousness; the authors flag a recurring intelligence interest in esoteric technology.
- **[Author sociology of science]** Stigma around the Phenomenon can end careers and invite accusations of instability. Some professional resistance is warranted, but personality, jealousy, politics, and institutional control also shape what counts as legitimate.
- **[Documentary scholarly case]** *Hamlet's Mill*, appearing around von Däniken's book, was written by MIT professor Giorgio de Santillana and German anthropologist/history-of-science professor Hertha von Dechend. It drew partly on Godfrey Higgins's 1836 *Anacalypsis* and Leo Frobenius (1873-1938), who located Atlantis in Nigeria.
- **[Theory summary]** *Hamlet's Mill* reads global mythology as coded astronomy, especially precession. The equinoctial point moves backward through zodiacal signs at about 2,160 years each; Aquarius would begin around 2600 CE; Hipparchus (190-120 BCE) traditionally receives credit for discovery.
- **[Astronomical detail]** Precession changes the north celestial pole: Thuban in Draco served around early pyramid times, whereas Polaris serves today. The theory says myths encoded alarm at this slow change.
- **[Criticism]** No quantitative/physical evidence proves ancient precession knowledge; the effect spans millennia, unlike eclipse or meteor; linguistic methods and myth readings were criticized as dated and idiosyncratic. The authors accept that the book did not prove its case.
- **[Author defense]** Its conceptual value is myth as a pre-literate data carrier whose storytellers need not understand the science. Strong claims of universality provoke valid contextual objections, but radical anti-universalism can Balkanize knowledge and dismiss all diffusion.
- **[Documentary support for limited claim]** Indian and Daoist alchemy from at least the first century CE, plus European alchemy, demonstrably use mythic/twilight language for technical content. This proves coded myth can occur, not that all myths encode precession.
- **[Third hypothesis]** Instead of science first or myth first, an anomalous contact experience could generate both myth and code simultaneously. This is the authors' speculative resolution.
- **[Epistemological claim]** Science reorganizes categories with evidence, but consciousness remains poorly understood and perhaps cannot know itself. Technology increases private isolation and mass vulnerability while death remains ignored.
- **[Historical architecture]** Narmer and First/Second Dynasty rulers used mastabas. Djoser's Third Dynasty Step Pyramid begins monumental stellar resurrection; Khufu's Great Pyramid follows in the source's stated Fifth Dynasty progression.
- **[Modern mausoleum comparison]** Cesar Chavez, Kim Il-sung, Ho Chi Minh, Lenin, and Mao have preserved or monumentalized bodies; Lenin and Mao are especially relevant as atheist communist leaders whose corpses occupy state shrines.
- **[Documentary design claim]** Lenin's mausoleum was deliberately modeled on Djoser's Step Pyramid and prompted by Tutankhamun's tomb discovery in 1923. The north-south Mao tomb and northeast-facing Lenin tomb differ in alignment, but both preserve ruler bodies for mass visitation.
- **[Historical quotation]** Lenin told H. G. Wells in 1920 that interplanetary communication would require revision of philosophical, moral, and social views and make violence obsolete through limitless technical potential.
- **[Documentary transmission]** The Soviet Union sent the first radio message to a possible extraterrestrial civilization toward Venus on November 19-24, 1962: MIR on November 19, LENIN and SSSR on November 24, all in Morse code. The authors note the unresolved assumption that recipients know Morse.
- **[State mythology]** Lenin was called a star or new sun in state-controlled media. In 1932 Kazimir Malevich proposed Lenin atop a spaceship-skyscraper, "Lenin as rocket," while Orthodox institutions were suppressed.
- **[Preservation science]** Lenin's arteries were severed and blood drained, preventing normal formalin circulation; injections entered organs, treatment repeats about every two years, and plastic replaces failed tissue. The authors say an Egyptian priest would reject this cosmetic sleeping appearance as inadequate bodily integrity.
- **[Egyptian mummification]** Four canopic jars preserve organs; natron dries the body; bitumen and other substances slow decay. The process unexpectedly preserves DNA better than embalming, enabling recovery from Tutankhamun; high pyramid radioactivity is reported, with Temple suggesting it aided preservation.
- **[Author functional inference]** Egyptians may not have anticipated cloning but deliberately preserved walking, speaking, and reproductive capacity in body and organs. The Opening of the Mouth enabled identity to inhabit the body in the Doubleworld even though priests knew the corpse would not physically rise before them.
- **[Mission architecture analogy]** Priests are Mission Control, pyramid is launch vehicle, pharaoh is astronaut, mummy is hibernating traveler. This is an author analogy, not an Egyptian phrase except for the documented celestial journey.
- **[Scientific thought experiment]** Special relativity's twin problem lets a two-year near-light-speed traveler return after thirty Earth years. The authors construct an explicitly fanciful ancient case in which an Egyptian woman enters torpor, travels, returns unchanged after thirty years, and inspires sarcophagus, stellar alignment, and immortality ritual.
- **[Boundary around speculation]** The chapter calls that scenario fanciful and dramatic, one way to fit architecture and rite into a logical narrative, not recovered history.
- **[Modern technology comparison]** Proposed astronaut torpor uses glass-like sarcophagi for months/years; NASA values psychological fitness for International Space Station and Mars-duration missions. Egyptian texts likewise demand physical and spiritual readiness for dangerous travel.
- **[Liminal identity]** Death leaves physical remains while personhood persists between This World and Doubleworld. Offerings and recitations both preserve social memory and, in Egyptian belief, give the dead substance and agency.
- **[Comparative afterlife technology]** Catholic Purgatory and Tibetan *Bardo Thodol* guide souls through intermediate stages. The Tibetan deceased lama receives recited instructions through six consciousness levels toward rebirth; the Dalai Lama's successor is identified by birth time, objects, and familiar people.
- **[Historical religious development]** Third Dynasty deceased kings identify with Horus; Fifth Dynasty texts shift toward Osiris. Because Osiris maps to Orion, Great Pyramid alignment elaborates rather than discards the earlier circumpolar/Set system.
- **[Alternative astronomical theory]** One proposal says Great Pyramid shafts form a Milky Way passage rather than point precisely to Orion/pole. Egyptians called the Milky Way the Winding Waterway, associated with star-covered Nut, and Pyramid Texts say the pharaoh crosses it to reach circumpolar stars.
- **[Archaeological case]** A Tomb of Osiris replica lies 114 feet below Gizeh in the Shaft of Osiris: stone sarcophagus on an island within a manmade canal, with three levels and ancient second-level sarcophagi. Postwar vandalism destroyed four columns and damaged the coffin.
- **[Comparative architecture]** Catholic churches orient altars east; Chinese *feng shui* and Indian *vaastu* encode celestial relationships; Stonehenge and North American Mound Builder sites also align astronomically. The authors ask whether some transported the dead, not merely tracked seasons.
- **[Mound Builder research]** William F. Romain's "azimuths to the Otherworld" find Hopewell burials aligned to solar/lunar points, often Moon/west. Adena sites can predate Solomon's Temple; without writing, orientation and grave objects are the surviving evidence.
- **[Book of Gates]** By the Eighteenth Dynasty, the Duat has twelve gates, likely twelve night hours. The dead travel by boat with gods/assistants, face two gods and a serpent at each gate, must name them, confront fire and Apophis, and have the heart weighed against Maat's feather at gate five.
- **[Historical theology]** Set, despite later opposition to Osiris/Horus, protects Ra by wounding Apophis; red dawn/dusk is Apophis's blood. The authors read this as survival of earlier Set worship.
- **[Modern telepresence analogy]** *Ka* and *ba* are compared with avatars, robots, Internet presence, Skype, music video, and drones. A US operator sees through and fires from a drone over Afghanistan; an Egyptian might call the human-headed flying double a *ba*.
- **[Digital immortality analogy]** Peter and Tom communicate through electronic doubles; Tom's music videos may persist a century, giving a visible/audible *ka*. The authors distinguish ancient reality categories from modern metaphor and ask whether technological extension is a practical immortality.
- **[Final synthesis]** Spaceflight, hibernation, electronic remote action, Mission Control, relativity, sarcophagus, stellar routes, and soul doubles were imitated or anticipated by Egyptian systems. The closing question leaves both possibilities open: space travel and resurrection machine may be the same project.

## Concepts
- **Identity after death** — persistence of individual memories, thoughts, and selfhood after bodily failure, distinguished from simple longevity or revival.
- **Alien mortality** — the implication of alleged crash corpses and the absence of immortal experiencers: advanced beings may heal or revive but still die.
- **Alchemy / transformation** — chemical, medical, psychological, and spiritual mutation; lead-to-gold and elixir goals correspond to individuation and resurrection.
- **Doubleworld** — Egyptological term for the adjacent divine/dead domain that intersects this world through image, statue, mummy, tomb, and ritual doorway.
- **Resurrection machine** — Toby Wilkinson's phrase for Djoser's Step Pyramid as a device to propel the royal Horus to undying stars; the authors generalize it to Egyptian funerary technology.
- **Ka** — youthful psycho-spiritual double/life force, tied linguistically in the chapter to intention, thought, magic, fertility, and sexuality.
- **Ba** — mobile soul/personality, later shown as a human-headed bird, able in texts to walk, fly, and copulate; joins *ka* after death.
- **Akh** — effective, activated agency produced by *ka/ba* union, allowing living or dead persons to cause change in This World.
- **Sheut / ha / ib or ab / ren** — shadow, body, heart, and name, additional components preserved or addressed by funerary practice.
- **Ka chapel / false door** — tomb site where offerings cross toward the Doubleworld and sustain the dead.
- **Opening of the Mouth** — ritual animation of mummy, statue, or coffin image so it can breathe, speak, eat, and function in the other realm.
- **Great Adze / Thigh of Set** — Big-Dipper-shaped meteoric-iron implement used in Opening of the Mouth, combining celestial origin, north, and immortality symbolism.
- **Circumpolar stars / Orion / Sirius** — stellar destinations and stages: never-setting northern stars mark immortality; Orion/Osiris and Sirius mark death-resurrection movement.
- **Winding Waterway** — the Milky Way in Egyptian texts, crossed by the dead king toward circumpolar immortality.
- **Pyramid, Coffin, and Going Forth texts** — evolving Egyptian instruction systems for after-death transformation, celestial navigation, obstacles, and divine identification.
- **Duat / Book of Gates** — Egyptian Underworld and its later twelve-gate map, with named guardians, boat travel, moral weighing, and serpentine danger.
- **Pyramidiot** — Egyptological insult for poorly evidenced pyramid speculation; the authors accept many criticisms while objecting to blanket exclusion.
- **Orion Correlation Theory** — Bauval's claim that Gizeh mirrors Orion's belt and encodes a construction/reference epoch around 10,500 BCE.
- **Sirius Mystery / Dogon knowledge** — Temple's claim that Dogon religion contains pre-telescopic knowledge of Sirius's binary nature, disputed on cultural-transmission grounds.
- **Coded myth** — *Hamlet's Mill* thesis that oral stories preserve technical astronomy even when transmitters do not know it; demonstrated in limited alchemical cases but not proven universally.
- **Universalism / diffusionism / Balkanization** — debate over cross-cultural patterns: sweeping common-origin claims can erase context, while forbidding comparison can fragment human knowledge beyond synthesis.
- **Secular religion** — state veneration of atheist leaders through preserved bodies, mausoleums, pilgrimage, celestial metaphors, and promises of enduring political identity.
- **Torpor / twin problem** — suspended animation plus relativistic time difference, used in the chapter's speculative model for apparent immortality.
- **Bardo Thodol** — Tibetan guidance through six intermediate consciousness levels toward rebirth, compared structurally with Egyptian after-death instructions.
- **Azimuths to the Otherworld** — Romain's term for solar/lunar orientations of Hopewell and other North American mortuary sites.
- **Remote presence / technological double** — drones, Skype, recordings, avatars, and robots as modern functional analogues of *ka* and *ba*.
- **Technological immortality** — continued operation or perceptibility of a person's image, voice, data, or agency after physical death.

## Experiments, examples & case studies
- **[Reported encounter pattern: Anne Strieber]** Dead relatives/persons appear alongside alien beings in some contact accounts. The observation's outcome is a question about post-death identity; the source gives no case count, dates, institutions, or controlled study.
- **[Film cases: 1951 and 1952]** *The Day the Earth Stood Still* makes Klaatu's revival temporary; *Red Planet Mars* imagines aliens living to 300. The authors use both as conceptual controls separating resurrection, longevity, and immortality.
- **[Historical occult case: Cagliostro, 1784]** Before hieroglyphic decipherment, Cagliostro creates Egyptian Freemasonry from projected mysteries. The outcome illustrates how unreadable texts invite invention.
- **[Decipherment case: Champollion, 1820s]** The Rosetta Stone enables actual translation and sharply changes the evidentiary field. Joseph Smith's nonexistent "Reformed Egyptian" serves as a counterexample of unconstrained translation claims.
- **[Hellenistic chronology]** Alexander's 323 BCE conquest, the Ptolemaic third-to-first centuries BCE, Septuagint production, Hermetic collection, and Caesar's circa 48 BCE Alexandrian destruction locate Greek-Egyptian synthesis historically.
- **[Mainstream Egyptological case: Djoser, circa 2630 BCE]** North-aligned Step Pyramid is described by Wilkinson as a resurrection machine for Horus among undying stars. This is the chapter's strongest documentary bridge between architecture and celestial immortality.
- **[Materials case: arsenical copper]** Adding a small amount of arsenic can harden copper; Egyptians possessed orpiment/arsenic sulfide. The authors say this gives a terrestrial answer to one construction objection and protects the broader inquiry from a weak alien-tool claim.
- **[Egyptian creation cases]** Atum scatters seed through solo masturbation; Ptah offers another solitary creation; Shiva ejaculates into cosmos after interruption. The authors interpret the nonordinary reproduction as seeding/directed-panspermia imagery.
