# Glossary

Legend: unit pointers use each source note's frontmatter `chapter:` number (the knowledge-base unit number), which is not the book's own printed chapter number. Units 0–4 are front matter (dedication, Tom's and Peter's acknowledgments, Vallée's foreword, prologue); units 5–12 are the eight main chapters ("Alien Genesis" = printed Ch. 1 … "Planet Earth: The Hot LZ" = printed Ch. 8); unit 13 is the Endnotes. All definitions are the book's own, attributed where apt; the authors are Tom DeLonge & Peter Levenda ("the authors"), writing *Sekret Machines: Gods* (2017).

## Concepts

- **Advaita / nonduality** — The Sanskrit term for the collapse of opposites the authors adopt as the volume's needed epistemology: the Phenomenon cannot be understood while thought rigidly separates science/religion, mind/matter, normal/paranormal, or alien/human. Represented as $+1 + -1 = 0$. Units: 11, 12. Related: the Phenomenon, science-and-mysticism partnership.
- **Alien (the term)** — The authors' provisional and admittedly inadequate label for the intelligence behind contact; they concede its pejorative use demonizes people and belief and reduces speakers to "tinfoil hat" stereotypes, but keep it for lack of a better word. In older traditions "Alien" is first a *theological* quality (Jonas's wholly transcendent God) before it means extraterrestrial. Units: 4, 5, 7. Related: the Phenomenon, alien God.
- **Alien Genesis / ur-punkt** — The proposed original contact (or contacts) — the "origin point" — from which the authors intend to reconstruct human civilization as a ten-thousand-year Cargo Cult. Units: 4, 5. Related: Cargo Cult, paleocontact.
- **Alien God** — Hans Jonas's term for the wholly transcendent Gnostic deity, "absolutely alien" to a universe it neither made nor governs; and the Mandaean Stranger/Messenger who descends through planetary spheres to liberate humans through knowledge. Units: 5, 12. Related: Demiurge, Gnosticism, cosmic prison.
- **AMP (Anomalous Mental Phenomena)** — The neutral official term adopted (to reduce the "giggle factor") for precognition, telepathy, psychokinesis, remote viewing, and "remote perturbation," pursued by US agencies through contractors like SRI and SAIC. Units: 5, 13. Related: remote viewing, STAR GATE, giggle factor.
- **Anthropocentric sovereignty** — Wendt & Duvall's modern political order constituted by reference to humans alone; it cannot decide the "exception" of a superior non-human authority without undermining the basis of its own power. Units: 6. Related: authoritative taboo, non-object, "reality is whatever the king says."
- **Apkallu / abgal** — The seven Mesopotamian sages (Babylonian *apkallu*, Sumerian *abgal*), often non-human and pre-Flood, who taught civilization; the proposed antecedent of the biblical *bene elohim*. Oannes, rising fish-clad from the Persian Gulf to teach agriculture and astronomy (via Berossus), is the vivid case. Units: 6, 7. Related: bene elohim, Oannes, sons of God.
- **Astral plane** — The "starry plane," traced through Plato and Plotinus: the soul travels outward through planetary and stellar spheres and returns with knowledge, while spirits enter through a human "material basis." Mediumship and channeling are its secularized descendants. Units: 5. Related: material basis, celestial flight.
- **Authoritative taboo** — The institutional need *not* to ask what UFOs are, because a genuine answer might force an exception to human sovereignty; hence "the UFO can be known only by not asking what it is." Units: 6. Related: anthropocentric sovereignty, non-object, disclosure.
- **Axis mundi** — The world-axis joining underworld, Earth, and sky, represented by the ziggurat, the central tree/pole, the body's central channel, or a monument's north–south line. Units: 5, 6, 8, 10. Related: ziggurat, Sushumna, Aztlan.
- **Baetylus** — A sacred stone, often meteoric; the word derives from Jacob's Beth-El ("house of God"). It joins heavenly descent, divine presence, and physical site. Units: 7. Related: gate of heaven, meteoric iron.
- **Bene elohim / divine council** — The "sons of God" of Genesis 6, read (via Heiser and Penchansky) as non-human ministers or a divine council able to assume bodies, mate with women, and produce the Nephilim. Units: 6, 7. Related: sons of God, apkallu, Nephilim.
- **Cargo Cult** — The volume's master analogy: a religion formed when people interpret incomprehensible delivered technology and its operators as divine, preserve leftover artifacts as sacred relics, and reproduce the encounter through ritual imitation without understanding its mechanism. Applied to civilization itself, to the Works of the Chariot, and to Egyptian funerary practice. Units: 4, 5, 7, 9, 10. Related: Alien Genesis, secret machines, simulacrum.
- **Celestial flight / ascent** — Movement from Earth to the stars, heavens, or underworld by ladder, chariot, mount, pole, or altered state, reversing the gods' original descent; its cross-cultural recurrence is treated as possible memory of contact. Units: 7, 9, 10. Related: merkava mysticism, Pace of the Stars, stairway to heaven.
- **Chaoskampf** — The Near Eastern battle between divine forces that produces cosmic order (explicit in the *Enuma Elish*, ideologically suppressed in monotheistic Genesis). Units: 6, 11, 12. Related: Enuma Elish, Gigantomachy, war in heaven.
- **Consciousness (as control interface)** — The authors' hypothesis that anomalous craft, and contact itself, may respond to trained consciousness rather than manual controls — motivated by (not proven from) the absence of steering in ancient accounts and in Lazar-type reverse-engineering reports. Units: 7, 10. Related: altered-state control interface, sekret machine of consciousness.
- **Cosmic prison** — The Gnostic universe of concentric spheres with Earth as its deepest dungeon, guarded by planetary Archons. Units: 5, 12. Related: Archons, Alien God, matter–spirit dualism.
- **Demiurge** — The subordinate Platonic creator of the material world in Gnostic Genesis, opposed to spirit and mistaken for the highest God; in the Gnostic reading he forbids the fruit while the Serpent (the Alien God) offers liberating knowledge. Units: 5, 12. Related: Alien God, Gnosticism, serpent.
- **Directed panspermia** — Crick and Orgel's proposal that life's genetic material was deliberately seeded on Earth by an intelligence; introduced as reported science and a "platform for research," with fuller treatment deferred to Book Three. Units: 5, 8, 9. Related: panspermia, genetic code as message.
- **Disclosure** — Public acknowledgment of alien contact. The authors regard government-centered disclosure as both strategically unlikely and conceptually "manifestly absurd," because it assumes governments control a contact they have demonstrably failed to control, and because admitting that failure would expose institutional impotence. Units: 4, 6. Related: authoritative taboo, national security, cultural bias.
- **DNA consciousness** — John K. Grandy's reported claim that DNA is "conscious" enough to organize its functions, with consciousness scaling from quantum particles through atoms and molecules to humans; used speculatively to reinterpret blood sacrifice and the sacrificial victim. Units: 8. Related: psycho-biological connection, genetic selection, blood sacrifice.
- **Enuma Elish** — The ~1,000-line Babylonian creation epic (found at Nineveh, 1849): Apsu and Tiamat precede creation; Marduk kills Tiamat to form sky and earth and makes humanity from the blood of the defeated Kingu for labor. The authors treat it, not Sitchin's reconstructions, as the strong documentary base. Units: 5, 6. Related: Marduk, Tiamat, Kingu, slave race.
- **Gate of the Gods / Babylon** — The liminal overlap between "This World" and the "Other World," named after *Babili*; in ritual architecture its most concentrated form is the blue-glazed summit chamber of Marduk's ziggurat where the god descends annually. Units: 6. Related: Two Worlds interpretation, ziggurat, sacred marriage.
- **Genetic code as message / "the Kazakh signal"** — shCherbak and Makukov's reported 2013 claim (the "WOW! signal of the terrestrial genetic code") that non-random arithmetic/ideographic patterns and a symbolic "zero" in the code imply deliberate construction and off-planet origin. Reported as speculation, deferred to Book Three. Units: 8, 13. Related: directed panspermia, DNA consciousness.
- **Gigantomachy** — The Greek "war of the Giants" against the Olympians; the authors read it both as a memory of non-human conflict and as the symbolic revolt of matter/chaos against spirit/order, parallel to Tiamat/Marduk and the Nephilim. Units: 12. Related: chaoskampf, Nephilim, war in heaven.
- **Giggle factor** — The phrase handwritten beside "SAP" and "LIMDIS" on a January 1995 DIA-related memorandum, evidencing official awareness of ridicule around psychic/anomalous research. Units: 13. Related: AMP, GRILL FLAME, STAR GATE.
- **Gnosticism / gnosis** — A first-centuries-CE family of mystical currents claiming privileged knowledge (*gnosis*) beneath scripture, in which spirit/light is imprisoned in matter and salvation is knowledge-enabled escape; its reversed Genesis makes the Serpent an awakener and the creator a Demiurge. Units: 5, 12. Related: Alien God, Demiurge, cosmic prison.
- **GRILL FLAME / remote perturbation** — Code names for US military remote-viewing work; the declassified 1995 retrospective assigns Army MIRADCOM responsibility for a "remote-perturbation" (mental sabotage) program. Units: 13. Related: AMP, STAR GATE, remote viewing.
- **Hot LZ** — Vietnam-era slang for a helicopter landing zone under fire; the volume's closing image of a divided, weaponized Earth that would be dangerous to visitors and easy to dominate. Units: 12. Related: advaita, the Phenomenon.
- **Merkava / hekhalot mysticism** — Jewish "chariot"/"palaces" ascent practice derived from Ezekiel's vision, involving solitary altered consciousness and passage through guarded celestial levels, with the risk of mistaking the enthroned Metatron for God. Units: 7. Related: Ezekiel's vision, celestial flight, Metatron.
- **Nephilim** — Interpreted here (rejecting Sitchin's "fallen ones") as an Aramaic-derived word meaning *giants* — the offspring of the *bene elohim* and human women; Gilgamesh is read as a Nephilim-like figure. Units: 6, 11, 12. Related: bene elohim, sons of God, Gigantomachy.
- **Non-object** — An anomalous thing rendered institutionally *unseen* rather than merely unidentified; science and the state preserve their categories by refusing it object status. Units: 6. Related: authoritative taboo, rejected knowledge.
- **Oannes** — The fish-clad amphibious sage of Berossus who rose daily from the Persian Gulf to teach agriculture and astronomy, refusing terrestrial food; the most vivid apkallu tradition and a recurring "non-human teacher" model. Units: 6, 7. Related: apkallu, abgal.
- **Opening of the Mouth** — The Egyptian/Babylonian rite animating a mummy, statue, or coffin so it can breathe, speak, eat, and act in the Doubleworld, using a Big-Dipper-shaped meteoric-iron adze (the "thigh of Set"). Units: 7, 9. Related: ka, resurrection machine, thigh of Set.
- **Pace of the Stars / Pace of Yu** — The living Daoist ascent in which an adept internalizes, circles, and "treads" the Big Dipper's seven visible (and two invisible) stars as gates toward the polestar, using the body as "launch pad" and consciousness as vehicle; the halting Pace of Yu is its shamanic root. Units: 10. Related: celestial flight, Big Dipper, dark stars.
- **Paleocontact** — The authors' inferred prehistoric contact with non-human/celestial powers, used to explain the recurring structure of origin myth, ascent ritual, and monumental architecture; distinguished sharply from the weak "ancient astronaut" particulars they reject. Units: 5, 6, 9. Related: Alien Genesis, the Phenomenon.
- **The Phenomenon** — The authors' umbrella term for the cross-historical complex that includes sightings, physical traces and contact, anomalous communication, apparent violations of physical law, observer trauma, religious visions, telepathy, and institutional confusion; deliberately broader than "UFO." Units: 2, 4, 5, 6. Related: contact, Two Worlds interpretation, sekret machine.
- **Psycho-biological connection** — The chapter-8 proposal that worldwide blood sacrifice, blood symbolism, and creation-from-divine-substance constitute a category of evidence — a bodily/genetic debt owed to absent creators — distinct from architecture or myth alone. Units: 8. Related: blood sacrifice, DNA consciousness, dead gods.
- **Rejected knowledge** — The "Other World" as modern receptacle for religion, hallucination, the paranormal, and any observation outside accepted science; the modern equivalent of the ancient underworld. Units: 6. Related: non-object, Two Worlds interpretation.
- **Resurrection machine** — Toby Wilkinson's phrase (adopted by the authors) for Djoser's north-aligned Step Pyramid as a device to propel the royal *ka* to the undying circumpolar stars; generalized to Egyptian funerary technology as a "technology of identity." Units: 9. Related: ka, ba, akh, Opening of the Mouth, Doubleworld.
- **Reversed conjuration** — The "Alien Genesis" thought experiment inverting magician and summoned spirit: the apparent flying saucer becomes the other world's magic circle and *humans* become the summoned spirits. Explicitly disclaimed as true; offered only to open consciousness-centered hypotheses. Units: 5. Related: ceremonial magic, control system, magic circle.
- **Sacred marriage (hieros gamos)** — The annual divine–human union at the ziggurat summit (Marduk and the high priestess), read by the authors as ritual memory of non-human/human sexual contact; a living Yogyakarta sultan–goddess rite is cited as a survival. Units: 6, 13. Related: sons of God, Nephilim, Gate of the Gods.
- **Science-and-mysticism partnership** — The volume's stated method: a non-denominational "spiritualized refinement" joined to material science, because the Phenomenon manifests in both physical and consciousness dimensions — a "shotgun wedding" that would transform both. Units: 5, 10. Related: advaita, multidisciplinary approach.
- **Secret machine / sekret machine** — Technology whose essence stays hidden when observers lack the words to describe it; ritual and arcane language preserve the encounter while concealing its operation. The spelling *sekret* evokes Cold War Eastern Europe, "secretion," a machine that is "secreted," and the mystical *secret*. Units: 4, 10. Related: Cargo Cult, sekret machine of consciousness.
- **Sekret machine of consciousness** — The brain and nervous system reconceived as an interior contact technology whose trained operation may connect with the craft in the sky and eventually enable interstellar travel. Units: 10. Related: consciousness as control interface, celestial flight.
- **Shamanism** — Proposed as the earliest recognized religion: a socially recognized specialist enters altered states, travels between worlds, and undergoes initiatory death/dismemberment and rebirth; its structure is compared with UFO-encounter trauma. Units: 5, 7. Related: initiatory dismemberment, liminal space, ecstatic flight.
- **Slave race** — The explicit Mesopotamian function of created humanity: labor that relieves the gods and supplies them food and drink (from Kingu's blood), needing none of Sitchin's gold-mining addition. Units: 6. Related: Enuma Elish, Kingu, apkallu.
- **Sons of God** — The *bene elohim* who took "daughters of men" and fathered the Nephilim (Genesis 6); the recurring model of divine–human interbreeding the authors trace across Mesopotamian, Greek, and Indian traditions. Units: 6, 7, 12. Related: bene elohim, Nephilim, sacred marriage.
- **Sovereignty and the UFO** — Wendt & Duvall's 2008 political-theory paper (cited with page references) arguing that acknowledging the UFO would challenge anthropocentric state authority; the source of the "authoritative taboo" and the "known only by not asking" formulation. Units: 6, 13. Related: anthropocentric sovereignty, authoritative taboo.
- **STAR GATE** — The umbrella program name (with GONDOLA WISH, GRILL FLAME, CENTER LANE, SUN STREAK) under which US remote-viewing research ran; declassified files ground the volume's claim that agencies treated the Phenomenon as consciousness-implicating. Units: 13. Related: AMP, remote viewing, GRILL FLAME.
- **Stepped-down appearance** — The proposal that the Phenomenon adopts forms just beyond a period's own technology (the 1897 airships, later saucers) so witnesses can notice and imagine it — "not looking to be ineffable, just wonderful." Units: 6. Related: cultural filtering, the Phenomenon.
- **Thigh of Set** — The Big-Dipper-shaped meteoric-iron adze used in the Opening of the Mouth; also a name for the Dipper, joining heaven-descended material, magnetism/north, gods, and immortality. In *War*'s companion note it also glosses the Grail "wound in the thigh." Units: 7, 9, 13. Related: Opening of the Mouth, meteoric iron, Big Dipper.
- **Two Worlds interpretation** — The working diagram of "This World" (ordered, human, visible) and an "Other World" (non-human, chaotic, unrecognized) whose narrow overlap is the Phenomenon's domain; a simplification that does not exclude multiple worlds or dimensions. Units: 6. Related: Gate of the Gods, rejected knowledge.
- **Vymanika Shastra** — A 20th-century *channeled* text (shown so by a 1974 critical study) elaborating ancient Indian "aircraft"; the authors reject it as evidence for genuine Vedic technology while accepting older Vedic aerial-vessel references as suggestive. Units: 11, 13. Related: vimana, documentary criticism.
- **War in heaven** — The recurring primordial conflict among non-human factions (Tiamat/Marduk, asura/deva, Light/Darkness in Manichaeism, Titans/Olympians, angels/fallen angels) the authors argue cultures remember "from different allegiances and vocabularies." Units: 11, 12. Related: chaoskampf, Gigantomachy, consubstantial opponents.
- **Wheel / mandala / flying disk** — The authors' proposal that these are *cognate* representations of a single real object or event: the Buddhist *ratana-cakka* (distinguished from the moon, flying as vehicle and weapon), the Kalacakra mandala, the winged disk of Ashur/Ahura-Mazda, and Ezekiel's wheeled chariot. Units: 11. Related: cakra, Kalacakra, faravahar.
- **Ziggurat** — The stepped sacred architecture linking Earth, the summit deity, and descending underworld levels; both temple and modeled world-axis, and the concentrated form of the Gate of the Gods. Units: 6. Related: axis mundi, Gate of the Gods, sacred marriage.

## People Index

- **George Adamski** — 1950s "contactee" claiming an ongoing relationship with Venusians; cited as the benign-contact model and template for the tall blond "Nordic" type, with many regarding him as fraudulent. Units: 4, 7.
- **Robert Bauval** — Proponent of the Orion Correlation Theory dating Giza's pyramids to c. 10,500 BCE; the flashpoint of the Hawass/Krupp controversy the authors use to show how personality and politics distort scholarly critique. Units: 9.
- **Berossus** — Third-century-BCE priest of Bel Marduk who recorded the Oannes tradition, the vivid apkallu source. Units: 6.
- **Francis Crick** — Co-discoverer of DNA's structure who (with Leslie Orgel) proposed directed panspermia; the volume's scientific anchor for the "seeded code" thesis, deferred to Book Three. Units: 5, 8, 9.
- **Erich von Däniken** — Author of *Chariots of the Gods?* (1968); credited with opening the ancient-contact question but criticized for arbitrary monument-decoding and a Nazi-linked editor (Utermann). Units: 5, 9.
- **John K. Grandy** — New York researcher whose reported peer-reviewed work proposes "DNA consciousness" scaling from quantum particles to humans; used speculatively in the sacrifice and genetics arguments. Units: 8.
- **Zahi Hawass** — Egyptian antiquities gatekeeper who attacked Bauval's motives and access; his Cairo walkout from Hancock's talk is the authors' exhibit of status-defense over rebuttal. Units: 9.
- **Michael S. Heiser / David Penchansky** — Biblical scholars whose "divine council" reading of the *bene elohim* underwrites the authors' apkallu/sons-of-God synthesis. Units: 6.
- **Hans Jonas** — Scholar of Gnosticism whose "the alien taken absolutely is the wholly transcendent" supplies the volume's "Alien God" and cosmic-prison vocabulary. Units: 5, 12.
- **C. G. Jung** — Cited for the mandala/*rotundum* archetype and the reading of the UFO as a projected symbol of psychic wholeness (individuation) — the authors' main *alternative* to their own object-hypothesis. Units: 11.
- **Jeffrey J. Kripal** — Historian of religion whose refusal to separate "the real, the religious, and the fictional" (*Authors of the Impossible*) is the project's methodological license. Units: 3, 4, 5.
- **Bob Lazar** — Named only as a report of a captured-craft "control problem" (no visible steering) used to *pose* the consciousness-as-interface question, not to endorse the captured-craft claim. Units: 7.
- **Marduk** — Babylonian creator-god who defeats Tiamat, orders the cosmos, and wins the Tablet of Destinies; the pivotal figure of the *Enuma Elish* and the chaoskampf. Units: 5, 6.
- **Muhammad** — His *Mi'raj* (nocturnal ascent from the Ka'aba to Jerusalem and through the seven heavens on the Buraq with Gabriel) is a central case in the celestial-flight pattern. Units: 7.
- **Andrija Puharich** — Army medical officer, occultist, and advocate of weaponizing ESP; convener of the 1952–53 Round Table séances with "the Nine," the elite contact circle threaded from the prologue into *War*. Units: 4.
- **Hal Puthoff / Russell Targ / Ingo Swann** — The SRI remote-viewing figures (with Vallée's early participation) linking a major physical-UFO investigation to experimental consciousness research; reserved for fuller treatment in Book Two. Units: 5, 13.
- **Zecharia Sitchin** — Self-taught translator whose Annunaki/"twelfth planet"/gold-mining thesis the authors reject as philologically unsupported, while treating his popularity as evidence of an unmet hunger for an origin account. Units: 5, 6, 8.
- **Robert Temple** — Author of *The Sirius Mystery* (Dogon knowledge of Sirius B); his inspiration by Arthur Young ties the Dogon controversy to the Nine/Ennead consciousness network. Units: 9.
- **Jacques Vallée** — Astronomer, computer scientist, and long-time UFO researcher; author of the foreword, the model of the multidisciplinary method, and the source of the "control system" reading of the Phenomenon. Units: 2, 3, 5.
- **Alexander Wendt / Raymond Duvall** — Political theorists whose "Sovereignty and the UFO" (2008) supplies the authoritative-taboo argument. Units: 6, 13.
- **Toby Wilkinson** — Egyptologist whose description of Djoser's Step Pyramid as "a resurrection machine designed to propel its royal owner" to the undying stars is the authors' strongest mainstream bridge between architecture and celestial immortality. Units: 9.
- **Arthur Young** — Bell-helicopter inventor turned paranormal researcher; one of the human "Nine," later identifying them with the Egyptian Ennead, and the link between the Sirius/Dogon work and the contact-consciousness circle. Units: 4, 9.
